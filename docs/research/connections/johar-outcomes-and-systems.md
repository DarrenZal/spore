# Johar — Outcomes & Systems: From Predicted Goods to Emergent Cohesion

**Source:** Indy Johar, "Outcomes & Systems: From Predicted Goods to Emergent Cohesion," Substack.
**URL:** https://indyjohar.substack.com/p/outcomes-and-systems-from-predicted

## What It Is

A substantial essay (4280w) that argues for a paradigmatic shift from "predicted goods" (fixed outcome states delivered by centralised authority) to "emergent cohesion" (coherent futures arising from within the system through distributed agency). The essay contends that in complex systems, the very act of predefining a goal space "risks collapsing the diversity, adaptability, and latent intelligence of the system." Predicted outcomes encode power asymmetries — whose future is predicted, whose epistemology shapes the end state — and compress the possibility space that systems need to remain adaptive.

The alternative is not the absence of direction but a different relationship to it. The essay proposes five structural shifts: (1) from outcome delivery to capacity building — the strategic unit becomes "agentic capacity" (the distributed ability to sense, interpret, act, and learn together), (2) from vectorised control to relational development — stewarding the quality of inter-agent relationships rather than forcing trajectories, (3) from static plans to rotating attractors — provisional goals as temporary orientation points rather than final destinations, (4) from central authority to hybrid multi-horizon stewardship — operating simultaneously on compliance realities, transitional infrastructures, and long-horizon investments, (5) from predicted endpoints to emergent coherence — success measured by relational maturity (hosting diversity without fragmentation, reconfiguring in response to feedback, generating evolving coherence without external command).

The essay names "relational maturity" as the measure of system growth: the ability to host increasing diversity without fragmenting, sustain feedback without collapse, reconfigure in response to emergent tensions, and generate internal alignment through dialogue rather than enforcement.

## What Spore Already Does

1. **Emergent coherence through coordination loop:** Spore's sense → propose → commit → learn cycle is designed for emergent cohesion — coherent direction arising from distributed participation and ongoing revision rather than centralised goal-setting.

2. **Agentic capacity as design target:** The grammar's distributed governance, overlapping membership, and commitment protocols are infrastructure for agentic capacity — enabling agents to sense, interpret, act, and learn together within structured coordination.

3. **Rotating attractors through claim lifecycle:** Spore's claims are revisable, contestable, and subject to attestation decay — the governance equivalent of "rotating attractors" that orient without fixing. The learning membrane treats all knowledge as provisional.

4. **Multi-horizon stewardship:** The grammar operates across multiple horizons simultaneously: operational commitments (immediate), strategic commitments (medium-term), and constitutional commitments (long-term, load-bearing). This is the multi-horizon governance the essay calls for.

## What the Essay Offers

### 1. The Critique of Predicted Outcomes as Power Inscription

The essay's strongest contribution is the recognition that fixed outcomes are not neutral targets but power inscriptions: they encode whose future is being pursued, whose epistemology counts, and whose possibility is being compressed. For Spore, this reinforces the grammar's design: the commitment framework does not prescribe outcomes but provides the conditions under which outcomes can emerge from distributed participation. The constitutional layer constrains process (sovereignty, contestability, forkability) without constraining substance.

### 2. From Vectorisation to Relational Development

The essay distinguishes "vectorised" governance (organising the system to move toward a single predefined target) from "relational development" (governance that evolves through the deepening of inter-agent relationships). In vectorised systems, deviations are treated as inefficiencies. In relational systems, deviations are information. For Spore, this provides vocabulary for what the grammar already embodies: the discourse graph, multi-projection, and contestability mechanisms treat divergence as signal rather than noise. The grammar is relational development infrastructure.

### 3. Emergent Coherence as the Goal Space

The essay redefines the goal space itself: not a destination but "a relational, systemic capacity — a living, sensing, evolving field of shared orientation." Goals are not enforced but arise through emergent coherence; they are not universal but contextually situated and plural; they are not final but iteratively reframed. For Spore, this maps directly to the grammar's relationship with canon: canon is not a fixed body of truth but an evolving field of tested commitments that gains coherence through the learning membrane's staged promotion.

### 4. Relational Maturity as Success Measure

The essay proposes "relational maturity" as the measure of system success: hosting diversity without fragmentation, sustaining feedback without collapse, reconfiguring in response to tension, generating alignment through dialogue rather than enforcement. This connects to the relational-topology-and-context-design synthesis from infrastructure wave 1 — both name the quality of inter-agent relations as the decisive measure. For the complexity theme, relational maturity is the concept that bridges the worldviews-cohesion family to prior work: it names what "cohesion" actually means in a system that refuses fixed endpoints.

## Open Questions

1. **How does "emergent cohesion" relate to the coherence concept in governance-under-volatility?** Both name coherence as the measure of governance success. Governance-under-volatility frames coherence as resilience under turbulence (keeping the system deciding and revising). This essay frames coherence as relational maturity (hosting diversity and generating alignment through dialogue). Whether these are the same concept applied at different scales or distinct concepts that should eventually converge is unresolved.

2. **Does the critique of predicted outcomes apply to Spore's own staged promotion?** The learning membrane uses staged promotion to move claims toward canon. Is "canon" a predicted outcome — a fixed knowledge state — or an emergent coherence that arises from the membrane's process? The essay suggests the latter, but whether the grammar's promotion criteria should be explicitly framed as conditions for coherence rather than tests for truth is worth examining.

3. **Where does worldviews-cohesion-and-systems-change end and holding-complexity-beyond-control begin?** Both families address systems change under complexity. The fork-in-the-system essay names the civilisational structural condition. The outcomes-and-systems essay names the governance response (emergent cohesion over predicted outcomes). Whether these are the same family at different levels of abstraction or genuinely distinct families with different design implications will depend on Batch 2 processing.

The essay provides the vocabulary for what Spore already embodies: emergent coherence through the coordination loop, rotating attractors through the commitment lifecycle, relational maturity through distributed governance. At 4280w with concrete governance mechanisms and a clear success measure (relational maturity), this is a strong anchor for the family.
