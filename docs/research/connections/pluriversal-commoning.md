# Pluriversal Commoning

This bridge note grounds two Spore/IC canon surfaces — Spore's `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol` (cross-field composition between distinct worlds) and IC's `intelligence-primitives` / `memory-layers` (interoperability-as-institutional) — against the P2P Foundation wiki's Escobar–Gudynas–Mignolo–Bresnihan lineage on the pluriverse. It is Wave 3 of Pass 2 Phase B and cross-cuts Spore and IC: the pluriversal framing names a single structural correction both projects must absorb, at different surfaces of their respective canons.

The bridge note's central move is to import a **political, not technical**, reading of translation across ontologies. Pass 1's `knowledge-commons-governance` and the Tier 1a field-scan treated interoperability as a layered negotiation problem (technical + semantic + rights). The wiki's Escobar–Gudynas–Mignolo–Bresnihan cluster names a more specific hazard: when the knowledge systems being interoperated are *ontologically* distinct — built on different assumptions about what knowledge *is*, what counts as value, and what counts as a subject of rights — a coordination layer framed as a neutral technical substrate performs a colonial operation by default, converting plural worlds into a single world with an interface. This note carries disconfirming hypothesis **DH-IC-1** from Phase A (pluriversal framing — translation is political, not technical, not a neutral coordination layer) as the note's load-bearing tension and encodes it in an R-claim rather than resolving it.

## 1. What "pluriverse" names

Arturo Escobar, cited at length on the wiki's `Pluriverse` page, introduces the pluriverse as "a multiplicity of mutually entangled and co-constituting but distinct worlds" — a concept for the global world built against what Escobar calls the **One-World doctrine** (OWW): the assumption that all human and non-human life can be coordinated under a single ontology (typically, the ontology of individuals, markets, and abstracted commodities). The Zapatista dictum Escobar cites in the `Ontological_Dimension_of_Commoning` page is the operational form of the concept: *Un mundo donde quepan muchos mundos* — a world where many worlds fit.

Carlos Tornel's framing on the same page sharpens the non-relativist reading: "The pluriverse is not a celebration of endless difference, nor a call for relativism. It is a political project — a collective 'no' to destruction and domination, and many 'yeses' to other ways of living." The pluriverse is not post-truth pluralism and not a weak tolerance stance; it is a specific political refusal to absorb distinct worlds into one world plus a specific commitment to maintaining the conditions under which those worlds persist.

For Spore and IC, this first move matters because both projects invoke composition vocabulary — cross-field composition (Spore), interoperability-as-institutional (IC) — without currently specifying what kind of composition is being performed when the composed surfaces carry different ontologies. The wiki's pluriverse concept names a class of compositions where the ontologies are not merely differently expressed but differently constituted, and where absorption of one surface into another is the default failure mode rather than a deviation.

## 2. The ontological dimension of commoning

Escobar's `Ontological_Dimension_of_Commoning` page states the load-bearing position directly: "Within relational worlds, the defense of territory, life and the commons are one and the same. This is the ontological dimension of commoning. ... The perseverance of communities, commons, and movements and the struggles for their defense and reconstitution can be described as ontological." The inverse is equally load-bearing: "what occupies territories and commons is a particular ontology, that of the universal world of individuals and markets (the OWW) that attempts to transform all other worlds into one; this is another way of interpreting the historical enclosure of the commons."

This reframes enclosure itself. Classical commons theory treats enclosure as the privatization of a shared resource. Escobar treats enclosure as an ontological operation: the conversion of relational worlds (where territory, life, and commons are one and the same) into a single world of individuals-and-markets where territory is property, life is labor, and commons are resources available for rent. Under this reading, a coordination surface that imposes a market ontology on a relational commons is itself enclosure, regardless of whether any physical fence is erected or any legal title transferred.

For Spore's `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol`, the implication is specific: federation across fields whose ontologies include both market-based and relational worlds cannot be framed as a neutral technical composition operation without performing an enclosure move on the relational worlds. Federation architecture must either (a) explicitly refuse market-ontology defaults at the protocol layer, or (b) explicitly decline to federate across ontologies whose composition would require the absorption of one by the other.

## 3. Buen Vivir as concrete pluriversal ontology

The wiki's `Buen_Vivir` and `Good_Living` pages supply a concrete, non-abstract instance of what a pluriversal ontology actually contains. Eduardo Gudynas's formulation on the `Buen_Vivir` page names several load-bearing properties that a One-World coordination surface would be forced to erase. **Decolonization of knowledge** is first: "recognizing, respecting and even taking advantage of the diversity of forms of knowledge. Breaking (or trying to break) with the dominant power relations, abandoning the pretension of a privileged knowledge which should master and direct the meeting of cultures and knowledge forms. This is more than epistemological relativism, as it lies on a decolonization of knowledge." **Extended communities** is second: "Political communities (in the sense of hosting actors with political expression) are not restricted to people, and there is a place in them for the non-human (in some cases they may be other beings or elements of the environment, or spirits)." **Another ethic to recognize and assign values** is third — the claim that "Nature becomes the subject of value," which Gudynas specifies as "a radical change compared to the prevailing Western ethic, where everything around us is an object of value."

René Ramírez on `Good_Living` closes the ontological specification: "Good Living is based not only in 'having' but mainly in 'being', 'do' and 'feel'." The wealth of a community is measured in time, relation, and non-dominating reciprocity rather than in goods-produced, capital-accumulated, or transactions-closed. "Economics deals with public, private and common goods but has forgotten about what Aristotle calls 'relational attributes', which relates to the questions of how we create a society and how human beings relate to nature. We can take this into account by measuring, not through monetary means, but by measuring time."

This is load-bearing for IC specifically. An `intelligence-primitives` surface that models inter-agent understanding as "deciphering or simulating the movements of others" — the representationalist reading the bridge note `ic.connection.autopoiesis-and-structural-coupling` already declines (§9 R3) — also commits a One-World move at the ontology layer when the "others" in the model are actors from a relational-world commons whose agency includes non-human subjects (rivers, mountains, spirits, ancestors) and whose valuation is in relational attributes rather than in goods-exchanged. The autopoiesis note's enactive reframe is necessary but not sufficient: it corrects the cognitive-science framing without yet correcting the ontological framing of what kinds of agents and values are being coupled in the first place.

## 4. Bresnihan's more-than-human commons: the relational subject

Patrick Bresnihan on `More-Than-Human_Commons` gives the positive specification of the relational ontology the pluriverse names: "the starting point is not an individual subject separated from other people and the world around them, but a relational subject who is always already caught up in a world that is intimately shared. This understanding is not based on an ideal but on the materially and socially constituted relations and practices that tie humans and non-humans together within a particular collective or territory." The etymology Bresnihan draws on — commons from "com" (together) + "munis" (under obligation) — collapses the commons-as-resource framing that underwrites classical property theory: "The commons is not a 'thing' that we have access to because we hold a title deed or authorization, but something that is ours because we produce and care for it, because we common."

Bresnihan's page also names the historical mechanism by which the One-World doctrine was applied to pluriversal commons: "capitalist enclosure and biopolitical control necessarily involve the de-valorizing and 'invisibilizing' of those myriad, situated relations and practices of (re)production that exist between people and the manifold resources they rely on (De Angelis 2007; Federici 2001; Shiva 2010)." The move from noun to verb — from "commons" as resource to "commoning" as continuous practice — is the linguistic correlate of the pluriversal move from One-World ontology to many-worlds ontology: in both cases, the static/relational distinction tracks an underlying claim about what kind of thing is being coordinated.

For Spore's `commons-as-verb` vocabulary (v2 concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml) and for the existing `boundary-commoning` bridge note, the implication is that cross-field composition between commons whose commoning practices are relational and commons whose commoning practices are productionist is not a symmetric composition — it requires active preservation of the relational character against a default drift toward productionist framing.

## 5. Mignolo's decolonial cut: Occidentalism, not just capitalism

The wiki's `Indigenous_De-Colonial_Movement_in_Latin_America` page carries Walter Mignolo's sharpening of the diagnosis via Fausto Reynaga: "from an indigenous perspective, however, the problem is not capitalism alone — it is Occidentalism, which includes both capitalism and Marxism." The load-bearing operational move is Mignolo's distinction between de-Westernisation (questioning who controls capitalism) and de-coloniality (questioning the capitalist economy and its epistemology together). The pluriverse inherits the de-colonial position: "de-coloniality frontally questions the capitalist economy, whereas de-Westernisation only questions who controls capitalism."

This is load-bearing for both projects because both Spore and IC inherit substantial vocabulary from the Western commons-studies canon (Bauwens, Bollier, De Angelis, even the P2P Foundation wiki itself) whose own sources are primarily Occidental — European and North American — even when they cite non-Western traditions. Mignolo's cut warns that citing Buen Vivir, Ubuntu, Pachamama, or Mama Kiwe while leaving the coordination-substrate architecture untouched performs a de-Westernisation move (recognition) without a de-coloniality move (restructuring). The wiki's own position on the `Indigenous_De-Colonial_Movement` page makes the failure mode visible: "The 'Latin left' (led by criollos and mestizos, that is, 'white' Bolivians) is grounded in the genealogy of European thought. Broadly speaking, however far it may have branched out, its trunk is Marxism-Leninism." The warning generalizes: a P2P-commons canon whose trunk is Ostrom-Bauwens-De Angelis with pluriversal vocabulary grafted on is grounded in the genealogy of European thought, however far it may have branched out — and the grafting does not by itself perform decolonization.

## 6. Tensions (cautions for any Spore/IC canon edit)

**Decentralization-myth-bundle header**: Pass 2 Phase B Wave 1 Seeds 2+3 + Wave 2 Seed 1 (peer-governance-wikipedia-opposition) together form the decentralization-myth bundle — the cross-cutting opposition discipline that any Pass 2 supporting bridge note invoking P2P, peer-to-peer, decentralized, federation, or commons vocabulary must reference. This note invokes "federation," "commons," and (implicitly) peer-coordination vocabulary. The three failure modes the bundle names — topology-without-governance (Seed 2), production-without-asset-redistribution (Seed 3), peer-structure-without-administrator-accountability (Seeds 1+7) — all apply to any pluriversal composition surface the note's R-claims propose. The pluriversal move does not displace the bundle; it adds a fourth failure mode (ontology-absorption-as-translation, named below as §6.3).

The three main tensions specific to this note:

1. **Pluriversal framing risks becoming Occidental-recognition without Occidental-restructuring (Mignolo's cut).** The most-available canon move after reading this note is to add a "pluriversal-commoning" alias to IC interoperability-as-institutional and Spore federation-protocol vocabulary while leaving the underlying coordination architectures unchanged. Mignolo's distinction between de-Westernisation and de-coloniality makes this move the failure mode, not the solution. Canon adoption must either carry a restructuring obligation — admitting that coordination architectures grounded in the Ostrom-Bauwens-De Angelis trunk require specific interventions (§5 above) to avoid performing a One-World operation on pluriversal participants — or explicitly decline the pluriversal framing rather than grafting it onto an unchanged architecture.

2. **Relational-ontology integration risks under-specifying authorization operations** that Spore's membrane vocabulary and IC's memory-governance vocabulary already carry. The more-than-human commons extends political community to non-human actors (rivers, mountains, ancestors, spirits); adopting this framing at the canon layer without specifying *how* a river or ancestor is authorized to speak in a federation surface — through which human stewards, with which ethnolinguistic and legal discipline, under what conditions a dispute between a human and non-human stakeholder resolves — leaves the authorization surface under-specified and invites capture by whoever claims to speak for the non-human participant. Canon adoption must import the more-than-human vocabulary together with an explicit authorization-operation discipline, not separately.

3. **Ontology-absorption-as-translation is the fourth decentralization-myth failure mode and is the load-bearing concern of DH-IC-1.** A coordination surface that frames translation between ontologies as a technical problem — schemas, protocols, interoperability layers — defaults to translating into the dominant ontology (typically the market-ontology the coordination substrate itself inherits). This is the ontological analog of topology-without-governance (Seed 2): an apparent pluralism at the surface that conceals an actually-monist coordination at the substrate. The wiki's Zapatista dictum *un mundo donde quepan muchos mundos* is explicitly incompatible with one-world-with-a-translation-interface; it requires that the translation itself be political negotiation (who decides what is translatable, on whose terms, at what cost to the less-dominant ontology) rather than a technical coordination primitive. IC canon must not absorb pluriversal vocabulary without concurrently re-framing translation as political.

4. **Buen Vivir is not fully decolonial per Gudynas's own reading.** The `Buen_Vivir` page records Gudynas's caveat that Buen Vivir "owes as much to political philosophy as it does to indigenous worldviews. ... It certainly doesn't require a return to some sort of indigenous, pre-Colombian past." This caveat is load-bearing: Buen Vivir is a composite concept drawing on both indigenous cosmovisions *and* Western feminist and environmental critiques. A canon adoption that treats Buen Vivir as a pure indigenous-knowledge primitive inherits a purity claim Gudynas himself rejects. The pluriversal move must be made without over-authenticating any single vocabulary as "the" pluriversal substrate; the point is the multiplicity, not a specific alternative-but-still-unified ontology.

5. **Federation participants may not want to interoperate.** P-IC-4 in Phase A proposed that "pluriversal IC might instead focus on *protecting incompatibility* and treating translation as political negotiation, not technical standardization." This is the strong reading of DH-IC-1 and goes beyond "translation is political": it is that the strongest pluriversal move may be to architect for refusal-of-federation as a first-class operation, not only for federation-with-translation. A memory-layer or federation surface that cannot honor "we decline to interoperate with you" as a well-formed output has already performed a One-World move. This point is not fully resolved in the R-claims below.

## 7. Disposition

**Disposition: clarify existing term.** The wiki evidence warrants a coordinated clarification across three Spore/IC canon surfaces: (a) Spore's `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol` should import the ontological-dimension reading of enclosure and explicitly decline market-ontology defaults at the protocol layer OR decline to federate across ontologies whose composition would require absorption; (b) IC's `intelligence-primitives` and `memory-layers` should re-frame translation-across-ontologies as a political, not technical, coordination surface per DH-IC-1; (c) both canons should acknowledge the pluriverse, the more-than-human commons, and the ontological dimension of commoning as external lineage for composition vocabulary the projects already carry, with the decolonial-vs-de-Westernisation discipline named in §5 imported as canon-review gate. None of these clarifications require new canon primitives. The R-claims below do not become canon PRs during the Move 0 moratorium posture; they are proposed clarifications for canon review.

The disposition is **clarify existing term** rather than **add primitive** because the pluriversal concept does not propose new coordination primitives — it proposes a discipline of refusal-and-restructuring applied to existing composition primitives. Adding "pluriversal-commoning" as a canon primitive without the discipline would commit the de-Westernisation-without-de-coloniality failure mode (§5, §6.1) that the concept itself critiques.

## 9. Review Claims

**R1** [review claim] [target: ic.intelligence-primitives] [concept: pluriversal-commoning]
IC's `intelligence-primitives` canon should re-frame translation-across-ontologies as a **political coordination surface**, not a technical interoperability primitive. The canon text should state (a) that the interoperability-as-institutional framing already imported in Pass 1 is necessary but not sufficient: institutional governance of a translation interface does not by itself decolonize the translation it performs; (b) that translation across ontologically-distinct worlds (Escobar's OWW vs. relational worlds per C4, C5, C9) is a negotiation over whose ontology the shared substrate inherits, with power asymmetries that are load-bearing rather than incidental; (c) that a canonical output of the pluriversal `intelligence-primitives` surface must include "refusal-to-interoperate" as a well-formed primitive, not only "interoperate-with-translation." This R-claim **encodes Phase A disconfirming hypothesis DH-IC-1**: translation across ontologies is political, not technical, and a coordination layer framed as neutral performs a colonial operation by default. The R-claim does not propose adding a canon primitive; it proposes re-framing the existing interoperability surface to carry the political-translation discipline explicitly.
*R1 is supported by C1, C2, C4, C5, C11. Carries DH-IC-1.*

**R2** [review claim] [target: spore.mycorrhizal-federation-protocol] [concept: pluriversal-commoning]
Spore's `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol` canon should import Escobar's ontological-dimension reading of enclosure (C4) and explicitly specify the protocol's posture toward cross-ontology federation. The canon text should state (a) that federation between fields whose ontologies include both market-based and relational worlds cannot be framed as a neutral technical composition operation without performing an enclosure move on the relational worlds; (b) that the protocol must either decline market-ontology defaults at the protocol layer (e.g., composition primitives that do not presuppose individual-subject agency, exchange-value accounting, or property-based access) OR explicitly decline to federate across ontologies whose composition would require absorption; (c) that the Zapatista dictum "un mundo donde quepan muchos mundos" (C5) is the operational test for federation legitimacy under the pluriversal reading — a federation surface that cannot host many worlds *without* translating them into a shared substrate has already performed a One-World move. This composes with the existing `boundary-commoning` bridge note's R1 on scope distinction (within-field vs. between-field rules): the pluriversal reading specifies a further distinction inside between-field rules between ontology-preserving and ontology-absorbing compositions.
*R2 is supported by C4, C5, C9, C10.*

**R3** [review claim] [target: ic.memory-layers] [concept: memory-governance]
IC's `memory-layers` canon should name the **decolonial-vs-de-Westernisation discipline** (C11) as a first-class canon-review gate on any clarification that imports non-Western epistemological vocabulary (Buen Vivir, Ubuntu, Pachamama, Mama Kiwe, Sumak Kawsay, more-than-human commons). The gate specifies: citing the vocabulary performs a de-Westernisation move (recognition); changing the memory-governance architecture to carry the relational-ontology constraints that make the vocabulary coherent (C7 — Nature as subject of value, decolonization of knowledge, non-instrumental rationality, extended communities including non-human actors) performs a de-coloniality move (restructuring). The canon must not absorb the vocabulary without concurrently performing at least one restructuring move from the four named in C7; otherwise the pluriversal framing decays into recognition-without-restructuring, which is the failure mode Mignolo (C11) names and Gudynas's non-purity caveat (C12) compounds. The decentralization-myth bundle applies here by proximity: the recognition-without-restructuring failure mode shares structure with topology-without-governance (Seed 2) — both perform apparent pluralism at the surface while retaining monist coordination at the substrate.
