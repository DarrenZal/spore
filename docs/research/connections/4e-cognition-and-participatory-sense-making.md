# 4E Cognition and Participatory Sense-Making

This bridge note extends Wave 1's `autopoiesis-and-structural-coupling.md` from the biological-systems level to the **collective cognitive** level. Wave 1 grounded IC's memory-layers in Maturana-Varela's organizational closure and Varela-Thompson-Rosch's enactive reformulation; this note completes the cognitive-science arc by (a) formalizing the 4E frame (embodied, embedded, enacted, extended — plus Vervaeke's emotional and exaptive) that operationalizes enaction as a multi-dimensional constraint and (b) importing De Jaegher and Di Paolo's participatory sense-making as the inter-subjective primitive IC needs at the interoperability surface. For IC this is load-bearing: governance is not primarily rule-making, it is **participatory sense-making across memory-governance boundaries** — the dyadic (or n-adic) interaction process by which two or more memory-layers, agents, or knowledge commons co-enact shared understanding, itself a governable autonomous unit distinct from its parties.

## 1. Bridge to Wave 1

Wave 1's note (`ic.connection.autopoiesis-and-structural-coupling`, §§1–5) established three distinctions for IC: operational closure vs. informational openness (§1); structural coupling as recurrent perturbation rather than transmission (§2); cognition as enaction rather than representation (§3). It also sketched the 4E frame (§4) and introduced participatory sense-making (§5). **This note does not repeat those arguments.** It extends them in three specific ways Wave 1 left unresolved:

- **4E as a multi-dimensional specification**. Wave 1 lists the 4E dimensions but treats them as a single frame. This note pulls each of the six dimensions (4E + Vervaeke's two) into separate constraint-bearing claims against IC's intelligence-primitives, so the primitives can be audited dimension-by-dimension rather than wholesale.
- **Participatory sense-making as a governance primitive, not just a cognitive-science finding**. Wave 1 records De Jaegher-Di Paolo's dyadic-autonomy claim but leaves its governance consequence implicit. This note states explicitly that the interaction process is a **first-class governable unit** with its own operations (entry/exit, cadence, mutual incorporation, repair) that memory-governance must address directly, and threads that claim into IC's existing `interoperability-as-institutional` surface.
- **Collective (co-cognising) primacy**. Wave 1 cites Jose Ramos's collapse of the individual/collective divide (Embodied_Cognition page) in passing. This note foregrounds it: for IC, the **unit of cognition is the coupled group-in-environment**, not the individual agent with network glue. That reframes memory-layers as sediments of group coupling history, not as individual-agent archives. This load-bears DH-IC-2 (selfhood-as-assembly — selfhood itself is the coupling product, not the precondition) and DH-IC-3 (cognition-is-enaction — the interaction dynamic is where meaning is generated, not the symbol layer on top of it).

The Wave 1 disconfirming hypotheses DH-IC-2 and DH-IC-3 are inherited here and threaded through the R-claims below. The proposals in this note are consistent with Move 0 moratorium posture — they propose clarifications of existing IC canon language, not new canon primitives.

## 2. 4E is a six-dimensional audit grid, not a single frame

The wiki's `4E_Cognitive_Science` page (citing Alexander Beiner) catalogs the four core dimensions, and Vervaeke's extension adds two more. For IC, each dimension is a specific constraint on what intelligence-primitives can legitimately claim. Read as a grid rather than a slogan:

- **Embodied**: cognition is inseparable from physical body, gestures, sensations; per Barbara Tversky, spatial reasoning is already embodied even when apparently abstract. For IC, this rules out intelligence-primitives modeled as disembodied symbol-manipulation — any primitive that claims to operate on "pure information" decoupled from sensorimotor grounding is working outside the 4E tradition.
- **Embedded**: cognition is inseparable from the environment to which the agent has adapted; a whale is embedded in ocean the way a human is embedded in speech community, artifacts, tools. For IC, this rules out memory-layer designs that assume a fixed neutral substrate; the layer and its environment co-specify each other.
- **Enacted**: every action changes the world and thereby changes the possibility space the agent operates in; enaction is how agents carve out the world they know. For IC, this means routing operations are not neutral transport — they enact the interaction possibilities they appear to merely carry.
- **Extended**: cognition moves through other people, technology, and environment; tools become body (the driver perceives the car's width without thinking). For IC, this means memory-layers are part of the cognitive apparatus of the agents that use them, not external accessories — which has direct governance consequences for who has standing to modify them.
- **Emotional** (Vervaeke): "all cognition is about caring or not caring about taking a risk which has affective consequences." For IC, this rules out intelligence-primitives that model coordination as affect-free optimization; care is not a soft-skill overlay but a cognitive primitive.
- **Exaptive** (Vervaeke): cognition re-purposes existing cognitive machinery — the tongue's move from food-manipulation to speech is the archetype; concept-use exapts spatial-movement machinery. For IC, this means intelligence-primitives are built by re-purposing existing coupling regimes, not by designing new ones from first principles; the IC design surface is fundamentally conservationist.

The six dimensions do not reduce to one another. IC's intelligence-primitives must satisfy all six (or explicitly decline a specific dimension with reasons), not satisfy "4E" as a unified badge. This is the audit discipline Wave 1 named but did not operationalize.

## 3. Cognition is co-cognising — the group is the primitive unit

Wave 1 cited Jose Ramos's framing from the `Embodied_Cognition` page in a supporting role. Read in full, it is the structural claim this note wants IC to inherit: "embodied cognition is the idea that our cognising of the world around us is located in us as individuals (cognising) and as groups (co-cognising). We are primarily social beings interacting and co-cognising with others, 'structurally coupled' into cultural (e.g. language, religion, tradition), and ecological / geo-graphic contexts (Maturana, 1998, p. 174)."

Two things to note. First, the parenthetical construction — "individuals (cognising) and as groups (co-cognising)" — treats the two as parallel, not hierarchical. The group is not a collection of cogniser-individuals; it is itself a cognising unit at its own level, with its own coupling history. Second, the embedding is plural: cultural AND ecological/geographic contexts. An IC memory-layer is structurally coupled simultaneously into linguistic contexts (shared vocabulary, conventions, frozen concept registries), institutional contexts (governance norms, commons law), and ecological contexts (the material substrate of servers, networks, fabrication infrastructure).

This collapses Cartesian individual-first models of IC. The right default is not "an agent with memory-layers, connected to other agents via interoperability-as-institutional surfaces." The right default is "a coupled group-in-environment whose memory-layers are the sediment of its coupling history and whose interoperability surfaces are its internal coordination dynamics." The individual agent is a secondary abstraction, useful for some purposes but not the primitive.

DH-IC-2 (selfhood-as-assembly) lands here: if the group is the cognising primitive, then individual selfhood is assembled out of the group's coupling dynamics, not the other way around. For IC this is operational: memory-layer access patterns, attribution chains, and routing dynamics *construct* the individual agents they appear to merely serve. Which means governance of those mechanisms is governance of selfhood-formation, with the ethical and political weight that implies.

## 4. Participatory sense-making as an operational governance primitive

The `Participatory_Sense-Making` page (De Jaegher and Di Paolo, extended via Fuchs) makes a claim Wave 1 recorded but did not fully exploit: "the interaction process itself can take on a form of autonomy (operationally defined). This allows us to reframe the problem of social cognition as that of how meaning is generated and transformed in the interplay between the unfolding interaction process and the individuals engaged in it."

Two words carry the weight: **operationally defined**. The dyad's autonomy is not a metaphor, a hand-wave, or an emergent property to be admired. It is an operational claim — the interaction process has its own dynamics (entry conditions, cadence, mutual-incorporation steps, breakdown-and-repair cycles, exit conditions) that are neither fully owned by either party nor reducible to their individual contributions. Fuchs and De Jaegher (2009) sharpen the phenomenological side: "Intersubjectivity, it is argued, is not a solitary task of deciphering or simulating the movements of others but means entering a process of embodied interaction and generating common meaning through it."

For IC this is load-bearing. The existing `interoperability-as-institutional` framing already names the surface ("interoperability is a governance problem as much as a technical one — schemas, protocols, and trust frames all matter"). Participatory sense-making specifies what is to be governed: the **interaction dynamic itself**, as an autonomous unit with its own operations. Concretely, the operations the IC canon should acknowledge at the interoperability surface include:

- **Entry / exit conditions**: when does a dyad (or n-adic process) become a coupling unit? When does it dissolve? These are governance acts, not background events.
- **Cadence and perturbation regime**: at what frequency do the parties perturb each other? What is the response time? These are coupling dynamics, not protocol performance metrics.
- **Mutual-incorporation protocols**: Fuchs's "common intercorporality" — the extent to which parties begin to co-specify each other's internal states. IC governance should name when mutual incorporation is permitted, restricted, or required.
- **Breakdown and repair**: what counts as interaction-layer failure (as distinct from party-layer failure)? What repair operations are licensed? These are first-class governance questions once the interaction is treated as autonomous.

None of these are currently named in IC canon. All of them become visible once participatory sense-making is imported as a governance primitive — which is what this note proposes.

## 5. The reproduction-primacy implication

Phase A synthesis §8a notes that commoning in the wiki is **first an act of reproduction** (care, provisioning, territorial stewardship), not coordination. Enactive cognition, read carefully, makes the same move at the cognitive-science level: cognition is first the maintenance of autopoietic organization under structural coupling (per Wave 1's C5), and only secondarily symbol-manipulation over representations. Sense-making is a kind of reproductive labor — the ongoing work of regenerating a coupling between a living system (individual or group) and its world.

For IC this has a specific consequence: memory-governance is **reproductive-labor governance**. Documenting, preserving, attributing, granting and revoking access — these are care operations, not administrative operations. The enactive frame provides the theoretical backing for treating them that way: the cognition the memory-layer supports is itself an act of reproductive self-maintenance by the group-in-environment, and the memory-layer is part of that reproductive apparatus.

This is not a metaphor — it is the specific position the 4E/enactive lineage takes. It also directly connects to DH-IC-3 (cognition-is-enaction): if cognition is enaction, and enaction is reproductive-maintenance under coupling, then IC's intelligence-primitives should be readable as **reproductive-coupling primitives**, and anything that fails to be so readable is out of frame.

## 6. Decentralization-myth bundle

Phase A synthesis §8d names a cross-cutting opposition discipline: **decentralization theater + digital-labor free-labor trap + administrator capture** mutually reinforce, and any IC primitive that claims "peer-to-peer" or "decentralized" inherits all three critiques by proximity unless explicitly declined. This bridge note's 4E/participatory-sense-making frame sits near this bundle and should explicitly decline it.

The decline is specific. Participatory sense-making names the interaction dynamic as autonomous, but this is not the same as claiming the dynamic is "decentralized" in the ideological sense. A dyad's autonomy does not entail symmetric authority, flat topology, or absence of governance — on the contrary, the operational definition of dyadic autonomy requires a governance surface (entry conditions, cadence, repair), which is precisely what administrator-capture, decentralization-theater, and free-labor critiques target as absent in nominal-P2P systems. IC's adoption of participatory sense-making should foreground that governance surface, not perform a "peer-to-peer" aesthetic over it. Cross-references: `spore.connection.decentralization-theater-opposition` (Wave 1), `ic.connection.digital-labor-peer-production-opposition` (Wave 2), `ic.connection.peer-governance-wikipedia-opposition` (Wave 2). IC canon language that invokes 4E or participatory sense-making should name-check these opposition notes so the frame is not read as a decentralization-theater endorsement.

## 7. Disposition

**Disposition: clarify existing term.** The wiki evidence warrants IC importing four additional clarifications on top of Wave 1's three:

1. **4E as a six-dimensional audit grid** (embodied / embedded / enacted / extended / emotional / exaptive), with each dimension constraining IC's intelligence-primitives separately;
2. **Co-cognising as the primitive unit** (group-in-environment, not individual agent with network glue), with individual selfhood as an assembly product;
3. **Participatory sense-making as an operational governance primitive** at the interoperability-as-institutional surface, specifying entry/exit, cadence, mutual-incorporation, and breakdown/repair operations on the interaction dynamic itself;
4. **Memory-governance as reproductive-labor governance**, grounded in the enactive reading of cognition-as-maintenance.

None of these require new IC canon primitives; they sharpen the theoretical backing of primitives already present (memory-layers, intelligence-primitives, interoperability-as-institutional, memory-governance). The R-claims do not become canon PRs during the Move 0 moratorium posture; they are proposed clarifications to canon language when review opens.

## 8. Claim Register

**C1** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Characteristics · orn:p2p-wiki.page:4E_Cognitive_Science]
4E cognitive science holds that cognition is embodied (inseparable from physical body, gestures, sensations), embedded (inseparable from adapted environment), enacted (every action creates new realities that constrain or open possibilities), and extended (moving through other people, technology, and environment as when tools become body). These four dimensions are not collapsible to one another; each names a distinct constraint on legitimate cognitive-primitive design.

**C2** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Characteristics · orn:p2p-wiki.page:4E_Cognitive_Science]
Vervaeke extends the 4E frame with two further dimensions: emotional ("all cognition is about caring or not caring about taking a risk which has affective consequences") and exaptive (cognition re-purposes existing cognitive machinery — concept-use exapts spatial-movement machinery, the tongue's move from food-manipulation to speech is archetypal). The full audit grid is six-dimensional, not four-dimensional.

**C3** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Embodied_Cognition]
Lakoff and Johnson, as cited by Jose Ramos on the Embodied Cognition page: "Understanding emerges from interaction, from constant negotiation with the environment and other people… the nature of our bodies and our physical and cultural environment impose a structure on our experience… recurrent experience leads to the formation of categories, which are experiential gestalts with those natural dimensions." This establishes that category-formation itself is a coupling product, not a representational operation.

**C4** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Embodied_Cognition]
Jose Ramos's formulation on the same page: "embodied cognition is the idea that our cognising of the world around us is located in us as individuals (cognising) and as groups (co-cognising). We are primarily social beings interacting and co-cognising with others, 'structurally coupled' into cultural (e.g. language, religion, tradition), and ecological / geo-graphic contexts (Maturana, 1998, p. 174)." This parallel-level construction treats the group as a cognising unit at its own level, not as an aggregation of individuals — collapsing any clean individual/collective divide in cognitive-primitive design.

**C5** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Participatory_Sense-Making]
De Jaegher and Di Paolo's participatory sense-making: "the interaction process itself can take on a form of autonomy (operationally defined). This allows us to reframe the problem of social cognition as that of how meaning is generated and transformed in the interplay between the unfolding interaction process and the individuals engaged in it. The notion of sense-making in this realm becomes participatory sense-making." The operational-definition clause is load-bearing: the dyad's autonomy is not metaphorical.

**C6** [confidence: high] [anchor: §More information · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Participatory_Sense-Making]
Fuchs and De Jaegher (2009) specify the dyadic dynamic at both systems and phenomenological levels: meaning-generation happens "(1) from a dynamical agentive systems point of view as an interaction and coordination of two embodied agents; (2) from a phenomenological approach as a mutual incorporation, i.e. a process in which the lived bodies of both participants extend and form a common intercorporality. Intersubjectivity, it is argued, is not a solitary task of deciphering or simulating the movements of others but means entering a process of embodied interaction and generating common meaning through it." This directly rejects simulation-and-representation models of inter-agent understanding.

**C7** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Relation to cognition · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Autopoiesis]
The Autopoiesis page locates the enactive reformulation downstream of autopoietic theory: "With the publication of The Embodied Mind in 1991, Varela, Thompson and Rosch applied autopoesis to make non-representationalist, and enactive models of mind, brain and behavior, which further developed embodied cognitive neuroscience, later culminating in neurophenomenology." This places 4E, embodied cognition, and participatory sense-making in a single theoretical lineage (Maturana-Varela → Varela-Thompson-Rosch → De Jaegher-Di Paolo) that IC is invoking as a whole when it claims any part.

**C8** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Characteristics · orn:p2p-wiki.page:4E_Cognitive_Science]
Within the 4E frame, the **enacted** dimension carries a specific operational claim: every action changes the world and thereby changes the possibility space the agent operates in. For any system claiming the enactive tradition, routing and interaction operations are not neutral transport — they constitute the possibility space they appear to merely carry. This rules out any framing that treats protocol surfaces as affect-free information-channels decoupled from the cognitive work they support.

**C9** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Embodied_Cognition]
The Lakoff-Johnson framing as recorded on the page emphasizes that categories are "experiential gestalts with those natural dimensions" formed from "recurrent experience." This implies that the vocabulary (frozen concepts, shared schemas, interoperability surfaces) through which a group coordinates is itself a coupling sediment — a product of recurrent group-environment experience, not a neutral scaffold laid over pre-existing agent intentions.

**C10** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Participatory_Sense-Making]
The page's framing of participatory sense-making as a way to "reframe the problem of social cognition" (not merely extend the existing frame) is structural: it replaces the default cognitivist picture in which individual cognisers construct models of each other with a picture in which the interaction dynamic itself carries cognitive work distinct from either party. This structural replacement — not an addition — is what makes participatory sense-making a governance primitive rather than a cognitive-science decoration.

## 9. Review Claims

**R1** [review claim] [target: ic.intelligence-primitives] [concept: enactive-cognition]
IC's intelligence-primitives should be auditable against the 4E six-dimensional grid (embodied, embedded, enacted, extended, emotional, exaptive) — each dimension naming a separate constraint that primitives must either satisfy or explicitly decline with reasons. Specifically, primitives modeled as disembodied symbol-manipulation fail the embodied dimension; primitives that assume a neutral substrate fail embedded; primitives that model routing as affect-free transport fail enacted and emotional; primitives designed from first principles (rather than re-purposed from existing coupling regimes) fail exaptive. Canon text should replace "4E" as a single badge with a dimension-by-dimension audit protocol. This threads DH-IC-3 (cognition-is-enaction): the audit dimensions are the specific form the enaction constraint takes in IC design.
*R1 is supported by C1, C2, C3, C8.*

**R2** [review claim] [target: ic.intelligence-primitives] [concept: collective-agency]
IC's intelligence-primitives should treat the **coupled group-in-environment**, not the individual agent, as the primitive unit of cognition. Individual agents are secondary abstractions assembled out of group coupling dynamics — their selfhood is a coupling product, not a precondition. Operational consequences: memory-layer access patterns, attribution chains, and routing dynamics actively construct the individual agents they appear to serve, which means governance of those mechanisms is governance of selfhood-formation and carries that ethical weight. Canon text should name the co-cognising group as the default primitive and the individual as a useful secondary abstraction, not the reverse. This is the direct IC consequence of DH-IC-2 (selfhood-as-assembly).
*R2 is supported by C3, C4, C9.*

**R3** [review claim] [target: ic.intelligence-primitives] [concept: interoperability-as-institutional]
IC's interoperability-as-institutional surface should acknowledge **participatory sense-making as an operational governance primitive**, and name four classes of operation on the interaction dynamic itself (not merely on the parties): (i) entry/exit conditions — when does a dyadic or n-adic coupling unit come into being and when does it dissolve?; (ii) cadence and perturbation regime — at what frequency do parties perturb each other, with what response times?; (iii) mutual-incorporation protocols — when is the "common intercorporality" Fuchs names permitted, restricted, or required?; (iv) breakdown and repair — what counts as interaction-layer failure (distinct from party-layer failure) and what repair operations are licensed? None of these are currently named in IC canon; all of them become visible once the interaction dynamic is treated as an autonomous governable unit. Decline the decentralization-theater reading explicitly: dyadic autonomy requires a governance surface, it does not deny one. Cross-reference `spore.connection.decentralization-theater-opposition`.
*R3 is supported by C5, C6, C10.*

**R4** [review claim] [target: ic.memory-layers] [concept: memory-governance]
IC's memory-governance framing should be read as **reproductive-labor governance** under the enactive frame. Documenting, preserving, attributing, granting and revoking access are care operations (reproductive maintenance of the coupling between group-in-environment and its world), not administrative operations. Canon text should state that the cognition the memory-layer supports is itself an act of reproductive self-maintenance by the group-in-environment, and the memory-layer is part of that reproductive apparatus. This aligns IC's memory-governance with the reproduction-primacy insight from Phase A synthesis §8a (commoning is first reproductive, then coordinative) and with `spore.connection.reproductive-commoning` at the cross-project field family level.
*R4 is supported by C7, C8, with Wave 1 predecessor note §3 (cognition as autopoietic maintenance).*

**R5** [review claim] [target: ic.connection.knowledge-commons-governance] [concept: knowledge-commons]
IC's knowledge-commons-governance framing should acknowledge that the **shared vocabulary, schemas, and interoperability surfaces** through which a commons coordinates are themselves coupling sediments — products of recurrent group-environment experience, not neutral scaffolds over pre-existing agent intentions. This has a governance consequence: frozen concept registries, canonical schemas, and protocol agreements are first-class commons goods under memory-governance, not implementation details. Modification of these surfaces is modification of the cognitive substrate of the commons, and should be governed accordingly (entry conditions for new terms, deprecation protocols for retired ones, repair operations for broken semantic couplings). Worked example for canon discussion: what is the governance operation when two knowledge commons attempt to merge or exchange vocabularies, beyond schema-matching? Participatory sense-making says this is a mutual-incorporation operation on the interaction dynamic, not a data-integration task.
*R5 is supported by C4, C9, C10.*
