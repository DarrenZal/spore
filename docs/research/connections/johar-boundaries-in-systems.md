# Johar — The Problem With 'Boundaries' in Systems Thinking

**Source:** Indy Johar, "The Problem With 'Boundaries' in Systems Thinking," Substack.
**URL:** https://indyjohar.substack.com/p/the-problem-with-boundaries-in-systems

## What It Is

A dense, technically precise essay (1337w) that attacks the foundational metaphor of systems thinking — "boundaries" — and proposes a rigorous replacement. The essay argues that boundary language carries container metaphysics even when softened: "porous," "semi-permeable," and "fuzzy" boundaries still treat the line as primary and couplings as secondary. The actual observable phenomenon is not disconnection but differential propagation — impacts do not pass through everything equally. Some disturbances transmit quickly, others slowly, others locally, others amplify.

The essay proposes Markov blankets as a more defensible primitive: a statistical condition (screening-off via conditional independence) rather than a geometric image (enclosure). A Markov blanket is not a spatial interface but a neighbourhood in a dependency graph — a set of variables that, when conditioned upon, renders the rest statistically irrelevant for a given description. This shifts the practical object of systems design from "drawing better boundaries" to "shaping mediation structures" — identifying the variables that concentrate dependency, understanding how they filter or amplify perturbations, and tuning coupling gains and timescales.

Critically, the essay treats coherence as conditional and gradient rather than absolute. Conditional independence is approximate and varies by dimension and timescale, producing "conditional-independence gradients" rather than clean separations. Breakdown is not a breach of an edge but a regime reconfiguration — the loss of conditions under which screening relations hold.

**Note on scope:** This essay is about systems ontology — how coherence and individuation work in coupled systems. It is distinct from entangled-freedom-and-being, which is about the developmental condition of human freedom under entanglement. The boundary-making family addresses the question "what is a system?" while the entangled-freedom family addresses the question "what is a free agent within entangled conditions?"

## What Spore Already Does

1. **Holonic architecture as mediation structure:** Spore's holons are not containers with fixed boundaries but nested, overlapping coordination scopes (semilattice, not tree). Membership is overlapping, governance is distributed, and what holds a holon together is not a boundary but a set of shared commitments and protocols — precisely the "mediating variables" the essay describes.

2. **Attestation decay as regime sensitivity:** Spore's claims decay over time without re-attestation. This is structurally similar to the essay's point that screening relations are conditional and shift under changing regimes — what was once a valid claim may lose its relevance as conditions change.

3. **Multi-projection as gradient sensing:** Spore's eight graph projections surface different views of the same underlying coordination field. This maps to the essay's conditional-independence gradients — different dimensions of the system exhibiting different pass-through properties depending on which variables are conditioned upon.

## What the Essay Offers

### 1. A Precise Replacement for Boundary Language

The essay offers vocabulary that is more precise than "boundaries" and more operational than "everything is connected." Differential pass-through names the observable phenomenon. Markov blankets name the rigorous statistical condition. Mediation structure names the design target. Conditional-independence gradients name the practical topology. This vocabulary allows Spore to describe its holonic architecture without relying on container metaphors that the grammar's own design contradicts.

### 2. Breakdown as Regime Reconfiguration

The essay reframes dissolution not as "too much energy breaching an edge" but as "the loss of conditions under which screening relations hold." When timescale separations collapse, mediating variables saturate, or coupling gains shift, the effective dependency structure reorganises. For Spore, this reframes governance failure: a holon does not fail because its boundary is breached but because the conditions that made its coordination tractable no longer hold. This connects to the governance-under-volatility synthesis — volatility changes the regime under which governance screening relations operate.

### 3. Shaping Mediation Rather Than Drawing Boundaries

The practical implication is that governance design becomes "shaping mediation structures" — identifying which variables concentrate dependency, understanding filtering and amplification, and tuning coupling properties. For Spore, this reframes the grammar's protocols and commitment structures as mediation infrastructure: they determine which influences propagate through the coordination field, with what gain, and at what timescale. The grammar's design is not about enclosing scope but about structuring transmission.

## Open Questions

1. **Should Spore adopt mediation-structure vocabulary explicitly?** The grammar currently uses "holons," "commitment protocols," and "projections" to describe its coordination architecture. The essay suggests these are mediation structures — sets of variables that concentrate dependency and structure transmission. Whether the grammar should carry this vocabulary as a design principle (every protocol is evaluated as a mediation structure) or leave it as a theoretical framing is unresolved.

   *Resolved (2026-04-07):* Yes. "Mediation structure" was enacted as a named design target in the "Interface Design: Mediation Over Demarcation" section of `holonic-network-architecture.md`.

2. **How does conditional independence relate to Spore's multi-projection?** The essay's conditional-independence gradients suggest that different projections of the same system may exhibit different screening properties. Whether Spore's eight graph projections should be understood as eight different conditioning operations — each rendering different dependencies negligible — is an interesting structural analogy that may or may not be operationally useful.

   *Closed (2026-04-07):* Conditional-independence gradients were cut from the active core during translation. Carlile's three boundary types provide equivalent analytical power without the statistical apparatus. See `boundary-making-translation-memo.md`.
