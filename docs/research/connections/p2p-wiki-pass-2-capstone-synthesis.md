# P2P Foundation Wiki — Pass 2 Capstone Synthesis

This is the closing artifact for Pass 2 of the P2P Foundation wiki intake program. Pass 1 (`p2p-wiki-post-intake-synthesis`, commit `82c2ae7`) tested four cross-project hypotheses and three per-project theses against 18 bridge notes, 256 wiki-anchored source claims, and 183 governance clusters. Pass 2 — executed in three waves (6 + 7 + 7 = 20 new bridge notes) across 2026-04-16/17 after the post-Phase-A user review — was organized differently. It did not start from outcome-testable hypotheses. It started from **structural gaps** that the Phase 0 discovery sweep revealed: areas of the wiki corpus whose absence from Pass 1 looked load-bearing rather than incidental.

Pass 2 is therefore written against a different question: *what did Pass 1 not see because it asked the right question of the wrong shelves?* The answer came out of Phase A's four-agent reading of 73 wiki pages and is organized here around the **four structural insights** that only emerged from reading across agents — none of them visible to any single agent alone.

Like the Pass 1 capstone and like the Tier 1a field scan before it, this is a **meta-note**. Its narrative synthesizes across the 20 new Pass 2 bridge notes and the 18 Pass 1 bridge notes; it carries no C-claims or R-claims of its own. The wiki-anchored evidence is one layer down, in the cited bridge notes. The repository default license applies to this note; per-page attribution is carried by the underlying bridge notes, each of which is independently CC BY-SA 4.0 where wiki-derived.

## §2 Four Structural Insights

Pass 1 reported three per-project outcomes and three cross-project findings (power-capture convergence, boundary-commoning convergence, market-dependence opposition axis). Pass 2 found something different and more load-bearing: four cross-cutting structural patterns that reframe how all three projects are motivated at the canon layer, not just what individual primitives should say. Each insight is below with (a) a one-sentence statement, (b) evidence from specific Pass 2 bridge notes, (c) what it changes for Spore/IC/PM, (d) what remains unsettled.

### §2.1 Reproduction primacy

**Statement.** Reproductive labor — care, provisioning, territorial stewardship — is a first-order power-capture concern, not a derivative cultural layer; its invisibilization is the historically prior capture mechanism, and every existing P2P primitive treats it as naturalized substrate rather than coordination surface. The three-layer coordination stack (reproduction / production / governance — see §2.3) is the architectural expression of this insight: without the reproduction layer, the other two layers cannot sustain.

**Evidence.** Primary bridge note is [`reproductive-commoning.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/reproductive-commoning.md) (Wave 2; Federici's *Communalization of Housework* and *Women and the Commons*, Bhattacharya's *Social Reproduction Theory*, Bresnihan's *More-Than-Human Commons*, Dyer-Witheford's *Circulation of the Common*). Reinforced by [`solidarity-economy-and-mutual-aid.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/solidarity-economy-and-mutual-aid.md) (Wave 3; tontines/ollas communes/CSAs as reproductive-economy operational exemplars), [`gift-economy-and-ongoing-obligation.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/gift-economy-and-ongoing-obligation.md) (Wave 3; Mauss gift-dynamics as reproductive obligation substrate), and [`hess-ostrom-knowledge-commons-framework.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/hess-ostrom-knowledge-commons-framework.md) (Wave 2; reframes knowledge-commons as care work, not access work). The Phase A disconfirming hypothesis **DH-Spore-1** (reproductive-labor invisibilization as first-order power-capture concern) was resolved **YES** at user decision and is encoded as R5 of the reproductive-commoning bridge note, targeting `spore.mycorrhizal-federation-protocol`.

**What it changes.** For **Spore**: `term.field` re-reads as a site of material reproduction (Federici's housing-as-spatial-commons lineage), not a container where separate-from-commons activity happens; `relational-agency-and-holons` re-reads with care as primary coordinating practice (Bresnihan's commons-is-a-verb); `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol` must name reproductive-labor invisibilization alongside protocol lock-in, gatekeeper roles, and data asymmetry as capture mechanisms; `mycelial-holarchy-architecture` imports Dyer-Witheford's A-C-A' circuit (Associations produce Commons to sustain further Associations — reproductive and productive labor integrated at the foundation). For **IC**: memory-governance re-reads as preserving-care rather than knowledge-access; attribution encodes ongoing obligation (Mauss/Pinchot gift-obligation), not just recognition. For **PM**: commitment-pooling acknowledges the tontines/ollas communes genealogy as pre-monetary operational prior art documented by Federici; commitment-pooling cannot be framed as contemporary protocol novelty without acknowledging the centuries-long women-led commoning lineage it encodes.

**Unsettled.** The reframing-vs-renaming question is explicit and open. If reproduction is first-order, do Spore's existing primitives (`mycorrhizal-federation-protocol`, `mycelial-holarchy-architecture`) need structural renaming to make reproduction visible in canon language, or is reproduction an emergent layer that the existing primitives can carry once their motivating language is updated? The reproductive-commoning bridge note is explicit on this point (§8): it proposes motivating-language edits plus one doctrine extension (R5), not new primitives. But the bridge note also flags that if the reframing is applied honestly across Spore's canon, the canon will *read differently*, even if none of the primitive slugs change. §6.3 of this capstone carries the tension forward as held open, not resolved.

### §2.2 Boundary theory as unifier across all three projects

**Statement.** `boundary-commoning` is the single concept that appears — directly, or through a direct cognate — in every bridge note across both passes. Spore's mycorrhizal-federation, IC's memory-governance, and PM's CVLE are all boundary-making operations at different scales. Pass 1 flagged boundary-commoning as a cross-3-project convergence (3 clusters). Pass 2 makes the pattern general: *every* bridge note is working a boundary question, whether named as such or not.

**Evidence.** Thirteen of twenty Pass 2 bridge notes cite `boundary-commoning` directly in their concepts list: [`reproductive-commoning.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/reproductive-commoning.md), [`pluriversal-commoning.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/pluriversal-commoning.md), [`autopoiesis-and-structural-coupling.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/autopoiesis-and-structural-coupling.md), [`bioregional-scope-legal-constraint.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/bioregional-scope-legal-constraint.md), [`commons-law-and-charter-lineage.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/commons-law-and-charter-lineage.md), [`cosmo-local-production.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/cosmo-local-production.md), [`decentralization-theater-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/decentralization-theater-opposition.md), [`faircoin-filtering-membrane-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/faircoin-filtering-membrane-opposition.md), [`hess-ostrom-knowledge-commons-framework.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/hess-ostrom-knowledge-commons-framework.md), [`openwashing-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/openwashing-opposition.md), [`peer-governance-wikipedia-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/peer-governance-wikipedia-opposition.md). The remaining seven notes carry boundary-theoretic structure through cognates: `filtering-membrane` (faircoin opposition; De Angelis's own cell-biology framing), `structural-coupling` (autopoiesis; Maturana-Varela's boundary formalism), `bioregional-scope` (ecological boundary-matching), `cosmo-local-production` (subsidiarity-as-boundary-logic), `commons-law` (Charter of Forest as legal boundary codification), `open-cooperativism` (Bauwens' glocal boundary), `stigmergy` (coordination by shared-medium traces, i.e., environmental-boundary operations). The single explicit opposition note on the concept is the FairCoin filtering-membrane refutation: Dallyn & Frenzel 2021 show De Angelis's filtering-membrane fails when boundaries are insufficiently specified — a direct refutation of the concept's operational surface that must be carried alongside any Spore adoption of membrane vocabulary.

**What it changes.** For **Spore**: `boundary-commoning` is not a cross-project convergence finding (Pass 1) but the *architectural pattern* all three projects instantiate at different scales (Pass 2). This changes how Spore canon motivates the mycorrhizal-federation-protocol and mycelial-holarchy-architecture: both are boundary-making apparatus, and the governance question is *which kind of boundary* (filtering-membrane in De Angelis sense / subsidiarity in cosmo-local sense / ecological in bioregional sense / legal in commons-law sense / ontological in pluriversal sense) — not whether a boundary exists. For **IC**: memory-layers re-reads as autopoietic boundaries (operationally closed, informationally open), not as porous storage tiers; interoperability-as-institutional re-reads as boundary-negotiation, not layered-standardization. For **PM**: CVLE re-reads as a four-function boundary apparatus (curation = membrane selection, valuation = within-pool vs between-pool distinction, limitation = extraction boundary, exchange = cross-boundary flow). Bioregional scope is the *outermost* boundary PM must operate within; no 501(c)(3) can legitimately represent a bioregion.

**Unsettled.** Pass 2 makes the pattern visible but does not resolve which boundary-theory variant to canonize where. Maturana-Varela autopoietic closure and De Angelis filtering-membrane and Escobar pluriversal ontological difference are not the same concept; they are three distinct boundary-theoretic traditions that cannot be merged without losing each's operational surface. The Pass 1 boundary-commoning bridge note imported the De Angelis tradition; Pass 2 shows it must be carried alongside the FairCoin refutation and the autopoiesis/pluriverse alternatives. No single boundary-theory variant is sufficient; canon should specify per-surface which variant is load-bearing.

### §2.3 Three-layer coordination stack

**Statement.** Complete commons-coordination infrastructure requires three layers: **reproduction** (care, provisioning, territorial stewardship), **production** (associational / platform / cooperative), and **governance** (Ostrom design principles, commons law, bioregional scope). Absence of any layer invites a specific failure mode. Pass 1 operated largely at the governance layer; Pass 2 names the other two layers and shows they are structurally load-bearing. Every P2P protocol implicitly operates at one layer; most projects — including Spore/IC/PM — need to specify where they sit.

**Evidence.** Pass 2 surfaces each layer explicitly:

- **Reproduction layer.** [`reproductive-commoning.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/reproductive-commoning.md) (Federici/Bhattacharya/Bresnihan/Dyer-Witheford), [`solidarity-economy-and-mutual-aid.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/solidarity-economy-and-mutual-aid.md) (tontines/ollas/CSAs/BAMA/Mutual Aid societies as operational instances), [`gift-economy-and-ongoing-obligation.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/gift-economy-and-ongoing-obligation.md) (gift-obligation as reproductive-flow substrate). Frozen-concepts v2 slugs: `reproductive-commoning`, `solidarity-substrate`, `gift-obligation`, `commons-as-verb`.
- **Production layer.** [`platform-cooperativism-constructive.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/platform-cooperativism-constructive.md) (Scholz/Bauwens/Conaty-Bollier), [`cosmo-local-production.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/cosmo-local-production.md) (Ramos/Bauwens/Kostakis; Fab Labs; DGML), [`rea-valueflows-semantic-layer.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/rea-valueflows-semantic-layer.md) (Foster/Haugen; REA ontology as bidirectional-flow semantics). Frozen-concepts v2 slugs: `platform-cooperativism`, `open-cooperativism`, `cosmo-local-production`, `open-hardware-commons`, `rea-ontology`.
- **Governance layer.** [`commons-law-and-charter-lineage.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/commons-law-and-charter-lineage.md) (Charter of the Forest 1217 / Magna Carta 1215 / modern trusts), [`bioregional-scope-legal-constraint.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/bioregional-scope-legal-constraint.md) (four-level ladder; bioregional outer boundary), [`hess-ostrom-knowledge-commons-framework.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/hess-ostrom-knowledge-commons-framework.md) (DPs as empirical patterns, not blueprint). Frozen-concepts v2 slugs: `commons-law`, `bioregional-scope`; v1: `polycentric-governance`, `inclusive-governance`.

**Failure modes by missing layer** (Phase A §8c, reinforced by opposition notes):
- Governance without reproduction → workforce burnout and extractive-by-default culture (implicit in [`digital-labor-peer-production-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/digital-labor-peer-production-opposition.md); Kleiner critique).
- Governance without production → rules without flows; vocabulary without substance (implicit in [`openwashing-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/openwashing-opposition.md)).
- Production without governance → platform capture (Uber/Airbnb as historical evidence; [`platform-cooperativism-constructive.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/platform-cooperativism-constructive.md)).
- Semantics without governance → openwashing ([`openwashing-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/openwashing-opposition.md)).
- Law without governance → enclosure via regulation ([`decentralization-theater-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/decentralization-theater-opposition.md)).
- Reproduction without production → commoning without scale ([`solidarity-economy-and-mutual-aid.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/solidarity-economy-and-mutual-aid.md); COVID-19 mutual-aid groups fragile at scale).

**What it changes.** Every canon surface must specify which layer(s) it operates at. **Spore**'s federation protocol sits at the governance layer but implicates the production and reproduction layers through the instances it federates. `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol` must specify: federation-agreements carry governance-layer clauses (Ostrom DPs, commons-law scaffolding); federated instances carry production-layer clauses (platform-cooperativism / open-cooperativism structure, REA-compatible semantics); instance operations carry reproduction-layer clauses (visible reproductive labor, no invisibilization). **IC**'s memory-governance is governance-layer; but the memory-layers it governs carry production-layer semantics (REA/ValueFlows) and reproduction-layer provenance (gift-obligation). **PM**'s CVLE is production-layer semantics; its pool-structure is reproduction-layer (commitment-pooling as provisioning commons); its federation is governance-layer (bioregional-scope outer boundary, commons-law ladder).

**Unsettled.** The three-layer frame is descriptive-structural, not prescriptive. Pass 2 does not claim that every primitive must touch all three layers. It claims that primitives whose *absence* at a given layer has historically invited a specific failure mode must specify what replaces that layer's work if not itself present. This is a canon-writing discipline, not a canon-composition rule. The question of whether Spore/IC/PM primitives should be promoted to "three-layer aware" or left layer-specific is open.

### §2.4 Decentralization-myth bundle

**Statement.** Three failure modes ride together whenever "decentralized" or "peer-to-peer" appears in canon vocabulary: (a) **decentralization theater** (Yarvin; Brastaviceanu 2025; systems that display decentralization while retaining admin keys / protocol-governance / operational dependencies); (b) **digital-labor-as-free-gift** (Scholz/Kleiner; unpaid peer production appropriated by platforms into shareholder value); (c) **administrator-capture-in-peer-governance** (Kostakis 2010 on Wikipedia; Gallus on admin-class accumulation). Every Pass 1 and Pass 2 bridge note that touches P2P / decentralized / peer-production primitives inherits all three critiques by proximity unless the canon explicitly declines each. Pass 2 introduced a corpus-wide **3-sentence header** convention: any note touching P2P / decentralized / peer-production vocabulary carries a header acknowledging the bundle and linking to the three opposition notes.

**Evidence.** The three opposition notes are [`decentralization-theater-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/decentralization-theater-opposition.md) (Wave 1; Yarvin + Brastaviceanu 2025 blockchain-DAO limits), [`digital-labor-peer-production-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/digital-labor-peer-production-opposition.md) (Wave 1; Kleiner venture-communism critique + Scholz digital-labor), and [`peer-governance-wikipedia-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/peer-governance-wikipedia-opposition.md) (Wave 2; Kostakis 2010 + Simonite 2013 + Gallus administrator-class drift). The bundle is explicitly cited as a 3-sentence header in [`platform-cooperativism-constructive.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/platform-cooperativism-constructive.md) (Wave 3), [`cosmo-local-production.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/cosmo-local-production.md) (Wave 3), and [`rea-valueflows-semantic-layer.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/rea-valueflows-semantic-layer.md) (Wave 2). The [`faircoin-filtering-membrane-opposition.md`](/Users/darrenzal/projects/spore/docs/research/connections/faircoin-filtering-membrane-opposition.md) note (Wave 1) supplies the empirical closing evidence: Dallyn & Frenzel 2021 (*Antipode*) document FairCoin's failure as the confluence of all three (convertibility pressure that exposed the decentralization theater, volunteer-labor dynamics that drained the project, administrator-capture of the valuation function).

**What it changes.** The corpus-wide convention now is: any bridge note across Spore/IC/PM that uses the words "decentralized," "P2P," "peer-to-peer," or "peer production" as positively-valued primitives carries the 3-sentence header pointing at the three opposition notes. Canon surfaces adopting such language inherit the same discipline: the language must either be (a) scoped away from these critiques (e.g., "federated multi-stakeholder co-op" rather than "decentralized platform"), or (b) carried alongside explicit acknowledgment of the three failure modes. **Spore** `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol` is a test case: "federation" per se does not inherit the bundle, but "peer-to-peer federation" does. **IC** `intelligence-primitives` have been cleaner on this (IC never marketed as "decentralized AI") but the memory-layers primitive crossing between instances must carry the discipline. **PM** `protocol` language around "peer-matching" must explicitly decline decentralization-theater framing and carry platform-cooperativism constructive framing in its place.

**Unsettled.** The 3-sentence header is a convention not a canon primitive. Whether the convention should be promoted into canon writing rules (applied to the canon layer, not just research/bridge notes) is a canon-steward decision for the post-moratorium review.

## §6 Unresolved Tensions Carried Forward

Three tensions from Pass 2 must be held open in canon review, not resolved prematurely. Each has an explicit trigger condition for re-opening and an explicit evidence threshold for resolution.

### §6.1 DH-PM-1 — Accounting-dependence deeper than market-dependence

**Tension.** Pass 1 R7 (market-dependence decline) and Pass 2 `rea-valueflows-semantic-layer` R-claim (REA as semantic substrate for commitment-pooling) jointly imply PM can structure coordination around use-value anchors rather than exchange-value. Anitra Nelson's *Non-Market Socialism* wiki page (cited in Pass 2 Phase A) argues *any* accounting encodes exchange-value logic: money is "not a neutral tool" but "the basis of practices that developed and maintain class and private property." On Nelson's reading, REA/ValueFlows's bidirectional tracking — despite being commensurability-preserving (no single numeraire) — still encodes commensurability-via-attribution and thus leaks market logic through the back door. PM's mutual-credit and commitment-pooling may be fighting the wrong enemy.

**What PM needs to decide to even frame this.** Four operational questions that canon review must answer before the tension is frameable:
1. Does PM's CVLE valuation function treat "valuation" as a pool-internal operation or a cross-pool commensurability operation?
2. Does REA's bidirectional-tracking carry a numeraire (even implicit, via attribution-ordering)?
3. Does PM's matching operation treat "compatibility" as an accounting calculation or a relational negotiation?
4. Does bioregional scope (§3.3 item 7) constrain the answer differently in bioregional vs non-bioregional deployments?

**Trigger to re-open.** If PM's Phase 0 deployment at the Victoria LHC workshop (May-June 2026) surfaces operational experience with the CVLE valuation function that provides empirical data on (1)–(3), the tension should be re-opened using that data.

**Trigger to resolve.** If an external source supplies an operational definition of "post-accounting coordination" that does not collapse to "no quantitative state at all" and is compatible with REA's bidirectional-tracking semantics, the tension should be resolved in the direction of that source. No such source is currently on the P2P wiki; Nelson's text is critical but does not operationalize an alternative.

**What happens if we let it sit.** PM ships with a market-dependence stance (Pass 1 R7) but does not take an accounting-dependence stance. Canon is consistent; the tension is visible in footnotes. This is acceptable for the current review window.

### §6.2 DH-IC-1 — Pluriversal incommensurability

**Tension.** Pass 1's interoperability-as-institutional framing treated cross-surface translation as a layered-negotiation problem. Pass 2 `pluriversal-commoning` introduces a different framing: the knowledge systems being interoperated are sometimes *ontologically incommensurable* (different assumptions about what knowledge *is*, what counts as a subject of rights, what counts as value), and a coordination layer framed as a neutral technical substrate performs a colonial operation by default. IC's memory-governance layer appears to assume a meta-ontology sufficient to cover any domain IC might remember; pluriversal framing refuses that assumption.

**Can IC actually be pluriversal?** The honest answer for the current canon window: *probably not fully, but the constraint is actionable.* IC's memory-layers can decline default flattening (don't assume all retrieved content translates to all surfaces); can decline meta-ontology claims (don't claim the embedding-space is ontology-neutral); can decline universal translation (treat cross-ontology queries as political negotiations routed through human stewards, not technical operations). What IC cannot do with current architecture is carry ontologies whose rules of inference differ fundamentally from Western propositional logic. The canon commitment should be: IC declines the colonial-default operations but does not claim to *be* pluriversal.

**Trigger to confront.** When IC's memory-layers deploy across a bioregion that includes indigenous knowledge systems whose epistemic sovereignty is asserted explicitly (e.g., Coast Salish traditional ecological knowledge, as surfaced through the Salish Sea coordination substrate Darren is working within), the tension must be confronted operationally rather than held in canon footnote.

**Trigger to resolve.** If canon review produces a specific per-layer policy specification that names which ontologies a memory-layer declines to flatten and which cross-layer operations route through human stewards, the tension resolves into operational constraint. Otherwise, hold.

**What happens if we let it sit.** IC canon adopts the declination discipline (no meta-ontology claims, no universal-translation claims) but does not claim full pluriversal architecture. This is honest and operationally viable.

### §6.3 Reproduction-primacy reframing question

**Tension.** §2.1 states reproduction primacy. The `reproductive-commoning` bridge note §8 explicitly chooses reframing (motivating-language edits plus R5 doctrine extension) over renaming (changing primitive slugs to make reproduction visible). But the note flags that if the reframing is applied honestly across Spore's canon, canon *reads differently* even when no primitive slug changes. The open question is whether canon-reads-differently is sufficient, or whether Spore's primitives actually need structural renaming to encode reproduction-primacy at the lexical layer where canon is indexed.

**Trigger to re-open.** Two conditions:
1. Canon review executes the §5.1 item 2 edit (`term.field` re-motivation) and finds that the motivating-language edit is insufficient — i.e., downstream canon readers continue to read `field` as coordination surface rather than reproductive apparatus despite the updated language.
2. A cross-project R-claim ledger review surfaces persistent mis-reads of the reproduction-primacy framing in Pass 2+ bridge notes authored after the edit shipped.

**Trigger to resolve.** Either (a) canon review decides that reframing is sufficient and the tension closes in favor of "no renames needed," or (b) canon review decides that renaming is required and proposes specific candidate renames (see §5.4). Neither is ready yet.

**What happens if we let it sit.** Spore canon ships with motivating-language edits in `term.field`, `relational-agency-and-holons`, `mycorrhizal-federation-protocol`, and `mycelial-holarchy-architecture` that encode reproduction-primacy in the prose layer. No slugs change. Canon-review monitors whether downstream reads correctly, and re-opens the tension if mis-reads persist. This is the current default.
