# P2P Foundation Wiki — Field Scan

This note elevates `tmp/p2p-wiki-synthesis-2026-04-20.md` — a meta-synthesis from the P2P Foundation wiki review — into the learning field as a field-scan bridge note. It is the Tier 1a artifact of the wiki-intake plan (`sequential-questing-sparrow`) and seeds subsequent Tier 1b anchor notes (boundary-commoning, protocol-society, knowledge-commons-governance, commitment-pooling-and-mutual-credit).

This is a **meta-note**: its C-claims are project-level implications and tensions drawn from the synthesis across many wiki pages, not page-grounded atomic claims. Anchors reference sections of the synthesis note itself, not individual wiki pages. Page-anchored C-claims with `orn:p2p-wiki.page:*` RIDs begin at Tier 1b (e.g., `boundary-commoning.md`).

## 1. Corpus and scope

The P2P Foundation wiki is a 41K-page prior-art corpus relevant to Spore (multi-agent coordination, collective-agency substrate, federation), Intelligence Commons (governed knowledge infrastructure, provenance, memory governance), and Poietic Match (commitment pooling, coordination substrate, mutual-credit-adjacent routing). Live-synced into personal KOI 2026-04-14 (96,775 chunks embedded at 1024-dim); filesystem mirror at `/Users/darrenzal/projects/p2pfoundation-wiki` on branch `live-sync`.

## 2. High-signal pages from the review

Fourteen pages were logged as high-signal during the synthesis pass (see `tmp/p2p-wiki-synthesis-2026-04-20.md` §High-signal pages for the full table). The four cluster anchors that seed Tier 1b are:

- `Boundary Commoning` (Spore, IC) — bridge concept for cross-commons composition.
- `Protocol Society` (Spore) — protocols as voluntary rule systems organizing conduct and communication.
- `Understanding Knowledge as a Commons` + `Framework for Analyzing the Knowledge Commons` (IC) — governed knowledge commons framing.
- `Commitment Pooling` + `Sarafu Network` + `Commercial Credit Circuit` (PM) — commitment-pooling lineage and commercial adjacencies.

Corroborating anchors: `Polycentric Climate Governance`, `Inclusive Governance and Generative Value in Knowledge Commons`, `What the Commons Is Missing`, `Market Dependence Theory`, `Collective Intelligence`.

## 3. Per-project working theses

**Spore** — Tested as a multi-agent coordination and collective-agency claim: does the grammar add something structurally new for coordinating bounded actors and shared intelligence, or does it mostly repackage known commons and federation patterns with stronger formalism?

**Intelligence Commons** — Tested as a governed knowledge commons and memory architecture, not just a custom AI stack.

**Poietic Match** — Tested against mutual credit, coordination market, reputation, and commons-exchange prior art to clarify what layer it is actually trying to inhabit.

## 4. Cross-project patterns and risks

Three patterns recur across all three projects: polycentric governance with shared boundaries and adaptive rule systems; interoperability as social and institutional (not merely technical); and value-capture + power-capture as recurrent failure modes. Three shared risks: mistaking elegant vocabulary for operational governance; allowing external extraction or internal elite capture under commons legitimacy; and using market-friendly language without deciding whether the system is actually reproducing market imperatives.

## 5. Vocabulary corrections

- **Spore `membrane`** ↔ external `boundary commoning` / `boundary governance`. Commons lineage preserves the idea of controlled crossing, translation, and connection without implying full alignment with anti-institutional positions.
- **IC `memory architecture`** ↔ external `governed knowledge commons infrastructure`. Foregrounds governance, provenance, and shared stewardship over retrieval-and-storage framing.
- **PM `matching`** ↔ external `coordination substrate` / `mutual coordination` / `routing`. PM sits in a larger family than matching alone; likely needs multiple outward-facing labels for different contexts.

## 6. Disposition

**Disposition: clarify existing term.** The wiki evidence warrants small-to-medium clarifications to Spore's membrane/federation framing, IC's knowledge-commons positioning, and PM's coordination-substrate language. It does not warrant new canon primitives during the current review window. Page-anchored proposals (with `orn:p2p-wiki.page:*` RIDs) will come through Tier 1b bridge notes once the anchor pages are fully read in the local clone. This field-scan captures the seed synthesis; Tier 1b does the anchor work.

## 7. Claim Register

**C1** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Spore — Non-obvious implications #1]
Spore's strongest external cousin in the P2P wiki corpus is not generic decentralization but polycentric coordination with explicit boundaries and shared rule systems; this framing sits closer to collective agency than to simple platform coordination and should anchor outreach language about what Spore is.

**C2** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Spore — Non-obvious implications #2]
"Boundary commoning" names coordination that occurs at the edges between distinct commons systems — shared rule-making at boundaries without full merger — and is likely the cleanest external cousin of Spore's membrane composition vocabulary for audiences in the commons tradition.

**C3** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Spore — Non-obvious implications #4]
The portability claim for Spore should be narrowed: what travels first is not "the whole grammar" but a coordination kit combining boundaries, role relations, rule systems, and cross-boundary connection patterns that lets multiple actors act together without centralizing control.

**C4** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Spore — Tensions / cautions #1]
Commons governance literature does not support an anti-governance reading; monitoring, sanctions, conflict resolution, and adaptive rules are load-bearing. Any framing of Spore's collective agency that elides these operations is structurally too soft against the prior art and will misread the tradition it draws on.

**C5** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Intelligence Commons — Non-obvious implications #1]
The wiki prior art consistently reframes AI memory infrastructure as knowledge-commons-governance infrastructure: retrieval is one layer; preservation, interoperability, incentives, and access rules are equally central. IC's external positioning should lead with the commons-governance frame.

**C6** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Intelligence Commons — Non-obvious implications #2]
Interoperability in the knowledge-commons literature (Wilbanks/Rossini and successors) is social and institutional as much as technical — it spans technical interfaces, semantic understanding, and rights/governance conditions simultaneously. Technical-only interoperability framings understate the surface area IC actually sits on.

**C7** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Intelligence Commons — Tensions / cautions #1]
Knowledge-commons literature repeatedly surfaces power capture (curator/admin class formation) and value capture (external extraction of commons-generated value) as first-order risks. A high-functioning retrieval stack can still fail as a knowledge commons if these governance dynamics stay implicit; IC needs explicit safeguards, not narrative claims.

**C8** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Poietic Match — Non-obvious implications #1]
"Commitment pooling" is established language in the P2P wiki with genuine lineage (Sarafu Network, mutual-credit-adjacent systems), not merely local jargon. The term can be used confidently in concept work even where buyer-facing surfaces need translation to procurement language.

**C9** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Poietic Match — Non-obvious implications #2]
The four metabolic functions named in the wiki — curation, valuation, limitation, exchange (CVLE) — may describe PM's real substrate better than generic "matching" does. PM may be closer to a pool/routing substrate than to a standalone matchmaker; comparing PM's model to CVLE explicitly should sharpen the canon grammar.

**C10** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Poietic Match — Non-obvious implications #3]
The Commercial Credit Circuit tradition (invoice-backed liquidity, tax compatibility, procurement-safe language) offers PM a separate commercial wedge distinct from commons-exchange framing. This is valuable for buyer-legible surfaces even when the underlying coordination logic differs from commitment pooling.

**C11** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Poietic Match — Tensions / cautions #2]
Market Dependence Theory (Wood and successors) warns that systems using commons language can quietly reinstate competition and market imperatives as the governing social logic; PM must be explicit about whether it is below, beside, or a redesign of market coordination, because ambiguity here reproduces what commons language claims to move beyond.

## 8. Review Claims

**R1** [review claim] [target: spore.term.field] [concept: boundary-commoning]
Spore's field/membrane lexicon should formally acknowledge "boundary commoning" as the external cousin-concept for cross-field composition, including it as an aliased entry in lexicon documentation of inter-field composition. The primary clarification: boundary commoning names rule-making at the edge without full merger, which maps directly onto the composition operations Spore already performs but describes them with language recognizable to commons audiences.
*R1 is supported by C2, C1.*

**R2** [review claim] [target: ic.intelligence-primitives] [concept: knowledge-commons]
IC's externally-facing framing should present as governed knowledge commons infrastructure rather than AI memory stack, with commons-governance, preservation, interoperability-as-institutional, and anti-capture elevated to first-class concerns alongside retrieval primitives. Concrete: (a) add commons-governance as a peer primitive to retrieval; (b) name power-capture and value-capture safeguards as explicit invariants, not cultural assumptions; (c) treat interoperability as a cross-cutting concern spanning technical, semantic, and rights layers.
*R2 is supported by C5, C6, C7.*

**R3** [review claim] [target: pm.grammar] [concept: curation-valuation-limitation-exchange]
PM grammar should explicitly compare its model against the wiki's four-function commons substrate (curation / valuation / limitation / exchange) and state where PM sits in that family; clarifying PM as a pool/routing substrate rather than a matchmaker will sharpen both the grammar and downstream product decisions. Concrete: (a) add a grammar section mapping PM primitives onto CVLE; (b) disambiguate PM's relationship to market dependence theory (below/beside/redesign) in project-vision; (c) clarify that buyer-facing surfaces may use different language than grammar-level primitives.
*R3 is supported by C8, C9, C11.*
