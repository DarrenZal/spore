# Obstruction-Aware Sheafification

**Synthesized from:** sheaf-theory-formalization.md, holonic-network-architecture.md, knowledge-architecture.md:89, project-learning-membrane.md:97–169

---

## 1. The Core Claim

Spore is not "a sheaf." It is infrastructure for **governed sheafification and obstruction management.** The learning membrane is a presheaf → sheaf lifecycle: intake assembles local data with restriction maps but no guarantee of global coherence; governance review is the process that tests or enforces gluing. Unresolved tensions are preserved obstructions (H1 cohomology), not errors — they localize exactly where local canons cannot merge.

---

## 2. The Lifecycle

| Stage | Sheaf Language |
|:---|:---|
| Intake | Presheaf construction — local data attached to stalks, restriction maps declared |
| Triage / Translate | Provisional map refinement — compatibility edges sketched, not yet tested |
| Bridge note | Section proposal — explicit claim about what glues with existing canon |
| Claim projection | Compatibility surface construction — source claims mapped to review claims, targeting canon locations |
| Convergence / Synthesis | Gluing test — do claims from distinct sources agree at overlaps? |
| Governance review / Promotion | Sheafification attempt — can local sections be assembled into a global section? |
| Preserved tension | Obstruction (H1) — non-zero cohomology class; localized, not merely "undecided" |

---

## 3. What Membranes Are

A membrane is a **bidirectional boundary** where two complementary operations meet:

- **Sheaf-like operations** (constraint, query, restriction): data is pulled inward or filtered through compatibility conditions — contravariant, requiring consistency
- **Cosheaf-like operations** (intake, accumulation, assembly): data is pushed outward or assembled from parts — covariant, allowing aggregation

The learning membrane performs both. Promotion governance (authorization, legitimacy, contestation, revocation, promotion thresholds) is *not* captured by restriction maps — it operates on top of the compatibility layer the maps formalize.

---

## 4. The Portfolio as Atlas

Each repo is a bounded context with dense local language. Cross-project coherence comes from selective translations between stalks, not a shared namespace (knowledge-architecture.md:89). This is the atlas picture: overlapping local coordinate charts with explicit transition maps, not a single global coordinate system. The five-layer knowledge architecture is a stratified site with **mixed arrows** — restrictions flow downward (canon to public) and accumulations flow upward (raw to governed) — so it is not purely a sheaf. The portfolio's living overlap is the cover; restriction maps are the transition functions.

---

## 5. Why Semilattice Structure Matters Here

Sheaves do not *require* semilattice structure in general. But sheaf language is *useful* for Spore precisely because of Spore's semilattice topology (holonic-network-architecture.md: Alexander, "A City is Not a Tree"): agents participate in multiple overlapping wholes simultaneously, so overlaps are real and non-trivial. If that overlap collapses — if the system became a tree — gluing would reduce to composition with no interesting cohomology. The system would lose its living cross-cutting structure, and sheaf language would become vacuous.

---

## 6. Guardrails

- **Federation protocol ≠ the sheaf.** The transport layer moves data; the sheaf lives in the claim/translation/compatibility layer above it.
- **Restriction maps ≠ privacy or authorization.** They formalize compatibility and projection only. Membrane governance (authorization, contestation, revocation, promotion thresholds) exceeds what restriction maps can express.
- **Sheaf Laplacian energy** is a candidate compatibility signal or diagnostic tool — not "the" variational free energy, not foundational.
- **Active inference** is a downstream research lane (see active-inference-federation-research-prompt.md), not a foundational claim about what Spore is.
- **Markov blankets** were explicitly cut from the active membrane core. Do not reintroduce them.
