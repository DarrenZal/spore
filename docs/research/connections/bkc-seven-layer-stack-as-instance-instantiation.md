# Bridge Note: BKC Seven-Layer Stack as Instance Instantiation

This bridge note maps BKC's seven-layer operational stack to Spore's primitive, pattern, and foundation surfaces. It complements `spore.connection.bkc-as-instance-family`, which registers BKC as a federation-encounter instance-family, and `spore.connection.bioregional-coordination-as-peer-instance-family`, which registers the broader peer canon-bearing repo for agentic bioregionalism.

The purpose is asymmetry closure. BKC already depends on Spore in frontmatter and project positioning. Spore already recognizes BKC as an instance-family. What was still thin was the Spore-side mapping of BKC's own seven-layer operational stack, layer by layer, without turning BKC's operational specificity into Spore doctrine.

This is a connection-layer bridge note. It maps; it does not merge. BKC remains the author of its coordination-stack narrative, BioFi typology, Hub Cultivator/TBFF operational mechanics, and Octo runtime. Spore supplies the grammar and pattern substrate against which those operations become legible.

## §1 BKC's Seven-Layer Stack

BKC's authoritative stack is `~/projects/BioregionKnwoledgeCommons/BioregionalKnowledgeCommoning/docs/coordination-stack.md`. The layers, verified against that file on 2026-04-29, are:

1. **Mapping workshop** — `coordination-stack.md:40-54` describes the workshop as a sensing event where people, organizations, projects, places, offers, needs, and commitments become visible.
2. **Knowledge graph** — `coordination-stack.md:58-77` describes a reviewed graph of people, organizations, locations, offers, needs, and commitments, sovereign to the Greater Victoria node.
3. **Commitment pool** — `coordination-stack.md:81-106` describes a shared field where offers and needs compose into vouchers, routing, redemption, and fulfillment evidence.
4. **Flow funding** — `coordination-stack.md:110-145` describes Hub Cultivator trust-based stewardship and TBFF threshold-based algorithmic redistribution, both reading from and writing back to the graph.
5. **AI agents** — `coordination-stack.md:149-165` describes Octo and related agents as graph-navigation and intake-assistance surfaces with human review before commons entry.
6. **Federation** — `coordination-stack.md:169-188` describes holonic KOI nodes, local sovereignty, edge approval, cryptographic signing, and signals rather than data dumps.
7. **Bioregional finance** — `coordination-stack.md:192-226` describes BFFs, the four BioFi facility types, 4 Returns framing, and the way finance reads from and writes back to the earlier layers.

The stack is not a generic Spore reference architecture. It is BKC's operational story. Spore's role is to show which parts of the story instantiate existing grammar and where BKC exceeds Spore's foundation-layer specificity.

## §2 Layer-by-Layer Mapping

### §2.1 Layer 1: Mapping Workshop

**BKC layer.** The workshop convenes people in place, names actors and projects, maps what exists, surfaces gaps, records offers and needs, and seeks concrete pledges.

**Spore mapping.** This is a compositional mapping, not a lossy one. The workshop is a **Field** condition: a shared coordination space where offers, needs, and gaps become common enough to act on. It activates **Intent** through offers and needs, **Signal** through articulated availability and requirement, **Commitment** through concrete pledges, and **Joint-commitment** where the workshop itself establishes shared readiness to participate.

**Pattern/foundation handoff.** Co-presence-mode scope from `spore:ADR-0064-co-presence-field-condition-disposition` applies directly. A mapping workshop is usually co-presence-requiring, but the resulting graph and commitment flow may continue asynchronously.

**Mapping quality.** Clean but multi-primitive. It is not a failure that no single primitive owns the layer; workshops are coordination episodes composed over structural and verb primitives.

### §2.2 Layer 2: Knowledge Graph

**BKC layer.** Human-reviewed transcript outputs enter a living graph of people, organizations, places, offers, needs, and commitment states. The graph is sovereign to the GV node and selectively visible beyond it.

**Spore mapping.** Each entity can be read as a **Holon** at the appropriate scale. The node's visibility controls instantiate **Membrane** sovereignty. Claims, relationships, offers, and commitments become **Evidence** or evidence-bearing graph projections depending on their status and provenance.

**Pattern/foundation handoff.** `spore.federated-knowledge-exchange` supplies the pattern for sovereign knowledge nodes exchanging selectively. `spore.governance-artifacts` and graph-projection doctrine supply the text/graph distinction; the graph is a projection and operational memory, not an authority source above governing text.

**Mapping quality.** Mostly clean. The lossiness is ontological: BKC's domain entity types are not Spore primitives, and should not be renamed as such. The bridge maps structural role, not vocabulary ownership.

### §2.3 Layer 3: Commitment Pool

**BKC layer.** Offers become vouchers, are verified, enter a pool, route toward needs, and generate fulfillment evidence when redeemed.

**Spore mapping.** This is the cleanest pattern mapping: **Commitment**, **Joint-commitment**, **Evidence**, and `spore.commitment-pooling` all apply directly. The pool is a shared **Field** where individual commitment vectors compose into collective capacity.

**Pattern/foundation handoff.** BKC implements the commitment-pooling pattern natively. The Grassroots Economics / commitment voucher lineage is related implementation substrate; Spore owns the pattern articulation, not the whole lineage.

**Mapping quality.** Clean. BKC's voucher lifecycle is operational specificity over a Spore pattern.

### §2.4 Layer 4: Flow Funding

**BKC layer.** Hub Cultivator uses trusted steward judgment for seasonal allocations. TBFF uses participant-set thresholds and algorithmic redistribution. Both mechanisms use graph evidence and write allocation outcomes back as evidence.

**Spore mapping.** `spore.maintenance-economics` is the foundation substrate: reproductive labor, provisioning, maintenance, translation, and infrastructure costs must be canon-legible without Spore prescribing a compensation algorithm. Flow funding also reads commitment-pool evidence and writes back **Evidence**.

**Pattern/foundation handoff.** This is where BKC is more operationally specific than Spore. Hub Cultivator and TBFF are BKC/Regenerate Cascadia/MycoFi operational mechanisms. Spore foundation doctrine names the boundary and legitimacy requirements; the compensation and redistribution protocols remain pattern/protocol layer.

**Mapping quality.** Lossy by design. Treating Hub Cultivator or TBFF as Spore canon would be a citation-direction error.

### §2.5 Layer 5: AI Agents

**BKC layer.** Octo and related agents traverse federated graphs, answer operational questions, assist transcript processing, and help structure offers and needs while keeping human review mandatory before commons entry.

**Spore mapping.** `spore.instance-model` maps this to the **Agent** aspect: a holon that perceives, decides, and acts through a node. In the BKC reference architecture, Octo is explicitly the agent aspect paired with BKC canon, KOI nodes, and Quartz sites.

**Pattern/foundation handoff.** Agent outputs are not independently authoritative. The `spore.positioning.agents-and-canons` frame and F4 representation-authority discipline keep agent-summary lower-priority than source layers. BKC's human-review gate before knowledge-base entry is operationally aligned with that discipline.

**Mapping quality.** Clean at aspect level, with authority constraints. The agent role maps cleanly; the validity of agent outputs depends on provenance and review.

### §2.6 Layer 6: Federation

**BKC layer.** Nodes nest holonically: GV, Salish Sea, Cascadia, Turtle Island. Each node controls its database, identity, consent settings, peer approvals, signatures, and shared signals.

**Spore mapping.** This instantiates **Holon** nesting, **Membrane** sovereignty, **Signal** exchange, and `spore.federation-protocol`. It also instantiates `spore.federated-knowledge-exchange`: sovereign nodes exchange signals and selected records without centralizing.

**Pattern/foundation handoff.** Protocol-version adoption and federation membership can involve **Joint-commitment** per `spore:ADR-0050-sociality-side-b-plus-primitive`. Concrete KOI transport, edge approval, ECDSA signing, and node topology are BKC/KOI operational details, not Spore foundation doctrine.

**Mapping quality.** Clean at grammar layer, specific at runtime layer. The runtime mechanics validate the grammar without becoming the grammar.

### §2.7 Layer 7: Bioregional Finance

**BKC layer.** BFFs connect global capital to ground-level regeneration through Bioregional Trust, Venture Studio, Investment Company, and Bioregional Bank forms, with 4 Returns and instruments such as grants, eco-credits, patient capital, bonds, lending, complementary currency, and obligation clearing.

**Spore mapping.** The best Spore substrate is `spore.maintenance-economics`, plus **Membrane** for capital boundary-crossing and **Evidence** for finance reading from and writing back to the coordination stack. The BFF layer also depends on prior commitment-pool and knowledge-graph evidence to make underwriting, grant, investment, and currency decisions legible.

**Pattern/foundation handoff.** Financial-instrument typology is BKC/BioFi-authored. Spore does not enumerate Bioregional Trust, Venture Studio, Investment Company, or Bank as canon patterns. It supplies doctrine for making maintenance economics legible and contestable; it does not prescribe the capital stack.

**Mapping quality.** Lossy and intentionally bounded. Layer 7 is richer than Spore foundation doctrine; the bridge must preserve BKC authorship.

## §3 Clean vs. Lossy Mapping

The clean mappings are Layer 1 as a composed coordination episode, Layer 3 as commitment pooling, Layer 5 as the Agent aspect of the instance model, and Layer 6 as federation plus federated knowledge exchange.

The partly lossy mappings are Layer 2 and Layer 4. Layer 2 maps cleanly at structural level, but BKC's domain ontology should remain local. Layer 4 maps to maintenance-economics, but its concrete funding mechanisms exceed Spore foundation doctrine.

The most lossy mapping is Layer 7. BFFs are operationally important, but Spore canon does not own the BioFi instrument typology. It can say how capital-layer work should remain legible, accountable, and membrane-governed; it cannot claim the facility enumeration as its own pattern.

This is the honest shape: BKC is not just an example of Spore. It is an instance-family with operational specificity that teaches Spore where its grammar is useful and where instance-level authorship should remain local.

## §4 Asymmetry Closure

BKC frontmatter and project positioning already cite Spore as upstream coordination grammar. `spore.connection.bkc-as-instance-family` closed one reverse citation by registering BKC as a federation-encounter instance-family. This note closes a different asymmetry: the seven-layer stack itself now has a Spore-side bridge note.

The two BKC bridge notes should be read together:

- `spore.connection.bkc-as-instance-family` maps BKC as an operational instance-family and reciprocal citation target for `spore:ADR-0068-federation-encounter-composition-pattern`.
- `spore.connection.bkc-seven-layer-stack-as-instance-instantiation` maps BKC's own seven-layer stack to Spore primitives, patterns, and foundation docs.

Neither note imports BKC canon into Spore canon. Both make citation direction explicit.
