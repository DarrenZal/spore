# P2P Foundation Wiki — Post-Intake Synthesis

This is the capstone artifact of the wiki-intake plan (`sequential-questing-sparrow`). The Tier 1a [`p2p-wiki-field-scan`](p2p-wiki-field-scan.md) set hypotheses *before* any page-grounded claims existed. Tiers 1b–3 then produced 18 page-grounded bridge notes across Spore, IC, and PM. This note stands back and asks what was actually found — which hypotheses held, which got refined, which were replaced by surprises — so canon stewards reading post-moratorium have a single reference document instead of 19.

Like the field scan, this is a **meta-note**: its C-claims synthesize across bridge notes rather than anchor to individual wiki pages. No `orn:p2p-wiki.page:*` RIDs appear in C-claim anchors; the wiki evidence is one layer down, in the cited bridge notes. The repository default license applies to this note.

## 1. What we looked for

Tier 1a advanced four testable hypotheses against the wiki corpus, plus three per-project working theses.

**Per-project theses (Tier 1a §3):**
- **Spore** — does the grammar add something structurally new for coordinating bounded actors and shared intelligence, or does it repackage known commons and federation patterns?
- **Intelligence Commons** — does IC stand up as governed knowledge commons / memory architecture, or only as a custom AI stack?
- **Poietic Match** — what layer does PM actually inhabit (mutual credit, coordination market, reputation, commons-exchange), and what is its strongest external lineage?

**Cross-project hypotheses (Tier 1a §4–§5):**
- **H1** Polycentric coordination with explicit boundaries and shared rule systems is the strongest external cousin frame (stronger than generic decentralization).
- **H2** Interoperability is social and institutional as much as technical.
- **H3** Value-capture and power-capture are first-order failure modes, not afterthoughts.
- **H4** Commons governance literature does *not* support an anti-governance reading — monitoring, sanctions, conflict resolution, adaptive rules are load-bearing.

Tier 1a also predicted specific vocabulary corrections — Spore `membrane` ↔ boundary-commoning; IC `memory architecture` ↔ governed knowledge-commons infrastructure; PM `matching` ↔ coordination substrate / routing — and asserted a `clarify existing term` disposition across the board (no new canon primitives during Move 0 moratorium).

## 2. What we found — per project

### 2a. Spore — thesis held, operational surface expanded

**Working-thesis outcome:** the grammar is not a generic decentralization claim. It is best read as a polycentric-governance claim with explicit boundaries and rule systems, instantiated through protocols. Spore's distinctive contribution is not novel primitives but the *composition* of four traditions (boundary commoning, protocol society, polycentric governance, institutions/extitutions) into a single grammar — each of which was already individually published prior art.

**Confirmations.**
- Boundary commoning (De Angelis via Birkinbine) is the cleanest external cousin for Spore's membrane/field composition vocabulary, with the additional operational surface that Tier 1a missed: Ostrom's eight design principles (monitors accountable to appropriators, graduated sanctions, conflict-resolution mechanisms, rights to organize) are load-bearing operational governance, not aspirational culture. Imported via `boundary-commoning.md` R1–R3.
- Polycentric governance is not a synonym for decentralization. De Filippi / Aligica-Tarko / McGinnis / Baldwin et al. converge on a structure-plus-process-plus-outcome frame with testable attributes (multiple decision centers with unique perspectives; overarching rule system with jurisdictional scope; spontaneous order with exit/entry/information-flow conditions). Documented drawbacks — transaction costs, democratic accountability deficits, exploitation risks, exclusion, veto points, externalities — must be carried alongside the positive traits. Imported via `polycentric-governance.md` R1–R2.
- Collective intelligence is a cultivated substrate, not an emergent property — Finidori's "CI is a commons that needs protection and a dedicated language" directly corroborates Spore's grammar-building project as published prior art. Elliott's empirical scale breakpoint (2–25 relies on social negotiation; 25–n is stigmergy-dependent) is more precise than the field scan anticipated.

**Refinements.**
- Protocol-society is a substrate-and-composition claim, not a replacement claim — protocols coexist with states and markets as enabling substrates per Schneider. Galloway's reading of protocol as "how control exists after decentralization" must be carried as vigilance discipline: protocol-mode power-capture is first-order, not cultural afterthought.
- Federation is three-layered (technical + institutional + counter-hegemonic-prefigurative-politics), not a synonym for decentralization. The Signal/Moxie trade-off is load-bearing: once federated, protocols become difficult to evolve. This is a real structural cost, not a cultural preference.
- Institutional and extitutional are complementary lenses, not opposites. Feinman's scale-law (institutional crystallization at demographic thresholds) rebuts indefinite extitutional-fluidity at scale — anti-institutional framings break at bioregional scale. Spicer's dialectical reading (institutions as containment machinery for extitutional overflow) adds failure-mode register that pure lens-complementarity framing lacks.

**Surprises.**
- Bourdieu's `field` concept enters Spore's vocabulary with commons-tradition lineage via Gare's institutionalist reading, not only as IC-internal coinage or Johar import. This gives Spore's `term.field` a second pre-existing commons-governance anchor.
- The most-opposed cluster in the entire intake is `spore.mycorrhizal-federation-protocol:power-capture` (3 supports / 8 opposes — see §3b). Federation-as-substrate is where canon reviewers will encounter the strongest cross-cutting pushback: commons literature itself warns that federation language can absorb market-dependence without declaring so.

### 2b. Intelligence Commons — thesis confirmed and operationalized

**Working-thesis outcome:** IC stands up as governed knowledge-commons infrastructure, not merely a custom AI stack. Hess/Ostrom, Wilbanks/Rossini, Pitiot, Herian and the broader commons-tradition provide a coherent external framing that reframes every IC primitive. The external label is not cosmetic — leading with "governed knowledge commons infrastructure" brings in design disciplines (four memory-governance moments; three-layer interoperability; newcomer-integration test; four trust primitives) that don't exist inside "AI memory stack" framing.

**Confirmations.**
- Hess/Ostrom lineage is operative external tradition; retrieval is one primitive among many; preservation, attribution, access, interoperability are equally load-bearing.
- Wilbanks/Rossini three-layer framing (technical + semantic + rights/governance) is the specific apparatus; HapMap is the canonical failure exemplar (patent-openness at one layer producing non-interoperability at another).
- Power-capture and value-capture are first-order; Pitiot's pairing (inclusive governance + generative value) explicitly addresses the "two dark zones" Ostrom leaves implicit.

**Refinements.**
- **Memory governance decomposes into four distinct moments**: attribution (Creditright, who is remembered), provenance (recursive chain-of-sources), preservation (defense against enclosure), access (A2K four components: human knowledge, information, knowledge-embedded goods, tools). Each has its own failure mode. Tier 1a treated attribution as a subset of preservation; the wiki tradition separates them as governance-critical.
- **Interoperability's semantic layer is a long-tail problem** — Science Commons Protocol: "once legally interoperable, only problems left are monstrously complicated technical and semantic ones." IC's retrieval/synthesis primitives should carry "semantic-is-not-free" as architectural assumption.
- **Inclusive governance is a mechanism-level question, not a participation-rate question** (per Stadler/Kostakis). The *newcomer-integration test* is sharper than abstract "inclusive" framing: Pitiot explicitly uses five-years-post-founding as an operational check — can a contributor arriving now participate at the same level as a founding member? Wikipedia's bureaucratization is the archetype failure mode.
- **Generative value is a discipline, not a checklist** — Kelly's five dimensions (purpose / membership / governance / finance / networks) must be evaluated simultaneously. Financial purpose alone (short-term match-count maximization) makes a system extractive regardless of stated intent.

**Surprises.**
- **Provenance and trust are a distinct verification surface** from memory governance, not a subset of it. Four primitives (attribution-as-Creditright, provenance chains, identity authentication, trust networks+metrics) have documented failure modes at empirical scale: Data Provenance Initiative audit of 4,000 AI datasets (power concentration), CA-hierarchy breaches (Diginotar, CNNIC, Symantec), citation cartels, reputation-system mutual-assured-destruction.
- **Peer review is a weak signal** (Jefferson et al. 2002 — "largely untested and effects uncertain"; major discoveries rejected before recognition). The wiki distinguishes communal-validation (a posteriori) from peer review (a priori institutional filtering) — a distinction IC's review patterns should inherit.
- **Administrator-class failure operates on accelerated timescales** in machine-mediated systems compared to human-governance Wikipedia. IC's embedding-space gatekeeping, canonical-vocabulary control, provenance-attribution asymmetry can accrue curator-class power much faster than decadal Wikipedia voting drift.

### 2c. Poietic Match — thesis deepened, with one major disposition shift

**Working-thesis outcome:** PM inhabits a larger family than "matching." It is a pool/routing substrate, genealogically continuous with commitment-pooling (Ruddick / Grassroots Economics / Sarafu) and pre-monetary labor-pooling (Mwerya / ROLA). Commercial Credit Circuit (C3 / WIR / Sardex) is a separate and operationally distinct wedge. Bioregional coordination supplies the governance-scope framing. Reputation markets — the one cluster where Tier 1a underestimated the disagreement — must be declined as a first-class primitive.

**Confirmations.**
- Commitment pooling is published prior art with genuine operational lineage — Sarafu runs 33 pools, 239 Community Asset Vouchers, 3,149 monthly active users as of September 2024, migrated to Celo in July 2023. This is not a proof-of-concept; it is the operational present of the Ruddick lineage at 14 years running.
- **CVLE (Curation / Valuation / Limitation / Exchange)** is the wiki's explicit first-order metabolic-operations family; "matching" is one case of the exchange function. This precisely matches Tier 1a's C9 but supplies the source-tradition receipts.
- Commercial Credit Circuit is a distinct commercial-legibility wedge with measurable protocol-level primitives: obligation-clearing reduces net internal debt 25%–50% (Sardex, peer-reviewed Nature Human Behaviour 2018); cyclic density and k-cycle centrality are measurable network-health properties; Credit Commons Protocol provides federation-by-recursive-ledger-composition.
- The Market Dependence Theory critique (Wood/Brenner) is a standing invariant, not a provisional caution. A coordination protocol can eliminate capital-labor extraction and still reproduce capitalism if it installs market-dependence as the substrate.

**Refinements.**
- Three lineages collapsed as "mutual credit" must be disambiguated: LETS-family (ledger-only, ~200-member ceiling, permanent-deficit drift documented over 40 years); WIR-family (central-issuer, explicitly *not* mutual credit per Jerome Blanc 2024 sustainability-science review); Sarafu / commitment-pooling-family (pool-of-commitments with CVLE).
- Bioregional governance is a distinct *fourth* level beyond polycentric, not a synonym for it (Resilience.Earth four-level ladder: centralized → decentralized → polycentric → bioregional). The anti-capture ontology is stricter than generic polycentric: "No 501(c)(3) organization can legitimately claim to represent or control a bioregion." PM's bioregional-scope deployments cannot be architected as single-owner platforms.
- The dual-structure pattern (membrane institution + autonomous DAO / network) is already-published prior art, not a PM invention. PM should inherit, not re-invent.

**Surprises.**
- **Reputation markets flipped from "clarify existing term" to `unresolved-tension`.** This is the single most significant dispositional surprise in the entire intake. The wiki corpus preserves a live two-decade disagreement between the Botsman-lineage (reputation-as-portable-currency, 2010–2014) and the Polanyian-lineage (Gandini: reputation as a fourth fictitious commodity after labor, land, money). Wilson & De Paoli 2019 establish that reputation-system design is *performative* — "conventional approaches to the design of such systems are rooted in a capitalist, competitive paradigm, relying on methodological individualism, and [...] the reputation technologies themselves thus embody and enact this paradigm." Adopting a reputation-market primitive enacts the paradigm regardless of stated governance. **PM canon stewards must decide where PM stands in this three-way split (Botsman / Polanyian / pool-scoped-valuation); the wiki corpus does not supply a default.**
- Mwerya / ROLA (pre-monetary labor pooling across Luo, Giriama, Duruma, Chonyi, Kamba, Kikuyu) is not ancestral framing; it is the actual operational ancestor. CICs are digital formalizations of pre-existing labor-commitment substrates, not new-monetary-inventions. This strengthens PM's lineage claim beyond what Tier 1a anticipated.
- **Obligation-clearing is a distinct primitive from matching** and from mutual-credit issuance — it operates on the residue (the graph of obligations) and is empirically documented. PM's routing substrate can import it without controversy.

## 3. Cross-project convergences

### 3a. Field families spanning all three projects

Query C (cross-project concept index) identified **two concepts with clusters in all three projects**:

- **power-capture** — 4 clusters across Spore, IC, PM. The single most-evidenced cross-project concept. Every bridge note that touches governance architecture (federation, memory layers, protocol, project-vision) surfaces it. Wiki's own editorial line (Gandini, Herian, Galloway, Pitiot, Wilson & De Paoli, DPI audit) treats it as first-order risk requiring explicit architectural safeguards, not cultural vigilance.
- **boundary-commoning** — 3 clusters across Spore, IC, PM. The cleanest cross-project bridge: Spore's `term.field`, IC's `memory-layers` (between-layer surface), PM's `project-vision` (within-pool vs between-pool scope). Tier 1a predicted this would be Spore-primary; it turned out to be a whole-family bridge concept.

**Ten further concepts span two projects**: linguistic-closure (IC+Spore), human-machine-complementarity (IC+Spore), coordination-substrate (PM+Spore), commitment-pooling (PM+Spore), reputation-market (PM+Spore), generative-value (IC+PM), interoperability-as-institutional (IC+Spore), memory-governance (IC+Spore), polycentric-governance (PM+Spore), inclusive-governance (IC+PM).

**What this tells canon stewards:** the three projects are more genealogically connected than Tier 1a suggested. The same commons-tradition vocabulary serves all three at distinct canon surfaces — which means decisions made in one project's canon review have read-across implications for the other two, and cross-project edges (relates_to) in the bridge notes are load-bearing infrastructure, not cosmetic links.

### 3c. The market-dependence opposition axis

Tier 1a flagged Market Dependence Theory as a caution (C11). Tier 3 elevated it to a standing invariant through two opposition notes that converge on a single pattern:

> Commons-adjacent vocabulary is not load-bearing without operational surface. Absorption without (a) participant commitments, (b) critical-feedback mechanisms, (c) tangible-outcome tracking, (d) explicit market-dependence position, (e) externality-pricing is reactionary — it reproduces what the language claims to move beyond.

The wiki itself does this work. Benkler's editorial line on Category:P2P_Market_Approaches refuses metaphor flattening: "Markets as actual institutional forms are a particular information process, generating information in a very particular form — prices. Other modalities of allowing unorganized individuals to decide on their actions [...] are not markets, except metaphorically." Wood/Brenner's distinction (market-enablement vs market-dependence) is a canonical left-critique preserved intact. Dietz's consensus-without-commitment formulation came from the P2P movement's own internal critique (post-Berlin 2013).

**Canon-review implication:** PM must state its position on Wood-Brenner explicitly before defaults bake in assumptions; Spore's federation and linguistic-closure surfaces must decline absorption of market-adjacent terms as neutral substrate vocabulary; IC's generative-value adoption must pass Kelly's five dimensions simultaneously or become ornamental.

## 4. What we didn't expect

1. **Reputation-markets disposition shift.** Tier 1a predicted `clarify existing term` across the board. Tier 2's `reputation-markets` note landed on `unresolved-tension` because the wiki corpus does not converge on a single meaning — Botsman-lineage and Polanyian-lineage actively disagree. This is the one cluster where PM cannot import a settled external definition; PM canon stewards must decide, and the decision is architectural (Wilson & De Paoli performativity finding: the choice propagates into observed behavior).

2. **Cross-three-project convergence on power-capture and boundary-commoning.** Tier 1a predicted per-project external cousins; the data shows a smaller set of concepts serving all three projects simultaneously. This means the wiki intake's strongest finding is not project-specific vocabulary correction but a shared commons-critique discipline (power-capture vigilance, boundary-as-governance-surface) that applies identically across Spore, IC, and PM.

3. **Claim-density balance.** Query E shows Spore 101 source claims (7 notes), IC 76 (5 notes), PM 79 (6 notes) — all 256 wiki-anchored, 100% RID-compliant. Spore's larger claim count reflects more bridge notes (including the opposition note), not a richer commons-tradition surface — per-note density is actually comparable (14.4 Spore / 15.2 IC / 13.2 PM). Tier 1a had guessed IC would be sparsest; the opposite was true when normalized by note count.

4. **Dead-anchor trail as corpus-health signal.** During Tier 1b/2 intake, agents hit two HFS+ case-folding artifacts (Boundary_Spanner, Collaborative_Intelligence) — canonical 500+ word wiki pages that the filesystem mirror had collapsed into case-variant siblings, causing agents to report them as missing. Both were later reconciled via migration 083's alias resolver. These aren't claim-quality defects; they are corpus-coverage gaps caused by the live-sync filesystem, and they're now detectable and fixable. The 129 unique wiki pages cited across the 18 intake notes are a lower bound — broader searchable coverage of the 41K-page wiki remains available through `unified_search` on the wiki surface.

5. **Dedup defect revealed branch-topology discipline matters.** Item 1.5 of the plan discovered 14 duplicate Sarafu claims caused by branch divergence between projection script runs. The root cause was patched (commit `9a80cc01`) with an early-return dedup on claim-content hash. This was a pipeline issue, not a claim-quality issue, but it surfaced a real lesson: live-syncing against a mutable wiki + projecting into a mutable graph requires pipeline-level idempotence discipline, not just note-level verification gates.

6. **The frozen concepts vocabulary worked.** `concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml v1` (20 entries, frozen 2026-04-15) successfully prevented slug fragmentation across 15 concurrent agents during Tier 1b/2 execution. No agent minted an off-yaml slug. This is a small but genuine methodological finding for future multi-agent intakes at this scale.

## 6. Unresolved tensions (not for this canon review)

Canon reviewers should *not* attempt to resolve the following during the current review window. They are signals for future work:

- **Machine-mediated governance gap.** Kelly, Pitiot, and Bollier all assume human participants with livelihood stakes. Agent-mediated memory, pipeline-mediated attribution, and automated retrieval don't map cleanly onto human-ownership frames. IC needs a machine-mediation supplement to generative-value and inclusive-governance before the commons-tradition adoption is fully operational. This is cross-project: Spore's protocol-mode power-capture and PM's encoded-paradigm-becomes-observed-behavior (Sarafu 2019 bonding-curve, Wilson & De Paoli 2019) inherit the same gap.
- **Within-commons vs cross-commons governance apparatus.** Ostrom / Hess / Pitiot / Bollier are stronger on governance *within* a commons than on governance *between* distinct commons. Boundary commoning (De Angelis) addresses the between-commons surface but does not supply the operational matrix Ostrom supplies for within-commons. IC's cross-peer federation and Spore's mycorrhizal federation will encounter this gap; neither project has enough external apparatus to inherit.
- **Attribution for mixed human/machine authorship.** Creditright (Fogel, Kelly) assumes human authorship. IC's embeddings, retrievals, and syntheses are mixed human/machine; Creditright doesn't cover the surface. Waiting for the commons tradition to catch up is not a viable strategy — IC will likely need to extend rather than inherit.
- **Bioregional-scope metric-authorship.** Austin Wade Smith: "every measure / metric is an agreement. Standards are based on consensus. Bioregions and locales must govern their own." PM's bioregional deployments cannot use PM-team-authored metrics as defaults; the affordance must be configurable-by-participants. This is a protocol-design constraint, not a canon-review concern, but it matters for downstream product decisions.
