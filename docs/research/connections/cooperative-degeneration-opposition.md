# Cooperative Degeneration (Mondragon / Kasmir) — Opposition

This Wave 2 Phase B opposition bridge note registers the **Sharryn Kasmir ethnography of Mondragon** (*The Myth of Mondragon: Cooperatives, Politics, and Working Class Life in a Basque Town*, SUNY Press) as the canonical counter-evidence against any Spore / IC / PM absorption of "cooperative at scale" framing (platform-cooperativism, open-cooperativism, worker-ownership as sufficient) without concurrent scale-degeneration mitigation. The disposition is **no change** — this note does not introduce new canon primitives; it registers an opposition edge against the constructive platform-cooperativism note scheduled for Wave 3 and against any PM / Spore canon text that invokes cooperative-at-scale as self-legitimating coordination architecture. The opposition is **paired** with the Wave 3 constructive note (`pm.connection.platform-cooperativism-constructive`): the constructive note will import Scholz (2016), Bauwens open-coop criteria, and Conaty-Bollier convergence; this opposition note constrains the constructive import by specifying the degeneration failure modes the constructive frame MUST address or explicitly decline.

Kasmir's thesis is ethnographic rather than ideological: Mondragon is the most commonly-cited empirical "proof" that cooperative-at-scale works. Her fieldwork shows the opposite — that at-scale cooperative federations reproduce labor-capital hierarchies *internally* via membership tiers, external (non-member) labor subcontracting, and a managerial class whose governance enthusiasm and external-facing authority is structurally higher than shop-floor workers'. The "myth" arises when the scholarly/popular literature de-contextualizes Mondragon from its political and historical environment, portrays it as apolitical, and ignores shop-floor experience. The myth is consequential: Kasmir argues it forms part of a global ideology that uses cooperative labor-management framing "to discredit labor unions and working-class organizations." For PM in particular — which is scoped as a matchmaking substrate inclusive of cooperative and commitment-pooling frames — the Kasmir reading is load-bearing: PM MUST NOT absorb platform-cooperativism / open-cooperativism language into canon as sufficient without carrying a stated position on scale-degeneration (membership tiers, non-member external labor, managerial-class drift), or PM inherits the myth by proximity.

## 1. Kasmir's thesis: cooperative at scale reproduces class structure internally

The Kasmir source page records the book's core claim: Mondragon is "considered to be the most successful example of democratic decision making and worker ownership," yet the scholarly and popular literature "idealizes the cooperatives by falsely portraying them as apolitical institutions and by ignoring the experiences of shop floor workers." The book's argument runs at two levels: (a) the political-economic level — cooperative labor-management framing is part of "a new global ideology that promotes cooperative labor-management relations in order to discredit labor unions and working-class organizations"; (b) the ethnographic level — Mondragon workers on the shop floor do not feel the strong identification with the cooperative that cooperative theory predicts. Kasmir's survey compared attitudes of workers in a Mondragon Group factory against a similar privately-owned factory and found similar dissatisfaction profiles — directly contradicting the narrative that cooperative ownership per se alters shop-floor worker subjectivity.

The review on the source page (Sráid Marx) notes that Kasmir "at places within her book ... presents the management of Mondragon as virtually a separate class from workers on the shop floor." As an anthropologist, Kasmir is "sensitive to the differences between the daily lives of workers and managers even where the income differences are relatively small compared to most capitalist enterprises." The differences are operational even when the income gap is small: "She notes that it is the cooperative's managers who are most enthusiastic about the cooperative and that it is they who invariably welcome visitors and present the views of the cooperative's members to outsiders." This is the key ethnographic finding for opposition-note purposes: the managerial class functions as the *external-facing representative* of the cooperative — they shape the narrative outsiders receive, including the narrative that reaches platform-cooperativism and open-cooperativism theorists. The mismatch between the external narrative and shop-floor experience is itself an artifact of managerial-class drift.

## 2. Membership tiers and non-member external labor as degeneration mechanisms

The source page does not itemize Mondragon's specific tier structure (full member / associate / non-member / external contract), but the Kasmir thesis and the Phase A Agent C synthesis both name the operational pattern: what looks from outside like "the cooperative" has hierarchical differentiation inside — full members with voting rights and profit-sharing, associate/temporary members with reduced rights, and non-member workers (external contract labor) whose work sustains cooperative production but who are structurally excluded from cooperative governance. This is the *reproduction of class relations at the cooperative's external boundary*: cooperative ideology applies to full members; the labor-capital relation is displaced outward to contracted non-members.

Agent C's synthesis (Seed 8 dossier, p.120–136 of `phase-a-agent-c-opposition.md`) surfaces the operational implication: "cooperative governance will drift toward manager capture despite formal worker ownership" and "commons-based organizing MUST establish explicit checks on coordinator/manager income ratios and authority spans, or economic inequality and power concentration will re-emerge over time." The Marx-tradition framing (via Sráid Marx's review on the source page) adds a coordinator-class lens: "cooperatives can still develop coordinator classes (managers) even with profit-sharing, replicating class structure at smaller scale." The review also notes — as partial counter-evidence — that Mondragon workers have repeatedly voted down management proposals to widen pay differentials, and that Mondragon "does not outlaw political activity" (a small politicised-worker group campaigned against the differential proposal and succeeded). These are real democratic moments; they do not refute the degeneration thesis but qualify it: degeneration is a *drift*, not an *inevitability*, and the drift is resistible where critical-feedback and political-organizing surfaces are preserved inside the cooperative. This qualification is material for Wave 3's constructive note — mitigation is possible, but requires specification.

## 3. Scale-degeneration pattern: small coop self-manages, large federation drifts

The cross-seed synergy the Phase A synthesis names (Pattern B: FairCoin × Mondragon) is the operative framing for PM: "postcapitalist boundary primitives (filtering membrane, coop governance) both fail in practice when insufficient member participation and monitoring exist." The scale-degeneration pattern: small worker-cooperatives with direct-democracy surface and resident political-feedback capacity can self-manage; as federations grow, organizational requirements (strategic planning, external market competition, translocal representation) accrue authority to a managerial class whose operational knowledge and external network make them structurally load-bearing. Worker-ownership on paper coexists with worker-governance-in-practice atrophy.

For PM specifically: PM's draft framing positions cooperative ownership (including platform-cooperativism and open-cooperativism lineages) as a substrate of participant coordination. The Mondragon case establishes that *ownership form does not itself determine governance dynamics at scale* — a co-owned federation can reproduce managerial governance. PM MUST carry this distinction in any canon language invoking platform-cooperativism or open-cooperativism: ownership-democratized (Bauwens multi-stakeholder criterion) ≠ governance-democratized (Kasmir shop-floor governance test). PM's pm.protocol and pm.project-vision text MUST NOT collapse these into a single claim that cooperative ownership implies cooperative governance at PM scale. The Wave 3 constructive note will import Scholz/Bauwens/Conaty-Bollier framing; this opposition note specifies that the constructive import must carry (not merely name) scale-degeneration mitigation — minimally: (a) explicit caps/checks on coordinator/manager authority and income ratios, (b) named bounds on non-member/associate labor fraction, (c) preserved political-organizing surface inside the federation, (d) periodic shop-floor-level governance-capacity audits distinct from ownership-share audits.

## 4. Cross-reference to constructive Wave 3 note

This opposition note and the Wave 3 constructive note (`pm.connection.platform-cooperativism-constructive`, to be authored) are **paired** as a constructive/opposition diad. The constructive note will import platform-cooperativism (Scholz 2016) and open-cooperativism (Bauwens, Conaty-Bollier) vocabulary into PM canon; this opposition note constrains the constructive import by specifying that the *nominal-cooperativism* failure mode must be declined. Three acceptance tests apply:

1. **Membership-tier test**: Does PM canon's platform-cooperativism absorption specify the permitted/prohibited structure of membership tiers and the governance rights of associate/non-member participants? Silence defaults to tolerating Mondragon-pattern hierarchy.
2. **External-labor test**: Does PM canon's open-cooperativism absorption specify how non-member external labor is handled — permitted with what conditions, prohibited, or structurally absent in the PM context? Silence defaults to tolerating external-labor subcontracting under cooperative branding.
3. **Scale-drift test**: Does PM canon specify the at-scale governance-capacity surface (not just ownership-structure) that PM participants must preserve as cooperatives federate? Silence defaults to accepting Mondragon-pattern managerial drift as the implicit trajectory.

The opposition edges below remain active until PM's platform-cooperativism / open-cooperativism canon text independently passes all three tests, at which point the disposition can be re-evaluated per the governance-map-protocol restriction process.

## 5. Why disposition is no change

Disposition `no change` here is an active opposition directive, not passive deferral. Absorbing Kasmir's critique as a set of positive canon clarifications ("PM now addresses coop degeneration by specifying X, Y, Z...") would itself risk the consensus-without-commitment pattern the Pass 1 opposition note names: substituting vocabulary for operational surface. The opposition-note form instead registers the critique as a constraint on the Wave 3 constructive import — the constructive note either carries the operational surface the three tests above require, or accepts that these opposition edges are durably active against PM canon invoking platform-cooperativism or open-cooperativism. ## 6. Tensions (cautions for any reader)

1. **Partial-counter-evidence risk**: The source page records real democratic-agency moments at Mondragon — workers voting down pay-differential proposals, politicised worker-organizing being permitted. A reader could read these as refuting the degeneration thesis. They do not. They qualify it: drift is resistible where political-organizing surface is preserved. This note's R-claims should be read as *drift-mitigation requirements*, not as *claims that cooperatives are irredeemable*. The Wave 3 constructive note will handle the redeemable side.
2. **Single-source-depth risk**: This bridge note anchors to a single Kasmir book-summary page (~6.4KB). Agent C's dossier flagged that body-text Grep sweeps may surface additional coop-critique material; title-level Grep undershoots. If Wave 3's constructive authorship surfaces richer coop-degeneration material elsewhere in the wiki, this opposition note should be revised to carry the additional anchors.
3. **Scope-bleed risk**: The Mondragon case is widely cited in Spore-adjacent bioregional-coordination literature (e.g., cooperative at bioregional scale). The optional R-claim against `pm.connection.bioregional-coordination` registers the scope-bleed: invoking cooperative-federation framing at bioregional scale without scale-degeneration mitigation inherits the Mondragon critique.
4. **Constructive-opposition interaction risk**: This note's R-claims become moot if the Wave 3 constructive note is *never authored* — absorption risk vanishes with no constructive import. R-claim activation therefore depends on Wave 3 actually importing platform-cooperativism / open-cooperativism vocabulary. If Wave 3 declines the constructive import, this note's opposition is preserved as a guard against future reintroduction.

## 7. Disposition

**Disposition: no change.** The Kasmir evidence warrants opposition edges against (a) any PM canon absorbing platform-cooperativism / open-cooperativism vocabulary without concurrent scale-degeneration mitigation (R1, R2), (b) Wave 3's constructive note by default — as a pairing constraint that specifies what the constructive note must carry (R3), and (c) optionally, Spore's bioregional-coordination framing if it invokes cooperative-federation at scale (R4). No canon primitives, aliases, or clarifications are added. The script encodes the opposition stance via `disposition=no-change` per `project_bridge_notes.py:591`. This stance may shift when and if PM canon text and the Wave 3 constructive note independently carry operational-surface specification on the membership-tier / external-labor / scale-drift tests.

## 8. Claim Register

**C1** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Mondragon,_Cooperatives,_Politics,_and_Working_Class_life_in_a_Basque_Town]
Kasmir's core thesis is that the scholarly and popular literature on Mondragon idealizes the cooperatives "by falsely portraying them as apolitical institutions and by ignoring the experiences of shop floor workers"; she shows this creation of the idealized image "is part of a new global ideology that promotes cooperative labor-management relations in order to discredit labor unions and working-class organizations" — establishing that cooperative-at-scale framings, when absorbed uncritically into coordination grammars, carry an ideological function (labor-management cooperation framed as replacement for labor-organizing) independent of the grammar's stated intent.

**C2** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Review · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Mondragon,_Cooperatives,_Politics,_and_Working_Class_life_in_a_Basque_Town]
Kasmir's ethnographic finding via comparison survey — "she is able to compare the attitudes of workers in a factory within the Mondragon Group to those in a similar privately owned one ... workers do not feel the strong identification with the cooperative that might be assumed" — establishes empirical counter-evidence to the theoretical claim that cooperative ownership per se alters shop-floor worker subjectivity; the similar-dissatisfaction-profile finding at the most-cited successful cooperative is load-bearing against any framing that treats ownership form as sufficient for governance-substrate transformation.

**C3** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Review · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Mondragon,_Cooperatives,_Politics,_and_Working_Class_life_in_a_Basque_Town]
The managerial-class differentiation Kasmir documents is operational even where income differences are "relatively small compared to most capitalist enterprises" — "she sees these relatively small but significant differences in income reflected outside the workplace also reflected in knowledge, responsibility and power within the cooperative," and notes that "it is the cooperative's managers who are most enthusiastic about the cooperative and that it is they who invariably welcome visitors and present the views of the cooperative's members to outsiders" — establishing that managerial-class drift is observable at the ethnographic level via (a) external-representation asymmetry (managers speak for the cooperative to outsiders) and (b) knowledge-responsibility-power concentration, and that this differentiation occurs independent of the magnitude of income inequality.

**C4** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Review · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Mondragon,_Cooperatives,_Politics,_and_Working_Class_life_in_a_Basque_Town]
Mondragon preserves partial democratic-agency surface — "repeated attempts by management to increase the allowed differential between management and shop floor pay have been repeatedly voted down by workers ... the cooperative does not outlaw political activity and the author records the actions of a small group of politicised workers who campaigned actively against the management proposal and succeeded" — establishing that managerial-class drift is resistible where political-organizing surface and direct-governance votes are preserved, and qualifying the degeneration thesis as a *drift pattern whose mitigation requires specific preserved surfaces* rather than an inevitability; this qualification is load-bearing for constructive imports that must specify the preserved surfaces rather than assume ownership form carries them.
