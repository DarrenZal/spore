# Autopoiesis and Structural Coupling

This bridge note grounds IC's `memory-layers` and `intelligence-primitives` canon in the primary theoretical lineage IC inherits but has not cited: Maturana and Varela's 1972 autopoiesis theory, Varela-Thompson-Rosch's 1991 enactive reformulation, the 4E cognitive-science tradition (embodied, embedded, enacted, extended), and Di Paolo / De Jaegher's participatory sense-making extension. Pass 1 of the P2P Foundation wiki intake flagged `knowledge-commons-governance` as IC's commons-audience frame; this note supplies the cognitive-science lineage underneath that frame, introducing three distinctions (operational vs. informational closure; structural coupling vs. representation; enaction vs. symbol manipulation) that IC's existing language does not yet make explicit.

The note is Wave 1 supporting-note 4 of Pass 2 Phase B. It carries two disconfirming hypotheses from Phase A (DH-IC-2 and DH-IC-3) as tensions the note records but does not fully resolve. The R-claims propose partial resolutions consistent with the Move 0 moratorium posture (no new canon primitives).

## 1. Autopoiesis is a boundary theory, not a closure theory

Maturana and Varela's 1972 definition (reproduced on the wiki's `Autopoiesis` page) is specific: an autopoietic machine is "organized (defined as a unity) as a network of processes of production (transformation and destruction) of components which (i) through their interactions and transformations continuously regenerate and realize the network of processes (relations) that produced them; and (ii) constitute it (the machine) as a concrete unity in space in which they (the components) exist by specifying the topological domain of its realization as such a network." Two things matter for IC: the system's *organization* is closed (components produce the network that produces them), and the system specifies its own *topological domain* (the space in which its components exist).

The wiki page then states the distinction this note wants IC to carry explicitly: "an autopoietic system is autonomous and operationally closed, in the sense that there are sufficient processes within it to maintain the whole. Autopoietic systems are 'structurally coupled' with their medium, embedded in a dynamic of changes that can be recalled as sensory-motor coupling." Operational closure is about component-regeneration; it is compatible with — and in fact *requires* — structural coupling with the environment. A cell regenerates its own components; it is not walled off from the medium. It is reached by perturbations from the medium and it reaches back into the medium, but what the medium does is trigger internal reorganizations, not deposit content.

This is the first distinction IC should inherit. "Closure" in the autopoietic tradition is not informational isolation. A memory-layer that is called "closed" in IC's sense must be compared carefully to autopoietic closure: if the layer regenerates its own structure through its own operations, it is operationally closed; if it seals against external perturbation, it is something else (and something autopoiesis does not actually describe).

## 2. Structural coupling is recurrent perturbation, not transmission

The same wiki page makes the operational claim explicit: autopoietic systems are "structurally coupled" with their medium, and this coupling "can be recalled as sensory-motor coupling." The medium perturbs the system; the system changes state; the system's operations then act on the medium; the medium changes; the cycle recurs. Coupling is a *dynamic*, not a channel. Nothing is transmitted. There is no message, no packet, no symbol passing across the boundary. There is only co-specification through recurrent perturbation.

Jonas Andersen's discussion entry on the same page generalizes the distinction to systems theory via Luhmann: "linear systems" (speech organs, air, hearing, telephones, the Internet taken as a medium) are technological input-output systems and are not autopoietic; social systems are dynamic and autopoietic, and "interaction between two autopoietic (psychic, social, or societal) systems will form another autopoietic system of a higher cybernetic order." The implication for IC: if memory-layers are modeled as channels (input → storage → output), they are not autopoietic in any serious sense. If they are modeled as recurrent perturbation dynamics that regenerate their own structure while remaining coupled to users, tools, documents, and other layers, the theoretical lineage holds.

This is the second distinction IC should carry: coupling tracks *dynamics*, not representations. Whatever IC routing does, it should be describable as coupling (who perturbs whom, with what frequency, producing what internal reorganizations) rather than as message-passing over a symbol-alphabet.

## 3. Cognition is enaction, not representation

The 1972 text is also the birthplace of the claim that living systems are cognitive systems. The wiki's `Autopoiesis and Cognition` page records the authors' own framing: "living systems are cognitive systems, and living as a process is a process of cognition." Cognition here is not symbol-manipulation or internal-model-construction; it is the ongoing maintenance of autopoietic organization under structural coupling. The cell's sensorimotor loop *is* the cognitive process.

Varela, Thompson, and Rosch's 1991 *The Embodied Mind* extended this into what is now called enactive cognition. The `Autopoiesis` page summarizes: "Varela, Thompson and Rosch applied autopoesis to make non-representationalist, and enactive models of mind, brain and behavior, which further developed embodied cognitive neuroscience, later culminating in neurophenomenology." The enactive claim is stronger than it sounds: cognition does not primarily consist in constructing internal representations of an external world; it consists in *enacting* a world through sensorimotor coupling. The world the organism knows is the world its coupling carves out.

This is the third distinction IC should carry: if IC's intelligence-primitives are conceived as symbol-manipulation protocols that build and compare representations, they are working in the cognitivist (Cartesian, representationalist) tradition that enaction specifically displaces. If they are conceived as sensorimotor coordination patterns — coupling regimes between agents, documents, and environments — they are working in the tradition IC's language already gestures toward (layers, boundaries, routing).

## 4. 4E extends the enactive frame across six dimensions

The wiki's `4E_Cognitive_Science` page, citing Alexander Beiner, states the frame: "as human beings our cognition is embodied, embedded, enacted, and extended." Cognition is **embodied** (inseparable from physical body, gestures, sensations — per Barbara Tversky's work on spatial reasoning), **embedded** (inseparable from the environment to which the agent has adapted — a whale is embedded in ocean), **enacted** (every action changes the world and thereby changes the possibility space the agent operates in), and **extended** (moving through other people, technology, and environment — tools become body, as when a driver perceives the car's width without thinking about it). John Vervaeke adds **emotional** ("all cognition is about caring or not caring about taking a risk which has affective consequences") and **exaptive** (re-purposing existing cognitive machinery — the tongue's move from food-manipulation to speech is the archetype, and concept-use exapts spatial-movement machinery).

The `Embodied_Cognition` page sharpens the point via Lakoff and Johnson: "understanding emerges from interaction, from constant negotiation with the environment and other people... recurrent experience leads to the formation of categories, which are experiential gestalts with those natural dimensions." Jose Ramos's framing in the same page collapses the individual/collective divide: "embodied cognition is the idea that our cognising of the world around us is located in us as individuals (cognising) and as groups (co-cognising). We are primarily social beings interacting and co-cognising with others, 'structurally coupled' into cultural (e.g. language, religion, tradition), and ecological / geo-graphic contexts (Maturana, 1998, p. 174)."

For IC, the 4E frame implies the primitive is not "an individual agent with memory" but "a coupled set of agents-in-environment with recurrent interaction dynamics." Memory is where the coupling history sediments; intelligence is what the coupling enacts. This is not a metaphor extension — it is the specific theoretical position the lineage IC is adjacent to actually holds.

## 5. Participatory sense-making: cognition is dyadic before it is individual

De Jaegher and Di Paolo's participatory sense-making, as recorded on the wiki, is the operational consequence of taking enaction seriously at the inter-subjective level. The `Participatory_Sense-Making` page states it directly: "the interaction process itself can take on a form of autonomy (operationally defined). This allows us to reframe the problem of social cognition as that of how meaning is generated and transformed in the interplay between the unfolding interaction process and the individuals engaged in it. The notion of sense-making in this realm becomes participatory sense-making."

The Fuchs/De Jaegher 2009 passage on the same page adds the dyadic autonomy claim precisely: meaning-generation happens "(1) from a dynamical agentive systems point of view as an interaction and coordination of two embodied agents; (2) from a phenomenological approach as a mutual incorporation, i.e. a process in which the lived bodies of both participants extend and form a common intercorporality. Intersubjectivity, it is argued, is not a solitary task of deciphering or simulating the movements of others but means entering a process of embodied interaction and generating common meaning through it."

This matters for IC's interoperability-as-institutional framing. If two memory-layers (or two agents, or two knowledge commons) are interacting, the interaction dynamic itself can become an autonomous unit — a dyad-level coordination process that neither participant fully owns. The routing layer should be designed so that dyadic autonomy is available as a first-class object, not an emergent accident. Pass 1's `knowledge-commons-governance` framing already asks the right questions (who governs the interoperability surface?); participatory sense-making adds the operational claim that the *interaction itself* is governable (and governed) distinct from the individual parties.

## 6. Tensions

Two disconfirming hypotheses from Phase A synthesis §4b are carried by this note. Both constrain any future IC canon edit that invokes autopoiesis or enaction.

1. **DH-IC-2: Autopoietic closure may rebut IC's permeable-layer framing.** Maturana-Varela strict closure requires component regeneration; IC's informal talk of "permeable" or "porous" memory-layers does not obviously meet this criterion. If IC adopts autopoiesis as a theoretical anchor, it must distinguish operational closure from informational openness explicitly. Partial resolution proposed below (§8 C1, §8 C2, §9 R1): IC memory-layers are informationally open (structural coupling with users, tools, documents, other layers) while organizationally closed (the layer regenerates its own structure through its own operations). This preserves the permeable-coupling behavior IC's language already describes while disciplining what "closed" means. This note does not establish that IC layers actually meet the autopoiesis criterion — that requires a canon-level operational test which is out of scope here.

2. **DH-IC-3: Enaction may render IC's symbol-based routing epiphenomenal.** If cognition is enaction and not representation, then any IC routing layer modeled as symbol-transmission-over-channels is working in the wrong theoretical tradition and the representations it manipulates may not be doing the cognitive work the design assumes. Partial resolution proposed below (§8 C4, §8 C5, §9 R2): reframe routing as a coupling mechanism whose surface behavior is expressible as sensorimotor norms (perturbation patterns, recurrent response regimes) rather than as a protocol over a symbol alphabet. The stronger version of the tension — that IC's existing protocol surface is itself the wrong unit of analysis — is not settled here. A fuller answer would require a routing-layer specification in enactive terms, which is deferred.

Additional secondary caution: Donna Haraway's critique, recorded on the wiki's `Autopoiesis` page ("nothing makes itself; nothing is really autopoietic or self-organizing"), proposes Sympoiesis ("making-with") as alternative. This is not load-bearing for this note — IC's use of autopoiesis is under structural coupling, which is precisely the "making-with" dimension Haraway prefers — but any canon adoption should name Haraway's critique rather than ignore it.

## 7. Disposition

**Disposition: clarify existing term.** The wiki evidence warrants IC importing three distinctions from the autopoiesis/4E lineage — operational closure vs. informational openness; structural coupling vs. representation; enaction vs. symbol manipulation — and acknowledging participatory sense-making as a primitive at the interoperability surface. None of these clarifications require new IC canon primitives; they sharpen the theoretical backing of primitives already gestured at by IC's memory-layers and intelligence-primitives surfaces. The R-claims below do not become canon PRs during the Move 0 moratorium posture; they are proposed clarifications to canon language when review opens.

## 8. Claim Register

**C1** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Autopoiesis]
An autopoietic system, per Maturana and Varela (1972), is "a network of processes of production (transformation and destruction) of components which (i) through their interactions and transformations continuously regenerate and realize the network of processes (relations) that produced them; and (ii) constitute it (the machine) as a concrete unity in space in which they (the components) exist by specifying the topological domain of its realization as such a network" — making autopoiesis a specification of organizational (component-regenerating) closure, not informational isolation.

**C2** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Autopoiesis]
Autopoietic systems are "autonomous and operationally closed" yet "structurally coupled with their medium, embedded in a dynamic of changes that can be recalled as sensory-motor coupling" — establishing that operational closure (sufficient internal processes to maintain the whole) is compatible with, and in fact requires, structural coupling to the environment. This is the distinction between operational closure and informational openness that IC's memory-layer language should carry explicitly.

**C3** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Discussion · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Autopoiesis]
Jonas Andersen's reading via Luhmann on the same page distinguishes autopoietic dynamics from linear input-output systems: media such as speech organs, air, telephones, and the Internet taken as conduits are "autarchic" (linear) and are not themselves autopoietic; interaction between two autopoietic systems forms an autopoietic system of a higher cybernetic order. This licenses treating multi-agent memory coordination as a higher-order autopoietic system rather than as message-passing over a channel, and rebuts the reading of "coupling" as packet transmission.

**C4** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Relation to cognition · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Autopoiesis]
"With the publication of The Embodied Mind in 1991, Varela, Thompson and Rosch applied autopoesis to make non-representationalist, and enactive models of mind, brain and behavior, which further developed embodied cognitive neuroscience, later culminating in neurophenomenology." This positions enactive cognition — cognition as sensorimotor coupling, not representation — as the theoretical tradition directly downstream of the autopoiesis lineage IC would be invoking.

**C5** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Autopoiesis_and_Cognition]
Maturana and Varela (1972) establish the identity "living systems are cognitive systems, and living as a process is a process of cognition" — situating cognition as ongoing autopoietic maintenance under structural coupling, rather than as symbol manipulation or internal-model construction. This is the primary-source foundation for IC's intelligence-primitives surface if IC claims any autopoiesis lineage.

**C6** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Characteristics · orn:p2p-wiki.page:4E_Cognitive_Science]
4E cognitive science (per Alexander Beiner's synthesis) holds that cognition is embodied (inseparable from physical body, gestures, sensations), embedded (inseparable from adapted environment), enacted (every action creates new realities that constrain or open possibilities), and extended (moving through other people, technology, and environment as when tools become body). Vervaeke's extension adds emotional (all cognition has affective/motivational aspect, "about caring or not caring about taking a risk") and exaptive (re-purposing existing cognitive machinery — concepts exapt spatial-movement machinery). Together these six dimensions constrain what IC's intelligence-primitives can legitimately claim to model.

**C7** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Embodied_Cognition]
Lakoff and Johnson, as cited by Jose Ramos: "Understanding emerges from interaction, from constant negotiation with the environment and other people… the nature of our bodies and our physical and cultural environment impose a structure on our experience… recurrent experience leads to the formation of categories, which are experiential gestalts with those natural dimensions." Ramos extends: cognition "is located in us as individuals (cognising) and as groups (co-cognising)," with agents "structurally coupled' into cultural (e.g. language, religion, tradition), and ecological / geo-graphic contexts." This collapses any clean individual/collective divide in IC's agency model.

**C8** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Description · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Participatory_Sense-Making]
Di Paolo and De Jaegher's participatory sense-making: "the interaction process itself can take on a form of autonomy (operationally defined). This allows us to reframe the problem of social cognition as that of how meaning is generated and transformed in the interplay between the unfolding interaction process and the individuals engaged in it." The dyad — not the individual — is the primitive unit for social meaning-generation. This grounds IC's interoperability-as-institutional framing at the operational level: the interaction surface between memory-layers, agents, or knowledge commons is itself a governable autonomous unit.

**C9** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §More information · orn:p2p-wiki.page:Participatory_Sense-Making]
Fuchs and De Jaegher (2009) make the non-representationalist claim explicit: "Intersubjectivity, it is argued, is not a solitary task of deciphering or simulating the movements of others but means entering a process of embodied interaction and generating common meaning through it." This directly rejects IC intelligence-primitives framings that model inter-agent understanding as simulation or mental-state-inference over symbolic representations, and specifies instead that the interaction dynamic itself carries meaning-generation.

## 9. Review Claims

**R1** [review claim] [target: ic.memory-layers] [concept: autopoiesis]
IC's memory-layers framing should import the autopoiesis distinction between operational closure and informational openness: memory-layers are informationally open (structurally coupled with users, tools, documents, and adjacent layers) while organizationally closed (the layer regenerates its own structure through its own operations). This rules out two misreadings — "closed = walled off" and "open = structureless" — and gives IC a principled criterion for when a memory-layer is autopoietic-in-the-strong-sense (it regenerates its own components) versus merely allopoietic (it is assembled by an external process for an external purpose, like the cell-vs-factory distinction in the primary source). The canon text should state that autopoiesis is invoked as an analogy under structural coupling, not as a verified property of IC layers, until an operational test exists.
*R1 is supported by C1, C2, C3. Partial resolution of DH-IC-2.*

**R2** [review claim] [target: ic.memory-layers] [concept: structural-coupling]
IC's routing layer should be described as a coupling mechanism — recurrent perturbation dynamics between memory-layers, agents, and environments — rather than as a symbol-transmission protocol over a message alphabet. Routing specifications should track coupling regimes (who perturbs whom, at what cadence, producing what internal reorganization) in preference to symbolic payload schemas. Existing protocol surfaces can be retained as implementation detail but re-described in coupling terms at the canon layer. Expected consequence: sensorimotor-norm language replaces representation-and-message language in canon docs; "channel" becomes a legitimate implementation term but not a canon primitive. Carry Haraway's sympoiesis critique as a named caution: the frame is "making-with," not self-making.
*R2 is supported by C2, C3, C4. Partial resolution of DH-IC-3.*

**R3** [review claim] [target: ic.intelligence-primitives] [concept: enactive-cognition]
IC's intelligence-primitives should be framed within the 4E tradition (embodied, embedded, enacted, extended — plus Vervaeke's emotional and exaptive) rather than in the cognitivist/representationalist tradition. Primitives should be specifiable as sensorimotor coordination patterns between agents-in-environment, not as symbol-manipulation operations inside individual agents. The individual/collective boundary in IC's agency model should be explicitly dissolved along Ramos's lines: cognition is located in individuals (cognising) AND in groups (co-cognising), "structurally coupled into cultural and ecological contexts." This directly rebuts any reading of IC as a Cartesian individual-cognition stack with networking glue.
*R3 is supported by C4, C5, C6, C7.*

**R4** [review claim] [target: ic.connection.knowledge-commons-governance] [concept: collective-agency]
IC's knowledge-commons-governance framing should acknowledge participatory sense-making as a primitive at the interoperability surface: the interaction process between two memory-layers, two agents, or two knowledge commons can itself take on dyad-level autonomy, and the interoperability surface is therefore a first-class governable unit rather than an artifact of the parties. Operational consequence: the interoperability-as-institutional framing (already present in IC's commons-audience language) gains a cognitive-science primary source and gains the operational claim that meaning-generation is located in the interaction, not merely in the parties. Canon text should name the dyad (or n-adic interaction process) as a governed unit distinct from its participants.
*R4 is supported by C8, C9.*

**R5** [review claim] [target: ic.intelligence-primitives] [concept: interoperability-as-institutional]
IC's interoperability-as-institutional surface should include governance operations for the interaction dynamic itself (perturbation-regime rules, cadence norms, mutual-incorporation protocols) rather than limiting governance to the parties and their artifacts. This is the operational form of R4 and the enactive reading of Pass 1's governed-knowledge-commons framing: if the dyadic interaction has its own autonomy, then interoperability governance has work to do at the interaction layer that the existing two-party-plus-schema framing does not reach. Worked example to solicit in canon discussion: what is governed when two memory-layers enter a mutual-incorporation dynamic, beyond the schema they share and the logs they produce?
*R5 is supported by C6, C8, C9.*
