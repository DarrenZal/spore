## Structural Note: The Diagnostic Split Decision

The decision to split the diagnostic from the full architecture and
promote it independently is itself a consequence-bearing governance
choice — arguably more structurally significant than the promotion
itself. It established that:

1. A family's confirmed-mechanism layer can be promoted independently
   of its design-contribution and design-hypothesis layers.
2. The diagnostic provides a foundation, but promoting the foundation
   does not retroactively validate the architecture built on it.
3. Explicit exclusion lists (what is NOT promoted) are as important
   as the promotion itself.

This split pattern may be reusable for other families where the
diagnostic is stronger than the full design architecture.
