## Why This Is `scope_refinement` And Not `genuine_reopening`

The underlying claim (diagnostic + standing + broad replacement
principle) was not re-examined. The human reviewer approved the
substance. What changed was the wording — specifically, removing
a fourth mechanism that had drifted into the confirmed layer
and tightening prescriptive language to diagnostic-only phrasing.
The ground was not questioned; the boundary of what counts as
confirmed was enforced more precisely.

If this had been a case where the reviewer's constraints led to
re-examining whether the diagnostic itself was well-supported,
that would be `genuine_reopening`. Here, the diagnostic stood;
only the expression was refined.

## Why `closure_signal` Is False

This revision was not triggered by a settled term losing contact
with operational reality. It was triggered by detected wording
drift — the text was saying more than the evidence warranted.
This is boundary enforcement, not closure detection. The term
"human economy" was not found to be a stopping rule; the problem
was that the promotion packet's language was broader than the
reviewer-approved scope.
