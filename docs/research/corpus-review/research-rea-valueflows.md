# REA and ValueFlows: an accounting ontology stretched toward the commons

The REA tradition is a 44-year formalization project that began as a single-firm accounting reform and was slowly reconceived — first by its own founders, then more radically by Bob Haugen and Lynn Foster — as a general-purpose economic-coordination ontology. Its load-bearing primitive is not "resource," "event," or "agent" taken singly, but the **duality relation** binding paired economic events. The central tension the tradition never resolves is whether this duality is ontology-neutral structure or an embedded market assumption that survives every attempt to repurpose REA for non-market coordination. That unresolved question sits directly on top of the Spore project's intent→commitment→evidence axis: VF's answer is to stratify primitives across three temporal modalities (plan → observation, with a knowledge layer behind both), but internal disagreements about whether commitment is a *primitive* or a *scheduled-event shadow* persist from the 1980s into open 2024 GitLab issues.

This synthesis treats the arc in four voices — McCarthy (what REA *is*), Geerts (what REA becomes when axiomatized), Haugen/Foster (what REA *should be for* in peer production), and Hruby plus the formalization lineage (how REA becomes reusable pattern). Internal contradictions are foregrounded; absences are treated as informative.

## §1 — Primitives

**Resource (typed, quantified).** McCarthy 1982 defines economic resources as "scarce and under the control of the enterprise" — a formulation that embeds both scarcity (neoclassical) and property/control (juridical) at the definitional root [paywalled primary; claim rests on Hall textbook secondary summary and Geerts-McCarthy 2000 paraphrase]. Current ValueFlows explicitly preserves typed quantification but splits the class: `EconomicResource` (instances, inventoried) versus `ResourceSpecification` (type-level, covering material/digital/service/currency/skill) [valueflo.ws/specification/all_vf/, open-access]. VF further adds `BatchLotRecord` for provenance-grade production tracing. The "control of the enterprise" clause is **quietly dropped** in VF — Haugen's 2005 essay *Beyond the Enterprise* explicitly argued "human beings need to get past the part of the definition of an economic resource as being 'under the control of an enterprise', and consider all the necessities of all life to be resources: air, water, soil fertility, etc." [mikorizal.org, open-access].

**Economic event (give/take with duality).** In McCarthy 1982, an event is an increment or decrement of resources under firm control, paired in duality with its mirror (give-take). The 2000 Geerts-McCarthy axiomatization formalizes this as Axiom 2: "All events effecting an outflow must be eventually paired in duality relationships with events effecting an inflow and vice-versa" [Geerts & McCarthy 2000, open-access PDF at valueflo.ws/linked-docs/]. VF preserves the primitive as `EconomicEvent` — "an observed economic flow, which could reflect creation or a change in the quantity, location, accountability and/or responsibility, of an economic resource" — but decouples the strict duality by introducing `Action` taxonomy (19 actions in VF 1.0.0: produce, consume, use, work, cite, transfer-all-rights, transfer-custody, transfer, modify, combine, separate, pickup, dropoff, accept, deliverService, move, copy, raise, lower) that licenses non-dual events (e.g., `work` has a provider but no reciprocal counterparty, `raise`/`lower` are inventory adjustments with no dual) [valueflo.ws/concepts/actions/, open-access].

**Agent (party to events).** McCarthy partitions into *inside* and *outside* agents via a ternary control relationship. Weber's critique later forced decomposition into binary participation relations. VF flattens: `Agent` is abstract, subclasses are `Person`, `Organization`, and — critically — `EcologicalAgent` ("a non-human being; or a functional group of non-human beings; or an ecosystem of living beings that includes non-humans; but it has agency and receives and/or provides economic/ecological resources") [valueflo.ws, open-access]. VF 1.0.0 **removed** `AgentRelationship` and `AgentRelationshipRole` as "out of scope of the core economic model for now" [valueflo.ws/introduction/status/] — a scope-reduction move with consequences discussed in §4.

**Commitment (future event, scheduled).** Not in McCarthy 1982. Geerts (1997, OOPSLA) is explicit: "Commitment is a term taken from Ijiri (1975)… a commitment such as sales order is excluded from the REA model" [open-access, jeffsutherland.com/oopsla97/guido.html]. Added by Geerts-McCarthy 2000 as a new first-class entity class with its own *reciprocal* relationship, *executes* relation to events, *reserves* stock-flow, and type-image. VF treats Commitment as a full primitive — "a planned economic flow that has been scheduled or promised by one agent to another agent" — with its own IRI `vf:Commitment`, persisting independent of events [valueflo.ws, open-access].

**Claim (owed resource).** Intensionally defined in Geerts-McCarthy 2000: "A claim with an outside agent exists where there is a flow of resources with that agent without the full set of corresponding instances of a dual flow" — i.e., a claim is *derived* from duality imbalance, not a primitive. VF retains this derivation: `vf:Claim` is triggered by events "according to rules agreed to elsewhere" and settled by further events [valueflo.ws/specification/model-text/, open-access].

**Fulfillment (linking commitment to executing event).** In Geerts-McCarthy 2000 the relation is *executes* (Ijiri's term). ISO 15944-4 and VF relabel this *fulfillment*. In VF RDF it is the property `vf:fulfills` (EconomicEvent → Commitment), but in hREA's GraphQL API `Fulfillment` is reified as a first-class associative entity with CRUD operations [docs.hrea.io, open-access]. **This asymmetry matters**: the normative RDF treats fulfillment as a relation; the dominant implementation treats it as an entity — an auditor reading the spec and an auditor reading the hREA database would see different ontological furniture.

**Reciprocity (bilateral flow structure).** Structurally baked in at two levels. Between events: duality (exchanges). Between commitments: the *reciprocal* relationship (Geerts-McCarthy 2000). VF uses paired properties `vf:stipulates`/`vf:stipulatesReciprocal` and `vf:realizes`/`vf:realizesReciprocal` at the Agreement layer. Crucially, VF decouples reciprocity from money: "reciprocity with or without money, money is not assumed" [valueflo.ws, open-access].

**Process (groups related events).** Not prominent in McCarthy 1982; elevated in Geerts-McCarthy via enterprise script → process → task. VF promotes `Process` to a core primitive ("an activity that changes inputs into outputs, by transforming or transporting economic resource(s)") with a specification layer `ProcessSpecification`. Processes generalize event grouping beyond exchange — they accommodate conversion/transformation, which was a Haugen priority since the Sensorica era where production workflows needed first-class modeling.

**Contract (bundle of commitments).** Geerts-McCarthy 2000 reifies `EconomicAgreement` above reciprocal commitments, partitioned into `Contract` (for transfers) and `Schedule` (for transformations). VF collapses this back under a single abstract `Agreement` — "a set of reciprocal commitments among economic agents, or a set of reciprocal economic events," described in the spec as "purposefully abstract… so that it can represent many existing (or not-yet-invented) kinds of agreements." No separate `Contract` class. `AgreementBundle` aggregates Agreements for line-item reciprocity.

**Stock-flow distinction (REA inherits from accounting).** McCarthy's stock-flow relation binds resource to event; this is REA's most direct inheritance from double-entry accounting. VF retains the duality at the *action-behavior* layer rather than the ledger layer: every EconomicResource carries both `vf:accountingQuantity` ("amount for which the agent has primary rights… sometimes thought of as ownership") and `vf:onhandQuantity` ("amount under direct control"), and actions carry separate `accountingEffect` vs `onhandEffect` properties [valueflo.ws/concepts/accounting/, open-access]. The VF homepage announces the philosophical stance: "a simple and elegant model for planning and recording real-world economic activity, as opposed to the conventional abstraction of debits/credits, although those can be derived."

**Intent (VF addition, not in classical REA).** First-class VF primitive: "a desired or proposed or planned or estimated economic flow, usually with only one agent associated" — provider *or* receiver, not both. Lynn Foster, in the GitLab issue #662 "Intents vs Commitments": "We added Intents (and Proposals) in response to the common use case of published offers/wants… Intents are not part of REA." The semantic ancestor is Haugen's 2005 *Needs/Abilities* in *Beyond the Enterprise*: "it is often useful to create a looser precursor of Commitments that might be called Needs… and Abilities. Needs and Abilities are not commitments, they are not yet connected to any other Economic Agent. Each is like one half of a commitment, looking for its mate" [mikorizal.org, open-access]. The state progression Intent → Commitment → EconomicEvent (published in `/concepts/flows/`) is **exactly the intent→commitment→evidence arc the Spore project is auditing**.

### The commitment-primitive-vs-derived contradiction

McCarthy 1982 has no commitment. Geerts-McCarthy 2000 treat it as a genuinely new primitive with independent structural furniture. But the layered architecture (accountability / scheduling / policy) invites a reading in which commitment is a *scheduled or reserved shadow* of an event — derived from "event + future modality + type-image." The ORM paired-transactions literature (Ševčík, FedCSIS 2016 [open-access, annals-csis.org]) makes this explicit: it argues that the apparent commitment/event distinction collapses into *contracted* versus *completed* sub-states of a single transaction entity, and that this collapse "also eliminates usage of the claim temporal entity." Weigand et al. (VMBO 2017, [open-access]) critique that Geerts-McCarthy commitment lacks an independent state-machine, implying derivation. Hruby (2006) pushes the opposite direction: commitment is a first-class *pattern* with explicit state and behavior. VF inherits Hruby's position but then undermines it from the other side: Foster's own GitLab #662 argues that the criterion for distinguishing Intent from Commitment should be "firmness of plan, not commitments of agents" — which, if adopted, would make Commitment a *degree-of-firmness shadow* of Intent plus Plan, i.e., partially derived. The disagreement is active and unresolved as of 2024.

## §2 — Layering

REA literature distinguishes three layers, and VF adopts all three while adding a fourth semantic seam.

**REA-over-accounting-ledgers layer (foundation).** McCarthy 1982 is a single "accountability layer" — what has happened. Its radical move was to *defer* debits/credits to derived views rather than treat them as primitive [MSUToday 2026, open-access]. This foundation is not merely historical: every subsequent extension has to respect the duality axiom at the accountability layer, because this is where REA's auditability claim lives. The 2006 Geerts-McCarthy paper [author PDF open-access; journal paywalled] bifurcates into *Accountability Infrastructure* ("what is / what has happened") and *Policy Infrastructure* ("what should, could, or must be"), with a scheduling register in between. Two semantic abstractions are formalized — *Typification* (is-a-kind-of, archetypal, transitive) and *Grouping* (is-a-member-of, collection-level) — along with five reusable policy patterns (Basic, Mirror, Compromise, Hybrid, Root). This is REA's most formally developed layer.

**ValueFlows process layer + peer-production orientation (extension).** VF adopts a three-modality stratification explicitly: *Knowledge layer* (ResourceSpecification, ProcessSpecification, Recipe, RecipeProcess, RecipeFlow, RecipeExchange, Action definitions), *Plan layer* (Intent, Commitment, Proposal, Agreement, Plan, planned Process), *Observation layer* (EconomicEvent, EconomicResource, Claim, executed Process). The same input-process-output graph pattern repeats at all three layers, which is how VF achieves what the spec calls "pre-fill" — a recipe pre-fills plans, a plan pre-fills commitments, a commitment pre-fills events. This is the peer-production contribution: the three layers are not merely architectural (as in Geerts-McCarthy) but operationally *isomorphic*, enabling coordination across time without a privileged ledger. The peer-production orientation shows in the primitives this layer *adds* — `Intent`, `Proposal`, `ProposalList`, `Plan`, `EcologicalAgent`, `Recipe` — each of which addresses a use case (public offers/wants, published needs, multilateral planning, non-human agency, process prototypes) that is peripheral to accounting but central to commons coordination.

**Ontology-logic layer (OCCO/OWL serializations, Hruby patterns, ORM).** Downstream of theoretical foundation. Hruby (2006) provides the pattern-language version — over 20 structural/behavioral business patterns organized into operational and policy levels, with GoF-style templates [Springer, print/paywalled; content reconstructed from reviews, ToC, and Gailly-Poels secondary]. Gailly & Poels (2005–2008) produced OWL/OntoUML reengineering using METHONTOLOGY; Laurier, Kiehn & Polovina (2018, *Applied Ontology*) produced REA2 — the most ambitious post-Hruby re-axiomatization — and Laurier, Horiuchi & Snoeck (2021, *J. of Information Systems*) made it executable. OntoREA (Fischer-Pauzenberger & Schwaiger 2017) formalizes claims as asset/liability/equity imbalances in OntoUML [open-access, Semantic Scholar]. ISO/IEC 15944-4:2007 codifies REA as "Accounting and Economic Ontology" [ISO, paywalled]. ORM/NIAM engagement through Hunka & Matula's NORMA work provides fact-based NIAM modeling. No "Peter Krivine" was located in the ORM-REA literature; the name may be misremembered or the figure too minor to surface — treat as unconfirmed. This layer is real and cumulative, but remains in-house: it reformalizes REA rather than revising its ontological commitments.

A fourth implicit layer emerges in practice: the **implementation seam**, where the normative VF RDF and the dominant hREA GraphQL API diverge (Satisfaction, Fulfillment, Appreciation, Settlement are properties in RDF but first-class entities in GraphQL; Scenario appears in hREA but was deferred from VF 1.0.0). This seam is where auditing questions actually land.

## §3 — Placement of universal concepts

**Trust.** Not load-bearing. REA's auditability substitutes for trust: the event log + duality axiom + type-image structure is supposed to make trust unnecessary by making activity fully reconstructible. Absence is notable — commons literature treats trust as central to peer coordination, but VF does not primitivize it.

**Care.** Absent as primitive. Care work is handled by expanding *which activities get logged* — Sensorica's NRP recorded Facilitation, Community Building, Training, Administration as work events [nrp.sensorica.co, open-access]. The ontology gives you primitives to log care; it does not tell you how to value it. Feminist-economics literature (Waring, Folbre, Vaughan) argues this is precisely the problem with accounting-derived ontologies — care is rendered legible only insofar as it fits the exchange-event frame.

**Identity.** Thin. Agent identity is assumed primitive (Person, Organization, EcologicalAgent) but not elaborated; VF 1.0.0 explicitly deferred AgentRelationship as out of scope. hREA's "network seed" concept handles group identity operationally but is not in the normative ontology.

**Memory.** Implicit in the event log. The Observation layer *is* the memory — every flow that happened, with provenance links (VF has partial PROV-O alignment via Haugen's 2016 wiki edits). No separate memory primitive.

**State.** Present at two levels: resource state (`stateEffect` property on actions, carrying `fresh`/`damaged`/`dirty` etc.) and commitment lifecycle state (implied by fulfillment). Critics (ORM/Ševčík 2016) argue REA lacks a proper state-machine for commitments.

**Signals.** Haugen's most distinctive contribution. The 2016 essay *How the Signals used by Capitalist Supply Chains could serve a Mutual Coordination Economy* [P2PF blog / Semantic Scholar, open-access] reframes REA events and intents as *coordination signals* independent of price — "demand signals for a Mutual Coordination Economy will be based on human and ecological needs in terms of use-value, and not on needs for profit (exchange-value)." In the ontology this manifests as Intent and Proposal being publishable signals of need/offer. Not a named primitive, but load-bearing in Haugen's framing.

**Agency.** Central — REA's "A." VF extends to non-human agency via `EcologicalAgent`. Agency is *structural* (role in events) rather than *psychological* (capacity for intention); intent as psychological state is displaced into the `Intent` primitive.

**Norms/protocols.** Present as the *Policy layer* (Geerts-McCarthy 2006) and as `RecipeExchange`/`Recipe` in VF. Weigand et al. 2017 critique that Geerts-McCarthy "do not provide a policy specification language" — i.e., the layer exists in principle but is under-tooled.

**Power/authority.** Structurally absent. McCarthy's inside/outside agent distinction presupposes a firm boundary and a hierarchy, but authority is not primitivized. VF 1.0.0 explicitly deferred this by removing AgentRelationship. Haugen's OVN-era work gestures at it ("no one works for anyone else" as principle) but does not encode it.

**Commitment.** Primary in VF's plan layer; contested primitive-vs-derived status (see §1 and §4).

**Intent.** VF's most notable addition beyond classical REA. First-class primitive; semantic ancestor in Haugen's 2005 *Needs/Abilities* — "each is like one half of a commitment, looking for its mate." Solves the problem that McCarthy's commitment presupposes a counterparty.

**Evidence.** Not primitivized as such, but structurally present in the Observation layer. An EconomicEvent *is* evidence — it is what actually happened. The `fulfills` relation links commitment to its evidence. For the Spore audit this mapping is clean: evidence ≈ EconomicEvent + provenance.

**Holon-like-unit (whole-and-part).** Partial. `Plan` is a logical collection of Processes; `Process` groups related Events; `Agreement` groups Commitments; `AgreementBundle` groups Agreements. Recursive composition exists (a Plan can contain Processes which contain Events), but REA does not theorize holons as such — the part-whole structure is pragmatic, not theoretical.

**Boundary.** McCarthy's inside/outside agent distinction is a boundary primitive — but tied to the single-firm view. VF weakens this: "coordination of economic activity both inside and between organizations, an enterprise is not assumed" [valueflo.ws]. Network-level boundaries (what's in a commons, what's out) are handled operationally (hREA network seeds) rather than ontologically.

**Reciprocity.** Structurally central (duality + reciprocal commitments). But VF explicitly declares reciprocity can exist "with or without money" — loosening reciprocity from exchange duality.

**Reproduction/continuity.** Weakly present. The Knowledge layer (Recipes, Specifications) encodes what can be *re-enacted* — this is continuity-as-pattern. But social reproduction in the feminist-economics sense (care, maintenance, household labor) is absent as primitive.

## §4 — Internal disagreements

**Accounting-first (McCarthy) vs peer-production-first (Haugen).** Foster stated the reframe most cleanly in 2015: NRP "does open accounting… but more as a side effect of being operational software for economic networks" [resilience.org, open-access]. Haugen's 2005 *Beyond the Enterprise* had already argued that REA should be treated "as a general-purpose economic ontology," not an accounting model. The critical move was stripping the "under the control of an enterprise" clause from resource definition. Yet the accounting-first framing wins back ground in VF 1.0.0: the release notes explicitly say scope was narrowed to "operational planning and observation of economic activity," with bioregional/pre-operational features and the AgentRelationship model *removed* for being "out of scope of the core economic model." This is a candid retreat toward accounting-operational discipline and away from peer-production breadth. Bonfire and hREA handle the peer-production social layer *outside* VF proper, which means the current architecture actually enforces the separation: VF-the-vocabulary is accounting-like; commons coordination happens in application layers above it.

**Bilateral vs multilateral flow modeling.** McCarthy's REA is fundamentally bilateral — "an arm's length collaboration between the entrepreneur and a trading partner." Axiom 3 (Geerts-McCarthy 2000) enforces exactly one inside and one outside agent per exchange. Multi-party exchanges must be decomposed into pairwise dualities. Haugen's 2016 Forum Issue #60 ("value equations and barter") is where the VF team openly struggled with multi-party barter; the resolution was *not* to remove bilateral duality at the event level but to add non-dual, non-bilateral primitives *above* it (Proposal bundling multiple Intents; Agreement bundling multiple reciprocal commitments). hREA today advertises "bilateral and multilateral trade requests," but the multilateral case is modeled as a *graph* of bilateral events against shared-accountable resources, not as a single multilateral primitive. True N→N commons provisioning ("pool in, distribute to many") stretches the model.

**Commitment as primitive vs derived from scheduled event.** The single highest-value disagreement for Spore. McCarthy 1982: commitment excluded. Geerts-McCarthy 2000: commitment added as first-class primitive with independent structure. Geerts-McCarthy 2006 layered reading: commitment as *scheduling image* of event — invites derivation. Hruby 2006: first-class pattern with explicit state. Ševčík (ORM, 2016): commitment dissolvable into contracted/completed sub-states of a single transaction. Foster's 2024 GitLab issue #662: criterion should be "firmness of plan, not commitments of agents" — which partially derives commitment from Intent + Plan. **The tradition has never agreed.** VF ships it as primitive but cannot close the theoretical question.

**REA's ontology-neutrality vs embedded economic assumptions.** REA is presented as neutral across domain and computing paradigm (Geerts-McCarthy 2006 explicitly) and independent of debit/credit convention. Critics locate at least four embedded assumptions: (a) **property**, in "scarce and under the control of"; (b) **exchange**, in Axiom 2's pairing requirement; (c) **valuation**, in requiring commensurability for pairing; (d) **reciprocity**, in commitment/event pairing presupposing a returning counterparty. Weigand (2020, *What is in an Accounting Ontology?*, CEUR Vol-2835 [open-access]) argues REA "is not actually an accounting ontology — it is an exchange ontology." Geerts's own 1997 OOPSLA paper flags duality as "hardwired in the conceptual structure." Haugen's reframe is that REA-as-ontology is neutral *only once* tacit defaults (enterprise boundary, monetary exchange, human-only agents, externalized externalities) are explicitly dropped — which is what VF's four "is not assumed" bullets do [valueflo.ws]. The disagreement is thus whether neutrality is a property of the formalism (McCarthy's view) or a property of how the formalism is deployed (Haugen's view).

**Crypto/tokenization — diluting or sharpening.** Haugen's stance is instrumental: tokens are plumbing. Holochain's agent-centric alternative to blockchain is favored; FairCoin/FreedomCoop integration sharpens (token as one resource among many) while speculative DAO tokenomics dilutes (token replaces the accounting model rather than being a resource within it). Academic engagement is sparse: Gal, Snoeck & Laurier (2021) use REA for smart-contract audit; Laurier et al. (2021) did a REA blockchain implementation. The DAO world, however, has not reached for REA — Web3-DAO (Fernández-Barrera et al. 2024) builds a governance-token ontology without citing REA. The structural critique: tokenization flattens agents to holders, collapses exchange duality into atomic swaps, and erases process/commitment/policy layers REA painstakingly distinguishes. This is evidence that crypto adoption has mostly *diluted* REA by bypassing it, not *sharpened* it.

## §5 — Critiques and limits

**Under-engagement with non-market economies.** The duality axiom is REA's structural signature and its structural limit. Gifts, care, ecological services, and most commons provisioning do not meet the outflow-inflow pairing requirement in any bounded frame. VF's actions taxonomy (work, cite, use, raise, lower) patches this by licensing non-dual events, but the *accounting* of these flows (how much care is "worth"?) is deferred to per-network "value equations" (Sensorica's term) rather than solved at the ontology level. Feminist economics has not systematically engaged VF; VF proponents cite feminist concerns, but the reciprocation is absent — a revealing asymmetry.

**Accounting bias limits ecological/social-value modeling.** Laurier & Poels (2012, *JETA* 9(1)) extended REA for environmental management accounting, but the framing is *extension*, not *reconception*. Resources remain commodified units that flow; waste is an extra outflow event rather than a different ontological category. Degrowth and ecological-economics literature engagement with REA is essentially zero.

**ValueFlows adoption slower than hoped.** The evidence is consistent and sympathetic insiders are candid. valueflows/valueflows has ~138 stars and small active-contributor count; the org moved to lab.allmende.io in 2019. hREA is at 0.1.0-beta ("Sapling") as of late 2025 despite development since ~2018. Sensorica's flagship `true_commons` repo runs on demo data with backend not deployed. Bonfire's VF integration is pre-1.0. Sacha Pignot's October 2025 Holochain ecosystem reality-check (hAppenings Community Substack, [open-access]) concedes "every major application remains in alpha or development stages" with production timeline "1-2 years." The only fully "complete" deployments are time-limited EU grants (Reflow, Interfacer, Dyne Digital Product Passport). The pattern is: the vocabulary matured toward 1.0; the implementations remain perpetually pre-1.0.

**Tooling ecosystem immature.** VF has partial JSON-Schema, a shared GraphQL reference schema, and Turtle/RDF as system-of-record. No SDKs for mainstream stacks; no VF-native BI/reporting tools; no validator comparable to SHACL suites. Repository hygiene is uneven — `valueflows-project-metarepo` is marked OUTDATED; `vf-examples-jsonld-context` has moved; several docs carry "out of date" warnings. Core maintainer count is small (Foster, Haugen, Pavlik, pospi, adaburrows). This is consistent with a research ontology maintained by a lean volunteer team, not a standardization effort backed by institutional resources.

**Parallel rather than integrative engagement from adjacent ontologies.** FIBO does not absorb REA (Bennett 2013 cites McCarthy as background, not foundation). CCO's commercial-exchange ontology (Merrell, Massin & Smith, CEUR Vol-2969) re-derives exchange duality from BFO realism and Walrasian economics without citing McCarthy. DAO ontologies ignore REA entirely. This is the strongest evidence that REA remains a small, internally cited community — rigorous on its own terms, but not a shared foundation for the broader ontology-engineering world.

## Conclusion

REA's arc is the story of a formalism repeatedly stretched past its origin frame and never quite breaking. McCarthy 1982 was a single-firm accounting reform. Geerts axiomatized it into a domain ontology by 2000 and layered policy semantics by 2006. Hruby turned it into a pattern language. Haugen spent 2012–2016 stripping the enterprise boundary, the control clause, and the monetary assumption — producing not a new formalism but a re-annotated REA, where every "is not assumed" bullet marks a tacit default made explicit. VF 1.0.0 shipped in 2024 as the mature-state snapshot, and immediately narrowed scope by dropping bioregional planning and agent relationships as "out of scope" — a move that resolves the accounting-vs-peer-production tension in favor of accounting discipline at the vocabulary layer, while outsourcing peer-production coordination to application-layer tools (Bonfire, hREA).

For the Spore audit the key findings are: (1) **The intent → commitment → evidence arc maps cleanly onto VF's Intent → Commitment → EconomicEvent state progression**, and this is the most direct structural affordance the tradition offers. (2) **Commitment's primitive-vs-derived status is unresolved** across the tradition's full arc — any Spore position on commitment will step into a live disagreement, not a settled consensus. (3) **Evidence is structurally present but not named** — VF treats the EconomicEvent *as* evidence, which means auditing frameworks downstream of VF inherit an observational epistemology rather than a separate evidentiary primitive. (4) **Signal is load-bearing in Haugen's framing but absent as primitive** — Spore's signal primitive has no direct counterpart, though Intent and Proposal operationalize parts of what signal might mean. (5) **Holon has no theoretical treatment** — recursive composition exists pragmatically (Plan > Process > Event) but the whole-part structure is not theorized. (6) **The duality axiom survives every reframing**; any Spore primitive that presupposes non-dual relationality (gift, care, pure stewardship) will stretch the tradition's load-bearing commitment. These are not gaps in a deficient tradition — they are the price paid for REA's auditability guarantee, which is why the tradition matters in the first place.

**Access-channel caveats.** McCarthy 1982 and Hruby 2006 — the two canonical primaries — are both access-limited for verbatim quotation and are treated here as primary-access-limited, with claims triangulated through open-access secondary summaries where possible.
