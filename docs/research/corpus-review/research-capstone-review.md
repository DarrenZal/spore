# External review: research-capstone.md

## Summary

Revise before feeding this capstone into Phase 4 diagnostic. The analytical core in §§1-5 is mostly usable, but §7 and §8 are not yet reliable enough: the v3 admission list does not consistently match the source syntheses or the frozen v2 vocabulary, and the priority ranking omits a concept the capstone itself calls one of the strongest reasons to expand canon. The strongest finding is that §7 mishandles identity and collective-intentionality at exactly the point where it should be doing the cleanest vocab governance work. The weakest section is §7, with spillover incoherence into §8.

## 2. Missing convergences / divergences

This belongs in §1, not only §3.

- **Trace / log / monitoring / archive is an omitted convergence.** Distributed Systems calls the log a primitive at [research-distributed-systems.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-distributed-systems.md) lines 77-79 and memory/logged state at 105-105; REA makes the observation layer the memory and event the evidence at [research-rea-valueflows.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-rea-valueflows.md) lines 66-66 and 82-82; Commons makes monitoring load-bearing at [research-commons-governance.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-commons-governance.md) lines 32-33; Governance-Process builds memory into archives and implementation evidence at [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 54-54 and 70-70; VSM gives S3* audit at [research-viable-system-model.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-viable-system-model.md) lines 30-30 and 92-92. - **Continuity/reproduction has wider support than §1 credits.** Governance-Process and Structured Disagreement are omitted from the supporter list even though [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 78-78 and [research-structured-disagreement.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-structured-disagreement.md) lines 90-90 clearly treat renewal/replication as load-bearing.

### Candidate additions to §2 divergences

- **Boundary ontology should be a named divergence.** The sources do not just disagree on whether boundary exists; they disagree on what a boundary is. Canonical layering treats it as passive architectural separation at [research-canonical-layering.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-canonical-layering.md) lines 18-23 and 60-60. Commons gives a double social/ecological boundary and semi-permeable membrane at [research-commons-governance.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-commons-governance.md) lines 20-21 and 82-82. Autopoiesis treats boundary as self-specified through operational closure at [research-autopoiesis-4e.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-autopoiesis-4e.md) lines 18-18 and replaces signal ontology with structural coupling at 78-78. Distributed Systems reduces boundary to partition/failure-cell limits at [research-distributed-systems.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-distributed-systems.md) lines 118-120. This affects Spore's membrane primitive directly and should not be left implicit in §5.

- **Identity semantics should be a named divergence.** The capstone treats identity mainly as a convergence with dissenters, but the sources show incompatible identity models: relational selfhood in [research-care-ethics.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-care-ethics.md) lines 87-101, kinship/territory identity in [research-pluriversal.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-pluriversal.md) lines 25-46, system identity in [research-viable-system-model.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-viable-system-model.md) lines 68-70, cryptographic identity in [research-distributed-systems.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-distributed-systems.md) lines 107-107, persistent tag identity in [research-trust-reputation.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-trust-reputation.md) lines 85-90 and 125-128, and authorship/sponsorship identity in [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 52-52. That is a substantive divergence, not just a dissent line.

- **Trust semantics could also justify a separate divergence if trust remains on the table.** [research-trust-reputation.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-trust-reputation.md) lines 206-221 split trust across expectation/calculus, relational act, and moralistic faith. The capstone mentions this in §2 stakes but does not elevate it to a standalone disagreement.

## 3. Missing gaps / miscategorized primitives

- **Temporality / futurity is a missing candidate gap.** The corpus repeatedly treats time-structure as load-bearing: logical time in [research-distributed-systems.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-distributed-systems.md) lines 20-30, outside-and-then in [research-viable-system-model.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-viable-system-model.md) lines 32-34 and 48-50, plan/observation layering in [research-rea-valueflows.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-rea-valueflows.md) lines 14-15 and 50-52, review periods and lifecycle states in [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 22-22 and 44-44, and path dependence in [research-agent-based-modeling.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-agent-based-modeling.md) lines 45-47. §3 should at least have evaluated whether Spore needs an explicit temporality/lifecycle concept.

- **Accountability / legitimacy is another missing candidate gap.** Commons makes monitors accountable to appropriators at [research-commons-governance.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-commons-governance.md) lines 32-33 and notes legitimacy/power blind spots at 72-72. Governance-Process enumerates powers and appeal structures at [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 26-26 and 64-64. Structured Disagreement treats reviewer authority as under-theorized at [research-structured-disagreement.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-structured-disagreement.md) lines 76-82. Even if Spore ultimately derives accountability from commitment + evidence + protocol, the omission from §3 is a gap.

- **Identity is miscategorized too narrowly as a property of holon.** §3 says "admit as a named property of holon" ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 66-71), but the source syntheses place identity on agents, systems, and institutional roles, not only on wholes/parts: [research-distributed-systems.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-distributed-systems.md) lines 107-107, [research-trust-reputation.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-trust-reputation.md) lines 85-90 and 125-128, [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 52-52, [research-viable-system-model.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-viable-system-model.md) lines 68-70. If Spore wants "identity" as a property, "property of holon" is too narrow.

- **Reciprocity as derived is premature.** §3 treats reciprocity as structurally derivable from paired commitments ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 103-106), but the sources make the symmetry/asymmetry split constitutive, not residual: [research-care-ethics.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-care-ethics.md) lines 215-223, [research-pluriversal.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-pluriversal.md) lines 62-72, and [research-commons-governance.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-commons-governance.md) lines 84-86. Derived status may still be right, but the capstone has not yet earned that conclusion.

- **Reproduction / continuity is internally unstable between §3 and §7.** §3 makes it a "named continuity property of field" ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 108-113), while §7 upgrades it to an admitted primitive ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 251-251). The document needs a single classification.

- **Power / authority is under-justified as a deferral.** §3 calls it the "second-sharpest gap" with paraphrase failure ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 94-99), but §7 defers it and §8 pushes it to Tier 3 ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 279-279 and 337-337). The corpus support is not massive, but [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 64-64, [research-care-ethics.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-care-ethics.md) lines 193-200, [research-pluriversal.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-pluriversal.md) lines 144-159, and [research-viable-system-model.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-viable-system-model.md) lines 74-76 make the issue sharper than a low-priority hold-open suggests.

## 4. §7 v3 admission list — critique

- **`care`** is the strongest admissible addition in §7, but the cross-check against existing frozen vocabulary is too thin. v2 already contains care-adjacent slugs such as `reproductive-commoning` at [concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml](docs/research/concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml) lines 140-144 and `commons-as-verb` at 167-167, so §7 should explain why a plain `care` or `care-commitment` slug is not already partially covered.

- **`protocol`** is a defensible admission candidate, but the slug discussion mixes ontology and rule taxonomy. `protocol-level`, `operational-rule`, `collective-choice-rule`, and `constitutional-rule` are not equivalent proposals. §7 should also distinguish this from the already-frozen `protocol-society` at [concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml](docs/research/concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml) lines 60-63.

- **`reproduction-continuity`** has a defensible rationale, but it overlaps materially with the already-frozen `reproductive-commoning` slug at [concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml](docs/research/concepts-p2p-wiki.yaml) lines 140-144. The capstone needs to say whether this is a broader primitive above that existing concept, a rename, or a near-duplicate.

- **`trust` as derived** is plausible, but the rationale is incomplete. The trust literature's internal split is not just engineering-vs-non-engineering; [research-trust-reputation.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-trust-reputation.md) lines 206-221 show incompatible trust ontologies even within the trust tradition. If trust stays derived, §7 should say that this semantic conflict is a reason for derivation.

- **`reciprocity` as derived** is not self-consistent with the rest of §7. The same section says care must likely be added because symmetry breaks, yet `reciprocity` is still treated as a paired-commitment derivative ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 257-257). That verdict is at best provisional.

- **`identity-as-relational` is not defensible as written.** The phrase "9 load-bearing" ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 259-259) reuses the broader §3 identity count, but the underlying sources disagree sharply on what identity is: cryptographic key/tag in Distributed Systems at [research-distributed-systems.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-distributed-systems.md) lines 107-107, persistent handle in Trust at [research-trust-reputation.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-trust-reputation.md) lines 85-90 and 125-128, individual authorship in Governance-Process at [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 52-52, and relational selfhood in Care and Pluriversal at [research-care-ethics.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-care-ethics.md) lines 87-101 and [research-pluriversal.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-pluriversal.md) lines 25-36. Either recount the relational subset or rename the term to plain `identity`.

- **`attestation-of-execution` is too narrow a rename for `evidence`.** It fits Distributed + REA + Governance-Process ([research-rea-valueflows.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-rea-valueflows.md) lines 82-82; [research-governance-process.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-governance-process.md) lines 70-70), but it does not fit Commons monitoring or Structured Disagreement's evidence-about-the-review-process ([research-commons-governance.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-commons-governance.md) lines 78-78; [research-structured-disagreement.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-structured-disagreement.md) lines 82-82). This should be framed as one admissible reading, not the corpus-wide settlement.

- **§1 reciprocity is over-framed as convergence.** The document itself says the symmetry/asymmetry split is central ([research-capstone.md](docs/research/corpus-review/research-capstone.md) lines 22-22 and 257-257). My verdict: reciprocity should either be reframed as "recurrent but semantically split" or moved out of the top-level convergence list.

- **§7 drops the caution level established in §§1-6.** §§1-6 are mostly literature-synthesis prose with caveats. §7-8 switch into decision-memo voice and start minting candidate slugs and rankings, but some caveats do not survive the transition. The identity count issue and the missing v2 cross-check on `collective-intentionality` are examples of that voice shift causing content errors.

- **No major dangling internal references found.** Section references such as reciprocity's return in §2/§7 and the trace/log return in §8 do correspond to material that exists.
