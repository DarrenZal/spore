This memo reassesses the 8 deferred Johar families after 5 canon enactments and the initial evidence-stack implementation (Phases 1–2 plus templates). It is a triage checkpoint only. No promotion, synthesis, evidence capture, or canon editing is happening here.

**What has changed since checkpoint 1:**

- The evidence posture is now explicit: `real_record` / `seed_record` / `source_incomplete_stub` with bolded provenance lead-ins. Demo/hackathon data is clearly marked as non-evidence.

Local evidence posture is now an explicit variable in the triage. Families that depend on local operational evidence to justify their distinctiveness or operational relevance must be assessed against the fact that the evidence stack contains no true local commitment-level `real_record` and that the promoted entries themselves have no operational governance traces yet. External operational precedent (e.g., CLC for commitment-based coordination) remains real precedent but is not local Spore/BKC evidence.

## 3. Per-Family Notes

### 3.1 Conversational Sovereignty

**What still seems distinct now:** Nothing beyond what checkpoint 1 identified. The entry/exit rights dimension remains the only thin remainder, and it is a design parameter, not a standalone family.

**What now seems absorbed, weakened, or newly clarified:** The 5 promotions have further filled the surrounding territory. Human-economy's enacted canon text covers commitment-based coordination (conversational sovereignty was partly about who can participate in which coordination processes). Holding-complexity covers the institutional capacity to hold plural perspectives open (conversational sovereignty was partly about how plural truths are navigated). The gap between what civic-truth, linguistic-closure, and legitimacy cover and what conversational sovereignty named is now negligible.

**How the evidence-stack reality changes its standing:** No effect. Conversational sovereignty is a structural concept, not an evidence-dependent claim. The evidence stack does not touch this family.

**Whether it matters for Spore as:** Design parameter. **What kind of next pass it would need:** None. This family is absorbed.

### 3.2 Informational Duty

**What still seems distinct now:** The obligation frame — "the duty not to pollute" the epistemic commons. This still addresses civic-truth's unresolved OQ3 (who governs the boundaries of the epistemic commons without recreating monopoly?). The concept bridges the mechanism (linguistic-closure) and the designed condition (civic-truth) with a specific governance obligation.

**What now seems absorbed, weakened, or newly clarified:** The 5 promoted entries have structured the surrounding territory more thoroughly. Linguistic-closure is now in canon as the failure mode. The grammar's structural correctives (claim lifecycle, discourse graph, attestation decay) are now named as anti-closure infrastructure. The gap that informational duty fills has narrowed: it is now specifically OQ3 of civic-truth — "who enforces the commons boundary" — not a broader epistemic-governance question.

At checkpoint 1, informational duty was assessed as a conditional survivor with the question: "does the duty frame add enough structural content to justify a standalone synthesis, or should it be folded into civic-truth?" The answer is now clearer. With 5 canon enactments providing the structural mechanisms, the remaining question (who enforces boundaries) is more naturally a design parameter or open question inside civic-truth than a standalone family. The duty frame is an answer to OQ3, not a separate design dimension.

**How the evidence-stack reality changes its standing:** Indirectly. The evidence stack confirms that even promoted entries have no operational governance traces yet. The informational duty concept presupposes operational governance at a level of maturity that does not exist — you need running governance before you can meaningfully test whether a duty-not-to-pollute is structurally necessary or whether the existing mechanisms (claim lifecycle, learning membrane) are sufficient. This makes the concept more remote from operational grounding than it was at checkpoint 1, further arguing for deferral as an open question rather than active pursuit.

**Whether it matters for Spore as:** Open question inside civic-truth. When civic-truth eventually enters a promotion batch, OQ3 should be addressed — and the informational-duty material (the speech-and-duty bridge note) is the resource for that. But this is follow-on work within civic-truth's promotion cycle, not a standalone family trajectory.

**What kind of next pass it would need:** Not a scoping assessment or corroboration pass. When civic-truth enters a future promotion batch, the informational-duty bridge note should be consulted to address OQ3. No standalone pass is needed.

### 3.8 Boundary-Making and Entangled Systems

**What still seems distinct now:** The formal vocabulary for describing system architecture using Markov blankets as a replacement for traditional boundary language. Differential pass-through as the observable phenomenon. Conditional-independence gradients as the topology. Mediation structures as the design target. Breakdown as regime reconfiguration rather than line-crossing. This vocabulary targets spore.lexicon and proposes a new primitive — not a refinement of existing vocabulary.

The bridge note is the most technically precise source document in the entire Johar corpus: 1337 words, referencing Markov blankets from probabilistic graphical models, proposing a rigorous alternative to container metaphysics. No other synthesized or promoted family addresses this territory.

**What now seems absorbed, weakened, or newly clarified:** Very little. The 5 promotions confirm the surrounding territory:

- Holding-complexity covers the overarching diagnosis (complexity exceeds control) but does not address boundary formalism. The diagnostic tells us *why* we need better coordination; boundary-making would tell us *how to formally describe the interfaces* between coordinating systems.
- Relational-topology covers patterning of connection and encounter but does not offer formal boundary vocabulary. Relational-topology is about the relational field itself; boundary-making is about the formal description of how systems individuate within coupled dependency graphs.
- Linguistic-closure covers the epistemic failure mode but not system boundaries.
- Human-economy and legitimacy cover governance mechanisms but not systems ontology.

The gap between the promoted/synthesized families and what boundary-making names has not narrowed. If anything, the promoted entries make the gap more visible: the grammar now has 5 enacted entries describing *why* coordination under complexity matters and *what* the coordination mechanisms are, but it still lacks formal vocabulary for describing *how systems maintain coherence through mediated coupling* — which is what boundary-making provides.

**How the evidence-stack reality changes its standing:** No direct effect. Boundary-making is a formal vocabulary proposal, not an evidence-dependent operational claim. The evidence stack is designed to observe commitment lifecycle, decision traces, and revision events — none of which directly test boundary vocabulary. However, the evidence stack's mediation-structure architecture (how evidence flows across boundaries between evidence types, how provenance crosses from one context to another) is itself an instance of the mediation-structure concept that boundary-making names. This is an interesting structural parallel but not a validation argument.

**Whether it matters for Spore as:** Future lexicon candidate (lexicon-level primitive). The grammar currently uses "holons," "membranes," "protocols," and "projections" to describe its coordination architecture. The boundaries-in-systems bridge note argues these are mediation structures — sets of variables that concentrate dependency and structure transmission. The vocabulary (differential pass-through, conditional-independence gradients, regime reconfiguration) would give the grammar a formally precise way to describe what holonic membranes do — currently described functionally ("holons participate in multiple overlapping wholes"), not formally.

**What kind of next pass it would need:** A scoping assessment — not a synthesis or corroboration pass. The assessment should determine whether the Markov-blanket vocabulary translates from complexity science / computational neuroscience formalism to governance and coordination design vocabulary. Karl Friston's active inference framework is the obvious starting point: it uses Markov blankets as a foundational concept for how biological systems maintain coherence through selective coupling with their environment. The question is whether this usage translates to institutional/governance design — whether "shaping mediation structures" is operationally useful as a design principle for coordination protocols, not just as a modeling tool.

If the scoping assessment finds that the translation works, boundary-making would enter a corroboration pass and eventually a promotion batch as a lexicon candidate. If the formalism does not translate beyond modeling, the family should be reclassified as an external comparative lens rather than a Spore family.

## 4. Effect Of The New Canon And Evidence Stack

### What the 5 promoted entries newly absorb

The 5 enacted canon entries have collectively absorbed more deferred-family territory than the 3 original promotions did at checkpoint 1:

- **Human-economy's promotion** further absorbs `money-capital-and-future-claims`. The enacted text names commitment-based economic coordination as the structural response to wage/transaction failure. The capital-as-future-claim framing is now a design parameter inside human-economy (how commitments handle temporal assumptions), not a standalone concept. It also further absorbs `conversational-sovereignty`'s participation dimension — standing and commitment protocols now cover who can participate and under what conditions.

- **Holding-complexity's promotion** further absorbs `civic-intelligence` and `institutional-transition`. The diagnostic slice (control failure, two attractors, adaptive coordination requirement) now names what both deferred families were trying to point at from less precise angles. It does *not* absorb `boundary-making` — the diagnostic tells us complexity exceeds control, but boundary-making tells us how to formally describe the interfaces between systems that must hold complexity together.

- The 3 original promotions (legitimacy, linguistic-closure, relational-topology) continue to absorb `conversational-sovereignty`, `spatial-service`, and large portions of `informational-duty`'s surrounding territory, as identified at checkpoint 1.

### What the evidence stack clarifies

The evidence stack establishes a concrete evidentiary discipline that makes several things explicit:

1. **The gap between design and operation.** All 5 promoted entries are enacted in canon as structural accounts. None have operational evidence traces. The evidence stack (Phases 1–2) provides the infrastructure to observe commitment lifecycle, decision-trace, and revision-depth evidence — but the artifacts currently in the stack are seed/stub records, not operational observations. This means the grammar's claims about how coordination works are still theoretical, not empirically tested.

2. **What kind of evidence each family would need.** The production evidence mapping (batch 1) identified specific validation targets for each promoted entry. Deferred families that depend on evidence the current stack cannot produce are further weakened. Specifically: `money-capital-and-future-claims` would need temporal-energetic claim tracking that the evidence stack is not designed for. `Boundary-making` would need formal vocabulary translation validation, which is a research question, not an evidence-collection question.

3. **Provenance discipline prevents false confidence.** The evidence-posture convention (`real_record` vs `seed_record` vs `source_incomplete_stub`) and bolded provenance lead-ins ensure that demo/hackathon data cannot be mistaken for operational evidence. This discipline matters for the triage because it prevents any deferred family from being revived on the basis of data that looks like evidence but isn't.

## 10. Guardrails

- **Promoted canon entries do not automatically validate adjacent deferred families.** Legitimacy's promotion does not strengthen conversational sovereignty. Linguistic-closure's promotion does not strengthen informational duty. Human-economy's promotion does not validate money-capital-and-future-claims. Holding-complexity's promotion does not validate boundary-making. Each family stands or falls on its own evidence and distinctiveness.

- **External operational precedent is not the same as local Spore/BKC evidence.** CLC's 26K-user commitment-based coordination is real precedent for the commitment-economy concept. But it is not local evidence that Spore's grammar works as described. The evidence stack requires local observation — operational governance traces from BKC pools, commitment lifecycle events, decision records with consequence-bearing metadata — before any claim about the grammar's operational validity can be made.

- **Demo/hackathon data must not be treated as production validation.** The 3 existing commitment artifacts are seed/stub records based on repo-documented facts and UI form presets. They are schema exercises, not evidence. The evidence-posture convention and bolded provenance lead-ins exist to prevent this confusion.

- **Dormant is an acceptable outcome.** Six of eight deferred families were already recommended for dormancy or absorption at checkpoint 1. The post-enactment, post-evidence-scaffold state confirms these assessments and reclassifies one more (informational duty) from conditional survivor to open question inside civic-truth. This is not a failure — it is the expected result of canon promotions and evidence-stack discipline clarifying what the grammar actually needs.

- **Distinctiveness matters more than thematic importance.** "This concept is important" is never sufficient to justify a family. Extractive lock-in is important. Money-as-future-claim is important. Institutional transition is important. But importance without distinctiveness from existing families produces redundant coverage, not deeper grammar. Boundary-making survives because it proposes vocabulary that nothing else in the grammar provides — not because boundaries are important.
