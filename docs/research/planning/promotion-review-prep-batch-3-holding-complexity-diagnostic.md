## 2. Review Tagging Profile

### `mechanism confirmed`

- **Control failure under manifest complexity.** A regulator cannot govern a system whose variety exceeds the regulator's own representational capacity. This is a provable mathematical constraint (Ashby's Law of Requisite Variety), not an observation or political claim. Beer operationalises it for institutions: institutions default to variety reduction when overwhelmed because they structurally cannot do otherwise. Two independent traditions (information theory, institutional cybernetics) converge on the same structural proof.

- **Authoritarian compression as one default response.** When institutions face complexity that exceeds their regulatory capacity, one observable default is to narrow degrees of freedom — enforce legibility, simplify coordination, constrain variation, and destroy local adaptive knowledge. This produces short-term stability while reducing adaptive capacity. Scott names it precisely as "authoritarian high modernism." Beer calls it "the default strategy of variety reduction." Tainter documents Diocletian's intensification. Three independent traditions (political theory/STS, cybernetics, historical sociology) with detailed historical cases (Soviet collectivisation, Tanzanian ujamaa, scientific forestry, Roman taxation).

- **Chaotic decomposition as the other default response.** When the costs of institutional complexity exceed its benefits, the other observable default is loss of higher-order coordinating structures — reorganisation at a lower level of complexity. Tainter defines collapse as "suddenly smaller, simpler, less stratified" — explicitly not random chaos but insufficient coordination relative to existing interdependence. Holling's release phase and poverty trap provide ecological evidence. Two independent traditions (historical sociology, systems ecology) with multiple civilisational cases (Roman Empire, Maya, Chaco Canyon).

- **Adaptive coordination as the independently confirmed general response requirement.** Navigating evolving conditions through iterative sensing and response rather than enforcing predefined outcomes. Snowden's probe-sense-respond in the complex domain directly matches: "Leaders who try to impose order in a complex context will fail." Folke's adaptive governance adds the institutional dimension: adaptive capacity requires deliberate institutional construction, not spontaneous emergence. Two independent traditions (complexity practice, institutional ecology). **This claim belongs in the confirmed layer only as the general finding** — not as one of five named capacities, not as a component of the five-capacity architecture.

### `design contribution`

- **Deliberate institutional construction of a third path.** The institutional-level claim that adaptive governance requires deliberate design — institutional entrepreneurs, shadow networks, bridging organisations, multi-year preparation — is corroborated by Folke and supported by March (exploration capacity must be institutionally protected against exploitation drift). The narrowly confirmable version: "a response beyond both default attractors is structurally possible and requires deliberate institutional construction." The naming of this as a "third path" with a specific structural architecture is where the claim crosses from confirmed mechanism into design contribution. The diagnostic can state that deliberate construction is required; it cannot specify what the third path contains beyond the general direction of adaptive coordination.

- **Energetic/material constraint as secondary context (if included).** The thermodynamic foundation is directly corroborated: coordination has irreducible energetic costs (Tainter), the economic process is entropy transformation (Georgescu-Roegen), energy hierarchy constrains institutional forms (Odum). Three independent traditions. The application — that a coordination grammar must operate under tighter resource constraints than distributed control — is Johar's design inference. **If energetic constraint enters the diagnostic at all**, it can only appear as: "coordination has thermodynamic costs and surplus is tightening, which constrains the institutional forms available for adaptive response." It cannot appear as: "the grammar must be designed for tighter constraints." The foundation is confirmed; the design consequence stays out.

### `design hypothesis`

- **Meta-goal stewardship / grown goals.** The grown-goals half is confirmed by March: goals are discovered through action, not rationally pre-specified; rational planning suppresses goal discovery. The meta-goal/object-goal distinction as a named structural category — conditions for goal-formation as structurally separate from object-level goals, requiring distinct institutional stewardship — has no independent match. March operates in the same space but does not name meta-goals as a structural category. The grown-goals mechanism is externally real; the distinction is Johar's. **This stays outside the diagnostic core.**

- **Basin-competition framing.** Tainter sees compression-then-decomposition as a single trajectory. Scott sees only compression. Holling uses ecological categories. The specific framing of two competing attractor basins is Johar's interpretive integration. The diagnostic can state that two default responses are empirically observable. It cannot state that they are competing basins in a structural fork. **This stays outside the diagnostic core.**

### `Johar-distinctive integration`

The following elements are documented as Johar's design architecture, built atop the confirmed diagnostic. They are not defects — they are the expected profile for original design work. But none enters the reviewable core:

- **Five-capacity architecture** (adaptive coordination, complexity-handling capacity, scaffolded relationality, interdependent sovereignty, anti-fragility through learning) — no external source independently names this package. Individual capacities have external parallels; the integration is entirely Johar's.
- **Civilisational optionality as evaluative criterion** — Taleb provides optionality as a heuristic; Sen provides capability as an evaluative framework; neither operates at civilisational-institutional scale. The synthesis is Johar's.
- **Reflexive-constraint clause** ("must be built under conditions that actively constrain its construction") — demonstrated by Folke's cases but not independently named as a structural feature.
- **Fork structure as integrated whole** — the specific three-way integration (control fails → two named attractor basins → third path requiring deliberate construction) is Johar's. The components are independently confirmed; the integration is not.

### `boundary-sensitive`

- **Adaptive coordination at the general-response vs governance-practice boundary.** Snowden's probe-sense-respond serves both holding-complexity (general response requirement) and governance-under-volatility (specific governance practice). The diagnostic uses Snowden for the general finding only. Governance-specific claims (decision ecologies, coherence as governance success measure, probabilistic governance) stay with governance-under-volatility. If the diagnostic absorbs the governance-practice level, it collapses the family boundary.

- **Compression attractor at the variety-reduction vs authority-consequence boundary.** Authoritarian compression in the holding-complexity diagnostic is structurally adjacent to the control-surveillance-energy spiral in the legitimacy family. Both use Scott, but for different structural claims. Holding-complexity uses Scott for variety compression under complexity overload. Legitimacy uses Scott for authority-consequence separation. The same phenomenon, different structural loads. Neither family's evidence should strengthen the other's specific claim.

## 4. Claim Decomposition

### A. Mechanism Confirmed

- Control-based coordination fails under manifest complexity as a provable structural condition: a regulator cannot govern a system whose variety exceeds the regulator's own representational capacity (Ashby, formal proof; Beer, institutional application).
- When institutions face this mismatch, authoritarian compression — narrowing degrees of freedom, enforcing legibility, constraining variation, destroying local adaptive knowledge — is an empirically documented default response (Scott, authoritarian high modernism; Beer, default strategy of variety reduction; Tainter, Diocletian's intensification).
- Chaotic decomposition — loss of higher-order coordinating structures, reorganisation at lower complexity — is the other empirically documented default response (Tainter, collapse cases across multiple civilisations; Holling, release phase and poverty trap).
- Adaptive coordination — navigating evolving conditions through iterative sensing and response — is the independently confirmed general response requirement, and it requires deliberate institutional construction rather than spontaneous emergence (Snowden, probe-sense-respond; Folke, adaptive governance as institutional capacity requiring institutional entrepreneurs, shadow networks, and multi-year preparation).

### B. Design Contribution

- The claim that a response beyond both default attractors requires deliberate institutional construction at the infrastructure level is corroborated (Folke, Kristianstad case). The naming of this as a structured "third path" is Johar's framing — the diagnostic can state the requirement without importing the path's specific architecture.
- The energetic/material constraint as secondary context: coordination has thermodynamic costs, energy surplus is tightening, and this constrains what institutional forms are available for adaptive response (Tainter, Georgescu-Roegen, Odum — foundation confirmed). The claim that this specifically requires a new coordination grammar is Johar's design inference and stays outside the diagnostic proper.

### C. Design Hypothesis

- Meta-goal stewardship as a structural category: the conditions for goal-formation are structurally distinct from object-level goals and require separate institutional stewardship. The grown-goals half is confirmed (March); the structural distinction is Johar's and remains untested.
- The two defaults as competing attractor basins: the diagnostic confirms both responses are empirically observable, but the specific basin-competition framing is Johar's interpretive integration. Tainter sees them as sequential, not competing.

### D. Johar-Distinctive Integration

The five-capacity architecture, civilisational optionality as evaluative criterion, the reflexive-constraint clause, and the fork structure as an integrated whole remain Johar's design contributions. These are built atop confirmed diagnostic findings and are internally coherent — the components are externally real, the synthesis is original. They represent the engineering response to a confirmed structural condition. No further literature search is expected to close the gap between confirmed components and the integrated architecture. The architecture should be evaluated on its own terms — internal coherence and eventual production viability — not by attempting to assemble external validation for a package no external source independently names.

### E. Boundary Checks

**Distinct from `legitimacy-and-shared-consequence` (promoted).** Legitimacy names what makes institutions viable: authority-consequence coupling, polycentric legitimacy, self-reinforcing escalation when coupling breaks. Holding-complexity's diagnostic names why control-based coordination fails structurally: variety overload, not authority-consequence separation. Both use Scott, but for different mechanisms (variety compression vs authority-consequence separation). Legitimacy's experimental evidence (DeCaro), configural evidence (Baggio), and polycentric framework (Ostrom, Scharpf, Hirschman) must not enter the diagnostic. The phenomena overlap; the structural loads are different.

**Distinct from `relational-topology-and-context-design` (promoted).** Minimal overlap at the diagnostic level. The five-capacity architecture includes "scaffolded relationality" — which is topology territory — but scaffolded relationality is in the non-reviewable remainder, not the diagnostic core. The diagnostic (control fails, two default responses, adaptive coordination required) does not address relational topology. Project Aristotle, Burt, Polzer, and Yang/Microsoft evidence stays with topology. "Context matters more than composition" (topology's finding) is not evidence for "control fails under complexity" (holding-complexity's diagnostic).

**Distinct from `human-economy-and-commitment` (promoted).** Both diagnose coordination failure under uncertainty, but at different structural layers. Human-economy addresses economic grammar: wage/transaction coordination fails because specifiability assumptions break under discovery. Holding-complexity addresses civilisational coordination logic: control-based coordination fails because complexity exceeds representational capacity. Hart, Williamson, and Simon's contract-failure evidence stays with human-economy. Ashby, Tainter, and Scott's control-failure evidence stays with holding-complexity. They converge in implication but differ in mechanism and evidence base.

**Distinct from `governance-under-volatility` (deferred).** This is the highest overlap risk. Both address what happens when control-based governance breaks. The attribution rule: general-response-requirement evidence (Snowden's probe-sense-respond as a structural finding about the complex domain) goes to holding-complexity. Specific-governance-practice evidence (decision ecologies, probabilistic governance, coherence as governance success measure) stays with governance-under-volatility. The diagnostic must not absorb the governance face, or it leaves governance-under-volatility with nothing to promote on its own terms. Folke's adaptive governance evidence is attributed at the institutional-construction level (holding-complexity) not the governance-practice level (governance-under-volatility).

## 5. Candidate Review-Ready Framing

Control-based coordination fails under manifest complexity as a provable structural condition: a regulator cannot govern a system whose variety exceeds the regulator's own representational capacity (Ashby). When institutions face this mismatch, two empirically documented default responses emerge. Authoritarian compression narrows degrees of freedom — enforcing legibility, simplifying coordination, constraining variation — producing short-term stability while destroying the adaptive capacity embedded in local knowledge (Scott, Tainter, Beer). Chaotic decomposition loses higher-order coordinating structures, reorganising at a lower level of complexity — not disorder, but insufficient coordination relative to existing interdependence (Tainter, Holling). Adaptive coordination — navigating evolving conditions through iterative sensing and response rather than enforcing predefined outcomes — is the independently confirmed alternative, and it requires deliberate institutional construction rather than spontaneous emergence (Snowden, Folke). The specific design architecture for this adaptive response — including the structural capacities required and the institutional forms they take — is a separate design question that this diagnostic does not resolve.

## 6. Reviewer Questions

A human reviewer should answer the following before any promotion discussion proceeds:

1. **Is the diagnostic strong enough to stand alone without the five-capacity architecture?** The diagnostic asserts four findings: control fails, compression is one default, decomposition is the other, adaptive coordination requiring deliberate construction is the confirmed response direction. Does this provide sufficient canon value as a standalone "why Spore exists at the civilisational level" entry, without specifying what the adaptive response contains beyond the general direction?

2. **Does adaptive coordination belong in the confirmed layer, or is it already drifting into design response?** The diagnostic uses Snowden's probe-sense-respond as the general finding. But within the full holding-complexity family, adaptive coordination is one of five named capacities. The diagnostic-safe version states "adaptive coordination is the confirmed general response requirement." Does this wording hold, or does the reader inevitably hear "the first of five capacities" even when the other four are not named?

3. **Is "deliberate construction" narrow enough to survive canon use without drift?** The diagnostic claims that adaptive response requires deliberate institutional construction (Folke). In canon, will this claim stay at the confirmed level ("institutional entrepreneurs, shadow networks, and multi-year preparation are required") or will it expand to imply the full fork architecture ("a third path must be deliberately constructed")?

4. **Should energetic/material constraint stay out entirely at review stage?** The thermodynamic foundation is confirmed (Tainter, Georgescu-Roegen, Odum). But including it as secondary context risks making the diagnostic dependent on a claim that requires more qualification than the other three findings. Would the diagnostic be cleaner and more stable with four mechanism-confirmed claims only, deferring energetics to a future design-context layer?

5. **Is the narrowness of this diagnostic a strength or a weakness for canon?** The four promoted entries each promoted a mechanism plus at least some design-contribution framing. This diagnostic would promote only the structural condition and the general response direction. Is the narrow scope an honest reflection of what the evidence supports, or does it produce a canon entry too thin to do useful work?

## 7. Suggested Disposition

**`promote with explicit qualification`**

The diagnostic layer is very strongly corroborated from multiple independent traditions — a formal mathematical theorem (Ashby), three distinct historical/analytical traditions (Tainter, Scott, Beer), a formal decision framework (Snowden), and an institutional-ecology case study (Folke). The evidence base for the four confirmed mechanisms is at least as strong as the evidence base for any individual promoted entry. The Dutch Water Boards case provides 800 years of positive evidence for deliberate adaptive governance resisting both default attractors.

The explicit qualification is: this entry promotes the diagnostic condition and the general response direction only. It does not validate the design architecture built on top of the diagnostic. The five-capacity package, the fork/basin framing as integrated whole, civilisational optionality, and the reflexive-constraint clause remain tagged as Johar's design contributions — evaluated by internal coherence and production viability, not by external textual match.

**Why not "hold pending narrower wording":** The wording in Section 5 is already very narrow — narrower than any promoted entry. The risk is not that the wording is too broad but that it will drift in canon use. That risk is managed by the qualification, not by further narrowing.

**Why not "hold pending more evidence":** No further literature search will strengthen the diagnostic. The evidence is as strong as it will get from external sources. The remaining evidentiary frontier is production evidence for the design architecture, which is outside this diagnostic's scope.

## 10. Guardrails

**A strong diagnostic does not validate the architecture built on top of it.** Ashby proves control fails. Tainter documents the attractors. Snowden confirms adaptive coordination. None of this validates the five-capacity package, the fork/basin framing as integrated architecture, civilisational optionality as evaluative criterion, or the reflexive-constraint clause. Promoting the diagnostic creates a foundation; it does not retroactively confirm the design architecture.

**The five-capacity package must remain out unless directly supported.** Individual capacities have external parallels. The unified five-element architecture does not. No further literature search is expected to close this gap. The architecture is Johar's design contribution and should be evaluated on its own terms — internal coherence and production viability — not by attempting to assemble external validation for a package no external source names.

**Adaptive coordination must not silently expand into full institutional design.** The diagnostic-safe version is: "adaptive coordination is the confirmed general response requirement." In canon use, this must not become: "adaptive coordination is the first of five capacities required to hold complexity." The distinction is between a confirmed finding (Snowden independently arrives at probe-sense-respond) and a design architecture (adaptive coordination as one element of a five-element package). If the former drifts into the latter, the diagnostic has failed.

**Energetic/material constraint should stay out unless it survives narrow wording.** The thermodynamic foundation is confirmed. But the diagnostic is strongest with four clean mechanism-confirmed claims. Adding energetic constraint as a fifth claim risks either making it too qualified to be useful or too unqualified to be honest. Reviewer Question 4 addresses this directly.

**Future review must preserve the split or the family should stay out.** The split is what makes the diagnostic reviewable. If in a future review the split is abandoned — if the diagnostic is widened to include the fork structure, the five-capacity architecture, or civilisational optionality — the family should be pulled from review rather than promoted on weaker terms. A narrow honest entry is more valuable than a broad dishonest one.

**Adjacent promoted families must not lend false certainty.** Legitimacy's strong evidence about authority-consequence coupling does not strengthen the compression-attractor claim. Relational-topology's empirical base does not validate "scaffolded relationality." Human-economy's commitment evidence does not confirm that a new coordination grammar is needed at the civilisational level. Each family stands on its own evidence.
