## 1. Scope and Rule

**Families in scope:**
1. legitimacy-and-shared-consequence
2. linguistic-closure
3. relational-topology-and-context-design

**This is a prep memo for future human review, not a promotion action.** No `promotion_status` field will change. No canon claims will enter the graph. No synthesis notes will be rewritten. The output is a set of draft review framings, a tagging format, and a clear accounting of what is safe to assert vs what must remain qualified — so that a future human review can proceed efficiently.

**Corroborated mechanism does not automatically validate the full design translation.** All 3 families passed their corroboration runs with success conditions met. In each case, 4 independent non-Johar traditions confirmed the structural mechanism. But corroboration tested whether the mechanism is *independently discoverable* — not whether the full family claim (mechanism + Johar vocabulary + design response + institutional application) is ready for canon. The gap between "the mechanism is real" and "promote this claim" must be bridged by explicit tagging, not by conflation.

**Johar-specific integration remains a live evaluation category.** Across all 3 families, external sources confirmed the structural mechanism while Johar's distinctive contribution was integration, naming, and proactive design response. This integration is not a defect. It is the expected profile for original design work. But it must be explicitly tagged in any promotion entry so that the canon distinguishes what the world confirms from what the grammar contributes.

## 2. Batch Rationale

### Why these 3 were selected together

These 3 families share a common profile that distinguishes them from the remaining 2 corroborated families:

1. **Mechanism fully confirmed** from 4 independent traditions with zero overlap per family.
2. **Johar-specific remainder is clearly separable** from the confirmed mechanism — it is vocabulary, naming, integration, or design hypothesis, not structural claims that override external evidence.
3. **Boundaries are clean** and stable through corroboration.
4. **The gap between "corroborated" and "reviewable" is a formatting question**, not an evidence question.

### Why holding-complexity-beyond-control is excluded

Holding-complexity's diagnostic layer is one of the strongest across all 5 runs (Ashby, Tainter, Scott, Beer). But the Johar-specific remainder is the widest of any family: fork structure, two-attractor basin framing, five-capacity package, meta-goal distinction, civilisational optionality, reflexive-constraint clause. The pass used a modified success condition. Including it here would either understate the family (promote only the diagnostic) or set a precedent for promoting large unvalidated design architectures. Better to let this batch establish the tagging format with cleaner families first.

### Why human-economy-and-commitment is excluded

Human-economy's replacement mechanism (commitment signatures with 4 types) has no direct external analog — the weakest link across all 5 runs. A targeted second pass on commitment-type differentiation in production cases would address this gap. Including it here would require promoting the commitment-signature taxonomy as unvalidated design proposal, setting a lower standard than the other 3 families justify.

### Why a narrow batch is better than a broad one

A narrow first batch of 3 families with clean profiles establishes the tagging precedent: how the canon distinguishes mechanism from contribution from hypothesis. Once the format is proven on clean cases, the harder families (holding-complexity, human-economy) can be addressed with the format already in place rather than inventing it under pressure from more ambiguous cases.

## 3. Review Tagging Format

The following tags should be used in any future promotion-review entry. Each claim or sub-claim within a family should carry exactly one primary tag.

### `mechanism confirmed`

**What it means:** The structural insight has been independently confirmed by 2+ non-Johar traditions, with the match being structural (same mechanism, different vocabulary). External sources arrive at the same account through genuinely independent intellectual routes.

**What kind of evidence justifies it:** Conceptual parallels from independent traditions (not shared upstream), empirical cases demonstrating the mechanism in real institutions, experimental evidence, or configural analysis across multiple cases.

**What it does not justify:** That Johar's specific vocabulary for the mechanism is externally confirmed. That the design response to the mechanism works. That the mechanism is the only mechanism operating in the domain. That the mechanism is sufficient by itself for institutional design.

### `design contribution`

**What it means:** Johar's naming, vocabulary, integration, or design response is a plausible engineering response to the confirmed mechanism. The component mechanisms are externally real. The integration is internally coherent, non-trivially derived, and adds value beyond the components. But no external source independently arrived at the same integration.

**What kind of evidence justifies it:** Confirmed components (each piece independently corroborated) + internal coherence (the integration does not contradict the components) + non-trivial derivation (the integration was not independently discovered, meaning it adds original value).

**What it does not justify:** That the integration is externally confirmed. That the design vocabulary is the only valid way to name the mechanism. That the design response has been production-tested.

### `design hypothesis`

**What it means:** A specific claim, parameter, or design mechanism proposed by the family that is plausible given the confirmed mechanism but has not been externally corroborated or production-tested. It remains an open question rather than a confirmed finding.

**What kind of evidence justifies it:** Plausibility from the confirmed mechanism + internal coherence + no contradicting evidence. The hypothesis should be clearly testable, even if not yet tested.

**What it does not justify:** Asserting the hypothesis as if it were confirmed. Using the hypothesis as evidence for other claims. Treating the hypothesis as a design constraint before testing.

### `Johar-distinctive integration`

**What it means:** A framing, synthesis, or architecture that combines independently confirmed components into a unified design vocabulary or design discipline. The components are real; the synthesis is Johar's. This is the expected profile for original design work and is not a defect — but it must be marked so the canon does not present it as externally confirmed structure.

**What kind of evidence justifies it:** Components independently confirmed + synthesis not independently discovered + synthesis adds non-trivial design value.

**What it does not justify:** Treating the synthesis as if it were externally confirmed. Collapsing the synthesis into the confirmed mechanism.

### `boundary-sensitive`

**What it means:** A claim or sub-claim that sits near the boundary with an adjacent family and could be attributed to either. Evidence for this claim must be carefully attributed, and the promotion entry must specify which family owns it and which family it must stay distinct from.

**What kind of evidence justifies it:** The claim addresses a mechanism that is structurally close to an adjacent family's territory. Corroboration evidence could plausibly serve either family.

**What it does not justify:** Double-counting evidence across families. Collapsing the boundary. Using adjacent-family evidence as a substitute for family-specific evidence.

## 5. Per-Family Claim Decomposition

### 5.1 Legitimacy-and-Shared-Consequence

**Mechanism confirmed:**
- Institutions that structurally couple authority to consequence-bearing are substantially more viable than those where authority and consequence separate. (Ostrom: design principles 2/3/4/5; Baggio: 8.7/10 vs 4.3/10 principles in successful vs failed commons; DeCaro: experimental confirmation.)
- No single governance axis suffices; multiple overlapping legitimacy sources are structurally necessary. (Ostrom: polycentric governance; Beetham: three irreducible dimensions; Scharpf: input/output both required.)
- When authority separates from consequence, self-reinforcing escalation produces institutional failure. (Hirschman: exit-voice death spiral; Scott: simplification-failure-further-simplification pattern.)

**Design contribution:**
- The 4-dimensional taxonomy (democracy, stewardship, experimentation, radical markets) as a specific architecture for polycentric legitimacy. Ostrom's polycentricity and Beetham's 3 dimensions are multi-dimensional but do not use this taxonomy. The taxonomy is a plausible design response built on confirmed multi-dimensionality.
- "Orchestration over dominance" as a named coordination mode. Implied by Ostrom's mutual adjustment but not independently named. Captures a real design orientation that the confirmed mechanism supports.
- The control-surveillance-energy spiral as a named escalation pattern. Scott and Hirschman describe the mechanism; the specific naming and framing as a spiral is Johar's contribution.

**Design hypothesis still unproven:**
- Civic economy as a third pole — autonomous from market and state, capable of interrupting the control spiral. Ostrom's commons are adjacent but the specific "civic economy" framing and its claimed role as spiral-interruptor are untested.
- The precise boundary between consequence-bearing and coercive over-binding within commitment grammar — acknowledged as an unresolved tension in the synthesis note itself.

**Johar-distinctive integration:**
The synthesis note integrates authority-consequence coupling (from institutional economics/political theory), polycentric governance (from commons research), and the control spiral (from political economy/STS) into a single design principle for coordination infrastructure. No external source independently synthesises these three into a unified institutional design discipline. The integration is plausible because each component is confirmed independently and they address the same structural problem from complementary angles. The integration adds value: it connects governance legitimacy to infrastructure design, which the external sources treat separately.

**Boundary checks:**
- **vs relational-topology:** Authority-consequence binding (legitimacy) is distinct from patterning-of-connection (topology). The spatial-politics source in the synthesis note touches both: "differential relational density produces political asymmetry." This boundary touch is noted but does not blur the distinction: legitimacy asks *who has standing and who bears outcomes*; topology asks *how the pattern of connection generates what relations are possible*.
- **vs governance-under-volatility:** Legitimacy is a structural requirement for institutional viability; governance-under-volatility names the governance condition under which that requirement becomes critical. Evidence about coherence under turbulence stays in governance-under-volatility.
- **vs civic-truth:** Legitimacy is the political-institutional condition; civic-truth is the epistemic commons condition. Evidence about holding plural truth is civic-truth, not legitimacy.

### 5.2 Linguistic-Closure

**Mechanism confirmed:**
- Categories, paradigms, and stabilised facts produce a sense of sufficiency that terminates inquiry — not because truth has been found but because the category feels adequate. (Peirce: 3 of 4 fixation methods are self-sealing; Kuhn: paradigms determine what can be perceived as a problem; Latour: black boxing makes construction invisible; Weick: enacted sensemaking treats categories as given.)
- The closure mechanism is a structural property of language/institutions, not a cognitive error. (All 4 traditions: the mechanism operates at the level of institutional categories, not individual cognition. This is what distinguishes it from confirmation bias.)
- Resisting closure requires institutional mechanisms, not individual cognitive discipline. (Peirce: only the scientific method has built-in self-correction; Weick: HRO principles as institutional anti-closure; Nosek et al.: pre-registration as structural reform; Latour: "matters of concern" requiring institutional arenas.)

**Design contribution:**
- *Naming* as the upstream closure mechanism — the claim that the act of giving something a word is the primary trigger. External sources confirm that categories terminate inquiry; the specific claim that naming is the upstream mechanism is Johar's precision-addition, well-supported but not independently stated at that level.
- The claim lifecycle, discourse graph, and attestation decay as specific anti-closure mechanisms. These are plausible design responses to the confirmed mechanism. The replication crisis reform (pre-registration, registered reports) is a domain-specific precedent; Spore's proposed mechanisms generalise the principle.
- The three-concept sequence: category inadequacy → linguistic closure → recursive intelligence. This is a Johar design framework that organises the confirmed mechanism (closure) between its condition (inadequacy) and its corrective (recursion). Plausible but not independently derived.

**Design hypothesis still unproven:**
- Proactive anti-closure infrastructure that works at general institutional scale (not just science or HROs). The corrective principle is confirmed; the specific claim that designed infrastructure (claim lifecycle, discourse graph) will successfully resist closure across all institutional domains has no production evidence.
- LLM amplification of closure risk (cheap coherence, high oracle density). Plausible forward-looking risk analysis but no empirical evidence specific to this claim; all corroborating sources predate the era.
- Whether structural mechanisms ensure *perceptibility* of closure, not just *revisability* of claims. The synthesis note itself identifies this as an open limitation.

**Johar-distinctive integration:**
The synthesis note integrates the stopping-rule mechanism (from pragmatism), paradigmatic foreclosure (from history of science), black boxing (from STS), and enacted sensemaking (from organisational psychology) into a single design vocabulary: linguistic closure as a named failure mode that the grammar's structural mechanisms (claim lifecycle, discourse graph, attestation decay, learning membrane) are designed to resist. No external source independently produces this integration. The integration adds value: it connects epistemological diagnosis to institutional design, which the external sources do not do at the same level of specificity. The corrective is the most distinctively Johar element — all external sources either propose reactive correctives (Kuhn: crisis), domain-specific reforms (Nosek: science), or operational principles (Weick: HROs), while Johar proposes general-purpose designed infrastructure.

**Boundary checks:**
- **vs perceptual-fidelity:** Closure is about *categories foreclosing what can be noticed* — a language/framing mechanism. Fidelity is about *the depth of sensing contact* with reality before and through abstraction. Kuhn's incommensurability touches both (paradigms shape what scientists perceive), but the mechanism is about categorical framing (closure), not sensing quality (fidelity).
- **vs civic-truth:** Closure is the pathology; civic-truth is the commons condition designed to resist it. Evidence about institutional design for holding plural truth is civic-truth. Evidence about how categories become self-sealing is closure. Latour's "matters of concern" sits near the boundary — it is an anti-closure mechanism but also a civic-truth design principle.
- **vs human-machine-complementarity:** Closure may be amplified by machine-generated text, but the mechanism itself is a property of language, not of machines. The LLM amplification claim should not bleed into the human-machine-complementarity family.

### 5.3 Relational-Topology-and-Context-Design

**Mechanism confirmed:**
- Organisational contexts, material arrangements, and network topologies actively *generate* relation, perception, identity, and power — they are not neutral containers. (Suchman: situation generates action; Orlikowski: social and material constitutively entangled; Dale & Burrell: "the organisation of space is simultaneously the organisation of power.")
- Network position generates outcomes controlling for individual attributes — topology delivers the advantage; the person is the vehicle. (Burt 2004: blind evaluation; the same idea in different positions produces different results.)
- Context variables predict coordination quality more strongly than composition variables. (Edmondson/Aristotle: 180+ teams; psychological safety is a group-level emergent property, not a selection outcome.)
- Structural decay of relational grounding is the default condition without active maintenance. (Roberts & Dunbar: longitudinal decay; Allen Curve: communication drops exponentially with distance; Yang/Microsoft: remote shift → network siloing.)

**Design contribution:**
- "Imaginary identity" as a named concept for the emergent sense of "we" produced by relational density patterns. The phenomenon is demonstrated (Polzer et al.: spatial arrangement generates competing subgroup identities; Hatch & Schultz: organisational identity emerges from relational processes). The naming and the claim that it is "always partial, positional, and structurally mediated" are Johar's.
- Context design as a named institutional design discipline — paradigmatically distinct from process design or formal structure design. The evidence supports the distinction (changing context changes outcomes, independent of process changes), but the naming of "context design" as a standalone discipline is Johar's integration.
- The claim that relational topology is "the real infrastructure of institutions." Each source demonstrates one facet (situation generates action, topology generates power, materiality constitutes practice, context determines quality). The integrative thesis that these are all facets of one generative mechanism — relational topology — is Johar's.

**Design hypothesis still unproven:**
- The 6-9 month alignment half-life as a specific quantitative parameter. Structural decay is well-supported (Roberts & Dunbar, distributed-team literature). But no source provides a half-life estimate. This is a testable hypothesis, not a confirmed finding.
- "Context metabolism" — the claim that contexts have incentive fields, recognition loops, biases, and learning architectures that collectively determine what inhabitants can perceive and how they evolve. No external equivalent exists. Plausible given the confirmed generative mechanism, but untested as a design concept.
- Scaling from team-level evidence (Aristotle, Polzer, distributed-team research) to institutional-level claims ("the real infrastructure of institutions"). Plausible but not directly demonstrated.

**Johar-distinctive integration:**
The synthesis note integrates situated action (from ethnomethodology), structural holes (from structural sociology), sociomateriality (from STS), psychological safety (from organisational behaviour), and structural decay (from cognitive/evolutionary psychology) into a single design discipline: context design as the design of relational topology generators. No external source independently produces this integration. The external sources each demonstrate one dimension; the claim that these dimensions constitute a single generative infrastructure — and that designing it is the central task of institutional design — is Johar's. This is the largest integration claim in this batch: it reframes the entire field of institutional design around topology rather than around formal structure, process, or culture.

**Boundary checks:**
- **vs legitimacy:** Topology asks *how the pattern of connection generates what relations are possible*; legitimacy asks *who has authority and who bears consequences*. An organisation can have good topology with poor legitimacy (well-connected but authority and consequence split) or poor topology with good legitimacy (legitimate but relationally fractured). Evidence about polycentric governance stays in legitimacy; evidence about how arrangements generate patterns of connection stays in topology.
- **vs agility-contribution-and-organizing:** Alignment-decay sits at their boundary. The *mechanism* of decay (context change → relational erosion) is topological. The *consequence* (loss of coordination capacity) is agility's territory. Evidence about decay rates and causes stays in topology; evidence about how to maintain adaptive coordination capacity stays in agility.
- **vs perceptual-fidelity:** Orlikowski's sociomateriality touches perceptual fidelity (material arrangements shape what agents can perceive). But the topology family's evidence focuses on *relational patterns* (who connects to whom), not on *quality of sensing contact* with pre-abstraction reality.

## 7. Family-Specific Blocking Questions

### 7.1 Legitimacy-and-Shared-Consequence

**Primary blocking question:** Is the Johar remainder merely vocabulary for a confirmed mechanism, or does the 4-dimensional taxonomy carry substantive architectural claims that go beyond naming?

If the taxonomy is vocabulary — a way of organising confirmed polycentricity — then promotion can proceed with the taxonomy tagged as `design contribution`. If the taxonomy carries the implicit claim that *exactly these 4 dimensions* are structurally necessary (not 3, not 5, not a different set), then the taxonomy is a structural claim that has not been externally tested. The answer determines whether the promotion entry says "polycentric legitimacy, which Johar organises into four dimensions" (vocabulary) or "legitimacy requires four specific dimensions" (structural claim). The former is safe; the latter is not currently defensible.

### 7.2 Linguistic-Closure

**Primary blocking question:** Is the institutional corrective sufficiently evidenced to enter canon as a design contribution, or should it wait for production evidence?

The mechanism (categories terminate inquiry) is very strongly confirmed. The principle (the corrective must be structural) is confirmed by Peirce, Weick/HRO, and the replication crisis reform. But the specific mechanisms Spore proposes (claim lifecycle, discourse graph, attestation decay) are Johar's design extrapolation from the confirmed principle. A reviewer must decide: is "the principle is confirmed and our specific mechanisms are a plausible design response" sufficient for promotion, or does the canon entry need to wait until the mechanisms have production evidence? The pre-review recommendation: promote the mechanism and principle now; tag the specific mechanisms as `design contribution`; flag production testing as the next evidence need.

### 7.3 Relational-Topology-and-Context-Design

**Primary blocking question:** Does the integrative thesis ("topology is the real infrastructure") need to be promoted alongside the confirmed components, or should the components be promoted individually and the integration deferred?

The components are independently strong: context generates (Suchman), topology generates power (Burt), materiality constitutes practice (Orlikowski), context > composition (Edmondson), decay is structural (Roberts & Dunbar). These could each enter canon as confirmed mechanisms without the integrative thesis. The integrative thesis adds value — it connects separate findings into a design discipline — but it is the largest Johar-distinctive claim in this batch. A reviewer must decide: promote the components with the integration tagged as `Johar-distinctive integration`, or promote only the components and defer the integration until production evidence demonstrates that designing topology as unified infrastructure produces better outcomes than addressing each component separately. The pre-review recommendation: promote with the integration tagged as `Johar-distinctive integration`, because the components are meaningless to grammar design without the integrative frame, and the frame is well-supported even if not independently derived.

## 8. Batch-Level Risks

### Risk 1: Over-crediting design translation from corroborated mechanism

The strongest risk across the batch. All 3 families have confirmed mechanisms. The temptation is to let confirmation of the mechanism validate the full design translation — to move from "categories terminate inquiry" (confirmed) to "the claim lifecycle prevents closure" (untested) without marking the gap. The tagging format exists to prevent this, but reviewers must enforce it. Every design claim must carry an explicit `design contribution` or `design hypothesis` tag.

### Risk 2: Pulling linguistic-closure into civic-truth or perceptual-fidelity

Linguistic-closure, civic-truth, and perceptual-fidelity form a triad within Superfamily A (Epistemic Commons). Promotion of linguistic-closure may create pressure to promote civic-truth and perceptual-fidelity simultaneously, or to absorb evidence from those families. The boundary is sharp (closure = pathology of categories; civic-truth = commons condition; fidelity = sensing quality), but the temptation to consolidate under "epistemic design" should be resisted. Civic-truth and perceptual-fidelity have not been corroborated and should not enter review on linguistic-closure's momentum.

### Risk 3: Pulling relational-topology into legitimacy

Legitimacy and topology share Superfamily F (Institutional Topology and Legitimacy). The spatial-politics source in the legitimacy synthesis note explicitly links relational topology to legitimacy ("differential relational density produces political asymmetry"). A reviewer may be tempted to merge them under "institutional design." The boundary is structural and must hold: legitimacy is about *who has authority and who bears consequences* (political question); topology is about *how arrangements generate patterns of connection* (infrastructure question). An organisation can have good topology with poor legitimacy or poor topology with good legitimacy.

### Risk 4: Treating legitimacy as fully settled because it is the strongest

Legitimacy is the cleanest candidate, but "cleanest" is relative, not absolute. The 4-dimensional taxonomy is still Johar-specific. The civic-economy claim is still a design hypothesis. The boundary between consequence-bearing and over-binding is still unresolved. A reviewer should not use legitimacy's relative strength as a shortcut to skip the tagging requirements.

### Risk 5: Letting the batch inherit momentum from the strongest family unevenly

Linguistic-closure and relational-topology are near-peers with different strengths — linguistic-closure has the cleanest mechanism match, relational-topology has the strongest empirical base. Neither should be dragged to legitimacy's level of readiness by proximity. Each family's `design hypothesis` items must be evaluated on their own evidence, not on the batch's general strength.

## 11. Guardrails

1. **Mechanism confirmation is not the same as canon-ready design translation.** The corroboration runs confirmed that the structural mechanisms are externally real. They did not confirm that the design responses work, that the vocabulary is the right naming, or that the integration captures the mechanism optimally. Promotion entries must carry tags that make this distinction visible.

2. **Johar-distinctive integration is not a flaw, but it must stay explicitly marked.** The 4-dimensional taxonomy, "imaginary identity," the claim lifecycle as anti-closure infrastructure, and "topology as the real infrastructure" are all plausible, coherent, and non-trivially derived. They are also all Johar's. The canon should carry them proudly — as design contributions, not as externally confirmed structure. The moment they lose their tags, the canon overclaims.

3. **Weaker adjacent families must not borrow strength from this batch.** Civic-truth has not been corroborated. Perceptual-fidelity has not been corroborated. Governance-under-volatility has not been corroborated. Agility-contribution has not been corroborated. These families should not enter review on the strength of the 3 families in this batch. If they are promoted later, they must be promoted on their own evidence.

4. **Future review should preserve boundaries rather than simplify them away.** The corroboration runs demonstrated that well-bounded families corroborate cleanly. Promotion should maintain these boundaries. A canon entry for legitimacy should not absorb topology evidence. A canon entry for linguistic-closure should not absorb civic-truth evidence. A canon entry for topology should not absorb agility evidence. Each family enters canon with its own evidence, its own boundaries, and its own honest accounting.

5. **No family should be promoted on elegance alone.** The synthesis notes are well-written and internally coherent. The corroboration evidence is strong. But the quality of the argumentation is not itself evidence for the claims. A beautiful framing of an unconfirmed mechanism is still unconfirmed. The tagging format exists to ensure that the canon distinguishes between what the evidence confirms and what the framing proposes.
