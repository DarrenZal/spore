## 3. Draft Language Principles

The following rules govern all three entries:

1. **Mechanism-confirmed text must be kept distinct from design contribution.** Confirmed claims are stated directly. Design contributions are introduced with explicit framing ("Johar contributes...", "the grammar proposes...", "as design vocabulary..."). The reader must be able to tell which is which without consulting the planning trail.

2. **Johar-distinctive integration can be named but must stay explicitly marked.** Phrases like "Johar integrates these into..." or "the grammar names this as..." signal that the integration is a design contribution, not an externally confirmed finding.

3. **Hypotheses must remain hypotheses.** Any claim tagged `design hypothesis` in the review packet must either stay out of the enactment text or be explicitly flagged as untested.

4. **Project-vision additions must stay compact.** Each project-vision paragraph should be 4-6 sentences. The document is a vision statement, not a research summary. Dense evidence citations belong in the synthesis notes, not in project-vision.

5. **Lexicon entries should define terms cleanly.** The lexicon format (per intent-pressure and stigmergy) defines a concept, explains how it works, relates it to the grammar, and states what it is not. The entry should not attempt to carry the full synthesis-note argument.

6. **Inline evidence citations are appropriate in the lexicon entry but not in project-vision.** Project-vision already operates at the framing level without inline citations. The lexicon entry may include a brief evidence note (as intent-pressure includes Bennett) but should not become a corroboration report.

# Linguistic Closure

The condition in which a category retains operational clarity while losing contact with the relational reality it describes. Language compresses reality into operable categories, and that compression produces a structural sense of sufficiency — a feeling that the category is adequate — that terminates inquiry before truth evaluation begins. This is not a cognitive error attributable to individual bias. It is a structural property of how categories operate in institutional contexts: paradigms determine what can be perceived as a problem, stabilised facts become invisible through their own success, and organisations treat enacted categories as given reality.

## How It Works

Closure operates through a stopping-rule mechanism. Naming compresses, compression feels sufficient, and sufficiency signals that inquiry can stop. The danger is not merely false belief but foreclosure of what can be noticed — categories determine what is perceivable before the question of truth arises. Four independent traditions converge on this structural account:

- **Logic of inquiry** (Peirce) — three of four methods of fixing belief are self-sealing; only the scientific method has built-in self-correction.
- **History of science** (Kuhn) — paradigms determine what can be perceived as a problem; "those that will not fit the box are often not seen at all."
- **Sociology of science** (Latour) — black boxing makes a fact's construction invisible through its own success; reopening requires institutional mobilisation.
- **Organisational psychology** (Weick) — enacted sensemaking treats imposed categories as given reality; preconceptions guide action that confirms preconceptions.

The replication crisis demonstrates both the failure mode (publication categories became self-sealing, producing a reproducibility crisis) and the structural reform (pre-registration, registered reports, and open data designed because individual virtue was shown to be insufficient).

## The Corrective Is Institutional

The corrective to linguistic closure is not better individual thinking. It is structural: institutional revisability, error-finding incentives, recursive inquiry, and designed openness to disconfirmation. The grammar's claim lifecycle, discourse graph, and attestation decay are structural mechanisms designed to resist this failure mode — they keep categories revisable and prevent settled terms from becoming permanent. Whether these specific mechanisms effectively resist closure at general institutional scale is an open design question, not a demonstrated fact.

## Relationship to the Grammar

Linguistic closure names the failure mode that Spore's structural mechanisms are designed to prevent. A grammar that compresses complex coordination into named primitives (holon, membrane, intent pressure) is itself subject to closure risk — any of its own terms can become stopping rules if the culture of use treats them as settled rather than threshold-opening. The claim lifecycle ensures that claims can be superseded. The discourse graph structures reasoning about the grammar's own evolution. Attestation decay prevents old endorsements from accumulating false authority. These are design responses to the confirmed mechanism, not proven infrastructure.

## What Linguistic Closure Is Not

- **Not confirmation bias.** Confirmation bias is a cognitive tendency in individuals. Linguistic closure is a structural property of categories operating in institutions. The mechanism operates at the institutional level — it determines what can be noticed before individual cognition engages.
- **Not post-structuralist undecidability.** The claim is not that meaning is philosophically impossible to fix. It is that categories, once fixed, produce practical sufficiency that stops inquiry — and that the corrective is institutional design, not philosophical dissolution.
- **Not an argument against naming.** Categories are necessary for coordination. The risk is not that naming happens but that naming produces a sense of adequacy that prevents revision. The corrective is structural revisability, not the absence of names.

## Source

The concept enters the Spore vocabulary via the Johar bridge notes (word-not-thing, recursive-intelligence). Johar adds the precision that naming is the upstream act that produces the closure-triggering category — a well-supported refinement stated at a level of specificity the independent traditions do not independently reach. The structural account (categories terminate inquiry; the corrective is institutional) is independently confirmed. The specific grammar mechanisms proposed as anti-closure infrastructure (claim lifecycle, discourse graph, attestation decay) are Johar's design contribution.

#### A. Safe Canon Core

- These are independently confirmed across ethnomethodology (Suchman), structural sociology (Burt), science and technology studies (Orlikowski), and organisational behaviour (Edmondson/Aristotle), with large-scale empirical support (Project Aristotle: 180+ teams; Yang/Microsoft: 61k employees).

#### B. Qualified Design Contribution

- "Imaginary identity" — the emergent sense of "we" produced by relational density patterns — is Johar's naming for a phenomenon demonstrated by Polzer (topology-generated subgroup identities) and Hatch & Schultz (identity from relational processes).
