## 8. Guardrails

- The foundation section is narrower than the original Johar family. The bridge note proposed five interconnected concepts anchored in Markov blanket formalism. The enacted section carries three: mediation structure as design target, the anti-container corrective, and clear operational boundaries with the Ostrom integration. This narrowing is deliberate and must be maintained.

- Enactment does not validate discarded formal apparatus. The Markov blanket formalism, conditional-independence gradients, and philosophical arguments against container metaphysics were excluded through a deliberate translation and narrowing process documented in the translation memo and scoping assessment. Their exclusion from the foundation section does not mean they are wrong — it means they are not load-bearing for the design principle as enacted.

- Research provenance and canon/foundation language remain distinct. The bridge note, corroboration memo, translation memo, and scoping assessment are research artifacts. The enacted section is a foundation artifact. These layers serve different purposes and should not be collapsed.

- Markov blankets must not silently re-enter later. No vocabulary from the Markov blanket tradition (screening, blanket, conditional independence, statistical boundary) should enter the foundation section even through paraphrase, analogy, or "clarifying" revision.

- Later cleanup should not broaden the enacted principle. The section is 180 words and 3 paragraphs. If future revisions expand it to accommodate differential transmission, regime reconfiguration, or the philosophical argument, the section has exceeded its spec. The narrowed core is the design: it is not incomplete.
