# Human Economy Replacement Mechanism Pass 2

## 1. Scope And Exact Question

This run targets only the replacement-mechanism gap in the `human-economy-and-commitment` family. The diagnostic layer (wage/transaction grammar breaks under discovery/uncertainty) and the standing/participation-infrastructure layer (participation requires security, capability, and membership conditions) are not being re-litigated here. Both were assessed as strong in corroboration pass 1 and remain unchanged.

**Exact question being tested:** Does the replacement mechanism — specifically the claim that economic coordination under discovery should shift to differentiated commitment-based primitives with distinct verification modes and governed renegotiation, organized by a four-type taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) — have any real external analog, functional equivalent, or production instantiation beyond what corroboration pass 1 already identified?

**Why the prior pass was insufficient on this layer:** Corroboration pass 1 found that Williamson's hybrid governance was the closest academic analog but that it was a broader category, not a direct match. No external source independently proposed commitment-type differentiation or governed renegotiation as the specific coordination primitive. The four-type taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) had no external parallel. The prior pass explicitly recommended "a targeted second pass focused specifically on commitment-type differentiation in production cases."

**What changed since pass 1:** Three promoted canon entries (legitimacy-and-shared-consequence, linguistic-closure, relational-topology-and-context-design) now establish the tagging format and evidentiary standard for promotion. Additionally, Will Ruddick / Grassroots Economics / CLC (Cosmo-Local Credit) protocol is now available as a candidate evidence source, and Spore's own `commitment-pooling.md` pattern document names CLC as "a key influence on the settlement and demurrage design."

## 2. Evaluation Framework

Every candidate source or case in this pass is classified into exactly one of the following categories.

### Direct external analog

An independently developed framework that closely matches the replacement mechanism itself — commitment-type differentiation organized by epistemic/governance character, with verification modes and renegotiation structure varying by commitment type. The match must be structural (same mechanism), not just adjacent vocabulary. The source must be independent of Johar and Spore.

**What it does not mean:** General use of the word "commitment" in governance. Frameworks that use commitment-based coordination without differentiating commitment types. Systems that distinguish contribution levels (more/less) without distinguishing contribution character (delivery vs. discovery vs. stewardship vs. risk).

### Partial analog

An independently developed framework that matches significant parts of the replacement mechanism but not the whole. Covers some commitment types or matches the differentiation principle without the specific taxonomy. The match is structural but incomplete.

**What it does not mean:** A system that is commitment-based in general but does not differentiate types. A system that differentiates types by a different principle (seniority, financial stake) rather than epistemic/governance character.

### Design lineage / precursor

A framework or system that influenced Spore's commitment primitive or that Spore acknowledges as an inspiration. It may share vocabulary, design intent, or structural approach — but because it influenced the design, it cannot count as independent corroboration of that design. Precursors help explain why Spore's commitment grammar takes the form it does, but they do not independently validate it.

**What it does not mean:** Independent corroboration. A precursor that influenced the design is evidence of design provenance, not evidence of independent convergence.

### Operational precedent

A working system that instantiates something adjacent to or overlapping with the replacement mechanism in production. It may not use the same vocabulary or organize its commitments by the same taxonomy, but it demonstrates in practice that some version of differentiated commitment governance works. Operational precedents are stronger than theoretical matches because they show the mechanism operating, but they may not match the specific taxonomy.

**What it does not mean:** A system that merely uses commitments as a coordination unit without differentiating them. A system whose commitment differentiation is by commodity type (eggs vs. transport) rather than governance character.

### Functional equivalent

A framework that achieves substantially the same governance effect under different language. The mechanism is recognizably similar — different verification standards, different renegotiation structures, different obligation profiles — organized by the epistemic character of the work, even though the framework does not call these "commitment types."

**What it does not mean:** A framework that addresses the same problem domain (uncertainty in work) without the specific mechanism (differentiated commitments). General management theory that acknowledges discovery is different from delivery without proposing differentiated commitment structures.

### False friend

A framework or system that superficially resembles the replacement mechanism but does not actually match on inspection. Includes systems that use commitment language without differentiation, systems that differentiate by a non-epistemic principle, and systems whose governance character is fundamentally different.

## 5. Best-Fit Findings

### Closest direct or partial analogs

**No direct analog found.** The four-type commitment taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) organized by epistemic/governance character as a named governance primitive has no close external match.

**Best partial analogs:**

1. **March exploration/exploitation (1991)** establishes the foundational principle that organizations must govern discovery and delivery fundamentally differently. This is the conceptual bedrock for sub-claim A. But March addresses organizational learning allocation, not commitment governance primitives. The step from "these require different governance" to "therefore different commitment types" is the family's extension.

2. **Williamson's governance-mode taxonomy (market/hybrid/hierarchy)** demonstrates that governance form should match transaction character, including uncertainty levels. Dispute resolution and adaptation mechanisms vary by mode. This is a governance-mode taxonomy, not a commitment-type taxonomy, but the structural logic (match governance to the character of what is being governed) is the same.

3. **Professional partnership structures** differentiate obligation profiles in a way that partially tracks governance function. "Of counsel" approximates a stewardship commitment (knowledge and continuity obligations without delivery targets). Equity partners bear explicit financial risk. Associates receive security-floor-like salaries. The match is suggestive but organized by seniority and economics, not by epistemic character as primary principle.

### Best operational precedents

1. **CLC / Grassroots Economics** — the strongest operational evidence that commitment-based economic coordination works at production scale (26,367 users, 188 pools, 745 vouchers on Sarafu.Network). Demonstrates that "commitments are the economy" is operationally viable, not just theoretically attractive. But does not differentiate commitment types by epistemic character and provides no standing mechanism.

2. **Mondragon** — 70 years of commitment governance at industrial scale. Demonstrates that membership-based participation with governed renegotiation and a security floor works. But does not differentiate commitment types by epistemic character.

3. **SMart Belgium** — demonstrates that creative/discovery work specifically requires hybrid membership infrastructure. Strongest domain fit to the discovery-specific claim. But the commitment structure is uniform (salaried autonomous worker), not differentiated by type.

### Best design lineage / precursors

1. **CLC / Grassroots Economics** — a key acknowledged influence on Spore's settlement and demurrage design. Spore's `project-vision.md` lists Will Ruddick as an inspiration. CLC's "commitments are the economy" principle is shared vocabulary. But because CLC influenced Spore's commitment grammar, it cannot count as independent corroboration of that grammar.

### Best functional equivalents

1. **Stage-gate R&D governance (Cooper 1990)** — the strongest functional equivalent for sub-claims A and C. Different evidence thresholds per gate. Different renegotiation structures (kill/continue/recycle decisions). Resource commitments that vary by epistemic phase. Widely adopted in production (product development, pharmaceutical trials, NASA TRL). The key limitation: stage-gate governs project phases, not interpersonal commitment relations. It is a project-management tool, not a commitment-type taxonomy.

2. **Pharmaceutical trial phase governance** — strongest evidence that epistemic uncertainty drives governance differentiation in high-stakes production. Phase I (small commitment, high uncertainty, safety verification) is structurally different from Phase III (massive commitment, lower uncertainty, efficacy verification). The differentiation tracks epistemic character. But the mechanism is regulatory pipeline stages, not commitment types between agents.

## 6. Will Ruddick / CLC Assessment

### Does CLC weaken, qualify, or overturn the prior statement that the replacement mechanism had "no direct external analog"?

**No.** CLC does not provide a direct external analog for the replacement mechanism. CLC strengthens the broader "commitments are the economy" claim by providing production evidence that commitment-based economic coordination is operationally viable at significant scale. But CLC does not address the specific replacement mechanism — commitment-type differentiation by epistemic/governance character.

### Best classification for CLC

CLC is best understood as a **combination of operational precedent and design lineage / precursor**:

- **Operational precedent:** CLC demonstrates at production scale that commitment-based coordination (not wage, not thin transaction) can organize economic activity. This provides real-world evidence for the broader frame that commitments can replace transactions as the unit of economic coordination. The 26,367 users, 188 active pools, and 745 active vouchers on Sarafu.Network represent genuinely meaningful production evidence.

- **Design lineage / precursor:** CLC is an acknowledged influence on Spore's commitment grammar. Spore's `commitment-pooling.md` explicitly states CLC is "a key influence on the settlement and demurrage design." Spore's `project-vision.md` lists Will Ruddick as an inspiration alongside Indy Johar. Because CLC influenced the design, its evidence for the broader commitment-based coordination claim cannot count as fully independent corroboration of Spore's commitment grammar.

### Which parts of Johar's replacement claim does CLC actually support?

- **"Commitments are the economy"** — strongly supported. CLC operationalizes commitment-based coordination as the economic primitive. Vouchers are redeemable claims on future delivery. Pools are governed clearinghouses for commitments. Settlement velocity replaces monetary velocity. This is genuine production evidence for the broadest layer of the replacement claim.

- **Governed renegotiation** — partially supported. CLC's pool stewards can adjust value indices, tighten limits, and mediate disputes. Ruddick explicitly describes graduated sanctions aligned with reciprocity. But renegotiation in CLC operates at the pool/steward level, not as a structural feature of different commitment types.

- **Federation of commitment pools** — strongly supported. CLC demonstrates that commitment pools can federate across communities while retaining local sovereignty. This directly validates Spore's commitment-pooling pattern.

### Which parts does CLC NOT support?

- **The four-type taxonomy (delivery / discovery / stewardship / risk)** — not supported. CLC treats all commitments as a single primitive: redeemable delivery claims. Differentiation is by commodity (maize, transport, consulting), not by epistemic character.

- **Verification modes varying by commitment type** — not supported. CLC's lifecycle (PROPOSED -> VERIFIED -> ACTIVE -> EVIDENCE_LINKED -> REDEEMED) is uniform across all commitments.

- **Standing / security floor** — not supported. CLC has no mechanism that provides participation security independent of deliverable output. Participation requires productive capacity.

- **Discovery-specific commitment governance** — not supported. CLC is optimized for delivery commitments (future goods and services). It does not address how to govern commitments where the output is unknown and must be discovered.

### Does CLC help with the four-type taxonomy specifically, or mainly with the broader "commitments are the economy" claim?

**Mainly the broader claim.** CLC provides strong evidence that commitment-based economic coordination works at scale. It provides no evidence for the specific four-type taxonomy. The gap between CLC's undifferentiated commitment primitive and Johar's four-type taxonomy is precisely the replacement-mechanism gap this pass is investigating.

### Does CLC count as independent corroboration for Spore, given its influence on Spore's own commitment primitive?

**Partially.** CLC and Johar's commitment economy appear to have developed independently — no cross-citation between Ruddick and Johar was found in either corpus. But Spore explicitly names CLC as an influence on its settlement and demurrage design, and lists Ruddick as an inspiration. This creates a lineage relationship: CLC influenced Spore, so CLC's evidence for the shared "commitments are the economy" claim is evidence of convergent design influence, not independent discovery of the same mechanism. For the broader claim, this is meaningful context. For the specific replacement mechanism (the four-type taxonomy), CLC provides no evidence either way, so the independence question is moot.

## 7. Replacement Mechanism Assessment

**Outcome: gap narrowed but still significant**

### Does the four-type commitment-signature taxonomy now have any real external analog?

No. The specific taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) organized as commitment types differentiated by epistemic/governance character remains without a direct external match. No source found in this pass proposes a commitment-type taxonomy organized by epistemic character as a governance primitive.

### Are there strong enough functional equivalents to justify treating parts of it as less Johar-specific?

Partially. The principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of the work — that discovery and delivery require structurally different governance — is well established (March 1991, stage-gate, pharmaceutical trials, Williamson). This means sub-claim A (differentiated commitment forms are needed) is no longer purely Johar-specific as a principle, even though the specific formulation as commitment types remains Johar's.

Stage-gate governance provides a genuine functional equivalent for sub-claim C (verification modes and renegotiation structure vary by commitment type). The evidence that different epistemic phases require different evidence thresholds and different kill/continue decision structures is robust and widely instantiated in production. The limitation is that stage-gate governs project phases, not interpersonal commitment relations — but the structural logic is transferable.

Professional partnership structures provide a weak-to-moderate functional equivalent for parts of sub-claim B. The "of counsel" role approximates stewardship commitment (knowledge and continuity without delivery targets). Equity partners approximate risk commitment. But the organizing principle is seniority and economics, not epistemic character.

### Is governed renegotiation / verification-mode differentiation now more strongly supported?

Yes, modestly. Stage-gate and pharmaceutical trial governance provide robust production evidence that verification modes and renegotiation points should and do vary by epistemic phase. This strengthens sub-claim C independently of Johar. The limitation remains that these are project-phase governance mechanisms, not commitment-type governance mechanisms.

### Is production evidence stronger than the theory match, or vice versa?

Mixed. The theory match is modestly stronger than before (March + stage-gate + Williamson provide a three-source foundation for the principle of epistemic differentiation in governance). Production evidence is strong for the broad claim (CLC, Mondragon, SMart) but weak for the specific claim (no production system differentiates commitment types by epistemic character). The gap is precisely at the intersection: the specific formulation as a commitment-type taxonomy with differentiated verification and renegotiation structures, applied to interpersonal obligation relations.

## 8. Promotion-Relevance Consequence

**Could enter a future batch with explicit qualification.**

The replacement mechanism gap is narrower than after pass 1 but remains significant. The honest position is:

1. **The diagnostic layer** remains strong and independently corroborated (Hart, Williamson, Simon — unchanged from pass 1). Ready for promotion as confirmed mechanism.

2. **The standing layer** remains strong and independently corroborated (Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom — unchanged from pass 1). Ready for promotion as confirmed mechanism.

3. **The broad replacement principle** ("commitments replace transactions as the coordination unit under uncertainty") is now moderately strong, with CLC adding production evidence to Williamson's theoretical foundation. However, CLC is design lineage, so the evidence is not fully independent. Promotable as confirmed principle with CLC acknowledged as operational precedent, not as independent corroboration.

4. **The specific replacement mechanism** (four-type taxonomy with differentiated verification and renegotiation) remains Johar-specific. Stage-gate provides a functional equivalent for the verification-differentiation principle, but no source proposes the taxonomy as a commitment-type governance primitive. Promotable only as tagged design contribution (Johar's engineering on confirmed components), not as externally confirmed mechanism.

5. **The composition claim** (standing composes with differentiated commitments without collapsing to wage) has no production evidence. Remains a design hypothesis.

A split promotion is now cleanly achievable using the batch-1 tagging format:

- **Mechanism confirmed:** Contracts fail under discovery/uncertainty. Participation requires security and capability conditions. Commitment-based coordination is a viable replacement for transaction-based coordination under uncertainty.
- **Design contribution:** The specific four-type commitment taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) organized by epistemic character. "Standing" as a named primitive within commitment grammar. Discovery-specific intensification.
- **Design hypothesis:** The composition of standing with differentiated commitment types creates durable participation without collapsing to wage grammar.

This is cleaner than the position after pass 1 because the functional-equivalent evidence from stage-gate and CLC production evidence makes the boundary between "confirmed principle" and "Johar-specific taxonomy" sharper.

## 10. Guardrails

- **Replacement mechanism evidence must stand on its own.** The strength of the diagnostic and standing layers does not substitute for weakness in the replacement mechanism. The diagnostic tells us what is broken; the replacement tells us what to build instead. These are different claims requiring different evidence.

- **Lineage is not the same as independent corroboration.** CLC influenced Spore's commitment grammar. It validates that commitment-based coordination works at production scale. It does not independently corroborate Spore's specific commitment-type taxonomy. Treating lineage influence as independent validation would undermine the evidentiary standard that made the batch-1 promotions credible.

- **Production precedent can matter even when taxonomy match is weak.** CLC's 26K-user production evidence for commitment-based coordination is genuinely significant. Mondragon's 70-year institutional evidence for membership-replaces-wage is genuinely significant. SMart Belgium's discovery-domain evidence for hybrid employment status is genuinely significant. These operational precedents strengthen the broader replacement claim even though they do not validate the specific four-type taxonomy. Production evidence and taxonomy match are both valuable but are not substitutes for each other.

- **The four-type taxonomy should remain Johar-specific unless a true analog is found.** Stage-gate governance provides a functional equivalent for the principle of epistemic differentiation but does not propose commitment types as governance primitives. Professional partnerships provide suggestive partial matches but are organized by seniority, not epistemic character. The four-type taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) is Johar's design contribution. It should be promoted as such — not as externally confirmed structure, and not as ungrounded speculation, but as engineering built on confirmed components (exactly the "plausible design translation" category identified in the promotion-readiness pre-review).

- **Importance to Spore does not lower the evidentiary bar.** The human-economy family is the most directly applicable to Spore's existing commitment grammar. Spore's commitment-pooling pattern, coordination ecology, and graph projections all depend on commitment-based coordination. This makes the family important to Spore's self-understanding, but importance is not readiness. The evidentiary standard for promotion must be the same as for the batch-1 families: confirmed mechanism tagged as confirmed, design contribution tagged as design contribution.
