# Human Economy and Commitment Corroboration Pass 1

## 1. Scope and Claim Check

**Family under test:** human-economy-and-commitment

**The exact claim being tested (from the synthesis note):**

Economic participation under discovery and complexity cannot be governed by wage or transaction grammar designed for predictable throughput. The replacement is commitment-based: bounded responsibilities under uncertainty, with standing for participation security, governed renegotiation for adaptive scope, and relational contribution tracking instead of role-output measurement. Participation itself requires capability conditions — material sufficiency, cognitive security, temporal wealth — without which formal rights or market access are structurally empty. The unit of economic coordination must shift from time-for-money (wage) or payment-for-receipt (transaction) to obligation-under-uncertainty (commitment). Commitment without standing is precarity with better vocabulary.

**Three layers tested:**

1. **Diagnostic:** why wage and transaction grammar breaks under discovery and uncertainty — that incomplete specifiability makes fixed-contract coordination structurally inadequate
2. **Replacement mechanism:** commitment, residual responsibility, renegotiable obligation — governance structures that handle what contracts cannot specify
3. **Participation infrastructure:** standing, membership, security floor, capability conditions — the structural preconditions without which commitment-based participation becomes precarity

**Adjacent families this must stay distinct from:**

- **agility-contribution-and-organizing:** Agility is about *infrastructural coordination capacity* — how optionality enables adaptive organising and how alignment decays over time. The economic-commitment family is about the *grammar of economic participation* — what coordination unit replaces wage and transaction. Evidence about alignment decay, context metabolism, or infrastructure-as-optionality is agility, not economic commitment. The two connect (contribution-based organising needs commitment grammar) but the mechanisms are different: agility is about coordination capacity; commitment is about the economic participation primitive.
- **market-legibility-and-moral-provenance:** Market legibility is about *what markets can perceive* — the selective perception gap and the translation infrastructure needed to restore it. Evidence about what markets are deaf to, or how to make substrate conditions visible, is market-legibility. Economic commitment is about *how economic participation is governed* when work cannot be specified. The two share the observation that thin transaction grammar is inadequate, but for different reasons: market-legibility addresses the perception gap; economic commitment addresses the governance gap.
- **priceability-and-system-world:** Priceability is about *what can enter economic reality* — the visibility regime that determines the boundary of the system world. Evidence about value grammar expansion or recognition architecture is priceability. Economic commitment is about *how participants coordinate* within whatever visibility regime exists. Priceability is upstream (what can be seen); commitment is about governance (how to coordinate once you can see).

**Success condition for this run:**

Find 2-4 non-Johar primary or primary-adjacent sources independently supporting:
- that under incomplete specification or uncertainty, economic coordination cannot rely only on fixed contract, wage, or thin transaction grammar
- and that viable coordination shifts toward governance structures built around residual responsibility, stewardship, membership, bounded obligation, or renegotiable commitment

Plus 1 strong production or institutional case where participation is actually structured through commitment-like governance and some standing/security-floor logic rather than pure wage or thin transaction logic.

If no single source or case captures the full Johar claim, say so plainly and identify which sub-claims are corroborated vs still Johar-specific.

## 2. Search Strategy

### Diagnostic lanes

**Lane 1: Incomplete contracts theory (Hart, Moore, Grossman)**

Why relevant: Hart's central insight is that real contracts are fundamentally incomplete — they cannot specify what happens in every contingency — and therefore the critical question is who has residual control rights. This directly addresses the diagnostic: wage/transaction grammar fails when work cannot be fully specified.

Expected evidence type: Conceptual parallel — the formal economics framework for why contract-based coordination fails under uncertainty.

What would count as a false friend: Generic claims that "contracts are imperfect" without the specific mechanism (residual control rights, governance structure as replacement).

**Lane 2: Transaction cost economics (Williamson)**

Why relevant: Williamson's discriminating alignment hypothesis argues that governance structures (markets, hybrids, hierarchies) must match transaction attributes (asset specificity, uncertainty, frequency). When uncertainty and asset specificity are high, market transactions fail and must be replaced by hybrid or hierarchical governance. His "hybrid governance" concept — commitment without full integration — is the closest academic analog to commitment-based primitives.

Expected evidence type: Conceptual parallel — the structural taxonomy showing when and why market/transaction governance fails and what replaces it.

What would count as a false friend: Generic organisational economics that discusses "firm boundaries" without the specific mechanism of governance-mode selection under uncertainty.

### Replacement-mechanism lanes

**Lane 3: Simon — employment as authority relation**

Why relevant: Simon's 1951 paper formalises why the employment contract takes the form it does: the employee accepts authority within a "zone of acceptance" precisely because specific tasks cannot be predetermined. This is the earliest formal account of bounded responsibility under incomplete specification.

Expected evidence type: Conceptual parallel — the formal model of how governance replaces specification when work is uncertain.

What would count as a false friend: Agency theory (principal-agent) that treats the problem as incentive alignment rather than governance structure.

### Participation-infrastructure lanes

**Lane 4: Polanyi — fictitious commodities and social embedding**

Why relevant: Polanyi's argument that labour is a "fictitious commodity" — inseparable from human life and therefore destructive when governed by pure market mechanisms — provides the deepest historical-structural foundation for why participation requires social embedding, not just market access.

Expected evidence type: Historical analysis — the structural argument that market governance of labour destroys the conditions for participation.

What would count as a false friend: Generic critiques of capitalism that attribute problems to greed or power without identifying the structural mechanism by which market governance destroys participation conditions.

**Lane 5: Standing / Sen / Nussbaum — security, capabilities, and occupational citizenship**

Why relevant: Guy Standing's precariat analysis describes what happens when work is organised without standing — seven forms of labour-related security are stripped, producing a class defined by the structural absence of participation conditions. Sen/Nussbaum's capability approach provides the philosophical foundation: formal freedom without substantive capability is empty.

Expected evidence type: Conceptual parallel + empirical analysis — the contemporary diagnosis of how participation grammar that strips standing produces precarity.

What would count as a false friend: UBI literature that addresses income floor without commitment structure. Generic "future of work" that describes trends without structural mechanism.

### False-friend / contrast lanes

**Gig economy:** Thinner contracts, not thicker commitments. The gig economy breaks the wage contract but replaces it with task-based micro-contracts — moving toward radical decomposition rather than commitment-based recomposition.

**Stakeholder capitalism / ESG:** Broad normative expansion inside standard firm grammar. Stakeholder theory addresses who firms should serve, not how economic participation is governed. The coordination unit remains the standard wage/equity relationship.

**UBI:** Security floor without commitment structure. UBI provides material sufficiency but does not require participation or contribution. It addresses one half of the family claim (standing) without the other (commitment).

**DAOs / token governance:** Often shareholder logic in new technical dress. Most DAOs use token-weighted voting and financial incentive alignment — closer to shareholder governance than commitment-based coordination.

**Agile management:** Organising pattern within existing firm grammar, not an economic participation primitive. Agile restructures how work is managed; the family claim restructures how economic participation is governed.

## 4. Best-Fit Corroborating Sources

### Source 1: Oliver Williamson — Transaction Cost Economics (1979, 1985)

**The specific claim it independently supports:** When transactions involve asset specificity and uncertainty, market/transaction governance fails because bounded rationality prevents complete contracts and opportunism exploits contractual gaps. The governance response is a shift from market to hybrid or hierarchical forms that manage renegotiation and commitment without requiring full contractual specification. Hybrid governance — where parties are committed to a relationship but retain separate identity, with disputes resolved through pre-agreed mechanisms — is the intermediate governance form between pure market and pure hierarchy.

**What it supports:**
- Wage/transaction breakdown under uncertainty: yes — the discriminating alignment hypothesis directly names when and why market governance fails (high asset specificity + uncertainty + bounded rationality)
- Residual responsibility or incomplete-specification governance: yes — the shift from market to hybrid/hierarchy IS a shift to governance structures that handle what contracts cannot specify
- Renegotiable/adaptive obligation: yes — renegotiation is central to the framework; the entire problem is governing the renegotiation that incomplete contracts make inevitable
- Standing/membership/security floor: partial — employment-as-hierarchy provides standing implicitly (the employee is inside the governance structure), but Williamson does not theorise standing as a distinct requirement
- Contribution beyond predefined output: yes — bounded rationality means contributions cannot be fully predefined; governance structures exist precisely to handle what falls outside specification

**Why it is better than nearby alternatives:** Williamson provides the most complete single-source corroboration because he covers both the diagnostic (WHY market governance fails) and the replacement (WHAT replaces it — hybrid governance with governed renegotiation). Hart provides a sharper diagnostic but his replacement mechanism is ownership/control, not commitment. Williamson's hybrid governance is structurally closer to the family claim's commitment-based coordination than any other source.

**Key limitation:** Williamson treats employment (hierarchy/wage) as one valid governance response, not as part of the problem. The family claim argues wage grammar itself is insufficient for discovery. Williamson's framework would classify the family claim as arguing for hybrid governance over both market AND hierarchy — a governance form that retains commitment without either market atomism or hierarchical authority.

### Source 2: Oliver Hart — Incomplete Contracts Theory (1986, 1990, 2016)

**The specific claim it independently supports:** Real contracts are fundamentally incomplete — "poorly worded, ambiguous, and leave out important things." When contracts are incomplete, the critical question is not what the contract says but who holds residual control rights — who decides about the things the contract does not cover. Any contract will be incomplete when the future is uncertain: "events will occur that the parties could not foresee." The governance structure (allocation of residual control rights) determines outcomes, not the contract terms.

**What it supports:**
- Wage/transaction breakdown under uncertainty: very strong — this is the foundational argument; complete contracting is impossible under genuine uncertainty
- Residual responsibility or incomplete-specification governance: yes — residual control rights ARE a form of bounded responsibility; the owner decides within the scope the contract does not cover
- Renegotiable/adaptive obligation: yes — renegotiation is central to the model; occurs "under conditions of symmetric information" when parties observe what the contract left out
- Standing/membership/security floor: not addressed — Hart's framework is about asset ownership, not participation conditions
- Contribution beyond predefined output: yes — non-contractible investments are the key variable; the contribution that matters is precisely what CANNOT be predefined

**Why it is better than nearby alternatives:** Hart provides the cleanest formal statement of the diagnostic: when work is incompletely specifiable, governance structure replaces contract terms as the determinant of outcomes. The intellectual route (formal economics, Nobel Prize-winning contract theory) is completely independent of Johar, confirming that the diagnosis of contractual inadequacy under uncertainty is robustly established in mainstream economics.

**Key limitation:** Hart's replacement mechanism is ownership of assets (who holds residual control rights), not commitment (mutual obligation under uncertainty). The family claim is more relational than Hart's framework, which is ultimately about bargaining power and property rights. Hart does not address standing.

### Source 3: Karl Polanyi — *The Great Transformation* (1944)

**The specific claim it independently supports:** Labour is a "fictitious commodity" — not produced for sale and inseparable from human life. Treating labour as a commodity governed by market mechanisms is structurally destructive: "To allow the market mechanism to be sole director of the fate of human beings would result in the demolition of society." Labour "cannot be shoved about, used indiscriminately, or even left unused, without affecting the human individual who happens to be the bearer of this peculiar commodity." The "double movement" — market expansion provoking protective institutional response — is the structural necessity of social embedding for economic participation.

**What it supports:**
- Wage/transaction breakdown under uncertainty: partially — Polanyi argues all labour requires social embedding, not just discovery labour; his argument is broader than the specific wage-specifiability failure
- Residual responsibility or incomplete-specification governance: not directly — Polanyi's framework is about social embedding, not governance mechanism design
- Renegotiable/adaptive obligation: not directly
- Standing/membership/security floor: very strong — the entire double-movement argument is that participation requires protective institutions (social insurance, labour law, welfare state) as structural conditions, not add-ons
- Contribution beyond predefined output: not directly

**Why it is better than nearby alternatives:** Polanyi provides the deepest historical-structural argument for why economic participation cannot be governed by pure market/transaction grammar. His argument is foundational: if even routine industrial labour cannot survive commodification, then discovery labour — where even the partial specifiability that makes wage contracts workable breaks down — requires social embedding even more acutely. The intellectual route (historical political economy, 1944) is completely independent of both institutional economics and Johar.

**Key limitation:** Polanyi addresses all labour, not discovery work specifically. He analyses 19th-century industrial work — the paradigm case of predictable throughput — and argues even this work requires protective institutions. The family claim's discovery-specific intensification (that wage grammar fails under discovery conditions, not just under market conditions) is an extension beyond what Polanyi argues.

### Source 4: Guy Standing — *The Precariat* (2011) + *Work After Globalization* (2009)

**The specific claim it independently supports:** A new class — the precariat — has emerged, defined not by what its members do but by what they lack: seven forms of labour-related security (labour market, employment, job, work, skill reproduction, income, representation). Those in the precariat have work but "without an anchor of stability or security" and "no secure occupational identity." The corrective is "occupational citizenship" — membership-based participation with rights, security, and obligations replacing transaction-based employment. The precariat demonstrates what happens when economic grammar strips standing: formal participation persists while its substance is hollowed out.

**What it supports:**
- Wage/transaction breakdown under uncertainty: yes — Standing explicitly describes how flexible/gig/task-based grammar strips the security conditions that made employment meaningful
- Residual responsibility or incomplete-specification governance: not directly — Standing diagnoses the problem but does not prescribe commitment-based governance mechanisms
- Renegotiable/adaptive obligation: not directly
- Standing/membership/security floor: very strong — seven securities decompose what standing means in practice; "occupational citizenship" is the closest external concept to Johar's "standing"
- Contribution beyond predefined output: not directly — Standing describes precarious work conditions but does not prescribe contribution-tracking alternatives

**Why it is better than nearby alternatives:** Standing provides the most direct contemporary diagnosis of what happens when participation lacks standing — the precariat IS "commitment without standing becomes precarity with better vocabulary." His seven-security decomposition is the most operationally precise account of what standing requires. Sen/Nussbaum provide the philosophical foundation (capabilities as preconditions for participation), but Standing provides the empirical diagnosis of what happens when those conditions are stripped. His "occupational citizenship" concept names the structural move from transaction-based to membership-based economic participation.

**Key limitation:** Standing analyses the precariat as a labour market condition, not as an epistemic condition of the work. He does not distinguish between work that is non-specifiable because of gig economy decomposition and work that is non-specifiable because of its discovery character. The family claim makes this distinction; Standing does not.

## 5. Production or Institutional Case

**Case: Mondragon Corporation (1956–present)**

**Dates:** Founded 1956 by Father Jose Maria Arizmendiarrieta in Mondragon, Basque Country, Spain. Currently ~70,000 worker-owners across ~100 cooperatives federated across finance, industry, retail, and knowledge divisions. Revenue EUR 11.2 billion (2024).

**What happened:** Mondragon replaced wage grammar with membership grammar as the economic participation primitive. The coordination unit is not time-for-money but obligation-under-membership:

- **Entry is commitment, not hire.** After a 6-12 month probation, the worker makes a capital contribution (~EUR 13,400-18,000, borrowable from the cooperative bank Laboral Kutxa) that opens a capital account tracking cumulative economic relationship. Entry grants one vote in the General Assembly regardless of capital level.
- **"Anticipos" not wages.** Worker-owners receive "advances" (anticipos) against annual surplus, not wages. When surplus is negative, advances can be reduced by democratic vote. Workers at Fagor voted to cut their own pay by 20% during the 2008-2013 crisis. This is a membership body renegotiating its own distribution terms, not an employer-employee contract negotiation.
- **Surplus allocation to capital accounts.** Profits distributed: ~60% reinvested, ~30% to individual capital accounts (proportional to hours and pay level), ~10% to community. Losses also allocated to accounts. Members share upside AND downside — obligation-under-uncertainty, not payment-for-receipt.
- **Democratically set wage ratios.** Each cooperative votes on its pay ratio (ranges from 3:1 to 9:1, averaging 5:1). A governed renegotiation mechanism, not a fixed contract.

**Whether positive production precedent, governance redesign, or contrast case:** Primarily a positive production precedent — 70 years of continuous operation demonstrating that membership + commitment + security floor can govern economic coordination at industrial scale. Also a governance redesign case: the 2013 Fagor Electrodomesticos bankruptcy (EUR 1.1 billion in debt, 5,600 jobs at risk) tested the system under extreme stress and it responded with structural mechanisms rather than collapse.

**How commitment, membership, standing, and renegotiation actually functioned:**

*Standing / security floor:*
- **Lagun Aro** (founded 1966): a cooperative social welfare body with EUR 5.6 billion in assets providing retirement, disability, and widowhood benefits — built when Spain excluded cooperative worker-owners from national social security.
- **Inter-cooperative reallocation:** When a cooperative faces distress, worker-owners are transferred to other cooperatives in the federation with retraining funded by Lagun Aro and wage differentials covered. After the Fagor bankruptcy, 95% of ~1,800-2,000 displaced worker-owners were relocated within the Mondragon network within six months. Lagun Aro paid 80% of salary for up to 24 months for those not yet relocated.
- **Solidarity fund:** In 2013, the Cooperative Congress created the Fondo de Reestructuracion — 103 cooperatives donated over EUR 70 million into a self-imposed bailout fund. An endogenous membership commitment to maintain standing, not a government intervention.

*Governed renegotiation:*
- Democratic vote to adjust compensation (the 20% Fagor pay cut)
- Pay ratios periodically renegotiated by democratic vote within each cooperative
- Cooperative Congress sets cross-cooperative policies (fund allocations, intercooperation rules)
- Venture agreements between cooperatives and the federation are renegotiable through governance

*Contribution beyond predefined output:*
- Mondragon's 10 Basic Cooperative Principles include "Participatory Management" (requiring progressive self-management), "Education" (systematic professional and cooperative training), and "Social Transformation"
- The principle of Sovereignty of Labour states profit allocation is based on "work contributed," not just output produced
- Workers participate in governance bodies, strategic planning, and cooperative education — contribution tracked across multiple dimensions

**Why this case is evidentially stronger than alternatives:**

1. **Scale and longevity.** ~70,000 members, 70 years continuous operation, EUR 11.2 billion revenue. This is not an experiment but a proven institutional form at industrial scale.
2. **Tested under crisis.** The Fagor bankruptcy was a severe stress test. The system's structural response (inter-cooperative reallocation, Lagun Aro benefits, solidarity fund, democratic wage adjustment) demonstrated that the governance mechanisms work under adversity, not just prosperity.
3. **All three layers present.** Membership replaces employment (replacement mechanism). Anticipos and democratic renegotiation replace fixed wage contracts (diagnostic confirmed by design). Lagun Aro, inter-cooperative reallocation, and the solidarity fund provide the security floor (standing).
4. **Endogenous design.** The security floor (Lagun Aro) was built BECAUSE the standard system failed cooperative members — a direct institutional response to governance gap.

**Whether it supports the family directly or only partially:** Partially direct with an honest limitation.

*What it demonstrates directly:* Membership + commitment + security floor can govern economic coordination at scale. The anticipos/capital-account structure is genuine obligation-under-uncertainty. The renegotiation mechanisms (democratic wage adjustment, Cooperative Congress) are genuine governed renegotiation. Lagun Aro + inter-cooperative reallocation is genuine standing/security floor.

*Honest limitation:* Mondragon's industrial cooperatives operate in sectors where work is relatively specifiable (manufacturing, retail, finance). The family claim's strongest domain is "discovery and complexity" — work that cannot be supervised conventionally or verified by output. Mondragon demonstrates that commitment governance works for industrial coordination; whether it works specifically for discovery/epistemic work is less documented. The knowledge division (Mondragon University, research centres) is closer to the claim's core domain but is the least-studied part of the federation.

*Supporting case — SMart Belgium:* For discovery-domain evidence, SMart Belgium (~120,000 members, founded 1998) is the stronger complement. SMart was founded explicitly because creative/freelance work cannot be governed by wage or freelance grammar. Its "salaried autonomous worker" hybrid status — membership creating employment status, guaranteed payment, social protection accumulation — demonstrates standing for inherently non-specifiable work. SMart's governance primitives are lighter than Mondragon's, but its domain fit to the discovery claim is stronger.

## 6. False Friends and Rejections

### Rejected: Gig economy / platform labour (Uber, Upwork, TaskRabbit)

The gig economy breaks the wage contract but replaces it with task-based micro-contracts — moving toward *thinner*, not thicker, coordination. It addresses the same diagnosis (wage-specifiability failure for flexible work) but proposes the opposite solution (radical decomposition rather than commitment-based recomposition). The gig economy strips standing (no membership, no security, no renegotiation) while maintaining the form of participation. This is precisely what the family claim argues against: transaction grammar made even thinner, not replaced with commitment grammar.

### Rejected: Stakeholder capitalism / ESG

Stakeholder theory (Freeman) argues firms should serve multiple stakeholders. But it operates within the standard firm grammar and typically addresses incentive alignment within existing corporate governance. The coordination unit remains the wage/equity relationship; what changes is who the firm claims to serve. The family claim is about changing the coordination primitive itself, not broadening the beneficiary set within existing primitives.

### Rejected: UBI / basic income

UBI provides a security floor (analogous to standing) but without commitment structure. UBI addresses material sufficiency without requiring participation or contribution — it is unconditional. The family claim specifically names standing as institutional membership *within* a commitment economy: membership that enables participation, not income that replaces it. UBI is one half of the family claim (security floor) without the other (commitment governance). Using UBI as corroboration would collapse the family claim into a welfare policy argument rather than a governance-primitive argument.

### Rejected: DAOs / token governance

Decentralised autonomous organisations use token-based coordination that superficially resembles commitment-based participation. But most DAOs operate on token-weighted voting and financial incentive alignment — closer to shareholder governance than to commitment-based coordination. The governance structure of most DAOs does not distinguish commitment types, does not provide standing/security floor, and does not govern renegotiation. Token-weighted voting is plutocratic, not membership-based. DAOs are a false friend because the surface (decentralised, participatory) resembles the family claim while the mechanism (capital-weighted, transaction-based) does not.

### Rejected: Purpose Foundation / steward ownership (noted as adjacent)

Steward ownership restructures ownership, not participation. Its two principles (self-governance by active stewards, purpose lock preventing extraction) address extractive ownership — a real governance problem — with genuine commitment mechanisms (irrevocable locks, golden shares). But steward-owned companies may use standard wage grammar internally. The model does not restructure how economic participation is governed for workers — it restructures how ownership and surplus are governed for the entity. Adjacent at the ownership layer, but the family claim is about participation primitives, not ownership form.

### Rejected: Agile methodology / SAFe

Agile restructures how work is managed within existing organisational grammar. It addresses the problem of specification under uncertainty (iterative development, sprint planning, retrospectives) but within the standard employment relationship. The coordination unit remains the wage contract; what changes is the management process. The family claim argues for changing the economic participation primitive, not the management methodology.

### Noted: Sen / Nussbaum — participation infrastructure without discovery specificity

Sen's capability approach and Nussbaum's ten central capabilities provide the canonical philosophical foundation for the standing claim — participation requires structural preconditions beyond formal rights or market access. Nussbaum adds the institutional-guarantee dimension: capabilities are justice requirements that must be structurally provided. These are strong supporting sources for the standing layer but do not address the specific discovery dimension (why uncertainty in work intensifies the standing requirement) or the replacement mechanism (what governance primitives replace wage grammar). They corroborate the GENERAL PARTICIPATION claim without addressing the SPECIFIC COMMITMENT claim.

## 7. Preliminary Corroboration Assessment

**Outcome: success condition met**

The success condition asked for 2-4 sources supporting that under incomplete specification, economic coordination must shift from fixed contract/wage/transaction to governance built around residual responsibility, stewardship, membership, bounded obligation, or renegotiable commitment — plus 1 production case. This is met by four sources from three independent traditions and one production case at scale.

### What is corroborated

**The diagnostic — strong corroboration from institutional economics:**

Three independent sources from formal economics confirm that standard contracts/transactions fail under uncertainty:

- **Hart (1986, 1990, 2016):** Contracts are fundamentally incomplete; residual control rights (governance structure) determine outcomes when specification fails. "Events will occur that the parties could not foresee when they started out."
- **Williamson (1979, 1985):** Bounded rationality + opportunism + asset specificity make market transactions structurally inadequate. Governance must shift from market to hybrid or hierarchical forms.
- **Simon (1951):** Employment exists as a governance form precisely because specific tasks cannot be predetermined. The "zone of acceptance" is the earliest formal model of bounded responsibility under incomplete specification.

The diagnostic is robustly established in mainstream economics. The claim that wage/transaction grammar fails under uncertainty is not controversial — it is the foundational insight of transaction cost economics and incomplete contracts theory.

**The replacement mechanism — moderate corroboration from Williamson's hybrid governance:**

Williamson's hybrid governance concept — commitment without full integration, with governed dispute resolution and renegotiation within structure — is the closest academic analog to the family claim's commitment-based primitives. The match: parties commit to a relationship without fully integrating, retain separate identity, resolve disputes through pre-agreed mechanisms, and renegotiate as conditions change. This maps to bounded responsibility, governed renegotiation, and contribution beyond predefined output.

Simon's "zone of acceptance" provides the micro-foundations: the employee commits to bounded responsibility (accepting authority within limits) in exchange for income security (the wage as standing). This is the specific primitive-level mechanism.

**However**, a crucial tension exists: both Williamson and Simon treat employment (wage + authority) as a valid governance response to uncertainty. The family claim goes further by arguing that wage grammar itself is insufficient for discovery/complexity work — that what is needed is not authority within bounded limits but mutual commitment with governed renegotiation. The family claim is essentially arguing for something beyond Williamson's hierarchy: a form closer to his hybrid category where commitment exists without hierarchical control.

**The standing/participation-infrastructure — strong corroboration from political economy and labour sociology:**

- **Polanyi (1944):** Labour as fictitious commodity; market governance of labour destroys social substance; protective institutions are structurally necessary, not optional welfare. The foundational argument that participation requires social embedding.
- **Standing (2011):** The precariat demonstrates what happens when standing is stripped — seven forms of security lost, hollow participation, "occupational citizenship" as the structural corrective. The most direct parallel to "commitment without standing becomes precarity with better vocabulary."
- **Sen (1999) / Nussbaum (2011):** Formal freedom without substantive capability is empty. Capabilities must be institutionally guaranteed at a threshold level.
- **Ostrom (1990):** Commons governance requires bounded membership with rights and obligations; open access destroys commons. Empirical evidence that participation requires membership, not just access.

**The production case — strong from Mondragon, with SMart Belgium as discovery-domain complement:**

Mondragon demonstrates all three layers at industrial scale: membership replaces wage grammar, anticipos + democratic renegotiation replace fixed contracts, Lagun Aro + inter-cooperative reallocation provide the security floor. SMart Belgium demonstrates the diagnostic most clearly for discovery-domain work: creative/freelance work requires membership infrastructure because wage/freelance grammar cannot govern it.

### What remains uncorroborated

1. **The commitment-signature taxonomy.** The four commitment types (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) have no external parallel. Hart/Williamson address incomplete specification generally; they do not differentiate commitment types by epistemic character. This taxonomy is Johar-specific.

2. **"Standing" as a named primitive distinct from generic social protection.** The external sources confirm that participation requires security, capability, and membership conditions. But they do not isolate "standing" as a distinct governance primitive — a specific mechanism within commitment economy that provides security floor without requiring specifiable work. Polanyi calls it social embedding; Standing calls it occupational citizenship; Sen calls it capability; Ostrom calls it membership. The family claim's contribution is naming standing as a *primitive* within a commitment grammar, not just a welfare condition alongside it.

3. **The exact parallel between Johar's commitment economy and Spore's commitment grammar.** The synthesis note argues that Spore's commitment protocols are already implicitly commitment-economy mechanisms. This is an internal design insight about Spore, not a claim that external sources could corroborate.

4. **The discovery-specific intensification.** All external sources address economic participation under uncertainty generally. The specific argument that DISCOVERY work (epistemic, non-supervisable, output-indeterminate) breaks wage grammar in a way that ROUTINE uncertain work does not is the family claim's distinctive contribution. Hart/Williamson address uncertainty; Simon addresses task non-specifiability; but none specifically analyses the epistemic character of discovery work as the condition that makes commitment governance necessary.

5. **The wage-as-problem framing.** Hart, Williamson, and Simon all treat employment (wage + authority) as a governance SOLUTION, not a governance failure. The family claim goes further by arguing that wage grammar itself is insufficient for discovery. The external evidence supports the diagnosis for market/transaction grammar but does not independently problematise the wage relationship as the family claim does.

### Whether the family stayed distinct from adjacent concepts

**Yes.**

- **Agility-contribution-and-organizing:** No evidence gathered here addresses coordination capacity, alignment decay, or context metabolism. The focus remained on economic participation grammar (what coordination unit replaces wage/transaction), not on organising capacity (how to maintain adaptive coordination).
- **Market-legibility-and-moral-provenance:** No evidence gathered addresses what markets can or cannot perceive. The focus was on governance mechanisms for participation, not on perception gaps in market coordination.
- **Priceability-and-system-world:** No evidence gathered addresses the visibility regime or value grammar expansion. The focus was on participation primitives, not on what enters economic reality.

Potential bleed: Polanyi's "fictitious commodity" argument touches market-legibility (markets cannot appropriately price labour because labour is not a commodity). And Standing's precariat diagnosis touches agility (precarious workers lack the conditions for adaptive contribution). These boundary touches are noted but do not blur the family.

### Whether the evidence is conceptual, operational, empirical, or mixed

Mixed — conceptual parallel (Hart, Williamson, Simon, Polanyi), empirical/contemporary diagnosis (Standing), philosophical framework (Sen, Nussbaum), institutional design evidence (Ostrom), and production evidence (Mondragon, SMart Belgium). The evidence base is strongest on the conceptual/diagnostic layer and the standing/participation layer, with the production case providing operational confirmation. The replacement-mechanism layer has conceptual support (Williamson's hybrids) but the specific commitment primitives the family proposes have no direct external analog.

### Whether the following remained Johar-specific

- **The commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk):** Yes, Johar-specific. No external source distinguishes commitment types by epistemic character. This is the family's original contribution to the governance vocabulary.
- **"Standing" as a distinct primitive rather than generic security:** Partially Johar-specific. The concept is independently corroborated (Polanyi's social embedding, Standing's occupational citizenship, Sen's substantive freedom, Ostrom's membership), but isolating it as a *named primitive within commitment grammar* — rather than as a general welfare condition — is the family's contribution.
- **The exact parallel between Johar's commitment economy and Spore's commitment grammar:** Johar-specific. This is an internal design insight about Spore.

## 8. Recommended Next Move

**Recommended: Move to relational-topology-and-context-design**

The human-economy-and-commitment corroboration has met its success condition. The diagnostic is robustly established in mainstream economics. The standing/participation-infrastructure layer is strongly corroborated from multiple independent traditions. The replacement mechanism is moderately corroborated through Williamson's hybrid governance. The production case (Mondragon) demonstrates all three layers at scale. The family's distinctive contributions (commitment-signature taxonomy, standing as named primitive, discovery-specific intensification) remain Johar-specific, as expected.

The next highest-value move is **relational-topology-and-context-design**, because:

1. **It completes the coverage of the top-5 priority families.** The three completed runs covered Superfamilies A/F (legitimacy), A (linguistic-closure), and D (human-economy). Relational-topology is in Superfamily F (Institutional Topology and Legitimacy), completing coverage across four of the six superfamilies.

Google's Project Aristotle (psychological safety as context variable) provides a well-documented production case. The tractability is high.

Human-economy used conceptual parallels + production cases.
