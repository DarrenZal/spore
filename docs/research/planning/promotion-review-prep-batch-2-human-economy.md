# Promotion Review Prep Batch 2 — Human Economy

## 1. Scope And Rule

This memo prepares only the `human-economy-and-commitment` family for possible inclusion in a future narrow review packet. It is prep for future human review, not promotion. No `promotion_status` values will change. No canon entries will be written. No synthesis-note prose will be rewritten.

The family must be handled through split-promotion, not as a single undifferentiated claim. The batch-1 tagging format (mechanism confirmed / design contribution / design hypothesis / Johar-distinctive integration / boundary-sensitive) is the standard. The split established in the batch-2 prep memo is binding: three confirmed-mechanism layers, one design-contribution layer, two design-hypothesis layers, and one Johar-distinctive integration layer.

The four-type commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) remains non-confirmed. Two full corroboration passes found no direct external analog. Stage-gate R&D governance and March's exploration/exploitation distinction provide functional equivalents for the *principle* that governance should vary by epistemic character, but no source independently proposes commitment types organized by epistemic character as a governance primitive. This qualification is not negotiable in any future review packet.

CLC (Grassroots Economics / Will Ruddick) is classified as operational precedent + design lineage/precursor. Because Spore's `commitment-pooling.md` names CLC as "a key influence on the settlement and demurrage design" and `project-vision.md` lists Ruddick as an inspiration, CLC cannot count as independent corroboration. It can count as production evidence that commitment-based economic coordination works at scale.

## 2. Review Tagging Profile

### `mechanism confirmed`

- **Wage / transaction grammar breaks under discovery and complexity.** Fixed-contract coordination is structurally inadequate when work cannot be specified in advance, supervised in execution, or verified by output. Hart (incomplete contracts, residual control rights), Williamson (discriminating alignment, bounded rationality + opportunism), Simon (zone of acceptance, authority replacing specification). Three independent traditions converge on the same structural account. The discovery-specific intensification — that discovery breaks wage grammar in ways routine uncertain work does not — is the family's extension beyond what these sources establish, but the core diagnostic is confirmed.

- **Participation requires standing / capability conditions.** Commitment-based participation without security, capability, and membership conditions produces precarity with better vocabulary. Polanyi (fictitious commodity — labour inseparable from human life), Standing (precariat / seven securities / occupational citizenship), Sen (capabilities as freedom — formal rights without substantive capability are structurally empty), Ostrom (commons membership as participation infrastructure). Four independent traditions converge. None address discovery-specific standing; the intensification under discovery is the family's extension.

- **Broad replacement principle: governance should vary by epistemic character of work.** Organizations must govern exploration and exploitation fundamentally differently; verification thresholds, resource commitments, and renegotiation structures should and do vary by epistemic phase. March (exploration vs exploitation, 1991), Cooper (stage-gate R&D governance, 1990), pharmaceutical trial phase governance (Phase I safety vs Phase III efficacy), NASA TRL (different acceptance criteria per level), Williamson (governance-mode selection matching transaction attributes). The principle is well-established. The step from "different governance for different work" to "different commitment types" is where the family's extension begins.

### `design contribution`

- **Governed renegotiation and verification-mode variation as commitment-type governance.** Stage-gate and pharmaceutical governance demonstrate that verification standards and renegotiation structures should vary by the character of what is being governed — this is confirmed in production at scale. The formulation as commitment-type governance — interpersonal obligation relations with type-specific verification — is Johar's. The design contribution is the translation from project-phase governance (well-established) to commitment-type governance (Johar-specific).

- **"Standing" as a named primitive within commitment grammar.** The concept of participation security is independently established (Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom). The specific formulation — standing as a named governance primitive that composes with commitment types within a commitment grammar — is Johar's vocabulary. External sources use security, capabilities, membership, occupational citizenship. The naming and integration into commitment grammar is the design contribution.

### `design hypothesis`

- **Four-type commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk).** Johar proposes that commitment types can be differentiated by epistemic/governance character into four categories, each with distinct verification modes and renegotiation structures. Two full corroboration passes found no external source that independently proposes commitment types organized by epistemic character as a governance primitive. Professional partnership structures provide weak partial match (of counsel approximates stewardship, equity partner approximates risk). The taxonomy is the family's most distinctive contribution and also its least externally grounded claim.

- **Standing composed with differentiated commitments.** Standing (security floor) and commitment differentiation (type-specific obligations) should compose without collapsing back into wage grammar. No production system combines a security floor with epistemic commitment-type differentiation. Mondragon demonstrates security floor + commitment governance but without epistemic differentiation; SMart demonstrates discovery-domain membership but without commitment-type differentiation. The composition claim is architecturally important but entirely untested.

### `Johar-distinctive integration`

- **Parallel to Spore's commitment grammar.** Spore's existing commitment protocols, holon membership, governed renegotiation, and commitment-pooling pattern are structurally parallel to the commitment-economy account. The grammar may already embody commitment-economy mechanisms without having named them. This parallel is explanatory — it helps explain why the grammar takes the form it does — but is not independent evidence. Treating Spore's own structure as confirming the economic theory it was built on would be circular.

### `boundary-sensitive`

- **Wage-as-problem framing reversal.** Hart, Williamson, and Simon all treat employment (wage + authority) as a governance *solution* to uncertainty. The family claim treats wage as the *problem* under discovery conditions. This is not a contradiction — the family argues wage works for routine work but fails for discovery — but the reversal means the family's replacement claim goes beyond what external sources support. The boundary between confirmed diagnostic (contracts fail under uncertainty) and extended claim (wage grammar itself is the failure mode) is sensitive and must be preserved in any review-ready framing.

## 4. Claim Decomposition

### A. Mechanism Confirmed

- Fixed-contract coordination (wage, transaction) is structurally inadequate when work cannot be specified in advance, supervised in execution, or verified by output. The coordination unit must shift from contract terms to governance structure under uncertainty. (Hart, Williamson, Simon — three independent traditions.)
- Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing: security, capability, and membership conditions without which formal participation rights are structurally empty. "Commitment without standing is precarity with better vocabulary." (Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom — four independent traditions.)
- The principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of work is well-established. Verification thresholds, resource commitments, and renegotiation structures do vary by epistemic phase in production systems. (March, stage-gate, pharmaceutical trial governance, NASA TRL, Williamson.)
- Commitment-based economic coordination is operationally viable at production scale, as demonstrated by CLC (26K users, 188 pools) and Mondragon (70 years, 70K worker-owners). CLC is acknowledged as design lineage for Spore, not independent corroboration.

### B. Design Contribution

- The translation from project-phase governance (stage-gate, pharmaceutical trials) to commitment-type governance (interpersonal obligation relations with type-specific verification and governed renegotiation) is Johar's. The structural logic is transferable; the specific formulation as commitment types between agents is original.
- "Standing" as a named primitive within commitment grammar — integrating independently established concepts (security, capabilities, membership, occupational citizenship) into a single governance primitive that composes with commitment types — is Johar's vocabulary and integration.
- Discovery-specific intensification — the claim that discovery work breaks wage grammar in ways routine uncertain work does not — extends beyond what Hart, Williamson, or Simon specifically analyze.

### C. Design Hypothesis

- The four-type commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) organized by epistemic/governance character has no external analog after two full corroboration passes. It is Johar's engineering proposal built on confirmed components.
- The composition of standing with differentiated commitment types — the claim that a security floor and epistemic commitment differentiation can be sustained together without collapsing back into wage grammar — has no production evidence. Mondragon and SMart approximate sub-components separately but do not demonstrate the composition.
- The claim that Spore's commitment grammar should explicitly adopt the four-type taxonomy remains untested as a design decision.

### D. Johar-Distinctive Integration

The structural parallel between Spore's commitment grammar and Johar's commitment-economy account is real: commitment signatures map to commitment protocols, standing maps to holon membership, governed renegotiation maps to commitment lifecycle revision, contribution mixes map to layered participation rights. This parallel explains why the grammar takes the form it does and identifies design territory the grammar has not yet explicitly named. But it is design context, not evidence. Spore was built with awareness of this conceptual territory, and CLC — an influence on Spore's commitment grammar — shares the "commitments are the economy" frame. The parallel should appear in any review packet as explanatory context, not as confirming evidence.

### E. Boundary Checks

**Distinct from `agility-contribution-and-organizing`:** Agility addresses infrastructural coordination capacity — how optionality enables adaptive organizing and how alignment decays over time. Human-economy addresses the grammar of economic participation — what coordination unit replaces wage and transaction. Contribution-based organizing needs commitment grammar, but the mechanisms are different: agility is about coordination capacity; commitment is about the economic participation primitive. Standing (participation security) must not be confused with alignment maintenance (agility's territory).

**Distinct from `market-legibility-and-moral-provenance`:** Market legibility addresses what markets can perceive — the selective perception gap and the translation infrastructure needed to restore it. Human-economy addresses how economic participation is governed when work cannot be specified. Both observe that thin transaction grammar is inadequate, but for different reasons: market-legibility addresses the perception gap; economic commitment addresses the governance gap. Evidence about what markets are deaf to belongs to market-legibility, not here.

**Distinct from `priceability-and-system-world`:** Priceability addresses what can enter economic reality — the visibility regime that determines the boundary of the system world. Human-economy addresses how participants coordinate within whatever visibility regime exists. Priceability is upstream (what can be seen); commitment is about governance (how to coordinate once you can see). The commitment-economy account does not depend on resolving the priceability question, and evidence about value grammar expansion or recognition architecture belongs to priceability.

## 5. Candidate Review-Ready Framing

Economic coordination under discovery and complexity cannot rely on wage or transaction grammar because these forms depend on specifiability, containment, and measurability assumptions that discovery structurally violates (Hart, Williamson, Simon; confirmed mechanism). Viable coordination shifts toward commitment-based primitives — bounded obligations under uncertainty with governed renegotiation — as demonstrated at production scale by Mondragon's membership governance and CLC's commitment-based economic coordination (operational precedent, with CLC acknowledged as design lineage for Spore's own commitment grammar, not independent corroboration). Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing: security, capability, and membership conditions without which formal participation rights are structurally empty (Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom; confirmed mechanism). The principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of work is well-established (March, stage-gate, pharmaceutical governance; confirmed mechanism). Johar's specific contribution is the proposal that this principle can be operationalized through a four-type commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) with type-specific verification and governed renegotiation — a design hypothesis built on confirmed components, not yet externally confirmed as a governance primitive. The composition of standing with differentiated commitment types is a design hypothesis with no production demonstration. The structural parallel to Spore's commitment grammar is explanatory design context, not confirming evidence.

## 6. Reviewer Questions

1. **Is the broad replacement principle strong enough to enter canon without the four-type taxonomy?** The diagnostic (contracts fail under discovery) and standing (participation requires security) are independently confirmed. The principle that governance should vary by epistemic character is established. Can these three layers constitute a meaningful canon entry even though the specific commitment-type taxonomy that operationalizes them remains a design hypothesis?

2. **Should standing be treated as part of the same canon entry or scoped as contextual infrastructure?** The family's sharpest formulation is "commitment without standing is precarity with better vocabulary" — an interdependence claim. But standing is independently established by Polanyi, Standing, Sen, and Ostrom. Would a reviewer treat standing as a component of the commitment-economy entry (preserving the compositional insight) or as a precondition referenced from elsewhere (avoiding the risk that standing smuggles the whole family into canon)?

3. **Is the design contribution (governed renegotiation / verification-mode variation as commitment-type governance) narrow enough to survive canon use without drift?** The translation from project-phase governance to commitment-type governance is the family's original engineering. In canon, will the distinction between "governance varies by phase" (confirmed) and "commitment types vary by epistemic character" (Johar's) stay sharp, or will it collapse under repeated use?

4. **Is the Spore grammar parallel explanatory only, or could it be mistaken for evidence?** The structural overlap between Spore's commitment grammar and Johar's commitment-economy account is real. In a review packet, can this parallel be framed clearly enough as design context that no reader treats it as confirming evidence?

5. **Does CLC's dual role (operational precedent + design lineage) need a specific review-packet safeguard?** CLC is the strongest production evidence for commitment-based coordination at scale, and also an acknowledged influence on Spore's settlement and demurrage design. Should the review packet include a formal statement separating CLC's two roles, or is the current qualification sufficient?

## 7. Suggested Disposition

**`promote with explicit qualification`**

The confirmed-mechanism layers (diagnostic + standing + broad replacement principle) clear the evidentiary standard established by batch 1. The design-contribution layers (governed renegotiation as commitment-type governance, standing as named primitive) are plausible engineering on confirmed components — the same "plausible design translation" category that batch 1 used for vocabulary and integration claims. The design-hypothesis layers (four-type taxonomy, standing-plus-differentiation composition) are clearly identified and honestly qualified.

The family is promotable only if the split is preserved in the canon entry. A canon entry that presents the four-type taxonomy as confirmed, or that treats CLC as independent corroboration, or that allows standing alone to carry the whole family into canon, would fail the evidentiary standard. The qualification is the condition: promote the confirmed layers as confirmed, tag the design contribution as Johar's, and mark the hypotheses as hypotheses.

This is the same posture that batch 1 used for linguistic-closure (where the proactive anti-closure infrastructure was tagged as design hypothesis) and relational-topology (where the 6-9 month half-life was tagged as design hypothesis). The human-economy family has a larger design-hypothesis layer than either of those, but the confirmed layers are independently strong enough to carry a meaningful entry.

## 10. Guardrails

- **Strong mechanism support does not confirm the four-type taxonomy.** The diagnostic tells us what is broken (wage/transaction grammar under discovery). The broad replacement principle tells us governance must vary by epistemic character. Neither confirms that the specific four-type commitment-signature taxonomy is the right operationalization. Hart and Williamson confirm the diagnostic; March and stage-gate confirm the principle; no one confirms the taxonomy.

- **CLC matters, but not as independent corroboration.** CLC demonstrates at production scale that commitment-based coordination works. This is genuinely significant evidence for the broad replacement principle. But CLC influenced Spore's commitment grammar — it is operational precedent and design lineage, not independent discovery of the same mechanism. Any future review packet must state this explicitly and not present CLC as convergent independent evidence.

- **Standing should not be allowed to smuggle the whole family into canon.** Standing (participation security) is the most strongly corroborated layer — four independent traditions converge. But standing alone is not the family's distinctive contribution. The family's claim is that standing *composes with* differentiated commitments to produce durable participation. If only standing enters canon, the compositional insight — which is the family's actual contribution — is lost. If standing carries the taxonomy along with it, the taxonomy enters without its own evidence. The review must treat these as separate questions.

- **The Spore parallel explains relevance; it does not prove the claim.** The structural overlap between Spore's commitment grammar and Johar's commitment-economy account is real and worth noting. But Spore was built with awareness of this conceptual territory. The parallel should appear in any review packet as design context ("this is why the grammar takes the form it does"), not as evidence ("the grammar's existence confirms the theory").

- **Future review must preserve the split or the family should stay out.** The split-promotion structure — confirmed mechanism / design contribution / design hypothesis — is not a formatting convenience. It is the condition under which this family is reviewable at all. If the split collapses during review (the taxonomy gets presented as confirmed, CLC gets treated as independent, standing carries the whole family), the evidentiary standard that made batch 1 credible is undermined. A reviewer who cannot maintain the split should defer the family rather than promote it blurred.
