## 4. What the First Four Runs Actually Validated

### The corroboration method is working

All four runs used the same structure: identify 2-4 external traditions likely to address the mechanism, launch parallel research agents per lane, synthesize results, write a structured memo with candidate evidence table, best-fit sources, one case, false friends, and assessment. This produced consistent results across four different families spanning four superfamilies. The method is transferable to the remaining target (holding-complexity).

### The target synthesis notes are specific enough to be externally testable

Every synthesis note had claims precise enough to generate falsifiable search queries. The claim registers (C1, C2, C3, R1) in each synthesis note provided the right level of specificity: concrete enough to search for, abstract enough to match against functional equivalents. The precision of the synthesis notes is a strength of the intake process.

### The strongest corroborations are mixed — conceptual plus empirical

Every run that succeeded strongly did so by combining conceptual parallels (multiple independent traditions converging on the same mechanism) with at least one empirically grounded case. Pure conceptual parallel without a case would have been weaker; a case without the conceptual foundation would have been anecdotal. The pattern:

| Run | Conceptual Traditions | Empirical Evidence |
|-----|----------------------|-------------------|
| Legitimacy | 4 traditions | Nepal irrigation (controlled comparison), Baggio configural analysis (69 cases), EU democratic deficit, DeCaro experimental |
| Linguistic-closure | 4 traditions | Replication crisis (institutional failure + reform), Mann Gulch (illustrative) |
| Human-economy | 3 traditions | Mondragon (70 years, ~70k members), SMart Belgium (~120k members) |
| Relational-topology | 4 traditions | Aristotle (180+ teams), Burt (blind evaluation), Yang/Microsoft (61k employees), Polzer (experimental teams), Allen Curve |

Relational-topology had the strongest empirical base. Legitimacy had the strongest cross-type mix (configural analysis + controlled comparison + experimental). Human-economy had the strongest production cases (operating institutions, not studies). Linguistic-closure had the most precise mechanism match (4 traditions independently naming the same stopping-rule structure).

### External sources confirm structural mechanism but not Johar vocabulary/integration

This is the most important finding across all four runs. In every case:

- **Confirmed:** The structural mechanism the family names (authority-consequence coupling, categories-as-stopping-rules, contracts-fail-under-uncertainty, context-generates-relation)
- **Not confirmed:** The specific Johar vocabulary for naming that mechanism ("orchestration over dominance," "linguistic closure" as a named primitive, "commitment signature," "imaginary identity," "context metabolism")
- **Not confirmed:** The integrative framing that combines components into a unified design discipline
- **Not confirmed:** The proactive institutional-design corrective (all external sources propose reactive correctives — crisis, revolution, competition, anomaly accumulation — while Johar proposes designed infrastructure)

This pattern suggests that Johar's work is best understood as a *design synthesis* built on independently discoverable structural insights. The structural insights are real (confirmed). The synthesis into a coordinated design grammar is original (not independently discovered). This is the expected profile for a design discipline: the physics is known; the engineering is new.

## 5. Method Lessons

### What kinds of targets corroborate cleanly

Families with a precise structural mechanism — "X causes Y through Z" — corroborate well. Legitimacy (authority-consequence coupling produces institutional viability/failure) and linguistic-closure (categories produce sufficiency that terminates inquiry) had the cleanest matches because their mechanisms are specific and independently nameable. Relational-topology also corroborated cleanly because its sub-claims are empirically testable (context > composition, communication drops with distance, topology generates faultlines).

### What kinds of claims are easiest to over-credit

- **Broad resonance as corroboration.** Many traditions say "context matters" or "language shapes thought" or "institutions need legitimacy." These are adjacent but do not corroborate the specific mechanism. Every run required careful false-friend filtering (Kahneman for linguistic-closure, UBI for human-economy, social capital for topology, Habermas for legitimacy).
- **Single-source cases carrying too much weight.** Mondragon appears in the human-economy run as the primary case but operates in manufacturing rather than discovery work. Nepal irrigation appears in the legitimacy run but is a commons governance case, not a general institutional case. Cases are strongest when paired with conceptual parallels; when they carry the argument alone, they are too narrow.
- **"Compatible with" counted as "independently supporting."** Several sources across runs were compatible with the family claim without independently arriving at the same structural account. Berger & Luckmann (mechanism without corrective), Granovetter (topology as emergent, not design variable), Sen (general participation, not discovery-specific) — all compatible but not independent corroboration of the full claim.

### How much weight to give production cases vs conceptual parallels

Production cases (Mondragon, SMart, Google Aristotle, Nepal irrigation, replication crisis reform) are necessary but insufficient. They demonstrate that the mechanism *operates in real institutions* but do not by themselves establish that the mechanism is *structural rather than contingent*. Conceptual parallels from independent traditions establish structural generality. The best corroboration combines both: multiple traditions confirming the mechanism IS structural (conceptual) plus at least one case showing it OPERATES as described (empirical/operational).

### When a family looks externally real but still too internally integrated for promotion

Human-economy-and-commitment is the case study here. The diagnostic is robust (Hart/Williamson confirm contracts fail under uncertainty). The standing claim is well-supported (Polanyi/Standing/Sen). But the specific replacement mechanism — commitment signatures with four types, governed renegotiation, standing as a named primitive within commitment grammar — lacks a direct external counterpart. Williamson's hybrids are the closest match but are a broader category. The family is externally *real* (the problem it names is confirmed) but the specific *design response* it proposes remains Johar's original contribution. This profile means: ready for promotion review on the diagnostic and standing layers, but the commitment-signature taxonomy should be marked as design proposal, not externally confirmed mechanism.

### How to treat named Johar primitives that only have external functional equivalents

Every run encountered this pattern. The guideline that emerged:

- If the external functional equivalent describes the *same mechanism* through different vocabulary (Peirce's "fixation of belief" = linguistic closure, Polanyi's "social embedding" = standing, Burt's "structural holes" = topology-generated asymmetry): **count as corroboration of the mechanism, not of the vocabulary**.
- If the external concept is *adjacent but operates at a different level* (Kahneman's cognitive bias vs linguistic closure's institutional mechanism, Sen's general capability vs Johar's discovery-specific standing): **note as supporting but not corroborating**.
- If the external concept *captures part of the claim but not the integration* (Williamson's hybrids capture commitment-without-integration but not the commitment-signature taxonomy): **mark the specific sub-claim as corroborated, flag the integration as Johar-specific**.

The promotion implication: named Johar primitives can enter the lexicon as design vocabulary once the underlying mechanism is externally corroborated, even if the specific term is Johar's. The term names a real mechanism; the name itself is the grammar's contribution.

## 6. Non-Merge Boundaries Hardened by Corroboration

### linguistic-closure vs perceptual-fidelity

**What the corroboration clarified:** The linguistic-closure run drew on Peirce, Kuhn, Latour, and Weick — all of whom address how categories/paradigms/black boxes/sensemaking terminate inquiry. None of these sources addresses the *quality of sensing contact* with pre-abstraction reality (perceptual fidelity). Kuhn's incommensurability touches the boundary (scientists in different paradigms perceive differently), but the mechanism is about how *categories shape perception* (linguistic closure), not about *how much relational richness sensing preserves* (perceptual fidelity).

**What evidence can transfer:** Kuhn's incommensurability could partially serve a future perceptual-fidelity corroboration, but only for the specific claim that categorical frameworks determine what can be perceived — not for the broader fidelity claim about sensing quality.

**What must remain family-specific:** Evidence about how naming terminates inquiry (closure) must stay distinct from evidence about how sensing preserves or degrades relational contact (fidelity). Closure is about *language foreclosing what can be noticed*; fidelity is about *the depth of encounter with what IS noticed*.

### legitimacy-and-shared-consequence vs relational-topology-and-context-design

**What the corroboration clarified:** The two runs drew on entirely non-overlapping external traditions. Legitimacy used Ostrom, Scharpf, Hirschman, Scott (political theory, institutional economics, EU governance, state theory). Topology used Suchman, Burt, Orlikowski, Edmondson (ethnomethodology, structural sociology, STS, organisational behaviour). No source appeared in both runs. This is strong evidence that the families address genuinely different structural questions.

**What evidence can transfer:** Ostrom's design principles address both legitimacy (consequence-binding) and potentially topology (how commons governance generates specific relational patterns for monitoring and enforcement). This was noted in the corroboration-target mapping's cross-target dependencies but was not used in either run. A future cross-family comparison could test whether Ostrom evidence serves both.

**What must remain family-specific:** Legitimacy evidence is about *who has authority and who bears consequences* (political question). Topology evidence is about *how arrangements generate patterns of connection, asymmetry, and identity* (infrastructure question). An organisation can have good topology with poor legitimacy (well-connected but authority and consequence are split) or poor topology with good legitimacy (legitimate but relationally fractured).

### human-economy-and-commitment vs agility-contribution-and-organizing

**What the corroboration clarified:** The human-economy run focused on the *grammar of economic participation* — what replaces wage/transaction as the coordination unit, what standing means, what commitment primitives look like. It drew on institutional economics (Hart, Williamson, Simon) and political economy (Polanyi, Standing). None of these sources addresses the *organising infrastructure* question — how to maintain adaptive coordination capacity, what alignment decay means, what context metabolism produces.

**What evidence can transfer:** The Mondragon case contains evidence relevant to both families: membership governance (economic commitment) and democratic renegotiation during crisis (adaptive organising). The economic mechanisms were attributed to human-economy; the adaptive-organising mechanisms could serve a future agility corroboration.

**What must remain family-specific:** Economic grammar evidence (commitment signatures, standing, wage-specifiability failure) must stay in human-economy. Organising infrastructure evidence (alignment decay, context metabolism, infrastructure-as-optionality) must stay in agility. The boundary is between *what the coordination unit is* (economy) and *how to maintain coordination capacity over time* (agility).

### relational-topology-and-context-design vs agility-contribution-and-organizing

**What the corroboration clarified:** The alignment-decay claim (6-9 month half-life) sits at their boundary. The topology run gathered evidence for structural decay (Roberts & Dunbar 2015, Allen Curve, Jarvenpaa & Leidner 1999) and attributed it to topology because the mechanism is topological: context change (loss of proximity, shared routines, spontaneous encounter) produces relational erosion. But the *consequence* of that decay — loss of adaptive coordination capacity — is the agility family's territory.

**What evidence can transfer:** Roberts & Dunbar's longitudinal decay evidence and the distributed-team literature could serve both families. The mechanism (decay from context change) is topology; the implication (loss of coordination capacity) is agility.

**What must remain family-specific:** Evidence about *what topology generates* (identity, asymmetry, faultlines, power distribution) stays in topology. Evidence about *how to maintain adaptive coordination capacity* (infrastructure-as-optionality, the paradox of lightweight, context metabolism as enabling self-organisation) stays in agility.

## 7. Hardest Unresolved Issues

### 1. Human-economy's replacement mechanism is the weakest link across the 4 runs

The diagnostic (contracts fail under uncertainty) and the standing claim (participation requires security and capability conditions) are both robustly corroborated. But the specific replacement mechanism — commitment signatures with four types (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk), governed renegotiation, contribution tracking beyond predefined output — has only Williamson's hybrid governance as a partial external match. The gap is not fatal (Mondragon and SMart demonstrate that commitment-like governance works in practice) but it means the commitment-signature taxonomy is the least externally grounded specific claim across all four runs. If any family deserves a deeper second pass, it is this one — specifically targeting the replacement-mechanism layer rather than re-doing the diagnostic or standing layers.

### 2. No single case is carrying too much evidentiary weight

Each run produced at least one strong case, and no run depends on a single case for its assessment. Mondragon is the strongest single case across all runs (70 years, ~70k members, tested under crisis) but it is supplemented by SMart Belgium and supported by Hart/Williamson/Polanyi/Standing on the conceptual side. Nepal irrigation is the second-strongest case but is supplemented by EU democratic deficit, DeCaro experimental, and Baggio configural analysis. No case needs to be de-weighted.

### 3. "Johar-specific integration" is stable across targets but differs in character

The pattern — external sources confirm mechanism, Johar provides integration and design vocabulary — holds across all four runs. But the *character* of what remains Johar-specific differs:

- **Legitimacy:** Johar-specific is primarily *vocabulary* (the four-dimensional taxonomy, "orchestration"). The structural mechanism is fully externally corroborated.
- **Linguistic-closure:** Johar-specific is primarily the *proactive design corrective* (claim lifecycle, discourse graph) and the *amplification under LLM*. The mechanism is fully corroborated.
- **Human-economy:** Johar-specific is partly *vocabulary* (commitment signatures, standing) but also a *structural claim* (the four-type commitment taxonomy, the wage-as-problem framing). The gap is more substantive.
- **Relational-topology:** Johar-specific is primarily *integration* (combining context-as-generator, topology-as-power, decay-as-structural into a single design discipline) and a *specific parameter* (6-9 month half-life). The components are independently corroborated; the unified frame is not.

This means promotion review should treat the families differently. Legitimacy and linguistic-closure are closest to "mechanism confirmed, vocabulary is design contribution." Relational-topology is "components confirmed, integration is design contribution." Human-economy is "diagnostic confirmed, replacement mechanism is design proposal."

### 4. Production evidence may be more valuable than further literature search for some families

The four literature-based corroboration runs have reached the point of diminishing returns for conceptual parallels. The structural mechanisms are established. What remains uncorroborated is mostly Johar's design response — which is better validated by *building and testing* than by *finding another author who agrees*. Specifically:

- **Linguistic-closure's proactive anti-closure infrastructure** (claim lifecycle, discourse graph, attestation decay) would be best validated by production evidence from running governance that tests whether these mechanisms actually resist closure.
- **Human-economy's commitment-signature taxonomy** would be best validated by an organisation that implements commitment-type differentiation and tracks whether the taxonomy produces measurable governance improvement.
- **Relational-topology's 6-9 month alignment half-life** would be best validated by longitudinal measurement in actual distributed teams.

Future work should shift the balance from literature corroboration toward production evidence where the literature has already confirmed the mechanism.

## 8. Next-Target Decision

**Selected next run: scoping pass for holding-complexity-beyond-control**

### Why this should be next

Holding-complexity is the most corpus-central family — every other complexity family and most infrastructure/governance families presuppose it. It names the grammar's raison d'etre: why Spore exists as a coordination grammar at all. Leaving it uncorroborated while reviewing the other four for promotion would create an asymmetry: the design justification would remain unvalidated even as the design components are confirmed.

However, the corroboration target mapping already flagged holding-complexity as the hardest target because its *specific framing* (the fork, two default attractors, five structural capacities, deliberate construction under constraining conditions) is the most distinctively Johar. The core diagnosis (control fails under complexity) is widely shared; the specific structural architecture is original. A full corroboration pass risks either (a) over-crediting broad resonance ("complexity science agrees that complexity is hard") or (b) under-finding the specific framing.

A scoping pass resolves this by determining *what is externally testable* before committing to a full run. The scoping pass should identify:

- Which sub-claims of holding-complexity have direct external counterparts (expected: the diagnostic, the representational-inadequacy claim, and possibly the two default attractors)
- Which sub-claims are Johar-specific integration (expected: the five capacities, the deliberate-construction-under-constraint claim, the energetics argument)
- Whether a full corroboration pass is likely to produce a "success condition met" or whether the best achievable outcome is "partial — diagnostic corroborated, design architecture Johar-specific"
- What the success condition for a full pass should be, given the target's distinctive difficulty

### Why the other three should wait

**Full corroboration pass for holding-complexity:** Premature without scoping. The risk of an unfocused full pass that produces broad resonance rather than precise corroboration is high enough to justify a cheaper scoping step first.

**Deferred-family triage checkpoint:** Lower priority than the fifth corroboration target. The 8 deferred families are less structurally important than the 5 priority families. Triage should happen after holding-complexity is at least scoped.

**Promotion-readiness pre-review for the first 4:** Tempting but premature. A promotion review should assess all 5 priority families together, not 4 of 5. Reviewing 4 now would either (a) defer holding-complexity's promotion indefinitely or (b) force a second review cycle once holding-complexity is corroborated. Better to scope holding-complexity first, then review all 5 in one pass.

### Success condition for the scoping pass

The scoping pass should produce:
- A decomposition of holding-complexity into sub-claims ranked by external testability
- For each sub-claim, a judgment of whether direct external corroboration is available, partial, or unlikely
- A recommended success condition for a subsequent full corroboration pass (which may be narrower than the other four runs)
- A decision: proceed to full pass, proceed with modified success condition, or accept partial corroboration

## 10. Guardrails

1. **External confirmation of mechanism is not yet promotion.** All four runs confirmed the structural mechanism. None confirmed the specific Johar design response. Promotion must account for both: the confirmed mechanism AND the status of the design vocabulary/integration. A family whose mechanism is confirmed but whose design response is untested should be promoted with the design response explicitly tagged as "design proposal, not externally confirmed."

2. **Functional equivalents count only when the mechanism is genuinely the same.** Peirce's "fixation of belief" is a genuine functional equivalent to linguistic closure (same mechanism, different vocabulary). Polanyi's "social embedding" is a genuine functional equivalent to standing (same structural requirement, different name). Williamson's "hybrid governance" is a partial functional equivalent to commitment-based primitives (overlapping territory, different specificity). Generic "cooperation matters" or "context matters" is not a functional equivalent to anything — it is adjacent vocabulary, not the same mechanism.

3. **Cases should not be stretched to cover missing conceptual support.** Mondragon demonstrates that commitment-like governance works at scale but does not independently validate the four-type commitment-signature taxonomy. Project Aristotle demonstrates that context > composition but does not independently validate the claim that relational topology is "the real infrastructure of institutions." A case demonstrates operational viability, not conceptual generality. Paired with conceptual parallels, cases are strong. Alone, they are anecdotal.

4. **Highly integrated Johar framings may need mixed evidence rather than direct textual matches.** The five families each integrated multiple independently confirmable sub-claims into a unified design discipline. No external source independently arrived at the same integration. This is expected for original design work and does not disqualify the integration — but it means that promotion review should assess the integration as a design contribution, evaluated by internal coherence and production viability, not by external textual match.

5. **The remaining work should preserve boundaries rather than reward conceptual sprawl.** The four runs demonstrated that well-bounded families with precise mechanisms corroborate cleanly. The worst outcome for holding-complexity would be a diffuse pass that collapses it into "complexity science agrees complexity is hard." The scoping pass should resist this by identifying the specific sub-claims that are externally testable and accepting that the Johar-specific integration may not be confirmable through literature search. Preserving the boundary between "the diagnosis is confirmed" and "the specific five-capacity architecture is Johar's design contribution" is more honest than forcing a match.
