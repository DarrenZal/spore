## 3. Candidate Corpus-Level Superfamilies

### Superfamily A: Epistemic Commons and Perceptual Infrastructure
**Category: design grammar**

The conditions under which Spore's grammar maintains quality contact with reality and holds plural truth. This superfamily names the failure modes (linguistic closure, perceptual narrowing), the designed condition (civic truth), and the sensing quality (perceptual fidelity) that together define what the learning membrane, discourse graph, and claim lifecycle are for at the epistemic level.

**Constituent families:** linguistic-closure (Intelligence), perceptual-fidelity (Intelligence), civic-truth (Governance)

**Why these belong together:** All three address the same structural question from different angles: how do systems maintain adequate contact with reality while operating through abstraction? Linguistic closure is the failure mode (language terminates inquiry). Perceptual fidelity is the quality to be preserved (depth of contact). Civic truth is the designed institutional condition (a commons that holds plural truth). Together they form a coherent design grammar for the epistemic layer of any coordination system.

**Why they should not be collapsed:** Each names a different structural element. Closure is a mechanism (how language compresses). Fidelity is a quality (what sensing preserves). Civic truth is an institutional condition (what the commons requires). Collapsing them into "epistemic design" would lose the precision of each. The learning membrane needs all three: it must resist closure (claim lifecycle), maintain fidelity (sensing design), and sustain the civic-truth condition (governed commons with doubt infrastructure).

**Note:** Human-machine-complementarity bridges this superfamily and the Developmental Substrate superfamily. Its core claim (some perceptual fidelity is human-irreplaceable; governance must require developmental agent participation) connects epistemic infrastructure to agent architecture. It does not sit cleanly in either superfamily and should be treated as a bridging concept.

### Superfamily B: Complexity Conditions and Adaptive Coordination
**Category: systems condition**

The civilisational and material conditions that make control-based coordination fail and adaptive coordination necessary. This superfamily names the overarching structural condition (complexity exceeds control), the governance face (volatility), the infrastructure face (post-permanence), and the material operating condition (biophysical reality). Together they form the structural diagnosis that makes Spore's grammar necessary — not as a design preference but as a response to conditions.

**Constituent families:** holding-complexity-beyond-control (Complexity), governance-under-volatility (Governance), post-permanence-and-adaptive-infrastructure (Infrastructure), planetary-systems-and-biophysical-reality (Complexity)

**Why these belong together:** All four describe why control-based coordination fails, from different vantage points: complexity science (holding-complexity), governance theory (governance-under-volatility), infrastructure design (post-permanence), and earth systems (planetary-systems). Each identifies the same structural mismatch: coordination architectures designed for stability operating under conditions of volatility, coupling, and decay. Together they form the most heavily evidenced structural claim in the corpus.

**Why they should not be collapsed:** Each names a distinct layer of the condition. Holding-complexity is the overarching diagnosis (the fork, the two attractors, the five capacities). Governance-under-volatility is the governance specification (coherence replaces control). Post-permanence is the infrastructure specification (optionality replaces durability). Planetary-systems is the material grounding (biophysical conditions are primary, not contextual). Collapsing them would lose the specificity of each layer's design implications. Spore's grammar needs all four: the structural diagnosis, the governance criterion, the infrastructure principle, and the material grounding.

### Superfamily C: Futures Navigation and Worldview Pluralism
**Category: institutional pattern**

How institutions relate to plural futures and competing worldview orientations without premature closure. This superfamily names the futures-navigation discipline (pathway portfolios, prophecy awareness) and the worldview-holding condition (emergent cohesion across deep difference). Together they address what keeps the grammar's orientation open — the mechanisms for preventing any single future or any single worldview from hardening into uncontestable canon.

**Constituent families:** pathways-prophecy-and-futures-design (Complexity), worldviews-cohesion-and-systems-change (Complexity)

**Why these belong together:** Both concern the grammar's relationship to what is not yet settled — futures and worldviews. Pathways-prophecy addresses how to navigate plural futures without single-track closure. Worldviews-cohesion addresses how to hold worldview contest as a structural feature rather than suppress it. Both invoke option-space preservation, reflexive awareness, and institutional capacity for productive divergence. Together they name the orientation-holding infrastructure.

**Why they should not be collapsed:** Pathways-prophecy is about navigating time (futures, forecasts, pathway portfolios). Worldviews-cohesion is about navigating orientation (competing civilisational orientations, emergent cohesion). The prophecy-conditioning layer (forecasts construct futures) and the worldview-contest layer (orientations interact recursively) are distinct design concerns. Collapsing them would lose the prophecy-awareness dimension, which is the distinctive contribution of pathways-prophecy beyond "having options."

**Weakness:** This is the thinnest superfamily — only two constituent families, both from the same theme (complexity). It is a real grouping but should be treated as a hypothesis rather than a confirmed structural cluster.

### Superfamily D: Commitment Architecture and Economic Coordination
**Category: design grammar**

The economic and organisational grammar for coordination under discovery. This superfamily names the shift from wage/transaction/role-based coordination to commitment/contribution/relational-based coordination — and the translation infrastructure required to make economic systems perceive what they currently cannot. Together these families describe what Spore's commitment grammar already does from a governance perspective and what it would need to do from an economic perspective.

**Constituent families:** human-economy-and-commitment (Economy), market-legibility-and-moral-provenance (Economy), priceability-and-system-world (Economy), agility-contribution-and-organizing (Infrastructure)

**Why these belong together:** All four address how economic and organisational coordination must be restructured: human-economy names the primitive unit (commitment signature), market-legibility names what markets cannot perceive and what translation infrastructure fixes, priceability names the upstream grammar that determines what becomes visible to coordination, and agility names the organising infrastructure that makes adaptive participation possible. Together they form a coherent design grammar for economic coordination under discovery.

**Why they should not be collapsed:** Each names a different structural element. Human-economy is about the unit of coordination (commitment vs wage). Market-legibility is about perception (what markets hear). Priceability is about the visibility regime (what can be priced at all). Agility is about organising infrastructure (optionality, alignment decay, context design). Collapsing them would lose the distinction between the coordination unit, the perception problem, the grammar problem, and the infrastructure problem. Note: market-legibility and priceability are the most overlap-prone pair in the corpus — their synthesis notes share two claim sheets and their open questions explicitly flag the boundary. They should be monitored for eventual merger, but currently the upstream/downstream distinction (priceability is grammar; legibility is perception) is sharp enough to justify keeping them separate.

### Superfamily E: Human Developmental Substrate
**Category: developmental substrate**

The human conditions that coordination under complexity presupposes. This superfamily names the ontological ground (entangled freedom), the developmental capacities (care, courage, presence), the identity process (inter-becoming), the subject-formation condition (consciousness sovereignty), and the inner condition (doubt, plural selfhood, relational maturity). Together they describe what agents must be and what they must be able to do for the grammar to work — conditions the grammar assumes but has not named.

**Constituent families:** entangled-freedom-and-being (Human-dev), care-courage-and-presence (Human-dev), identity-and-inter-becoming (Human-dev), consciousness-sovereignty-and-post-compliance (Human-dev), doubt-plural-selfhood-and-relational-maturity (Human-dev)

**Why these belong together:** All five name human developmental conditions that Spore's coordination grammar requires but does not provide. Entangled freedom is the ontological ground (what freedom means under entanglement). Care, courage, and presence are the capacities (what agents need to remain perceptually alive). Inter-becoming is the identity process (how selfhood is constituted through participation). Consciousness sovereignty is the subject-formation condition (what replaces compliance). Doubt and plural selfhood are the inner conditions (what makes relational multiplicity inhabitable). Together they form the developmental substrate beneath the grammar.

**Why they should not be collapsed:** Each names a different layer of the developmental condition. Entangled freedom is ontological (what being is). Care-courage-presence is capacity-level (what agents can do). Inter-becoming is processual (how identity forms). Consciousness sovereignty is institutional-formative (what subject-making institutions must become). Doubt-plural-selfhood is interior (what the inner condition of complexity-inhabiting is). Collapsing them into "human development" would lose the layered architecture. The grammar may need to respond differently to each layer: the ontological layer informs constitutional commitments, the capacity layer informs governance design, the identity layer informs membership patterns, the sovereignty layer informs the relationship between grammar and subject, and the doubt layer informs the learning membrane.

**Note:** This is the largest superfamily (5 families, all from one theme). It is coherent because the human-development theme was always the most internally unified theme. But its size means it should be treated as a layered architecture, not a single concept. Corroboration should target individual layers, not the superfamily as a whole.

### Superfamily F: Institutional Topology and Legitimacy
**Category: institutional pattern**

How institutions generate relational fields, distribute authority, bind actors into consequences, and maintain coherence over time. This superfamily names the infrastructural substrate of institutions (relational topology, context design) and the political condition for institutional viability (legitimacy bound to consequence). Together they address the institutional design layer that sits between the structural conditions (Superfamily B) and the human developmental substrate (Superfamily E).

**Constituent families:** legitimacy-and-shared-consequence (Infrastructure), relational-topology-and-context-design (Infrastructure)

**Why these belong together:** Both concern how institutions are structured as relational and political architecture. Legitimacy names the condition for institutional viability (authority and consequence must travel together). Relational topology names the infrastructure that generates the relational field within which legitimacy operates (topology of connection, distance, and encounter). Together they form the institutional design grammar — how to build institutions that maintain coherence, distribute power fairly, and bind actors into the outcomes they shape.

**Why they should not be collapsed:** Legitimacy is a political condition (who has authority, who bears consequences). Relational topology is an infrastructural condition (how connections are patterned, how context shapes perception). They are interdependent (topology shapes where legitimacy can form; legitimacy requires topology that binds consequence to authority) but operate at different levels. Collapsing them would lose the distinction between the political question (what makes institutions legitimate?) and the infrastructural question (what generates the relational field?).

**Weakness:** This is a thin superfamily — only two families, both from the infrastructure theme. It is real (both families are among the strongest in the corpus by source count) but should be monitored for whether it eventually absorbs governance-under-volatility or agility-contribution-and-organizing as the corpus matures.

## 4. Non-Merge Boundaries

### holding-complexity-beyond-control vs pathways-prophecy-and-futures-design

**Holding-complexity** names the civilisational structural condition: complexity exceeds control, two default attractors (authoritarian compression, chaotic decomposition) reduce complexity rather than holding it, and the third path requires deliberate construction of five structural capacities. It is the overarching diagnosis.

**Pathways-prophecy** names one specific capacity within that diagnosis: the design discipline for navigating plural futures through pathway portfolios, prophecy awareness, and reflexive meta-governance. It is a response to the condition, not the condition itself.

**Why they are adjacent:** Pathway design is explicitly listed as one of the five structural capacities holding-complexity requires. Four of pathways-prophecy's five sources also contribute to holding-complexity's convergence.

**Why collapsing them would lose precision:** Pathways-prophecy has a distinctive layer — prophecy conditioning — that holding-complexity does not name. The recognition that forecasts construct futures (not merely describe them) and that meta-governance of the portfolio is required to prevent pathway design from degenerating into disguised prophecy is a design concern that disappears if pathways is absorbed into the broader condition. The grammar needs both: the structural diagnosis (why adaptive coordination is necessary) and the specific futures-navigation discipline (how to approach the future without premature closure).

### worldviews-cohesion-and-systems-change vs planetary-systems-and-biophysical-reality

**Worldviews-cohesion** names the political-epistemic condition: systems change involves contest over worldview grammars, six competing civilisational orientations are becoming explicit, and emergent cohesion (not consensus) is the institutional capacity required.

**Planetary-systems** names the material operating condition: damping loss, coupling thresholds, degenerative volatility, and the collapse of the subject-object grammar are biophysical realities that change the physics of institutional action.

**Why they are adjacent:** They share one source (embracing-the-anthropocene) and both appear in the complexity theme. Both describe conditions under which separability assumptions fail.

**Why collapsing them would lose precision:** Worldviews-cohesion is about orientation (which worldview grammar structures governance). Planetary-systems is about material conditions (what the physics of action now requires). The political question (how to hold worldview contest) and the biophysical question (what the material operating condition demands) have different design implications. Using biophysical reality as a trump card that erases political choice is precisely the risk that planetary-systems itself flags in its open question 2. Keeping them separate preserves the grammar's ability to address both the material and the political dimensions of complexity without collapsing one into the other.

### relational-topology-and-context-design vs legitimacy-and-shared-consequence

**Relational-topology** names the infrastructural dimension: contexts and organisational forms are relational topology generators that shape what can be perceived, who relates to whom, and what imaginary identity emerges. Context design is the discipline; alignment decay is a structural parameter.

**Legitimacy-and-shared-consequence** names the political condition: institutions become viable when legitimacy and consequence are held together, polycentric legitimacy distributes authority across four dimensions, and orchestration replaces dominance.

**Why they are adjacent:** Relational topology generates the relational field within which legitimacy can form. Spatial-politics (a source for legitimacy) explicitly names relational topology as the mechanism through which political asymmetry is produced. Both concern institutional design.

**Why collapsing them would lose precision:** Topology is about the patterning of connection (an infrastructure question: how are agents related?). Legitimacy is about the distribution of authority and consequence (a political question: who has standing to decide, and who bears the outcomes?). You can have good relational topology with poor legitimacy (a well-connected institution where authority and consequence are split) or poor topology with good legitimacy design (a legitimate institution whose relational infrastructure produces fracture). They are complementary design dimensions, not redundant descriptions.

### entangled-freedom-and-being vs boundary-making-and-entangled-systems (deferred)

**Entangled-freedom** names the human developmental condition: freedom is participation within relation, being is prior to instrumental participation, and the developmental, economic, and relational conditions for exercising freedom are infrastructure.

**Boundary-making** names a systems-ontological concept: Markov blankets replace traditional boundary language, boundaries are differential pass-through structures rather than separating walls, and the grammar needs a vocabulary for conditional-independence gradients.

**Why they are adjacent:** Both use "entangled" and both reject separation as the primary organising principle. Both address how the grammar should handle the relationship between inside and outside.

**Why collapsing them would lose precision:** Entangled freedom is about human experience under entanglement (ontological, developmental, political). Boundary-making is about systems formalism under entanglement (mathematical, ontological, structural). The first asks: what does freedom mean when being is relational? The second asks: what does a boundary mean when systems are coupled? These are different questions with different design implications. Entangled freedom informs the grammar's human-developmental commitments. Boundary-making would inform the grammar's formal vocabulary for describing system architecture. Collapsing them would conflate the human question with the systems question.

### human-economy-and-commitment vs agility-contribution-and-organizing

**Human-economy** names the economic grammar: commitment signatures replace wage contracts, standing provides a security floor, and four commitment types (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) replace job descriptions.

**Agility-contribution** names the organising infrastructure: agility depends on infrastructure (not lightness), alignment decays over 6-9 months, and context design determines what adaptive organising is possible.

**Why they are adjacent:** Both argue for contribution-based participation over role-based fixity. Both connect to Spore's commitment protocols. The economy synthesis's "standing" and the agility synthesis's "infrastructure" address complementary aspects of the same problem: how to make adaptive participation possible.

**Why collapsing them would lose precision:** Human-economy is about the grammar of economic participation (what replaces the wage, what the unit of coordination is, what security floor is required). Agility is about the infrastructure of organising (what makes pivoting possible, what maintains alignment, what context conditions enable adaptation). You can have the right economic grammar (commitment signatures) with poor organising infrastructure (no alignment maintenance), or good infrastructure (context design) with the wrong economic grammar (still using wage contracts). Both are needed but they solve different problems.

## 6. Corroboration Priority Ranking

### Rank 1: linguistic-closure

**Why it matters at corpus level:** Linguistic closure is the failure mode that every other family implicitly warns against. It connects to civic-truth (closure is the failure civic truth resists), perceptual-fidelity (closure forecloses perception), worldviews-cohesion (worldview monopoly is closure at civilisational scale), doubt-plural-selfhood (certainty as closure), and the learning membrane itself (the membrane is anti-closure infrastructure). It has the broadest governance reach of any family (8 clusters across 2 projects, 9 sources, 45 supports).

**Why it is a better corroboration target than nearby alternatives:** Linguistic closure is the most practically testable family. The core claim — that naming compresses, compression feels sufficient, and sufficiency terminates inquiry — can be tested against the grammar's own operation. Does the claim lifecycle actually resist closure? Does the discourse graph surface when categories have lost contact? These are empirical questions. The concept also has the deepest resonance with established traditions: philosophy of language (Wittgenstein, Lakoff), STS (Jasanoff, Latour), post-foundational philosophy (Derrida, Rorty), and organisational theory (Weick on sensemaking). Finding independent sources should be tractable.

**What kind of corroboration would count:** (a) Another author or tradition that independently names the structural (not cognitive) mechanism by which language terminates inquiry — particularly in institutional or governance contexts, not just individual cognition. (b) A practical institutional case where a revision mechanism (analogous to the claim lifecycle) demonstrably prevented category ossification. (c) Historical examples of institutions that either succumbed to or resisted linguistic closure through structural design. Pragmatist philosophy (Dewey, Peirce) and STS literature on "black boxing" are the most promising search domains.

### Rank 2: legitimacy-and-shared-consequence

**Why it matters at corpus level:** The most institutionally grounded family. Names the condition for institutional viability: authority and consequence must travel together. Connects governance (volatility, coherence), infrastructure (relational topology, context design), and economy (commitment-based participation, provenance). Polycentric legitimacy draws directly on Ostrom's work. The control-surveillance spiral is empirically observable.

**Why it is a better corroboration target than nearby alternatives:** It has the most established institutional precedent. Polycentric governance (Ostrom), commons governance (Bollier, Hess), and institutional economics (North, Acemoglu) all address legitimacy and consequence-binding from independent traditions. The control-surveillance spiral is testable against empirical governance patterns. The 6-source convergence (strongest infrastructure family) makes the existing claim precise enough to corroborate against.

**What kind of corroboration would count:** (a) Ostrom's polycentric governance literature as an independent tradition arriving at plural legitimacy. (b) Commons governance cases that demonstrate authority-consequence binding or failure under consequence-separation. (c) Historical or contemporary examples of the control-surveillance spiral operating as described. (d) Institutional economics on how organisations fail when authority and consequence separate. This is the most tractable corroboration target in the corpus.

### Rank 3: holding-complexity-beyond-control

**Why it matters at corpus level:** Most corpus-central family — the grammar's raison d'etre. The overarching structural diagnosis (complexity exceeds control, two default attractors, third path requires deliberate construction). Strongest convergence in the corpus (7 sources, 27 supports). Every other complexity family and most infrastructure/governance families reference it or presuppose it.

**Why it is a better corroboration target than nearby alternatives:** If corroborated, it validates the entire grammar's design rationale. No other family carries as much structural load. But it is also the most distinctively Johar concept — the specific framing (the fork, the two attractors, the five capacities, civilisational optionality as criterion) is his original synthesis. Corroboration must match this specificity, not merely confirm "complexity is hard."

**What kind of corroboration would count:** (a) Complexity science literature (Kauffman, Holland, Page) on why control fails under manifest complexity — specifically the representational inadequacy claim, not just "systems are complex." (b) Institutional design literature that independently names the two default attractors (compression and decomposition) as the structural alternatives to deliberate complexity-holding. (c) Case studies of institutions that successfully built complexity-holding capacity vs those that defaulted to compression or decomposition. (d) Thermodynamics / energetics literature on the resource costs of coordination under tightening energy constraints. Note: the five-capacities framing is the most Johar-specific element and may not find direct corroboration — but the structural diagnosis (why control fails, what the defaults are) has broader support.

### Rank 4: human-economy-and-commitment

**Why it matters at corpus level:** The most directly applicable family to Spore's existing grammar. The structural overlap between Johar's commitment economy and Spore's commitment protocols is the strongest of any economy concept. Standing (as security floor for participation) and commitment signatures (as bounded responsibility under uncertainty) map directly onto the grammar's commitment lifecycle.

**Why it is a better corroboration target than nearby alternatives:** It connects the grammar's governance architecture to economic coordination — extending Spore's reach. The commitment-type taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) is immediately implementable if corroborated. The concept also has precedent in institutional economics (Williamson on transaction costs), cooperative governance (Ostrom again), and emerging steward-ownership models (Purpose Foundation, Patagonia).

**What kind of corroboration would count:** (a) Steward-ownership organisations or cooperatives that use commitment-based participation structures analogous to commitment signatures. (b) Institutional economics literature that independently arrives at "commitment under uncertainty" as the unit of economic coordination (rather than transaction or contract). (c) Any organisation operating on something recognisable as "standing" — institutional membership that enables participation without requiring specifiable work. (d) The Purpose Foundation's steward-ownership model as a production case for commitment-based economic governance.

### Rank 5: relational-topology-and-context-design

**Why it matters at corpus level:** The most operationally testable family. The alignment-decay claim (6-9 month half-life) is empirically checkable. Context metabolism (incentive fields, recognition loops, learning architectures determining what agents can perceive) is observable in any organisation. The concept connects to every design decision in the grammar: every protocol, projection, and holon architecture is a relational topology generator.

**Why it is a better corroboration target than nearby alternatives:** The testable claims (alignment decay as measurable parameter, context as upstream determinant of coordination capacity) distinguish it from more philosophical families. Organisational theory (Weick, Mintzberg), STS (Suchman, Orlikowski), and distributed systems research all address how organisational infrastructure shapes relation and perception. The "imaginary identity" concept (emergent sense of "we" produced by relational density patterns) has parallels in social identity theory and organisational behaviour research.

**What kind of corroboration would count:** (a) Organisational research on alignment decay — empirical data on how trust and shared purpose degrade in distributed teams, and at what cadence. (b) STS literature on how organisational infrastructure shapes what actors can perceive (Suchman's situated action, Orlikowski's sociomateriality). (c) Case studies of organisations that consciously designed relational topology (not just org charts) and the outcomes. (d) Research on "context engineering" or "context design" as a named discipline in organisational or systems design.

## 8. Guardrails

1. **Cross-theme recurrence within the Johar corpus is not independent corroboration.** When the same structural pattern appears in complexity, infrastructure, and governance themes, this measures the coherence of one author's thought, not the confirmation of the claim by independent observers. Source counts across themes are measures of conceptual breadth, not epistemic independence.

2. **Promotion still requires non-Johar evidence or production grounding.** No synthesis note should be promoted based solely on high source counts, strong internal convergence, or cross-theme recurrence within the Johar corpus. The promotion gate is non-negotiable: either an independent intellectual tradition arrives at the same structural account, or a production case demonstrates the concept operating in real governance.

3. **Deferred families should not be revived by momentum alone.** The fact that 21 families have been synthesized does not mean the remaining 8 should be. Concept-only families (civic-intelligence, institutional-transition) should be left dormant unless an anchor essay surfaces. READY-but-thin families should be evaluated against the completed corpus picture, not against the assumption that more synthesis is always better.

4. **Corpus-level work should preserve useful boundaries, not flatten them.** The superfamilies identified in this memo are planning hypotheses — tools for organizing corroboration and identifying design implications. They are not canon. Collapsing families into superfamilies for administrative convenience would lose the precision that individual synthesis notes provide. The boundaries flagged in Section 4 are load-bearing: they protect distinctions that matter for grammar design.

5. **The next phase is outward-facing, not inward-facing.** The corpus has been fully processed. Further work within the corpus (more synthesis notes, more backfills, more convergence queries) has diminishing returns. The binding constraint is now external: non-Johar corroboration, production evidence, and operational testing. The work should face outward from this point.
