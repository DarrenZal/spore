## 4. Disposition Decision

**Chosen value: `candidate pattern`**

Why `candidate pattern` fits now:
- The source contributed a recognizable design pattern — mediation over demarcation — that was enacted into the grammar's foundation architecture. A "candidate pattern" is a source that offers a reusable design approach the grammar can absorb, which is exactly what happened: the pattern was absorbed into foundation prose rather than becoming a standalone named concept.

Why `candidate primitive` no longer fits:
- A primitive is a standalone named concept that enters the lexicon as a new term with its own definition and formal status. The enacted outcome is a design principle section within an existing architecture document — it qualifies and informs existing vocabulary (membranes, protocols, interfaces) rather than introducing new vocabulary. The Markov blanket formalism that would have given the primitive its formal content was cut.

How the bridge note's function differs from the comparative note's function:
- The bridge note is **source-specific provenance**. It records what Johar's essay says, how it maps to Spore, what it proposed, and what happened to that proposal. It is the intake record for one external source.
- The comparative note is **convergence evidence**. It records that six independent traditions (none of them Johar) converge on the same design principle. It is the durable evidence base for the enacted foundation section.
- The bridge note initiated the inquiry. The comparative note records what independent evidence confirmed. These are different functions and should remain in different artifacts.
