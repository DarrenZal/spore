## 4. Refined Section 4A

**Exact replacement text for Section "4A. What Is Confirmed":**

### A. What Is Confirmed

- Fixed-contract coordination (wage, transaction) is structurally inadequate when work cannot be specified in advance, supervised in execution, or verified by output. Under conditions of discovery and complexity, governance structure rather than contract terms becomes the necessary basis for coordination. (Hart: incomplete contracts, residual control rights. Williamson: discriminating alignment, bounded rationality + opportunism. Simon: zone of acceptance, authority replacing specification. Three independent traditions converge on the diagnostic.)

- Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing: security, capability, and membership conditions without which formal participation rights are structurally empty. "Commitment without standing is precarity with better vocabulary." (Polanyi: fictitious commodity — labour inseparable from human life. Standing: precariat, seven securities, occupational citizenship. Sen: capabilities as freedom — formal rights without substantive capability are structurally empty. Ostrom: commons membership as participation infrastructure. Four independent traditions converge.)

- The principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of work is well-established. Verification thresholds and resource commitments do vary by epistemic phase in production systems at scale. (March: exploration vs exploitation, 1991. Cooper: stage-gate R&D governance, 1990. Pharmaceutical trial phase governance: Phase I safety vs Phase III efficacy. NASA TRL: different acceptance criteria per level. Williamson: governance-mode selection matching transaction attributes.)

**What changed and why:**

1. **Removed the fourth bullet entirely.** Operational viability (CLC, Mondragon, SMart) is production evidence, not a confirmed structural mechanism. The reviewer limited the confirmed layer to three mechanisms. Production evidence may be referenced in canon as supporting context with its own tag, but it does not belong in "What Is Confirmed."
2. **Replaced "The coordination unit must shift from contract terms to governance structure under uncertainty" with "governance structure rather than contract terms becomes the necessary basis for coordination."** The original prescriptive phrasing ("must shift") implies the replacement is confirmed. The revised phrasing stays diagnostic: it says what is structurally necessary without prescribing commitment-based primitives specifically.
3. **Removed "renegotiation structures" from the third bullet.** "Renegotiation structures" edges toward the design-contribution layer (governed renegotiation is Johar's formulation). The confirmed principle is that verification thresholds and resource commitments vary by epistemic phase. Renegotiation as a named governance feature is Johar's extension.

## 5. Refined Draft Canon Entry Language

> Economic coordination under discovery and complexity cannot rely on wage or transaction grammar because these forms depend on specifiability, containment, and measurability assumptions that discovery structurally violates. Under such conditions, governance structure rather than contract terms becomes the necessary basis for coordination. Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing: security, capability, and membership conditions without which formal participation rights are structurally empty. **What changed and why:**

4. **Removed "renegotiation structures" from the broad-replacement-principle sentence.** Keeps the confirmed principle clean.

## 6. Keep / Cut Guidance

### Keep

- "structurally inadequate when work cannot be specified in advance, supervised in execution, or verified by output" — accurate diagnostic, well-sourced
- "governance structure rather than contract terms becomes the necessary basis for coordination" — diagnostic framing without prescriptive overclaim
- "standing: security, capability, and membership conditions" — confirmed by four independent traditions
- "commitment without standing is precarity with better vocabulary" — accurate summary of the standing claim
- "the principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of work is well-established" — confirmed by March, stage-gate, pharma
- "Johar contributes the specific formulation of commitment-type governance" — correctly attributes design contribution
- "has no external analog after two corroboration passes" — honest qualification of the taxonomy
- "enters as a design hypothesis" — correct classification
- "explanatory design context, not confirming evidence" — correct framing of the Spore parallel
- "Johar's engineering on confirmed components" — precise description of what the taxonomy is

### Cut Or Avoid

- **"operationally viable"** — do not use in the confirmed-mechanism layer or in the canon entry. Operational viability is a production-evidence claim, not a structural-mechanism claim. It belongs in supporting material, not in canon-facing language.
- **"commitment-based coordination is operationally viable at scale"** — this sentence in any form must not appear in the confirmed-mechanism block. It recodes production evidence as mechanism confirmation.
- **"the coordination unit must shift"** — prescriptive overclaim. The diagnostic establishes what fails; it does not confirm what replaces it. Use diagnostic phrasing only.
- **"standing as participation infrastructure"** — acceptable as a description of what standing does, but must not be used to carry the taxonomy into canon. Standing is confirmed; the composition of standing with differentiated commitment types is not.
- **"CLC" in the confirmed-mechanism tag or block** — CLC is operational precedent and design lineage. It must not appear inside `[mechanism confirmed]` or in the same sentence as confirmed mechanisms without explicit separation.
- **"Spore grammar parallel" as supporting evidence** — the parallel is design context. Any phrasing that positions it as supporting evidence for the confirmed layer must be avoided.
- **"demonstrated by" before CLC/Mondragon in the confirmed-mechanism block** — "demonstrated by" signals confirmation. If production cases appear at all (not recommended for canon entry), use "production evidence exists in" or "operational precedent includes" — language that signals a different evidentiary category.
- **"renegotiation structures" in the confirmed-principle sentence** — edges into design-contribution territory. The confirmed principle is about verification thresholds and resource commitments varying by epistemic phase.

## 7. Refined Canon Boundary

1. Fixed-contract coordination (wage, transaction) is structurally inadequate under discovery and complexity because specifiability, containment, and measurability assumptions fail. Governance structure rather than contract terms becomes necessary.
2. Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing — security, capability, and membership conditions — without which formal participation rights are structurally empty.

- Standing-plus-differentiation composition — design hypothesis, no production demonstration.
- Governed renegotiation as a named commitment-governance feature — design contribution (Johar's formulation of a confirmed principle).

**Why this boundary is stable enough for later enactment drafting:**

The boundary follows the reviewer's explicit qualification exactly. Each excluded item has a clear reason for exclusion that does not depend on judgment calls about wording. The three confirmed mechanisms are each supported by multiple independent academic traditions with no lineage relationship to Spore. Nothing on the "outside" list can migrate into the confirmed layer through paraphrase, because the exclusion reasons are categorical (production evidence ≠ mechanism; design hypothesis ≠ confirmed; design lineage ≠ independence), not matters of degree.

## 10. Guardrails

- **Reviewer qualification is binding.** The `approve_with_explicit_qualification` disposition defines what may enter canon. The qualification terms are not suggestions — they are constraints. The enactment-drafting run must treat this memo's Keep/Cut lists as binding, not advisory.

- **Wording discipline matters as much as evidence discipline here.** The evidence base has not changed since the review. What has changed is the precision of the language that carries the evidence into canon. A sentence that is technically accurate but invites misreading is not safe for canon. The standard is: can a future reader reconstruct the correct evidentiary layer from the canon text alone, without access to the review packet?

- **Operational precedent must not be recoded as mechanism confirmation.** CLC demonstrates that commitment-based coordination works at production scale. This is important. It is not a confirmed structural mechanism. The distinction between "this works in practice" (operational precedent) and "this is why it works" (confirmed mechanism) must be preserved in all downstream drafting.

- **Hypotheses must remain visibly hypotheses.** The four-type taxonomy, the standing-plus-differentiation composition, and the discovery-specific intensification are design hypotheses. They are plausible, they are built on confirmed components, and they are Johar's distinctive contribution. None of that makes them confirmed. The tags must stay.

- **Later enactment should be narrower than the synthesis note, not broader.** The synthesis note is the full research record — it contains the convergence account, the claim register, the open questions, the Spore parallel. The canon entry carries only the confirmed-mechanism layer with design contribution and hypothesis explicitly tagged. If the enactment draft is longer or more assertive than the refined Section 4D in this memo, something has gone wrong.
