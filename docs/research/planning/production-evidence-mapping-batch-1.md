# Production Evidence Mapping Batch 1

## 1. Scope And Rule

This memo maps production evidence for the 5 promoted Johar-derived canon entries:

1. legitimacy-and-shared-consequence
2. linguistic-closure
3. relational-topology-and-context-design
4. human-economy-and-commitment
5. holding-complexity-beyond-control (diagnostic slice only)

This is not a promotion or enactment run. No `promotion_status` values change. No canon docs are edited. No synthesis notes are rewritten.

Enacted canon language remains the upper bound of what is currently confirmed. Each entry's canon text was approved through human review with explicit qualification, and the enacted boundaries were preserved in the enactment memos. Johar-specific remainder (design contribution, design hypothesis, Johar-distinctive integration) must stay visibly qualified unless operational evidence later strengthens it. This memo does not retroactively justify remainder — it identifies what evidence would look like if it were gathered.

## 2. Evidence Framework

Five evidence categories, applied per entry:

- **Already observable in repo / process** — Evidence that exists now in the Spore repo, in adjacent operational instances (BKC, koi-processor, Octo), or in documented project practice. Can be pointed at today without new instrumentation.

- **Observable with routine operational practice** — Evidence that would naturally appear if the grammar's mechanisms were exercised at even modest operational scale. Does not require new tooling — just running the protocols and observing what happens.

- **Requires explicit instrumentation** — Evidence that would need deliberate measurement, logging, or structured observation not currently built. The signal exists in principle but the sensing layer does not.

- **Long-horizon / difficult to validate** — Evidence that depends on extended time, institutional maturation, or conditions not yet present. Cannot be gathered in a near-term observation window regardless of instrumentation.

- **Possible disconfirming signal** — Observable conditions that would weaken or contradict the canon-safe core claim. Not hypothetical worst cases but plausible failure modes that the canon entry's structural account predicts should not occur if the claim is correct.

## 4. Per-Entry Mapping

### 4.1 Legitimacy-and-Shared-Consequence

#### A. Canon-Safe Core
- Authority and consequence must be structurally coupled for coordination infrastructure to be viable
- This coupling must be polycentric — no single legitimacy source suffices
- When authority separates from consequence, self-reinforcing degradation occurs (consequence-bearing participants lose voice or exit)
- The grammar's commitment protocols, contestability, and governance-from-artifacts are structural approaches to maintaining this coupling

#### B. Johar Remainder To Test
- The 4-dimensional legitimacy taxonomy (democracy, stewardship, experimentation, radical markets) as specific structural decomposition
- "Orchestration over dominance" as the named coordination mode that replaces singular authority
- Civic economy as a third pole that interrupts the control-centralisation spiral
- The specific degradation mechanism: insulation of decision-makers from effects

#### C. What Would Count As Production Evidence
- A governance decision where consequence-bearing was traced: who decided, who was affected, and whether affected parties had voice
- A case where authority-consequence decoupling was detected and corrected through governance mechanisms (contestability invoked, decision revisited)
- A case where polycentric legitimacy sources visibly balanced: e.g., a commitment pool decision where ecological, democratic, and stewardship considerations were each legible in the decision record
- Evidence that governance-from-artifacts (rather than informal authority) was the actual decision medium in a contested case
- A comparison between decisions made with consequence-bearing participants present vs absent, showing quality or legitimacy differences

#### D. What Would Count As Disconfirmation Or Warning
- Governance decisions where consequence-bearing participants had formal voice but were structurally ineffective (coupling exists on paper but not in practice)
- A case where polycentric legitimacy produced gridlock or incoherence rather than balance — multiple legitimacy sources blocking rather than counterbalancing
- Authority-consequence decoupling that did not produce the predicted degradation spiral — stable governance despite decoupling
- Commitment protocols functioning as compliance instruments rather than consequence-coupling mechanisms

#### E. Evidence Hooks
- **Commitment pooling records** — BKC pools track who committed what; the commitment lifecycle (PROPOSED -> VERIFIED -> ACTIVE -> EVIDENCE_LINKED -> REDEEMED) could be annotated with consequence-bearing metadata
- **Discourse / decision records** — BKC decision logs could trace which stakeholders bore consequences for which decisions
- **Governance-from-artifacts** — the spec-DAG and governance memory pattern already create durable decision records; consequence flow could be layered onto these
- **Contestability traces** — any invocation of contestability (claim challenged, decision reviewed) is evidence of the coupling mechanism operating
- **Federation governance** — cross-node decisions in the mycorrhizal federation would test polycentric legitimacy at scale

#### F. Validation Difficulty
**Medium** — The structural mechanisms exist in the grammar's design (commitment protocols, contestability, governance memory). BKC has operational commitment pools with real decisions. The gap is not that evidence is impossible but that consequence flow has never been explicitly traced. Adding consequence-bearing metadata to existing governance records is feasible. The harder part is generating enough contested governance decisions to observe the degradation pattern.

### 4.2 Linguistic-Closure

#### A. Canon-Safe Core
- Categories retain operational clarity while losing contact with relational reality
- This is structural (institutional), not cognitive (individual bias)
- The stopping-rule mechanism: naming compresses, compression feels sufficient, sufficiency terminates inquiry
- The corrective must be institutional — revisability, error-finding incentives, recursive inquiry

#### B. Johar Remainder To Test
- Naming as the upstream act that produces the closure-triggering category (precision-addition beyond what independent traditions reach)
- Whether the claim lifecycle effectively resists closure in practice (currently a design response, not proven infrastructure)
- Whether the discourse graph structures reasoning that actually reopens settled terms
- Whether attestation decay prevents old endorsements from accumulating false authority
- LLM amplification of closure risk (excluded from canon entirely)

#### C. What Would Count As Production Evidence
- An instance where a Spore term (holon, membrane, intent pressure, linguistic closure itself) was being used as a stopping rule, and the claim lifecycle or discourse graph surfaced the problem
- A claim that was superseded through the claim lifecycle — not just updated, but superseded because the original category had lost contact with what it described
- A governance discussion where attestation decay forced re-evaluation of a previously settled position
- Evidence that the learning membrane's staged promotion gates caught a case where convergence masked closure (convergent sources sharing a common discursive origin)
- A comparison between revision rates in governance artifacts vs what would be expected if closure were operating unchecked

#### D. What Would Count As Disconfirmation Or Warning
- The claim lifecycle operating as a versioning system without ever actually reopening inquiry — claims superseded for administrative reasons, not epistemic ones
- Attestation decay treated as a nuisance to be worked around rather than a revisability mechanism
- Discourse graph entries that are formally structured but substantively performative — structured reasoning that does not change outcomes
- A case where closure was detected but the grammar's mechanisms were insufficient to correct it (the corrective failed)
- Settled Spore vocabulary persisting unchanged for years despite changing operational reality

#### E. Evidence Hooks
- **Claim lifecycle traces** — the claims-evidence-attestation protocol already defines claim states (proposed, attested, superseded); operational instances would generate traces
- **Discourse graph** — discourse-as-governance pattern already defines proposals, arguments, evidence, decisions; operational use would produce revision records
- **Learning membrane workflow** — the comparative intake process (bridge notes, dispositions, staged promotion) is itself a closure-resistance mechanism; its operation generates evidence
- **Attestation decay** — not yet instrumented, but the design exists; any implementation would produce decay-triggered re-evaluation events
- **Governance memory** — the spec-DAG pattern preserves decision provenance; revision frequency and depth could be measured

#### F. Validation Difficulty
**High** — The claim is about what categories prevent people from noticing. Detecting closure requires observing something that, by definition, was not being observed. The grammar's mechanisms (claim lifecycle, discourse graph) exist as designs but have limited operational instances. The strongest near-term evidence would come from the Johar corpus program itself — the learning membrane process has already exercised staged promotion, boundary enforcement, and disposition assignment. But using the research workflow as evidence of its own effectiveness creates a circularity risk. Independent operational instances (BKC governance, federation decisions) would provide stronger evidence but require the discourse graph to be operationally active.

### 4.3 Relational-Topology-and-Context-Design

#### A. Canon-Safe Core
- Protocols, projections, and holon architecture actively generate patterns of relation, identity, perception, and power
- Context variables predict coordination quality more strongly than composition variables
- Structural decay of relational grounding is the default without active maintenance
- Designing these conditions is as important as formal governance rules

#### B. Johar Remainder To Test
- "Topology IS the real infrastructure" as an integrative thesis (excluded from canon — canon says "as important as," not "is")
- "Imaginary identity" as independently corroborated concept (excluded from canon)
- Context design as a named discipline with its own methodology
- 6-9 month alignment half-life as a specific decay parameter
- Context metabolism as a concept (incentive fields, recognition loops, learning architectures)

#### C. What Would Count As Production Evidence
- A case where changing a protocol or governance arrangement (context change) produced a measurable change in coordination quality — without changing who was involved (composition held constant)
- A case where the same participants in different Spore arrangements (e.g., different holon configurations, different pool structures) produced visibly different coordination outcomes
- Observable decay in coordination quality over time in the absence of active relational maintenance — and improvement when maintenance was restored
- Evidence that multi-projection (constitutional, commitment, discourse graphs) surfaced relational patterns that were invisible in any single projection
- A comparison between formal governance effectiveness and informal relational topology effects on actual decisions

#### D. What Would Count As Disconfirmation Or Warning
- Changing context/protocol without changing coordination quality — topology changes that made no difference
- Composition changes (adding/removing specific people) consistently outpredicting context changes in coordination outcomes
- Relational grounding persisting indefinitely without active maintenance — no observable decay
- Multi-projection revealing no additional relational insight beyond what a single governance view provided
- Context design interventions producing manipulation concerns rather than coordination improvement

#### E. Evidence Hooks
- **BKC pool federation** — multiple pools with overlapping participants but different governance arrangements; natural comparison surface
- **Holon architecture** — different holon configurations (team, organization, federation) create different relational topologies; comparing coordination quality across configurations
- **Multi-projection** — the 8 graph projections are designed to surface different relational structures; operational use would test whether they reveal patterns invisible in single views
- **Federation protocol** — cross-node exchange creates relational topology across boundaries; the mycorrhizal federation protocol governs these
- **Koi-processor entity resolution** — entity relationships in the knowledge graph are a machine-readable trace of relational topology
- **Meeting and decision patterns** — if operational instances track who participates, how decisions flow, and what communication patterns emerge, these are relational topology data

#### F. Validation Difficulty
**Medium** — BKC's pool federation provides a natural comparison surface: multiple pools with overlapping participants but different governance structures. The challenge is isolating context effects from composition effects — real-world coordination involves both changing simultaneously. The most tractable near-term test would be observing whether protocol changes (e.g., adding a new governance pattern, changing a decision process) produce coordination quality changes without changing participants. Decay observation requires longitudinal tracking.

### 4.4 Human-Economy-and-Commitment

#### A. Canon-Safe Core
- Wage and transaction grammar fails under discovery because specifiability, containment, and measurability assumptions break
- Under such conditions, governance structure rather than contract terms becomes necessary
- Participation requires standing: security, capability, and membership conditions
- Governance should vary by the epistemic character of work (well-established in production systems)

#### B. Johar Remainder To Test
- The 4-type commitment signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) as a universal decomposition
- Standing as a named primitive in the grammar (vs contextual infrastructure the grammar assumes but does not model)
- Standing-plus-differentiation composition as a design pattern
- Governed renegotiation as a named feature of commitment protocols
- The claim that Spore's grammar is already implicitly a commitment-economy grammar

#### C. What Would Count As Production Evidence
- A commitment pool where discovery-type commitments were governed differently from delivery-type commitments — and where the differentiation improved coordination outcomes
- A case where standing conditions (security, capability, membership) were absent and commitment-based participation was structurally empty as predicted — formal rights without effective participation
- Evidence that governed renegotiation (scope change under uncertainty) occurred and was auditable through the commitment lifecycle
- A comparison between contract-governed and commitment-governed coordination under genuine discovery conditions, showing the predicted structural mismatch in the contract case
- Evidence that verification thresholds varied by epistemic phase in a Spore-adjacent operational instance
- CLC/Grassroots Economics operational data showing commitment-based coordination functioning at production scale (this exists but has not been formally evaluated against the canon claims)

#### D. What Would Count As Disconfirmation Or Warning
- Commitment-based coordination failing under discovery for reasons the canon account does not predict — the problem was not wage/transaction grammar but something else entirely
- Standing conditions being present but failing to enable effective participation — standing necessary but not sufficient, and the gap is large
- Governed renegotiation producing scope creep indistinguishable from planning failure — the governance structure adding overhead without coordination benefit
- The 4-type taxonomy failing to capture real commitment types encountered in operational practice — important commitments that do not fit any of the four types
- Contract-based coordination performing adequately under discovery conditions, contrary to the structural mismatch prediction

#### E. Evidence Hooks
- **BKC commitment pools** — 23+ commitments tracked with lifecycle states; the most concrete operational instance of commitment-based coordination in the Spore ecosystem
- **CLC / Grassroots Economics** — the commitment voucher framework is a production-scale implementation of related primitives; documented in the Inspirations section and the commitment-pooling pattern
- **Commitment lifecycle** — PROPOSED -> VERIFIED -> ACTIVE -> EVIDENCE_LINKED -> REDEEMED already tracks commitment maturation; adding commitment-type metadata is feasible
- **Koi-processor task registry** — the task management system tracks tasks with status, owner, and project; could be extended with epistemic-phase metadata
- **Standing indicators** — membership, capability, and participation conditions are implicit in holon membership; making them explicit and tracking their presence/absence would test the standing claim

#### F. Validation Difficulty
**Medium** — Commitment pooling is already operational in BKC. CLC provides a larger-scale parallel. The main gaps are: (1) commitment-type differentiation has not been tried, (2) standing conditions are implicit rather than explicit, and (3) no systematic comparison between commitment-governed and contract-governed coordination under discovery conditions exists. The CLC comparison is the most tractable near-term validation target because it involves real economic coordination at scale, though it tests related but not identical claims.

### 4.5 Holding-Complexity-Beyond-Control (Diagnostic Slice)

#### A. Canon-Safe Core
- Control-based coordination fails under manifest complexity (provable structural condition: regulator variety < system variety)
- Authoritarian compression is one empirically documented default response (narrows degrees of freedom, destroys adaptive capacity)
- Chaotic decomposition is the other default (loses higher-order coordination, reorganises at lower complexity)
- Adaptive coordination — iterative sensing and response rather than enforcing predefined outcomes — is the independently confirmed general response requirement

#### B. Johar Remainder To Test
- Deliberate institutional construction as a required third path (design contribution, not confirmed mechanism)
- The five-capacity architecture (adaptive coordination, complexity-handling capacity, scaffolded relationality, interdependent sovereignty, anti-fragility through learning)
- Civilisational optionality as evaluative criterion (does the orientation widen or narrow legitimate response space)
- The reflexive-constraint clause (must be built under conditions that actively constrain its construction)
- The fork/basin-competition framing as integrated whole

#### C. What Would Count As Production Evidence
- A Spore institution facing complexity pressure and responding with adaptive coordination rather than compression or decomposition — observable through the coordination loop (sense -> propose -> commit -> learn)
- An observable compression event: a governance body narrowing participation, simplifying categories, or constraining variation under pressure — and whether the grammar's contestability mechanisms resisted it
- An observable decomposition event: coordination structures losing effectiveness, participants fragmenting into isolated clusters — and whether federation mechanisms maintained higher-order coherence
- Evidence that the coordination ecology's loop actually functions iteratively under stress — learning revising visions, evidence generating new intents — rather than stalling
- A comparison between Spore-governed and non-Spore-governed responses to similar complexity pressures

#### D. What Would Count As Disconfirmation Or Warning
- A Spore institution under complexity pressure collapsing into compression or decomposition despite the grammar's mechanisms — the grammar failing to provide the predicted adaptive capacity
- Adaptive coordination loops producing churn without learning — sensing and responding without improving coordination quality
- The grammar's own mechanisms producing compression (e.g., the spec-DAG becoming rigidly hierarchical, governance memory becoming a compliance archive rather than a living system)
- Complexity pressure not producing either attractor — stable institutions under complexity without adaptive coordination, contradicting the structural necessity claim
- Contestability mechanisms being invoked so frequently under stress that they destabilise rather than stabilise

#### E. Evidence Hooks
- **Coordination ecology lifecycle** — Vision -> Roadmap -> Intent -> Commitment -> Evidence -> Learning is the primary adaptive coordination loop; its operation under stress would test the diagnostic
- **Governance memory / spec-DAG** — the governance memory pattern preserves decision history; whether this memory enables adaptive revision or calcifies into compliance is an observable
- **Federation protocol** — cross-node coordination under the mycorrhizal federation protocol tests whether higher-order coherence is maintained across sovereign boundaries
- **Contestability invocations** — any use of contestability (claims questioned, decisions reviewed) under pressure is evidence of adaptive capacity
- **Forkability** — whether forking is exercised as a legitimate response to irreconcilable complexity (preserving optionality) rather than as failure
- **BKC operational history** — BKC as the most mature operational instance; its governance decisions under resource pressure or coordination challenges are the nearest available complexity-pressure data

#### F. Validation Difficulty
**Very high** — The diagnostic describes civilisational-scale structural conditions. Observing compression and decomposition attractors requires institutions under genuine complexity pressure, which Spore's operational instances have not yet experienced at sufficient scale. The coordination ecology loop has been designed but not stress-tested. Near-term evidence is limited to small-scale observations (BKC pool governance under disagreement, federation protocol under divergent node interests). Full validation requires institutional maturation and real pressure — which cannot be manufactured.

### Target 2: Discourse Revision Depth Tracking

**What to observe:** In BKC decision logs and Spore governance artifacts, track not just what was decided but whether revision genuinely reopened inquiry. Distinguish administrative updates from substantive re-examination. **Current status:** Decision logs exist in BKC. The discourse-as-governance pattern is documented. Revision depth is not currently tracked. Observable with routine operational practice.

### Target 3: Context-Change Coordination Comparison in BKC Pools

**What to observe:** When BKC pools change governance arrangements (new decision processes, new routing rules, new participation structures), track whether coordination outcomes change. Compare cases where context changed (protocol, structure) vs cases where composition changed (different participants) vs cases where both changed.

**Why high leverage:** Tests the relational-topology entry's strongest claim (context outpredicts composition). BKC pools provide a natural comparison surface with overlapping participants across different governance configurations. If context changes consistently produce coordination quality changes independent of composition, this is direct production evidence for the canon claim. If composition changes dominate, this is a disconfirming signal.

**Current status:** BKC has multiple pools with different governance arrangements. No systematic comparison exists. Requires explicit instrumentation (defining and measuring "coordination quality" across configurations). Observable with deliberate instrumentation, not routine practice.

## 9. Guardrails

- Promoted canon entries are still only partly operationally validated. Literature corroboration and human review confirm the theoretical grounding. Production evidence — whether the mechanisms actually operate as described in real coordination — has not been gathered for any entry.

- Production evidence is not the same as theoretical corroboration. Corroboration establishes that independent academic traditions converge on the structural claim. Production evidence establishes that the claim holds when the grammar's mechanisms are exercised in real conditions. These are different kinds of support and should not be conflated.

- Absence of instrumentation is not disconfirmation, but it is a real limit. The grammar's mechanisms may function exactly as described, but without observation, this cannot be claimed. The purpose of this memo is to make validation concrete — not to declare the entries unvalidated.

- Johar remainder should not be upgraded without observed evidence. Design contributions, design hypotheses, and Johar-distinctive integration entered the synthesis notes with explicit evidentiary tags. Those tags remain binding. If production evidence later strengthens a specific remainder claim, it should go through the same decomposition and review process that the confirmed-mechanism layer went through.

- The point is to make validation concrete, not to retroactively justify everything. This memo exists so that future evidence-gathering has clear targets, clear categories, and clear boundaries. It is not an argument that the promoted entries will be validated — it is a specification of what validation would look like.
