# Deferred Family Triage Checkpoint 1

## 1. Scope And Purpose

This memo reassesses the 8 deferred families after the first canon enactments (legitimacy-and-shared-consequence, linguistic-closure, relational-topology-and-context-design) and the completed second pass on human-economy-and-commitment.

This is a triage checkpoint, not a synthesis or promotion run. No synthesis notes will be written. No promotion statuses will change. No bridge notes, claim sheets, or graph projections will be created.

The goal is prioritization, dormancy, and structural clarity:
- Which deferred families still represent distinct, strategically important territory for Spore?
- Which have lost distinctiveness because promoted or near-promoted families now cover their ground?
- Which should remain dormant, and which are worth active pursuit?

**What has changed since these families were originally deferred:**
- 3 families promoted into canon with explicit mechanism/contribution/hypothesis tagging
- `holding-complexity-beyond-control` corroborated (diagnostic very strong, five-capacity architecture Johar-specific, modified success condition)
- `human-economy-and-commitment` second pass complete (four-type taxonomy still Johar-specific, CLC = operational precedent + design lineage, gap narrowed but significant)
- The tagging format (mechanism confirmed / design contribution / design hypothesis) is now established and tested
- The evidentiary standard for promotion is concrete, not abstract

## 3. Per-Family Notes

### 3.1 Conversational Sovereignty

**What still seems distinct:** The entry/exit rights dimension — the protected right to enter and exit good-faith dialogue, not just to participate. This is a sovereignty claim about the conditions of discourse, not about discourse quality.

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** Almost everything else. Civic-truth's synthesis covers the epistemic commons, doubt as infrastructure, and conversational protocols. Linguistic-closure's promoted canon entry covers the structural mechanism by which categories terminate inquiry. The "conversational" dimension is now addressed by two different promoted or synthesized families. The sovereignty frame alone is too thin.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** A non-Johar source that independently names conversational sovereignty as a governance primitive distinct from both epistemic commons design and free-speech protection. This would need to be about the structural conditions for dialogue, not about rights language.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** Marginally. The grammar's discourse graph and claim lifecycle already embody conversational infrastructure. The entry/exit frame could matter for membrane design (who can participate in which discourse spaces) but this is better addressed as a design parameter within the existing promoted families than as a standalone concept.

### 3.2 Informational Duty

**What still seems distinct:** The specific obligation frame — "the duty not to pollute" the epistemic commons as a boundary-enforcement mechanism. This directly addresses civic-truth's unresolved Open Question 3 (who governs the boundaries of the epistemic commons without recreating monopoly?). The concept bridges linguistic-closure (what goes wrong) and civic-truth (what the commons needs) with a specific governance obligation.

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** The general territory of epistemic governance is well-covered by linguistic-closure (mechanism) and civic-truth (commons condition). What informational duty adds is narrow: the obligation architecture, not the epistemic diagnosis.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** A non-Johar governance or legal tradition that names information duty as a structural governance primitive — not just free-speech theory or information ethics, but a governance obligation framework where the duty not to degrade shared epistemic conditions is treated as load-bearing. Environmental law's "duty not to pollute" applied to information commons is the most promising analog but would need to be more than metaphor.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** Yes, narrowly. If Spore's grammar eventually needs to specify boundary-enforcement mechanisms for its discourse graph and claim lifecycle, the duty frame could become structurally important. But this is later-stage work — the grammar needs to be operational before boundary-enforcement design becomes relevant.

### 3.3 Civic Intelligence

**What still seems distinct:** Nothing that the existing families don't already cover. "Civic intelligence" is a label for a capacity that holding-complexity (institutional capacity for complexity), governance-under-volatility (governance under volatile conditions), and human-machine-complementarity (collective intelligence design) all address from more precise angles.

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** Everything. The concept was already thin at deferral time (concept-only, no anchor essay). The three promoted canon entries and the two near-promoted families now cover every direction this concept could have gone.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** An anchor essay that names a specific capacity or mechanism not covered by the existing families. No such essay was identified in the full 228-essay corpus.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** No. The grammar's design is better served by the precise families (complexity-holding, volatility governance, human-machine complementarity) than by a broad label.

### 3.4 Institutional Transition

**What still seems distinct:** Nothing specific. "Transition" is a meta-pattern that every theme addresses: from control to coherence (governance), from permanence to adaptiveness (infrastructure), from compliance to emergence (human development), from wage to commitment (economy). Each of these transitions is better described by the family that names it.

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** Everything. The concept was already flagged as "too generic" at deferral time. The promoted canon entries (which describe specific structural conditions and mechanisms) make the generic transition frame even less useful.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** An anchor essay naming a specific transition mechanism or institutional form that isn't already covered by post-permanence, governance-under-volatility, or consciousness-sovereignty. None identified.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** No. The grammar describes specific transitions through specific families. A generic transition concept adds nothing.

### 3.5 Money, Capital, and Future Claims

**What still seems distinct:** The capital-as-future-claim framing — that capital is not stored value but a structured claim on future productive capacity, and that this reframing changes how governance should treat capital accumulation. There is genuine economic theory precedent (Keynes on animal spirits, Graeber on debt-as-social-relation, Minsky on financial instability).

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** Significantly weakened. Human-economy's strengthened replacement-mechanism picture (especially with CLC providing production evidence for commitment-based coordination at scale) absorbs much of the territory. Priceability covers the visibility-regime dimension (what the system world can perceive about value). Pathways-prophecy covers the futures-design dimension (how institutions relate to future claims). The family had only 2 Johar sources and no synthesis note — the thinnest base of any READY family.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** Either (a) a synthesis that demonstrates capital-as-future-claim is genuinely distinct from human-economy's commitment-as-coordination-unit, priceability's visibility-regime, and pathways-prophecy's futures-design, or (b) production evidence from commitment-based finance that operationalizes the capital-as-future-claim frame in ways the existing families cannot describe. The Keynes/Graeber/Minsky precedent is real but may be better treated as external corroboration for the existing economy families than as justification for a separate family.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** Not as a separate family. The grammar's commitment lifecycle already treats commitments as future-oriented claims. If capital-as-future-claim becomes relevant, it is more likely to enter as a refinement of human-economy or priceability than as an independent concept.

### 3.6 Extractive Lock-In

**What still seems distinct:** The specific downstream pathology — the five-dimensional coupling (industrial, labour, fiscal, asset, pension) that makes extraction self-reinforcing. This is the pathology side of what priceability and market-legibility diagnose from the grammar side.

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** Almost entirely absorbed. Priceability's synthesis explicitly names extractive-lock-in as the downstream consequence of what priceability excludes. Without an anchor essay naming the specific lock-in mechanisms, this remains a label for a downstream effect, not a concept with its own structural account.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** An anchor essay (Johar or otherwise) that describes the specific lock-in mechanisms — the five-dimensional coupling — with enough structural precision to distinguish it from "extraction is bad." This is a mechanism question: how does extraction become self-reinforcing? If that mechanism is articulated, the family could justify a separate synthesis. Without it, the concept is adequately addressed by priceability's downstream pathology analysis.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** Only as a pathology reference, not as a design primitive. The grammar is designed to resist extraction through its commitment and provenance mechanisms. Understanding the specific lock-in mechanisms that make extraction self-reinforcing could inform defensive design, but this is later-stage diagnostic work, not canon-level architecture.

### 3.7 Spatial Service and Civic Infrastructure

**What still seems distinct:** The service-provisioning dimension — how civic infrastructure is provisioned in space, not just how space shapes relations. The prior triage correctly identified this as a narrow remainder after relational-topology took the spatial-relational territory.

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** Significantly weakened by the promotion of relational-topology into canon. The enacted paragraph explicitly addresses "patterning of connection, distance, and encounter as a primary design variable" — which is the core spatial claim. The remaining service-provisioning dimension (how public services are designed and delivered in spatial contexts) is a policy question, not a structural grammar concept.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** A source demonstrating that spatial service provisioning is a governance primitive distinct from relational topology — that the provisioning of services in space involves structural mechanisms not captured by context design, connection patterning, or encounter architecture. The overlap is substantial enough that this is unlikely.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** No. The grammar's relational-topology canon entry now covers the structural dimension. Spatial service provisioning is an application context, not a grammar primitive.

### 3.8 Boundary-Making and Entangled Systems

**What still seems distinct:** The formal vocabulary for describing system architecture using Markov blankets as a replacement for traditional boundary language. This is technically sharp: boundaries as differential pass-through structures rather than separating walls, conditional-independence gradients as the formalism for describing what separates and what connects. No other synthesized or promoted family addresses this.

**What now seems absorbed or weakened:** Very little. Holding-complexity covers the overarching diagnosis (complexity exceeds control) but does not address boundary formalism. Entangled-freedom covers the human-developmental side (freedom as participation within relation) but not the systems-formalism side. Relational-topology covers patterning of connection but does not propose a formal boundary vocabulary. This family's territory remains genuinely unoccupied.

**What kind of evidence would be needed to justify revival:** Additional sources — either Johar sources from the unprocessed portions of the corpus that address boundary formalism, or non-Johar sources from complexity science, theoretical biology, or computational neuroscience that independently arrive at Markov blankets as coordination vocabulary (not just as a modeling tool). Karl Friston's free energy principle and active inference framework use Markov blankets as a foundational concept. The question is whether this usage translates to governance/coordination vocabulary, not just neuroscience formalism.

**Whether it still matters for Spore specifically:** Yes. The grammar currently lacks a formal vocabulary for describing its own membrane and boundary architecture. Holons have membranes, but "membrane" is described functionally, not formally. If the grammar needs to specify how information passes through boundaries (what is filtered, what is transparent, what is conditional), boundary-making provides the formal language. This is the only deferred family that targets spore.lexicon as its canonical home — it proposes a new primitive, not a refinement of an existing pattern.

## 4. Cross-Family Effects Of The New Canon

### What the promoted entries changed

The three canon promotions fundamentally altered the landscape for the deferred families, but not uniformly.

**Legitimacy-and-shared-consequence absorbed governance-adjacent deferrals.** The promoted canon entry covers authority-consequence coupling, polycentric multi-dimensionality, and self-reinforcing degradation. This directly absorbs the structural territory that `conversational-sovereignty` and `institutional-transition` were occupying from different angles. Conversational sovereignty's claim that dialogue requires protected conditions is now addressed by the legitimacy entry's assertion that "those who shape decisions are bound into the outcomes those decisions produce" — the protection comes from structural coupling, not from a separate sovereignty concept. Institutional transition's generic "from what to what" is now concretely answered by the legitimacy entry's specific degradation mechanism.

**Linguistic-closure absorbed epistemic-governance territory.** The promoted lexicon entry covers the structural stopping-rule mechanism, its institutional corrective, and the grammar's own closure risk. This directly weakens `conversational-sovereignty` (the epistemic dimension is now covered) and `civic-intelligence` (the epistemic capacity dimension is now covered by the institutional corrective). It also narrows `informational-duty`: the duty concept can no longer be about "the need for epistemic governance" (that's what linguistic-closure is for) and must instead be specifically about the obligation architecture — who bears what duty in maintaining epistemic commons quality.

**Relational-topology absorbed spatial-infrastructure territory.** The promoted canon entry explicitly addresses "patterning of connection, distance, and encounter as a primary design variable." This directly absorbs `spatial-service-and-civic-infrastructure`. The spatial dimension of governance is now covered by the promoted entry. What spatial-service might have added (service provisioning) is a policy application, not a grammar primitive.

### How the near-promoted families changed the deferrals

**Human-economy's strengthened picture weakened the economy deferrals.** The second pass established CLC as operational precedent (26K users, commitment-based coordination at production scale) and sharpened the boundary between the confirmed principle ("commitments replace transactions under uncertainty") and the Johar-specific taxonomy (the four types). This absorbs much of what `money-capital-and-future-claims` was trying to name: commitments as future-oriented economic primitives. It also further weakens `extractive-lock-in`, which was already identified as downstream of priceability — with human-economy now providing a stronger replacement-mechanism picture, the pathology side (lock-in) becomes even more of a diagnostic footnote than a standalone concept.

**Holding-complexity's strong diagnostic changed the complexity deferral landscape.** The corroborated diagnostic (Ashby's theorem + Tainter's historical cases + Scott's compression mechanism + Beer's institutional variety reduction) now provides the overarching "why" that `civic-intelligence` and `institutional-transition` were trying to name from narrower angles. But holding-complexity does not address boundary formalism — which is why `boundary-making-and-entangled-systems` remains genuinely distinct. The diagnostic tells us complexity exceeds control; boundary-making would tell us how to formally describe the interfaces between the systems that must hold complexity together.

### Net effect

The promoted canon entries and the near-promoted families together reduced the live deferred territory from 8 families to at most 2 families with distinct remaining claims:
- `informational-duty` (narrow: the obligation architecture for epistemic commons boundary enforcement)
- `boundary-making-and-entangled-systems` (narrow: formal boundary vocabulary for the grammar's membrane architecture)

The other 6 families have either been absorbed, were already dormant, or have lost their distinctiveness.

## 5. Survivors

### 5.1 Boundary-Making and Entangled Systems

**Why it remains distinct:** It is the only family — deferred or otherwise — that proposes a formal vocabulary for the grammar's boundary and membrane architecture. Markov blankets as conditional-independence gradients (not separating walls) would give the grammar a way to formally describe what passes through membranes, what is filtered, and what is conditionally transparent. No promoted or synthesized family occupies this territory.

**Why it matters strategically:** The grammar already uses membranes as a design concept (holon membranes, learning membrane). But "membrane" is described functionally, not formally. If Spore needs to specify interoperability protocols between holons, or describe how information should flow across governance boundaries, boundary-making provides the formal language. This is a lexicon-level contribution — it would add a primitive, not a pattern.

**What kind of next pass it would need:** A corroboration-style search for non-Johar sources that use Markov blankets or conditional-independence gradients as coordination vocabulary (not just as neuroscience or physics formalism). Karl Friston's active inference framework is the obvious starting point, but the question is whether the formal vocabulary translates to institutional/governance design. If it does, the family could enter a future promotion batch as a lexicon candidate. If it doesn't translate beyond modeling formalism, the family should be reclassified as dormant.

**Whether it belongs before or after a future batch-2 promotion review:** After. Batch 2 should focus on human-economy and possibly holding-complexity's diagnostic layer, which are both further along. Boundary-making needs its own corroboration pass before it can be batch-ready.

### 5.2 Informational Duty (conditional survivor)

**Why it remains distinct:** It addresses a specific unresolved question in the promoted canon: who governs the boundaries of the epistemic commons without recreating monopoly? The duty frame — an obligation not to degrade shared epistemic conditions — is structurally different from both linguistic-closure's mechanism account and civic-truth's commons-condition account. It names the enforcement side of what the promoted entries describe.

**Why it matters strategically:** If the grammar needs boundary-enforcement mechanisms for its discourse graph and claim lifecycle (which it eventually will), the duty frame provides a governance vocabulary for that enforcement. "Who bears the obligation to maintain epistemic quality?" is a question the promoted entries raise but do not answer.

**Why this is conditional:** The concept is narrow enough that it may be better addressed as a design parameter within an existing family (civic-truth or linguistic-closure) rather than as a standalone concept. Whether informational duty justifies its own synthesis note — let alone its own canon entry — depends on whether the obligation architecture is genuinely distinct from the mechanism and commons accounts. If it can be expressed as a paragraph within civic-truth's synthesis, it should not be a separate family.

**What kind of next pass it would need:** Not a corroboration pass (the concept is well-articulated). Instead, a scoping assessment: does the duty frame add enough structural content to justify a standalone synthesis, or should it be folded into civic-truth's open questions as a design-parameter addendum? This is a 1-run question.

**Whether it belongs before or after a future batch-2 promotion review:** After, if it survives scoping. And only if the scoping assessment determines it is genuinely distinct from civic-truth's territory.

## 7. Recommendation

**One narrow deferred-family follow-up.**

The follow-up should be a scoping assessment for `boundary-making-and-entangled-systems` — not a synthesis or corroboration pass, but a focused evaluation of whether the Markov-blanket boundary vocabulary translates from complexity science formalism to governance/coordination design vocabulary. This is the only deferred family with genuinely unoccupied territory that could contribute a new primitive to the grammar.

Why not "no deferred-family work now": Boundary-making is technically sharp, strategically relevant (the grammar needs formal boundary vocabulary), and its territory is not threatened by absorption. Leaving it entirely dormant risks losing a real contribution.

Why not "prepare a small deferred-family wave": Only one family (boundary-making) has strong enough standing to justify active pursuit. Informational-duty is a conditional survivor whose distinctiveness needs scoping before any work begins. Two families do not make a wave — and informational-duty may not survive scoping. The honest posture is one narrow follow-up, with informational-duty as a conditional second if boundary-making's scoping reveals something useful.

Why not informational-duty first: Boundary-making proposes a new primitive (lexicon-level). Informational-duty proposes a refinement of an existing relationship between promoted/synthesized families (governance-parameter level). The new primitive has more structural value if it succeeds.

## 9. Guardrails

- **Deferred families should not be revived by elegance alone.** The Markov-blanket concept is technically elegant. That is not sufficient for revival. It must also translate to governance vocabulary, have at least some external support, and address a real gap in the grammar that cannot be filled by the existing promoted or synthesized families.

- **Promoted canon entries do not automatically validate adjacent deferred families.** Legitimacy's promotion does not strengthen conversational sovereignty. Linguistic-closure's promotion does not strengthen informational duty. Relational-topology's promotion does not validate spatial-service. Each family stands or falls on its own evidence and distinctiveness.

- **Dormancy is an acceptable and useful outcome.** Six of eight deferred families are now recommended for dormancy or absorption. This is not a failure — it is the expected result of a first round of canon promotions clarifying what the grammar actually needs. The grammar is better served by 3 promoted entries with high evidentiary standards than by 8 deferred families with ambiguous standing.

- **Distinctiveness matters more than thematic importance.** "This topic is important" is not the same as "this concept is distinct from what already exists." Extractive lock-in is important. Institutional transition is important. But importance without distinctiveness produces redundant coverage, not deeper grammar.

- **The project should now optimize for clarity, not maximal coverage.** The corpus processing phase is complete. The corroboration phase has established evidentiary standards. The promotion phase has enacted 3 entries. The next move should be extending these standards to the remaining strong families (human-economy, holding-complexity diagnostic), not expanding the frontier into thin deferred territory.
