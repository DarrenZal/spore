## 4. Family Dossier

### A. What Is Confirmed

- Fixed-contract coordination (wage, transaction) is structurally inadequate when work cannot be specified in advance, supervised in execution, or verified by output. The coordination unit must shift from contract terms to governance structure under uncertainty. (Hart: incomplete contracts, residual control rights. Williamson: discriminating alignment, bounded rationality + opportunism. Simon: zone of acceptance, authority replacing specification. Three independent traditions converge.)

- Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing: security, capability, and membership conditions without which formal participation rights are structurally empty. "Commitment without standing is precarity with better vocabulary." (Polanyi: fictitious commodity — labour inseparable from human life. Standing: precariat, seven securities, occupational citizenship. Sen: capabilities as freedom — formal rights without substantive capability are structurally empty. Ostrom: commons membership as participation infrastructure. Four independent traditions converge.)

- The principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of work is well-established. Verification thresholds, resource commitments, and renegotiation structures do vary by epistemic phase in production systems at scale. (March: exploration vs exploitation, 1991. Cooper: stage-gate R&D governance, 1990. Pharmaceutical trial phase governance: Phase I safety vs Phase III efficacy. NASA TRL: different acceptance criteria per level. Williamson: governance-mode selection matching transaction attributes.)

- Commitment-based economic coordination is operationally viable at production scale: CLC (26K users, 188 pools — operational precedent, with CLC acknowledged as design lineage for Spore, not independent corroboration) and Mondragon (70 years, 70K worker-owners — membership-based commitment governance at industrial scale). SMart Belgium (120K members) demonstrates hybrid membership infrastructure in the discovery domain specifically.

### B. What Is Johar's Design Contribution

- The translation from project-phase governance (stage-gate, pharmaceutical trials — well-established) to commitment-type governance (interpersonal obligation relations with type-specific verification and governed renegotiation). The structural logic is transferable; the specific formulation as commitment types between agents is Johar's.

- "Standing" as a named primitive within commitment grammar — integrating independently established concepts (security, capabilities, membership, occupational citizenship) into a single governance primitive that composes with commitment types. External sources use different vocabulary (security, capabilities, membership, occupational citizenship). The naming and integration into commitment grammar is the design contribution.

- Discovery-specific intensification — the claim that discovery work breaks wage grammar in ways routine uncertain work does not. Hart, Williamson, and Simon analyse uncertainty broadly; none specifically analyse the epistemic character of discovery work as a distinct category. This extends beyond what external sources establish.

### C. What Is Still Hypothesis or Distinctive Integration

- The four-type commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) organized by epistemic/governance character. Two full corroboration passes found no external source that independently proposes commitment types organized by epistemic character as a governance primitive. Professional partnership structures provide weak partial match (of counsel approximates stewardship; equity partner approximates risk). Stage-gate provides a functional equivalent for the principle of verification-mode variation, but frames it as pipeline phases, not commitment types. The taxonomy is the family's most distinctive contribution and also its least externally grounded claim.

- The composition of standing with differentiated commitments — the claim that a security floor and epistemic commitment-type differentiation can be sustained together without collapsing back into wage grammar. No production system combines a security floor with epistemic commitment-type differentiation. Mondragon demonstrates security floor + commitment governance but without epistemic differentiation; SMart demonstrates discovery-domain membership but without commitment-type differentiation. The composition is architecturally important but entirely untested.

- The structural parallel to Spore's commitment grammar. Spore's commitment protocols, holon membership, governed renegotiation, and commitment-pooling pattern are structurally parallel to the commitment-economy account. The grammar may already embody commitment-economy mechanisms without having named them. This parallel is explanatory — it helps explain why the grammar takes the form it does — but is not independent evidence. Spore was built with awareness of this conceptual territory, and CLC (an influence on Spore) shares the "commitments are the economy" frame.

- The wage-as-problem framing reversal. Hart, Williamson, and Simon treat employment (wage + authority) as a governance *solution* to uncertainty. The family treats wage as the *problem* under discovery conditions. This is not a contradiction — the family argues wage works for routine work but fails for discovery — but the reversal means the replacement claim goes beyond what external sources support. The boundary between confirmed diagnostic (contracts fail under uncertainty) and extended claim (wage grammar itself is the failure mode) is sensitive.

## 5. Boundary Notes

**Distinct from `agility-contribution-and-organizing`:** Agility addresses infrastructural coordination capacity — how optionality enables adaptive organizing and how alignment decays over time. Human-economy addresses the grammar of economic participation — what coordination unit replaces wage and transaction. Contribution-based organizing needs commitment grammar, but the mechanisms are different: agility concerns coordination capacity; commitment concerns the economic participation primitive. Standing (participation security) must not be confused with alignment maintenance (agility's territory).

**Distinct from `market-legibility-and-moral-provenance`:** Market legibility addresses what markets can perceive — the selective perception gap and the translation infrastructure needed to restore it. Human-economy addresses how economic participation is governed when work cannot be specified. Both observe that thin transaction grammar is inadequate, but for different reasons: market-legibility addresses the perception gap; human-economy addresses the governance gap. Evidence about what markets are deaf to belongs to market-legibility, not here.

**Distinct from `priceability-and-system-world`:** Priceability addresses what can enter economic reality — the visibility regime that determines the boundary of the system world. Human-economy addresses how participants coordinate within whatever visibility regime exists. Priceability is upstream (what can be seen); commitment is about governance (how to coordinate once you can see). The commitment-economy account does not depend on resolving the priceability question, and evidence about value grammar expansion or recognition architecture belongs to priceability.

## 9. Guardrails

- **Strong mechanism support does not confirm the four-type taxonomy.** The diagnostic tells us what is broken (wage/transaction grammar under discovery). The broad replacement principle tells us governance must vary by epistemic character. Neither confirms that the specific four-type commitment-signature taxonomy is the right operationalization. Hart and Williamson confirm the diagnostic; March and stage-gate confirm the principle; no one confirms the taxonomy.

- **CLC matters, but not as independent corroboration.** CLC demonstrates at production scale that commitment-based coordination works. This is genuinely significant evidence for the broad replacement principle. But CLC influenced Spore's commitment grammar — it is operational precedent and design lineage, not independent discovery of the same mechanism.

- **Standing should not be allowed to smuggle the whole family into canon.** Standing is the most strongly corroborated layer — four independent traditions converge. But standing alone is not the family's distinctive contribution. The family's claim is that standing *composes with* differentiated commitments. If only standing enters, the compositional insight is lost. If standing carries the taxonomy along with it, the taxonomy enters without its own evidence. The review must treat these as separate questions.

- **The Spore parallel explains relevance; it does not prove the claim.** The structural overlap between Spore's commitment grammar and Johar's commitment-economy account is real and worth noting. But Spore was built with awareness of this conceptual territory. The parallel is design context ("this is why the grammar takes the form it does"), not evidence ("the grammar's existence confirms the theory").

- **Future review must preserve the split or the family should stay out.** The split-promotion structure is not a formatting convenience. It is the condition under which this family is reviewable at all. If the split collapses during enactment — the taxonomy gets presented as confirmed, CLC gets treated as independent, standing carries the whole family — the evidentiary standard that made batch 1 credible is undermined. A reviewer who cannot maintain the split should defer the family rather than promote it blurred.
