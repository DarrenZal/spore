## 4. What Promotion Would Actually Need

### Minimum threshold for moving from defer to serious promotion review

A family can move from `promotion_status: defer` to serious review when:

1. **The structural mechanism is externally confirmed** by at least 2 independent non-Johar traditions, with the match being structural (same mechanism, not just adjacent vocabulary).
2. **At least one empirical or operational case** demonstrates the mechanism operating in a real institution — not as theory but as observable practice.
3. **The Johar-specific remainder is clearly identified** — what is externally confirmed vs what is Johar's design contribution — so that promotion review can distinguish between these categories.
4. **Family boundaries are stable** — the family has stayed distinct from adjacent concepts through the corroboration process and there is no unresolved collapse risk.

All 5 families meet criteria 1, 2, and 4. The question is whether criterion 3 is resolved cleanly enough for each family to enable a meaningful promotion conversation. For legitimacy, linguistic-closure, and relational-topology, it is. For holding-complexity and human-economy, the Johar-specific remainder is large enough or the replacement mechanism is thin enough that promotion review would need to proceed differently.

### Role of external corroboration vs production grounding

External corroboration confirms that the structural mechanism is independently discoverable — it is a real feature of the world, not an idiosyncratic framework. But corroboration from *literature* confirms that *other scholars recognise the mechanism*. It does not confirm that Johar's *design response* works.

For families where the design response is the primary Johar contribution (all 5, to varying degrees), production evidence is more valuable than further literature corroboration. Specifically:
- **Linguistic-closure's anti-closure infrastructure** (claim lifecycle, discourse graph) would be best validated by production evidence from running governance.
- **Human-economy's commitment signatures** would be best validated by an organisation implementing commitment-type differentiation.
- **Relational-topology's 6-9 month half-life** would be best validated by longitudinal measurement in distributed teams.
- **Holding-complexity's five-capacity architecture** would be best validated by institutional design that implements the package.
- **Legitimacy's orchestration mode** would be best validated by polycentric governance that explicitly uses the framework.

The shift in evidence needs: from literature corroboration (phase now complete) to production grounding (phase not yet begun).

### When a Johar-specific integration is acceptable as design innovation

Johar-specific integration is acceptable as a design contribution — and potentially promotable as such — when:

1. **The component mechanisms are independently confirmed.** If the pieces are real, the architecture is a plausible engineering response.
2. **The integration is internally coherent.** The components fit together without contradiction and the architecture addresses the problem the components name.
3. **The integration is not trivially derivable.** Multiple external traditions confirmed the components but none independently arrived at the same integration — which means the integration adds value beyond the components.
4. **The integration is explicitly tagged.** The canon entry should state: "The structural mechanism is externally confirmed. The specific design vocabulary/architecture is Johar's contribution." This prevents overclaiming (treating Johar's framing as if it were externally confirmed) without dismissing it (treating it as ungrounded speculation).

All 5 families satisfy conditions 1, 2, and 3. Condition 4 is a formatting requirement for the promotion entry, not a readiness condition.

### When a family still needs one more targeted pass before any review

A family needs a second pass when:

- The *mechanism* is confirmed but the *replacement claim* (what replaces the broken thing) has no external analog at all — meaning promotion would require promoting an entirely unvalidated design proposal. This applies to **human-economy-and-commitment**: the diagnostic (contracts fail) is robust, but the specific replacement (commitment signatures with 4 types) has only Williamson's hybrids as a partial match.
- The corroboration pass used a *modified success condition* that explicitly accepts a wider Johar-specific remainder — meaning the standard for "corroborated" was intentionally narrowed. This applies to **holding-complexity-beyond-control**, though the diagnostic layer is strong enough that the diagnostic sub-claims do not need a second pass.

### The three categories: externally real mechanism → plausible design translation → promotable canon claim

| Category | Definition | What it requires | Which families fit |
|----------|-----------|------------------|--------------------|
| **Externally real mechanism** | The structural insight has been independently confirmed by non-Johar sources. The world works this way. | 2+ independent traditions + 1 case | All 5 — all passed corroboration |
| **Plausible design translation** | Johar's design vocabulary/architecture is a reasonable engineering response to the confirmed mechanism. It is internally coherent, non-trivially derived, and addresses the confirmed problem. | Internal coherence + components externally real + integration adds value beyond components | All 5 — the checkpoint's finding that "the physics is known; the engineering is new" applies across the board |
| **Promotable canon claim** | The family's claims (mechanism + design response) are ready to enter Spore's canon, with the mechanism tagged as confirmed and the design response tagged as design contribution. | All of the above + Johar-specific remainder clearly mapped + no unresolved replacement gaps + boundaries stable | Legitimacy, linguistic-closure, relational-topology. Holding-complexity diagnostic only. Human-economy diagnostic + standing only. |

The middle category — "plausible design translation" — is where most Johar-specific integration sits. It is not externally confirmed, but it is not speculation either. It is engineering built on confirmed physics. Promotion review should treat it accordingly: promotable as tagged design contribution, not as externally confirmed structure, and subject to future production validation.

- Nepal irrigation as controlled comparison (FMIS 70% vs AMIS 40%) + EU democratic deficit as contemporary case

- The evidence is multi-type (conceptual + empirical + experimental + configural) and multi-disciplinary (4 traditions with zero overlap)

**What is still thin:**
- Very little. The mechanism is the most thoroughly corroborated of all 5 families. The main gap is the absence of a *production case* where polycentric legitimacy was *deliberately designed using the structural account* the family provides. Nepal irrigation was discovered, not designed from this theory. The EU is a negative case. A positive case of designed polycentric legitimacy at institutional scale would strengthen the family further but is not required for promotion review.

**What is still Johar-distinctive:**
- The 4-dimensional taxonomy (democracy, stewardship, experimentation, radical markets) — Ostrom's polycentricity and Beetham's 3 dimensions are multi-dimensional but not this taxonomy
- "Orchestration over dominance" as a named coordination mode — implied by Ostrom but not independently named
- Civic economy as a third pole — Ostrom's commons are adjacent but not framed as "civic economy"
- The control-surveillance-energy spiral as a named escalation pattern — Scott and Hirschman describe the mechanism but do not name the spiral

**Best next evidence move:** Production evidence from Spore itself — if the grammar implements polycentric legitimacy with consequence-binding, the grammar's operational experience becomes evidence.

**Should it enter a future promotion-review batch soon?** Yes. This is the cleanest candidate.

- Proactive anti-closure infrastructure design as a general institutional principle — external correctives are reactive (Kuhn: crisis), domain-specific (Nosek: science), or operational (Weick: HRO for specific organisations)
- The claim lifecycle, discourse graph, and attestation decay as specific anti-closure mechanisms
- LLM amplification analysis (all sources predate the era)

**Best next evidence move:** Production evidence from the learning membrane itself. Does Spore's claim lifecycle resist closure better than a system without it? This is testable within the grammar's operation.

**Should it enter a future promotion-review batch soon?** Yes. The mechanism is the cleanest match of all 5 runs. The corrective can be promoted as tagged design hypothesis with the mechanism as confirmed foundation. The LLM amplification claim should be explicitly deferred.

### 5.3 Human-Economy-and-Commitment

**What is now solid:**
- Diagnostic is robust: Hart/Williamson/Simon confirm contracts fail under uncertainty. This is mainstream economics, not controversial.
- Standing/participation infrastructure is strong: Polanyi (fictitious commodity), Standing (precariat / occupational citizenship), Sen (capabilities), Ostrom (membership). Four independent traditions converge.
- Production case: Mondragon (membership + anticipos + Lagun Aro) demonstrates all 3 layers at industrial scale. SMart Belgium adds discovery-domain evidence.

**What is still thin:**
- **The replacement mechanism — this is the weakest link across all 5 runs.** Williamson's hybrid governance is the closest external analog but is a broader category. Simon's "zone of acceptance" is micro-foundational but treats wage-employment as valid solution. No external source independently proposes commitment-type differentiation or governed renegotiation as the specific coordination primitive. The family asks the reader to accept a specific governance form (4-type commitment signatures with renegotiation) that has no external direct match.
- **Discovery-specific intensification is untested.** The claim that discovery work breaks wage grammar in ways routine uncertain work does not is the family's distinctive claim, but neither Hart nor Williamson specifically analyse the epistemic character of discovery work. Mondragon operates primarily in manufacturing, not discovery.
- **Wage-as-problem framing is reversed from external sources.** Hart, Williamson, and Simon all treat employment (wage + authority) as a governance *solution* to uncertainty. The family claim treats wage as the *problem*. This is not a contradiction (the family argues that wage works for routine work but fails for discovery), but the reversal means the family's replacement claim goes beyond what external sources support.

**What is still Johar-distinctive:**
- Commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk)
- "Standing" as a named primitive within commitment grammar (vs generic social protection)
- Discovery-specific intensification
- Wage-as-problem framing
- The exact parallel between Johar's commitment economy and Spore's commitment grammar

**Best next evidence move:** A targeted second pass focused specifically on commitment-type differentiation in production cases. Look for organisations that distinguish between types of commitment or obligation in their governance (not just "commitment-based" generally, but differentiated commitment). Sources to try: R&D governance (how discovery projects are governed differently from delivery projects), partnership law (different partner types with different obligations), cooperative governance (how different contribution types are tracked). Alternatively: production evidence from Spore's own commitment lifecycle, if it implements type differentiation.

**Should it enter a future promotion-review batch soon?** The diagnostic and standing layers could enter a batch. The replacement mechanism (commitment signatures) should **not** enter a batch until either: (a) a targeted second pass finds external support for commitment-type differentiation, or (b) it is explicitly tagged as "design proposal, not externally confirmed mechanism." A split promotion — diagnostic + standing in, replacement mechanism tagged as hypothesis — is the honest option.

### 5.4 Relational-Topology-and-Context-Design

**What is now solid:**
- Generative mechanism confirmed from 4 independent traditions (Suchman, Burt, Orlikowski, Edmondson/Aristotle)
- **Strongest empirical evidence base of all 5 runs:** Burt (blind evaluation controlling for individual attributes), Aristotle (180+ teams, 250+ attributes), Yang/Microsoft (61k employees), Polzer (experimental teams demonstrating topology-generated faultlines), Allen Curve (communication decay with distance), Roberts & Dunbar (longitudinal relationship decay)
- Multiple evidence types: conceptual parallel + controlled experiment + natural experiment + field study + longitudinal + cognitive-evolutionary
- Failure mode strongly demonstrated: Polzer 3-3 split, Yang network siloing, Allen Curve, distributed-team conflict literature

**What is still thin:**
- **Integration claim at institutional scale.** Each source demonstrates one dimension: Suchman (situation generates action), Burt (topology generates advantage), Orlikowski (materiality constitutes practice), Edmondson (context determines team quality). The claim that relational topology is "the real infrastructure of institutions" integrates all four into a single thesis. The integration is Johar's — the components are independently confirmed but the unified frame is not independently named. This integration is well-supported by the component evidence but is not itself externally tested at institutional scale.
- **6-9 month half-life parameter.** Structural decay is well-supported (Roberts & Dunbar, distributed-team literature). But no source provides a specific half-life estimate. This parameter is a design hypothesis, not an externally validated finding.
- **Scaling from team to institution.** Project Aristotle demonstrates context > composition at the team level. Whether this scales to institutional-level topology (which the family claims) is plausible but not directly demonstrated.

**What is still Johar-distinctive:**
- "Imaginary identity" as a named concept (the phenomenon is demonstrated by Polzer et al.; the term is Johar's)
- "Context metabolism" (no external equivalent)
- The integrative claim that topology is "the real infrastructure"
- The 6-9 month alignment half-life as a specific parameter
- Context design as a named institutional design discipline (distinct from formal structure design)

**Best next evidence move:** Longitudinal measurement in distributed teams to test the half-life claim. Alternatively: production evidence from any organisation that explicitly designs relational topology (not org chart) as infrastructure. GitLab's "handbook-first" approach is adjacent but operates at the documentation layer, not the relational layer.

**Should it enter a future promotion-review batch soon?** Yes. The generative mechanism and failure-mode claims are the most empirically grounded of all 5 families. The integration and the half-life parameter can be tagged as design hypothesis in the promotion entry. The component evidence is strong enough to support the integration as a plausible design claim.

**What separates it from Rank 3:** Relational-topology has stronger empirical evidence than linguistic-closure. ### Rank 3: linguistic-closure

**Why it ranks third:** The cleanest mechanism match of all 5 runs — 4 traditions independently naming the same stopping-rule structure. Broadest corpus reach (8 governance clusters, 2 projects). **What separates it from Rank 4:** Linguistic-closure's Johar-specific remainder (naming as upstream mechanism, proactive anti-closure design) is narrower and more sharply defined than holding-complexity's (fork structure, five-capacity package, meta-goal distinction, civilisational optionality, reflexive clause). The replacement mechanism problem that weakens human-economy does not apply to holding-complexity's diagnostic — Ashby's theorem directly IS the control-failure claim. The Dutch Water Boards case is a positive case (deliberate adaptive governance that resisted both attractors), whereas Mondragon has a domain-fit limitation (manufacturing, not discovery).

### Rank 5: human-economy-and-commitment

**Why it ranks fifth:** The replacement mechanism — commitment signatures with 4 types — is the weakest link across all 5 runs. The diagnostic (contracts fail under uncertainty) is robust in mainstream economics but less distinctive than the other families' diagnostics. The standing claim is well-supported but is independently established (Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom) and does not depend on the family's distinctive contribution. The family's distinctive contribution (the specific replacement grammar) is the least externally grounded.

**What separates it from Rank 4:** Holding-complexity's diagnostic layer is very strong and corpus-central. Human-economy's diagnostic is mainstream economics — real but not distinctive. The family's importance to Spore (it directly corresponds to the commitment grammar) is high, but importance is not readiness. The replacement mechanism gap means promotion review would either promote only the diagnostic + standing (which are not distinctively Johar) or promote the commitment-signature taxonomy as unvalidated design proposal. Neither is as clean as the options available for the top 3 families.

## 9. Guardrails

1. **Corroborated mechanism is not enough on its own.** All 5 families passed corroboration. This confirms the structural insights are real. It does not confirm that the full family claims — including design responses, vocabularies, and architectures — are ready for canon. Corroboration tested mechanisms; promotion requires assessing the full claim including Johar-specific contributions.

2. **Design extrapolation must not be smuggled in as proof.** The consistent pattern across all 5 runs — mechanism confirmed, design vocabulary original — means promotion must explicitly distinguish between what is externally confirmed and what is Johar's design contribution. A canon entry that presents the commitment-signature taxonomy or the five-capacity architecture as if they were externally confirmed would be overclaiming. The tagging format (mechanism confirmed / design contribution / design hypothesis) exists precisely to prevent this.

3. **Johar-specific integration can be valuable without yet being promotable.** The five-capacity architecture, commitment-signature taxonomy, "imaginary identity," and "context metabolism" are plausible design contributions built on confirmed mechanisms. They are not speculation. But they have not been externally validated or production-tested. Treating them as "design contribution" rather than "confirmed mechanism" respects both their value and their current evidential status.

4. **Weaker families should not ride into review on the strength of stronger adjacent ones.** Human-economy's replacement mechanism is not strengthened by legitimacy's strong corroboration. Holding-complexity's five-capacity architecture is not validated by relational-topology's empirical base. Each family stands or falls on its own evidence. The batch structure exists to separate ready families from families that need more work.

5. **Promotion work should preserve family boundaries, not reward conceptual sprawl.** The corroboration runs demonstrated that well-bounded families with precise mechanisms corroborate cleanly. Promotion should maintain these boundaries. A canon entry for legitimacy should not absorb evidence from topology; a canon entry for holding-complexity should not absorb evidence from governance-under-volatility. Each family enters canon with its own evidence, its own boundaries, and its own honest accounting of what is confirmed vs what is contributed.
