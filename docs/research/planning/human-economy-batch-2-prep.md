# Human Economy Batch-2 Prep

## 1. Scope And Rule

This memo reassesses `human-economy-and-commitment` after the completed pass-2 investigation of the replacement mechanism. It is prep for possible future review, not promotion. No `promotion_status` values will be changed. No canon entries will be written. No synthesis-note prose will be rewritten.

The family must now be judged using a split-promotion structure. The three promoted batch-1 entries (legitimacy-and-shared-consequence, linguistic-closure, relational-topology-and-context-design) established a concrete tagging format: confirmed mechanism / design contribution / design hypothesis. That format is the standard for this assessment.

The four-type commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) remains the main point of caution. Two full corroboration passes found no direct external analog for the taxonomy. Stage-gate R&D governance and March's exploration/exploitation distinction provide functional equivalents for the *principle* that governance should vary by epistemic character, but no source independently proposes commitment types organized by epistemic character as a governance primitive. This caution is not negotiable unless new evidence emerges.

CLC (Grassroots Economics / Will Ruddick) is classified as operational precedent + design lineage/precursor. Because Spore acknowledges CLC as "a key influence on the settlement and demurrage design" and lists Ruddick as an inspiration, CLC cannot count as independent corroboration. It can count as production evidence that commitment-based economic coordination works at scale.

## 4. Readiness Assessment

**Is the family now clean enough for future review?** Yes, with a narrow scope. The diagnostic layer, standing layer, and broad replacement principle constitute a meaningful, promotable claim even without the four-type taxonomy. The split-promotion format separates what is confirmed from what is hypothesized with sufficient clarity for honest review.

**Is the split-promotion structure stable enough to survive canon use?** Yes. The boundary between confirmed mechanism (diagnostic + standing + broad principle) and design hypothesis (taxonomy + composition) is sharp. Stage-gate and CLC evidence make this boundary sharper than it was after pass 1 — the functional equivalents clarify exactly where the external evidence stops and Johar's contribution begins.

**Is the family's importance to Spore distorting the readiness judgment?** This is the most important risk to name. Human-economy is the family most directly applicable to Spore's existing commitment grammar. The temptation is to lower the bar because the grammar needs this concept. The honest assessment: the diagnostic and standing layers would pass the same standard that batch 1 used. The broad replacement principle is modestly weaker than batch 1's strongest confirmed mechanisms (legitimacy's experimental evidence, topology's controlled evaluations) but is adequate with CLC acknowledged as operational precedent rather than independent corroboration. The taxonomy would not pass batch 1's standard for confirmed mechanism — it is correctly classified as design hypothesis. Importance has not distorted the classification, but it should be monitored during review.

**Is the current evidence stronger on production precedent or conceptual match?** Mixed, and the mix matters. Production precedent is strong for the broad claim (CLC: 26K users, 188 pools; Mondragon: 70 years, 70K worker-owners; SMart: 120K members in discovery domain). Conceptual match is moderate-strong for the principle of epistemic governance differentiation (March, stage-gate, Williamson). But production precedent and conceptual match converge on the broad principle, not the specific taxonomy. The taxonomy has neither production precedent nor conceptual match — it remains Johar's engineering proposal.

## 5. Draft Review-Safe Framing

Economic coordination under discovery and complexity cannot rely on wage or transaction grammar because these forms depend on specifiability, containment, and measurability assumptions that discovery structurally violates (Hart, Williamson, Simon; confirmed mechanism). Viable coordination shifts toward commitment-based primitives — bounded obligations under uncertainty with governed renegotiation — as demonstrated at production scale by Mondragon's membership governance and CLC's commitment-based economic coordination (operational precedent, with CLC acknowledged as design lineage for Spore's own commitment grammar). Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing: security, capability, and membership conditions without which formal participation rights are structurally empty (Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom; confirmed mechanism). The principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of work is well-established (March, stage-gate, pharmaceutical governance; confirmed mechanism). Johar's specific contribution is the proposal that this principle can be operationalized through a four-type commitment-signature taxonomy (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) with type-specific verification and governed renegotiation — a design hypothesis built on confirmed components, not yet externally confirmed as a governance primitive.

## 6. Main Blocking Questions

1. **Is the four-type taxonomy too central to the family to omit from a review packet?** No. The diagnostic + standing + broad replacement principle constitute a coherent, reviewable claim. The taxonomy adds specificity and design direction but is not structurally necessary for the core claim. A review that promotes the confirmed layers and tags the taxonomy as design hypothesis is more honest than either including the taxonomy as if confirmed or omitting the family because the taxonomy is unconfirmed.

2. **Is the broad replacement principle now strong enough without the taxonomy?** Yes, with qualification. March + stage-gate + Williamson + CLC production evidence establish that governance must vary by epistemic character and that commitment-based coordination is a viable alternative to transaction-based coordination. The principle does not require the specific four-type taxonomy to be promotable — it requires only the claim that governance differentiation by epistemic character is structurally necessary, which is externally confirmed.

3. **Should standing be treated as part of the same family entry or as contextual infrastructure?** Same family entry. The family's core insight is that the diagnostic (wage breaks), the replacement (commitment-based governance), and the participation infrastructure (standing) are structurally interdependent. "Commitment without standing is precarity with better vocabulary" is the family's sharpest formulation — splitting standing into contextual infrastructure would lose the compositional claim that makes the family distinctive.

4. **Is the Spore parallel explanatory or overreach?** Explanatory. The structural overlap between Spore's commitment grammar and Johar's commitment-economy account is real and worth noting. But it is not independent evidence — Spore was built with awareness of this conceptual territory, and CLC (an influence on Spore) shares the "commitments are the economy" frame. The parallel should appear in a review packet as design context ("this is why the grammar takes the form it does"), not as evidence ("the grammar's existence confirms the theory").

5. **Does CLC's role as design lineage weaken the broad replacement principle?** No, but it constrains how the evidence is used. CLC demonstrates that commitment-based economic coordination works at production scale — that is genuine evidence for the broad principle regardless of the lineage relationship. But CLC's evidence cannot be presented as independent convergence on the same idea. The review packet must state clearly: CLC is operational precedent and acknowledged design influence, not independent corroboration.

## 7. Decision

**Ready for narrow batch-2 review prep.**

The split-promotion structure is stable. The confirmed layers (diagnostic + standing + broad replacement principle) clear the batch-1 evidentiary standard for confirmed mechanism. The design-hypothesis layers (four-type taxonomy + standing-plus-differentiation composition) are clearly identified and honestly qualified. No further literature search will close the taxonomy gap — the evidence base is what it is.

**Likely review mode:** Split promotion — same format as batch 1 (mechanism confirmed / design contribution / design hypothesis), applied within a single family entry rather than across families.

**Caution label:** `explicit_qualification_required` — the review packet must state upfront that the four-type commitment-signature taxonomy is a design hypothesis with no external analog, and that CLC is operational precedent + design lineage, not independent corroboration.

**What must be stated upfront in the future review packet:**
- The diagnostic and standing layers are externally confirmed and promotable as confirmed mechanism.
- The broad replacement principle is confirmed as a principle but the commitment-type formulation is Johar's.
- The four-type taxonomy enters as design hypothesis — Johar's engineering on confirmed components.
- CLC is operational precedent and acknowledged design influence on Spore, not independent corroboration.
- The composition claim (standing + differentiated commitments) is untested and enters as design hypothesis.
- The Spore parallel is design context, not evidence.

## 9. Guardrails

- **Strong diagnostic support does not automatically validate the replacement grammar.** The diagnostic tells us what is broken (wage/transaction grammar under discovery). The replacement tells us what to build instead (commitment-based governance with type differentiation). These are different claims. Hart and Williamson confirm the diagnostic; they do not confirm the four-type taxonomy.

- **CLC can matter without being independent corroboration.** CLC demonstrates at production scale that commitment-based coordination works. This is genuinely significant evidence for the broad replacement principle. But CLC influenced Spore's commitment grammar — the evidence is operational precedent and design lineage, not independent discovery of the same mechanism.

- **Production precedent and direct analog are different evidentiary roles.** CLC is strong production precedent for commitment-based economic coordination broadly. Stage-gate is a functional equivalent for verification-mode variation. Professional partnerships are a weak partial match for commitment-type differentiation. None of these are direct analogs for the four-type taxonomy. The family has production precedent for the broad principle and no direct analog for the specific taxonomy. These are different kinds of evidence with different implications for promotion.

- **The four-type taxonomy should remain qualified unless a real analog exists.** Two full corroboration passes found no source that independently proposes commitment types organized by epistemic character as a governance primitive. Stage-gate provides a functional equivalent for the principle of epistemic differentiation, but frames it as pipeline phases, not commitment types. The taxonomy is Johar's design contribution — plausible engineering on confirmed components, not confirmed structure.

- **The family should enter review only if the split is clean enough to keep future canon language honest.** The split-promotion format must produce canon language where a reader can distinguish what is externally confirmed from what is Johar's proposal. If the split blurs in review — if the taxonomy gets smuggled in as confirmed mechanism, or if CLC gets treated as independent corroboration — the evidentiary standard that made batch 1 credible is undermined.
