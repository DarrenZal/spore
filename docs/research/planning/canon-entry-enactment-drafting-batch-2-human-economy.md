## 3. Drafting Principles

1. **Confirmed mechanism must stay distinct from design contribution.** The confirmed-mechanism layer (diagnostic, standing, broad replacement principle) is stated directly. Design contributions (commitment-type governance, "standing" as named primitive, governed renegotiation) do not enter the project-vision paragraph. They live in the synthesis note and would appear in future pattern or lexicon entries if warranted.

2. **CLC cannot appear in the confirmed-evidence lane.** CLC is operational precedent and design lineage. It must not appear in the project-vision paragraph at all — the confirmed layer rests entirely on independent academic sources (Hart, Williamson, Simon; Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom; March, stage-gate, pharmaceutical governance). Including CLC in any form in a confirmed-mechanism paragraph risks readers treating it as part of the confirmation.

3. **Hypotheses must remain hypotheses — and absent from this entry.** The four-type taxonomy, the standing-plus-differentiation composition, and discovery-specific intensification do not appear in the draft text. They are design hypotheses that belong in the synthesis note, not in canon.

4. **The entry should explain relevance to Spore without using Spore as evidence.** The paragraph may connect the confirmed mechanism to the grammar's design choice ("this is why the grammar treats commitments as its binding primitive") but must not use the grammar's existence as evidence for the mechanism. Direction is: confirmed mechanism justifies design choice, not design choice confirms mechanism.

5. **Project-vision additions must stay compact.** The existing batch-1 entries are 4 sentences each. This entry should be 4-5 sentences. The document is a vision statement, not a research summary. Evidence citations, inline tags, and source lists belong in the synthesis note, not here.

6. **Use refined wording from the wording-refinement memo.** Where the wording-refinement memo's Section 5 and the review packet's Section 4D differ, the refinement takes precedence. Key refined phrasings: "governance structure rather than contract terms becomes the necessary basis for coordination" (not "the coordination unit must shift"); no "operationally viable" language; no CLC/Mondragon/SMart in the confirmed-mechanism block.

## 4. Canon-Safe Claim Boundary

**What the confirmed canon-safe claim is:**

Three structural mechanisms are independently confirmed:

1. Fixed-contract coordination (wage, transaction) is structurally inadequate under discovery and complexity because specifiability, containment, and measurability assumptions fail. Governance structure rather than contract terms becomes the necessary basis for coordination. (Hart, Williamson, Simon — three independent traditions.)
2. Participation in commitment-based coordination requires standing — security, capability, and membership conditions — without which formal participation rights are structurally empty. (Polanyi, Standing, Sen, Ostrom — four independent traditions.)
3. The principle that governance should vary by the epistemic character of work is well-established. Verification thresholds and resource commitments do vary by epistemic phase in production systems at scale. (March, stage-gate, pharmaceutical governance.)

**What remains outside the confirmed layer:**

- **The four-type commitment-signature taxonomy** (delivery, discovery, stewardship, risk) — design hypothesis with no external analog after two corroboration passes. Johar's engineering on confirmed components. Must not appear in the paragraph.
- **Standing-plus-differentiation composition** — design hypothesis with no production demonstration. Must not appear in the paragraph.
- **CLC as confirmation** — CLC is operational precedent and design lineage. It cannot appear in the confirmed-mechanism paragraph because Spore acknowledges CLC as a design influence.
- **The Spore parallel** — the structural overlap between Spore's commitment grammar and Johar's commitment-economy account is real but is design context, not evidence. The paragraph may note that the grammar responds to the confirmed condition; it must not suggest that the grammar's existence confirms the theory.
- **Operational viability** — "commitment-based economic coordination is operationally viable" is a production-evidence claim, not a structural-mechanism claim. Must not appear.
- **Governed renegotiation as named feature** — design contribution (Johar's formulation). Must not appear.
- **Discovery-specific intensification** — Johar's extension beyond what Hart/Williamson/Simon establish. Must not appear.

**What cannot appear in the enacted paragraph:**

- Any mention of CLC, Mondragon, or SMart
- Any mention of the four-type taxonomy or commitment signatures
- Any "operationally viable" or "demonstrated by" language
- Any phrasing where standing carries the taxonomy into canon
- Any use of "must shift" (prescriptive; use diagnostic phrasing)
- Any inline tags ([mechanism confirmed], [design contribution], etc.)
- Any phrasing suggesting the grammar's existence confirms the economic theory
