## 3. Drafting Principles

1. **Confirmed mechanism must stay distinct from design contribution.** The four confirmed findings are stated directly. Design contributions (deliberate institutional construction, the "third path" framing) do not enter the project-vision paragraph. They live in the synthesis note and in the wording-refinement memo.

2. **Deliberate institutional construction cannot appear in the entry.** The institutional-construction requirement is corroborated (Folke, March) but classified as design contribution by the reviewer. Including it in the project-vision paragraph risks paraphrase drift into the full fork architecture. The batch-1 and batch-2 entries carry only confirmed-mechanism text in project-vision; design contributions stay in the synthesis notes.

3. **Energetic/material constraint must stay out of the approved core entirely.** The reviewer explicitly stated this. The thermodynamic foundation is solid, but it cannot appear in the canon entry in any form — not as a confirmed mechanism, not as secondary context, not as a qualifier.

4. **The entry should explain relevance to Spore without using Spore as evidence.** The existing entries end with brief grammar-connection sentences (e.g., "The grammar's commitment protocols are a structural response to this condition"). The diagnostic entry is the foundational condition that motivates the grammar; its placement in project-vision.md makes this connection implicit. A grammar-connection sentence is optional and should only be included if it stays safely within the confirmed layer.

5. **Project-vision additions must stay compact.** The existing canon entries are 4-5 sentences each, with no source citations, no inline tags, and no evidence lists. The diagnostic entry must match this style. Source citations, inline tags, and evidence attribution belong in the synthesis note and planning memos, not in project-vision.md.

6. **Use the refined wording from the wording-refinement memo as the base.** Where the wording-refinement memo's Section 5 (refined draft canon entry language) and the earlier review packet's Section 4D differ, the refinement takes precedence. Key refined phrasings: "provable structural condition" (Ashby is a mathematical theorem); "empirically documented default responses" (not "attractors" or "basins"); "not disorder, but insufficient coordination relative to existing interdependence" (Tainter's definition of collapse, not the basin-competition framing); "the independently confirmed general response requirement" (the diagnostic-safe scope limiter).

## 4. Canon-Safe Claim Boundary

**What the confirmed canon-safe claim is:**

Four structural findings are independently confirmed:

1. **Control failure under manifest complexity** — a regulator cannot govern a system whose variety exceeds the regulator's own representational capacity. This is a mathematical theorem (Ashby), operationalised for institutions (Beer). Two independent traditions.
2. **Authoritarian compression as one default response** — institutions facing complexity overload narrow degrees of freedom, enforce legibility, constrain variation, destroy local adaptive knowledge. Three independent traditions (Scott, Tainter, Beer) with detailed historical cases.
3. **Chaotic decomposition as the other default response** — loss of higher-order coordinating structures, reorganisation at lower complexity. Two independent traditions (Tainter, Holling) with multiple civilisational cases.
4. **Adaptive coordination as the general response requirement** — navigating evolving conditions through iterative sensing and response rather than enforcing predefined outcomes. Two independent traditions (Snowden, Folke).

**What remains outside the confirmed layer:**

- **Deliberate institutional construction** — corroborated (Folke, March) but classified by the reviewer as design contribution only, not confirmed mechanism. The diagnostic can be read as implying the need for deliberate construction, but the claim cannot be stated explicitly in the confirmed-mechanism paragraph.
- **The five-capacity architecture** (adaptive coordination, complexity-handling capacity, scaffolded relationality, interdependent sovereignty, anti-fragility through learning) — no external source independently names this package.
- **Civilisational optionality** as evaluative criterion — Taleb + Sen assembled at civilisational scale is Johar's synthesis.
- **The reflexive-constraint clause** ("must be built under conditions that actively constrain its construction") — demonstrated by Folke but not independently named.
- **Integrated fork framing** (control fails → two basins → third path) — the components are confirmed; the integration is Johar's.
- **Energetic/material constraint** — stays out of the approved core entirely per reviewer instruction.

**What cannot appear in the enacted paragraph:**

- Any mention of deliberate institutional construction
- Any mention of energetic/material constraint or thermodynamic foundations
- Any mention of the five-capacity architecture or any individual capacity by name as part of a package
- Any mention of civilisational optionality, the reflexive-constraint clause, or the fork framing

- Any phrasing where adaptive coordination reads as one of five capacities rather than the general finding
