# ADR-0050 — Sociality Side-B Commitment plus Joint-Commitment Primitive Admission (Option H Comprehensive)

## Status

active (authored + activated 2026-04-22 under `canon-rebuild-phase-3b-5a` decision-gated plan; Option H — comprehensive Side-B: canonicalize implicit + Holon irreducibility extension + joint-commitment 9th primitive — per operator selection post decision-brief)

## Context

Phase 3b.1 (Care, ADR-0045), Phase 3b.2 (Norms, ADR-0046), Phase 3b.3 (Power allocational, ADR-0047), Phase 3b.3b (Johar three-form / modes-across-primitives, ADR-0048), and Phase 3b.4 (Reproduction-continuity, ADR-0049) closed same-day 2026-04-22. Phase 3b.5a addresses the last remaining load-bearing capstone §8 Tier-1 item: sociality-reducibility (Side A vs Side B).

### Capstone evidence

**§1 Convergence on relational identity** (line 20): *"Identity is relational / constituted through participation, not pre-given. Supporting traditions: Care Ethics, Pluriversal, Commons Governance (Bollier & Helfrich 'Nested-I'), Autopoiesis / 4E Cognition, Viable System Model (identity as S5's defining function), Philosophy of Collective Agency (List/Pettit functional group agents; Tuomela ethos)."*

**§2 divergence 1 — sociality-reducibility** (line 34): *"Is sociality reducible to individual mental states, or is there an irreducibly collective stratum? ... What's at stake for Spore: If A, Spore's primitives only describe individuals and holon is mere aggregation. If B, Spore needs a primitive that cannot be analyzed via individual intent/commitment — a joint-commitment or sense-making-field primitive. Convergence on relational identity (§1) tilts Side B; current canon is A-compatible. The single most consequential unresolved question for whether Spore's canon is complete. Note the coupling: the traditions that land on Side B are almost exactly the same set that treat identity as relational in §1 (Gilbert wing, Autopoiesis/4E, VSM, commoning-Commons, Pluriversal). The §1 convergence on relational identity and this §2 divergence on reducibility are not independent axes — they are a single structural joint."*

**§7 Frozen-concepts Appendix** (line 281): *"collective-intentionality / we-mode / joint-commitment (duplicate — already in v2 as `collective-agency` for the `collective-intentionality` strand) — the Side-B primitive (§2 divergence 1) that would make sociality irreducibly collective. Admission depends on whether Spore commits to Side B. `we-mode` / `joint-commitment` remain adjacent but not yet frozen. Defer to explicit Phase 4 decision."*

**§8 Tier-1 item 3** (line 311): *"Sociality-reducibility (Side A vs. Side B) is the single most consequential unresolved question for whether Spore's canon is complete. ... Recommended action: explicit ADR resolving Spore's stance, with an accompanying primitive admission (joint-commitment or participatory-sense-making) if Side B, or explicit note-of-limitation if Side A."*

**§8 item 14 (Ostrom self-reversal) corroboration** (line 337): *"§1–§3 tilt Side B."* Independent second signal of the tilt beyond §2 line 34.

### Side-B tilt already present in post-3b.1–4 canon

Capstone v2's "current canon is A-compatible" snapshot pre-dates Phase 3b.1–4. Honest reading of the 3b.1–4 canon: Side-B-**tilted** but not Side-B-**locked** (each ADR has a Side-A-compatible reading available; each motivates through Side-B evidence):

- **ADR-0002 (reproductive-commoning doctrine lens, 2026-04-18)** — *"there is no commons without commoning"* (Bollier/Helfrich) is Side B by quotation; doctrine-lens form is Side-A-readable as aggregate-practice.
- **ADR-0016 (field-holon-primitive-distinction, 2026-04-20)** — explicitly agnostic on reducibility as currently written. Natural locus for Option F's extension.
- **ADR-0045 (care-commoning doctrine, Phase 3b.1)** — care-relations as constitutive of holonic-coordination; Baier/Held/Mackenzie-Stoljar relational-autonomy lineage is Side B, but lens-form is Side-A-readable.
- **ADR-0047 (power-multi-layer, Phase 3b.3) — `asymmetric-commitment`** — captures Baier/Kittay/Folbre asymmetric-vulnerability; Baier's *Trust and Antitrust* 1986 critique of contract-centric moral philosophy is specifically Side B but again readable between-individuals.
- **ADR-0048 (Phase 3b.3b) — participatory-sense-making** — explicitly cited as constructed-power lineage; **closest to explicit Side-B commitment** among 3b.1–4 ADRs. Participatory-sense-making is capstone §7's named Side-B primitive candidate. Leaving it as doctrine-citation without Side-B commitment is a standing tension.
- **ADR-0049 (Phase 3b.4)** — Federicist invisibilisation of reproductive labor (labor-is-the-capture-site framing) treats labor as property-of-collective not sum-of-individual-properties; Side-B-leaning.

Aggregate: 3b.1–4 canon has been **tilting Side B structurally** without explicit commitment. Phase 3b.5a names the tilt, closes the structural ambiguity, and admits the capstone-recommended verb-primitive.

### The three-move structure of Option H

Per decision-brief §Recommendation + operator selection, Option H combines three moves addressing three distinct layers:

**Move 1 — Canonicalization of implicit Side-B commitment (Option E component).** ADR-0050 includes a canon paragraph in `project-vision.md` explicitly naming Spore's Side-B commitment and enumerating the 3b.1–4 ADRs that structurally tilted it. This is a "lenient" canonicalization — not claiming 3b.1–4 ADRs were Side-B-committed (they were not locked), but claiming Spore canon now commits to the tilt that has been growing structurally. Matches capstone's own *"§1–§3 tilt Side B"* language.

**Move 2 — Holon primitive irreducibility extension (Option F component).** ADR-0016 (field-holon-primitive-distinction) was explicitly agnostic on reducibility. Spore's Holon bullet in `project-vision.md` and the parallel in `constitutional-artifacts-and-graph-projections.md` are extended with an irreducibility clause: *"The whole is not reducible to an aggregation of parts; it exhibits organizational invariants and emergent properties that cannot be analytically recovered from constituent-analysis alone (List/Pettit supervenience-without-reducibility; Thompson dynamic co-emergence)."* This closes the capstone §2 concern *"if A, Spore's holon is mere aggregation"* at the structural layer. ADR-0016 is extended via prose; file unchanged.

**Move 3 — `joint-commitment` admitted as 9th primitive, 6th coordination verb (Option B component).** Gilbert's joint-commitment (*JC* 2013 Ch. 2: *"a commitment of two or more people, not a sum of personal commitments"*; formed by open expression of readiness under common knowledge; rescindable only by concurrence; produces directed obligations + demand-rights *"of a different kind"* from moral ones) enters Spore's primitive grammar as the sixth coordination verb. Joint-commitment operates at the joint/collective-compositional level, distinct from individual-scale Commitment which retains its symmetric-party and asymmetric-commitment (Layer-2 per ADR-0047) readings.

### Earning-test honest application (per 3b.3b parsimony-as-outcome-not-axiom discipline + 3b.4 precedent)

- **(a) Protocol-surface specifiability: marginal**. Gilbert's joint-commitment has crisper primitive-operations than might first appear:
  - **form-joint-commitment** — shared expression of readiness under common knowledge (analog to Commitment's `offer` + `accept`, but joint-form from the start).
  - **rescind-joint-commitment** — requires concurrence from all parties (no unilateral withdrawal — *contra* Bratman's symmetric planners; this is Gilbert's distinctive claim).
  - **hold-accountable-via-demand-right** — directed obligation + standing to rebuke (*Rights and Demands* 2018 ch. 1), distinct from moral-entitlement.
  - **extend-joint-commitment** — scope-expansion by concurrent renewal.
  These are thinner than Commitment's `bind-to-goal-with-conditions` but crisper than Reproduction-continuity's `regenerate-field-capacity`. Circularity problem (Schmid 2014 regress: Gilbertian communication of readiness presupposes joint-commitment) hurts (a) slightly but is a philosophical open-question, not a protocol-surface failure.

- **(b) Multi-scale operational implementations: strong**. Four identifiable scales, distinct joint-commitment regimes at each:
  - **Microscale — team-charter**: a small team forming a shared commitment to deliver an outcome together. Gilbert's walking-together paradigm scaled to project-team. Joint; rescindable-by-concurrence-only; demand-right-bearing.
  - **Mesoscale — organizational decision**: board-vote, consent-decision, dot-voting adoption. Aggregation rule yields collective attitude; this IS the discursive-dilemma substrate (List/Pettit).
  - **Macroscale — federation oath / protocol-adoption**: cross-repo federation ratifying a shared protocol version. Protocol-version-adoption cannot be reduced to bilateral agreements because version-compatibility is multi-party-simultaneous — the whole federation adopts v-next, or the federation fragments. **This is the clearest case of irreducibly-joint coordination in Spore's operational surface.**
  - **Meta-scale — protocol-design-by-committee**: author-set co-specifying a governance protocol under open-readiness common-knowledge. Authorship-as-joint-commitment.
  Each scale observably distinct from sum-of-individual-commitments; each passes earning-test (b) with evidence from Spore's instance families (BKC/Octo council decisions; PM matcher-pool consensus; personal-operator + federation protocol-version).

- **Net**: marginal-(a) + strong-(b). Earning-test profile parallels ADR-0049 Reproduction-continuity (2026-04-22, same day): both marginal-(a) with named counterpoint; both strong-(b) across four scales. Per 3b.3b parsimony-as-outcome-not-axiom discipline + 3b.4 admission precedent: admission is earned. The "two primitive admissions in one day" concern is legitimate but **addressed by the discipline itself**: parsimony bounds are determined by earning-test pass-count, not by prior primitive-count. Refusing B on "too many today" grounds after the test passes is re-asserting parsimony-as-axiom in practice, which 3b.3b canonized against.

### Relationship to existing Commitment primitive (not duplication)

Gilbert is explicit that joint-commitment is **not individual-commitment-summed**: *"a commitment of two or more people, not a sum of personal commitments"* (*JC* 2013 Ch. 2). So the new primitive does not duplicate Commitment; it adds a second verb at a different compositional level. Canon after ADR-0050:

- **Commitment** — operational binding by parties (offer/accept/attest/fulfill). Symmetric-default reading (Bratman interlocking plans — each intending *"that we J"* with meshing sub-plans), asymmetric variant (ADR-0047 Layer 2 — asymmetric-commitment). Remains 2nd coordination verb. Bratman continuity-thesis lineage (Side-A-readable at this primitive).

- **Joint-commitment (new)** — collective-compositional binding that is not sum-of-individual-commitments (Gilbert/Tuomela/List-Pettit lineage). Explicitly sui generis: the "commitment of two or more people" that is constituted by open expression of readiness under common knowledge, rescindable only by concurrence, producing directed obligations + demand-rights of a different kind from moral ones. 6th coordination verb. Side-B-committed at this primitive.

The two verbs coexist: Commitment handles individual-scale bindings (including asymmetric-commitment); joint-commitment handles joint-scale bindings. A coordination act where A commits to B and B commits to A separately is two commitments; a coordination act where A and B jointly commit (e.g., walking-together, contract-between-two-parties-as-joint-undertaking, federation adopting a protocol-version) is one joint-commitment.

### Relationship to ADR-0047 asymmetric-commitment (Layer 2)

ADR-0047 admitted `asymmetric-commitment` as Commitment's party-structure asymmetry axis. With ADR-0050 admitting joint-commitment as distinct primitive, the natural extension is **asymmetric-joint-commitment**: joint-commitments where parties bind disproportionately (e.g., land-treaty between state and indigenous nation; sacred-joint-commitment between ancestor-lineage and successor-lineage; federation-joint-commitment where larger peer sets more of the protocol-surface). This extension is not authored in ADR-0050 (future canon work if operational pressure surfaces); the compositional availability is noted as canon-implication.

### Relationship to ADR-0048 participatory-sense-making citation

ADR-0048 cited participatory-sense-making (De Jaegher/Di Paolo 2007) as lineage for constructed-power without admitting it as Spore primitive. Post-ADR-0050, participatory-sense-making remains cited-lineage-not-primitive; joint-commitment is the admitted Side-B primitive. Participatory-sense-making and joint-commitment are different Side-B positions within the philosophy-of-sociality literature — Gilbert's joint-commitment is obligation-generating and cognitivist, while participatory-sense-making is meaning-emerging and enactive/embodied. Spore commits to the Gilbertian form as Side-B primitive; the enactive form remains accessible as mode-of-joint-commitment-operation (future canon could clarify if operational pressure warrants).

### Relationship to ADR-0001 pluriversal-incommensurability (held-as-tension)

ADR-0001 holds "one-world vs many-worlds" (ontological pluriversality) as tension. ADR-0050 resolves sociality-reducibility (individual-vs-collective compositional axis). These are **different clusters** in capstone (§2 divergence 4 vs §2 divergence 1) but **coupled** per capstone §8 item 12: pluriversal-tradition is also Side-B on reducibility. Cross-reference without duplication:

- Ontological pluriversality remains held (ADR-0001 unchanged).
- Sociality-reducibility is resolved Side-B (ADR-0050).
- Pluriversal-specific forms of joint-commitment (sacred-commitment between ancestor-lineage and successor-lineage; kinship-commitment under customary-ritual frame) operate under both: joint-commitment as primitive (ADR-0050), incommensurability-as-frame (ADR-0001). The canon-frame recognition accommodates pluriversal-joint-commitments without forcing one reducibility-ontology.

### Philosophical narrowing

Spore picks a specific position within Side B: Gilbert-cum-List/Pettit (joint-commitment as verb-layer primitive + supervenience-without-reducibility at Holon structural layer). Alternatives within Side B remain accessible but unadmitted:
- **Participatory-sense-making** (De Jaegher/Di Paolo): cited-lineage via ADR-0048 for constructed-power mode; not admitted as primitive.
- **We-mode** (Tuomela): adjacent-but-not-frozen per capstone §7; compatible with joint-commitment primitive-form.
- **Searle we-intention**: pre-linguistic-primitive reading; compatible with joint-commitment primitive-form.
- **Schmid plural pre-reflective self-awareness**: internal Side-B critique of all cognitivist framings; not resolved by ADR-0050.
- **Ludwig singularist reduction** (Side A): explicitly not-chosen.
- **Bratman continuity-thesis**: retained as Side-A-readable for individual-scale Commitment only.

The narrowing is a philosophical bet consistent with Spore's primitive-structure (Holon is already whole-part; List/Pettit is the natural supervenience framing; Gilbert provides the sharpest verb-layer operations among Side-B candidates). Future canon can re-engage alternative Side-B positions if operational pressure warrants.
