# ADR-0064 — Co-presence-Field-Condition Disposition: Adopt-with-Scope-Conditioning (Option i)

draft (2026-04-23; Option (i) adopt-with-scope-conditioning + sub-option i.1 prose-only per operator Step-2 decision-gate)

## Context

### Deferral source: ADR-0055 R-Enc-1 residue + Trigger E-5

ADR-0055 (encounter-as-composition-framing-note, post-Phase-3b, 2026-04-22) performed the dedicated earning-test evaluation ADR-0048 line 73 mandated for Encounter as primitive candidate. The evaluation found (a) FAILS at verb-primitive level, with the specific per-operation finding that `co-presence-establish` (common-knowledge of mutual attention) is *"genuinely-distinct-but-substrate-like: a Field-condition in which verbs fire, not a coordination act performed by agents"* (ADR-0055 line 103). ### Step 0.5 audit: nine operational cases

The earning-test evaluation enumerated nine per-instance-family Field operational cases and classified each as co-presence-requiring, co-presence-non-requiring, or dual-mode / ambiguous:

1. **BKC/Octo bioregional stewardship meetings** (co-presence-REQUIRING, strong). BKC quarterly federation meetings and cross-node gatherings are explicitly synchronous events. The encounter framing note §3.2 identifies attendance-pledge as an irreducibly-joint case where "each attendance-pledge references the others' attendance-pledges, and the jointness-structure is load-bearing (a meeting with no joint-attendance-commitment is a broadcast, not an encounter)." Co-presence is operationally load-bearing — it conditions whether the verb-compositions fire as encounter-hosted or as broadcast/asynchronous.

2. **Federation protocol-version negotiation** (DUAL-MODE). During negotiation and deliberation (drafting phase), co-presence creates common-knowledge conditions for participatory deliberation where participants share a situation and jointly modify proposals. During dissemination and adoption (ratification phase), protocol-version adoption is asynchronous — each node adopts independently. Structurally identical to ADR-0063's Signal dual-phase finding (PSM during formation / SR during dissemination).

3. **IC memory-governance curation** (DUAL-MODE). Individual-async curation (a single steward updates attribution independently) — no co-presence required; collective-deliberation curation (IC governance council meets to decide contested attributions) — co-presence operationally important. IC's primary mode appears to be individual-async in current operations.

4. **PM `pm:MatchProposal` progressive-disclosure dialogue** (NON-REQUIRING). PM grammar.md frames match encounters as Signal + Joint-commitment + Intent + Evidence within bounded Field-conditions per ADR-0055; the protocol design supports asynchronous progressive disclosure via message exchange. `pm:TemporalWindow` provides scheduling coordination rather than common-knowledge-of-mutual-attention.

5. **Joint-commitment formation (ADR-0050 paradigm)** (AMBIGUOUS). Multi-party-simultaneous by construction per ADR-0050, which is co-presence-like in the sense that all parties must jointly act in the same coordination moment, but does not require synchronous presence — sequential ratification within a defined window can also constitute joint-commitment per Gilbert's structure. The common-knowledge structure can be achieved asynchronously.

6. **Spec-DAG amendment deliberation** (DUAL-MODE). Per ADR-0041 text-authoritative discipline, spec-DAG amendments are authoritative when committed as text — an individual asynchronous act. But amendment proposals typically arise from deliberation (discussion threads, design-review meetings) where co-presence creates common-knowledge of the problem-space. ADR-0063 classified spec-DAG amendment as dual-phase for PSM; same structure applies for co-presence.

7. **Octo autonomous research** (NON-REQUIRING, strong). Single-agent operations involve no co-presence requirement by definition — an autonomous agent operating within a Field does not require another party's mutual attention to do coordination work. Stigmergic traces substitute for co-presence: Octo leaves evidence artifacts that others encounter asynchronously.

8. **Governance-artifact authoring conversations** (REQUIRING in sync mode; NON-REQUIRING in async mode). Collective authoring in synchronous sessions (facilitated group writing, pair-authoring) requires co-presence; asynchronous authoring (PR-review, async text-comments) does not. Conditional per session type.

9. **DW stand-ups / design-reviews** (DUAL-MODE). Async text-based stand-ups (Slack status, CLAUDE.md brief) require no co-presence; synchronous design-reviews (calls, screen-shares, pair-programming) are co-presence-requiring because common-knowledge-of-mutual-attention determines what can be jointly evaluated in real-time.

Aggregate classification: **2 co-presence-requiring / 2 co-presence-non-requiring / 5 dual-mode/ambiguous.** The 5/9 dual-mode ratio is the highest across the three structural-primitive audit sweep (ADR-0062 Membrane: 2 supporting / 3 counter / 3 ambiguous; ADR-0063 Signal: 3 PSM-supporting / 3 SR-supporting / 3 dual-phase; ADR-0064 Field: 2 requiring / 2 non-requiring / 5 dual-mode).

### Cross-tradition evaluation: resolving the ADR-0055 single-cluster block

The plan's authoritative rule on tradition-breadth (for overcoming the ADR-0055 R-Enc-1 single-cluster-support block) applies a 7-cluster taxonomy with admission thresholds: ≥3 full clusters for (a) Field sub-stratification or (d) property-on-Field; ≥2 clusters for (e) derived slug or (i) scope-condition; 1+ cluster acceptable for (f) decline / (g) framing-note / (h) park.

**Cluster A — Common-knowledge-philosophy** (FULL):
- Lewis (1969) *Convention: A Philosophical Study* — the logical structure of common knowledge as foundation for coordination conventions.
- Schiffer (1972) *Meaning* — formal common-knowledge treatment for communicative meaning.
- Chwe (2001) *Rational Ritual: Culture, Coordination, and Common Knowledge* — public rituals create common knowledge precisely because participants observe each other observing the same spectacle; common knowledge is the coordination mechanism. Counted under Cluster A per the Chwe-overlap counting rule (primarily canonical common-knowledge-philosophy work that happens to formalize common-knowledge game-theoretically).
- In-repo support: ADR-0055:103 and `docs/research/connections/canon-framing-encounter-as-composition.md:97` directly cite Lewis/Schiffer for co-presence-establish. Counts as 1 full cluster.

**Cluster B — Micro-sociology / interaction-order** (FULL — GENUINELY INDEPENDENT FROM CLUSTER A):
- Goffman (1963) *Behavior in Public Places: Notes on the Social Organization of Gatherings* — co-presence as primary analytical category for face-to-face interaction. "Focused interaction" (two or more people cooperating to sustain a single focus of cognitive and visual attention) is the sociological operationalization of co-presence-as-coordination-substrate. "Unfocused interaction" names the contrasting case where people are merely co-located without mutual attention.
- Goffman (1967) *Interaction Ritual: Essays on Face-to-Face Behavior* — interaction ritual as structured co-presence.
- Goffman (1974) *Frame Analysis: An Essay on the Organization of Experience* — frames as shared interpretive structures that participants jointly establish.
