# Governance Artifacts and Graph Projections

This document bridges the holonic/mycelial architecture and the pattern language. It defines how normative artifacts (visions, agreements, commitments) relate to each other, how they project into graphs, and how they connect to real-world state through sensors.

## Governance Artifacts

A governance artifact is any normative artifact that says "this is what we intend":

- **Visions** — long-horizon orientation (values, direction, constraints)
- **Agreements** — reciprocal commitments between parties
- **Declarations** — public signaling commitments
- **Roadmaps** — sequenced commitments translating vision into structured needs
- **Policies** — standing rules that govern behavior within a domain
- **Roles** — named positions with scope, authority, and accountability
- **Domain definitions** — bounded areas of responsibility and decision-making

All governance artifacts are commitments at different levels of specificity. A vision is a constitutional commitment ("we commit to this direction"). A roadmap is a sequenced commitment. A pool entry is an economic commitment. These form a commitment ecology — not a hierarchy where one replaces the other, but an ecology where different kinds serve different roles.

## The Coordination Ecology

Six coordination verbs — intent, commitment, joint-commitment, evidence, signal, reproduction — form a recurring loop across three structural primitives — field, holon, membrane. The within-episode loop (intent → commitment / joint-commitment → evidence → signal) is an ecology, not a pipeline — it circulates: evidence can generate new intents, commitments can reveal intents that weren't visible before, signals transmit live perturbation alongside attested evidence, and any of these verbs can feed any other. Commitment and joint-commitment are distinct compositional levels of binding: Commitment binds individual parties; joint-commitment binds parties jointly as a single irreducibly-collective act (Gilbert, *Joint Commitment* 2013). Reproduction (ADR-0049) operates at a distinct cross-episode time-scale: it is the coordination labor that sustains the verb-loop across time, actor turnover, and generational succession — the verb that makes the next loop-instantiation possible rather than running within one.

- **Intents** signal — they are pre-commitment coordination artifacts. An intent is a declared or inferred directional stance: "I want," "I offer," "I need," "I will if three others commit." Intents are where plurality enters. Not all intents become commitments; governance is partly about how intents are surfaced, negotiated, combined, deferred, or refused. The broad Spore sense of `intent` is the umbrella surface across the shared canon; PM's `pm:Intent` is a downstream protocol artifact that records one specialized, durable form of intent for matching.

- **Commitments** bind — accepted, governed, tracked. Commitments stabilize one or more intents into a durable relation with specified terms, parties, attestation, and fulfillment conditions.
    - *Three senses* of "commitment" recur in canon and must not be conflated: **operational commitments** (terms/parties/attestation/fulfillment — the coordination primitive itself); **orientation commitments** (visions and directional declarations — artifact-types that a coordination context may author); **constitutional commitments** (foundational value-choices the project publishes — the seven items at `project-vision.md` §"Constitutional Commitments"). The primitive is the operational sense; the others are artifact-types or meta-commitments.
    - *40-year REA lineage* (ADR-0053): The REA tradition has lived in disagreement on commitment-as-primitive for 40 years — McCarthy 1982 excluded → Geerts-McCarthy 2000 added → Hruby 2006 as pattern → Ševčík 2016 collapses to scheduled-event sub-states with firmness-degree → Foster 2024 (ValueFlows) makes firmness-of-plan partially derived from Intent. Gilbert/Tuomela treat commitment as sui generis; Bratman/Ludwig reduce it to stability-of-intention.
    - *Spore's stance* per ADR-0044 + ADR-0053: **commitment is primitive with illocutionary force per Gilbert/Searle/Tuomela**, explicitly rejecting the Ševčík scheduled-event-shadow reading. The corpus does not settle this; Spore names a position rather than reporting consensus.
    - *Joint vs. individual scales*: The joint-scale Side-B reading (Gilbert vs. Bratman at the *we-intend* layer) is closed via ADR-0050's `joint-commitment` admission as 9th primitive (below). The residual individual-scale Bratman-individual-reduction debate (commitment-as-stability-of-intention at the I-intend layer) remains philosophically open and operates as canonically-acknowledged residue (ADR-0053 R-Comm-1). Commitment operates at the individual-scale binding layer; joint-scale binding is a distinct verb.

- **Joint-commitments** bind jointly — the irreducibly-collective binding of two or more parties as a single coordination act, *not* a sum of personal commitments. Gilbert's *"commitment of two or more people"* (*Joint Commitment*, OUP 2013 Ch. 2) formed by open expression of readiness under common knowledge, rescindable only by concurrence (no unilateral withdrawal), producing directed obligations and demand-rights *"of a different kind"* from moral ones.
    - *Operations*: form-joint-commitment (shared expression of readiness); rescind-joint-commitment (concurrence required); hold-accountable-via-demand-right (directed obligation + standing to rebuke); extend-joint-commitment (scope-expansion by concurrent renewal).
    - *Compositional distinction*: Commitment binds individuals (two parties entering separate commitments is two commitments); joint-commitment binds parties jointly (two parties entering a commitment together is one joint-commitment).
    - *Paradigm case*: federation-scale protocol-version-adoption cannot be reduced to bilateral agreements because version-compatibility is multi-party-simultaneous by construction.
    - *Composition*: composes with ADR-0047's `asymmetric-commitment` mode to produce `asymmetric-joint-commitment` (parties binding disproportionately — land-treaty, sacred-joint-commitment with ancestor/land under ADR-0001, federation-joint-commitment with size-asymmetry).
    - *Canon slug*: `joint-commitment`; frozen slug `collective-agency` (v2) remains the broader umbrella for collective-capacity talk. Other Side-B primitive candidates (participatory-sense-making per De Jaegher/Di Paolo; we-mode per Tuomela; Searle we-intention) remain accessible as cited-lineage or alternative-positions without canon-admission.
    - Admitted 2026-04-22 per ADR-0050 (capstone §8 Tier-1 item 3 recommended action for Side-B; earning-test marginal on protocol-surface specifiability — Schmid 2014 circularity objection live — and strong on multi-scale operational implementation).

- **Evidence** validates — attested observations, fulfillment records, provenance-tracked claims. Evidence grounds the ecology in observed reality. Spore evidence is *attestation-of-execution*, not epistemological evidence: it tracks whether commitments were fulfilled and in what state, not what counts as knowledge in general. Fulfillment records are one subtype inside this larger family, not the whole of it: PM's fulfillment-facing evidence family is a protocol-layer specialization, while Spore keeps `evidence` as the broader epistemic relation.

- **Signals** transmit — live coordination perturbations, stigmergic traces, algedonic urgency (bypassing hierarchy when conditions demand). Distinct from Evidence: evidence attests state; signal transmits flow. Spore preserves signal-as-primitive even against Autopoiesis's reduction of Shannon-signal to structural coupling — a principled commitment to sender-receiver ontology at the **operational coordination-grammar layer** (supported across MARL/VSM/DS/ABM/REA/Trust), explicitly acknowledging the **enactive structural-coupling objection** (Maturana/Varela/Thompson/Di Paolo) as a standing critique at the cognitive-science / enactive-grounding layer that does not defeat operational signal-as-primitive (ADR-0053; capstone §8 Tier-2 item 9). Participatory-sense-making (De Jaegher/Di Paolo 2007) as a deeper Side-B answer remains held-open per capstone §8 Tier-3 item 16. See ADR-0007.

- **Reproduction** sustains — the coordination labor by which the verb-loop, the field-substrate, and established commitment-patterns persist across time, actor turnover, and generational succession. Reproduction operations include enact-succession (facilitator-role transfer, stewardship rotation, institutional succession — Debian DPL-rotation, W3C maturity levels), transmit-knowledge (apprenticeship, doc-DAG renewal, longitudinal evidence-transmission), replicate-commitment-pattern (ritual cycles, annual review cadences, standing-agreement renewal), and regenerate-field-capacity (the reproductive labor that sustains coordination substrate — Federici/Folbre/Kittay's primary political-economy subject). Distinct from the four within-episode verbs: intent/commitment/evidence/signal coordinate *inside* a coordination moment; reproduction coordinates *across* moments by making the next instantiation possible. Admitted 2026-04-22 per ADR-0049 (capstone §3 verdict "admit as primitive"; earning-test marginal on protocol-surface specifiability, strong on multi-scale operational implementation). Related to but distinct from `reproductive-commoning` (ADR-0002 cross-cutting doctrine — practice-lens on the visibility-of-reproductive-labor axis) and `care-commoning` (ADR-0045 cross-cutting doctrine — practice-lens on the asymmetric-relational axis): Reproduction is the primitive verb both doctrines operate over; the three elements overlap at reproductive-care-work without reducing to each other. Relates to ADR-0047's `asymmetric-commitment` — power-over-reproduction Federicist theme operates through asymmetric-commitment over the reproduction primitive. Canon slug `reproduction-continuity`. See ADR-0049 for earning-test results and care-reproduction gap-residue articulation.

**Structural primitives host the verbs.** Field (substrate), holon (part-whole recursive unit), membrane (interface) define the space the verb-loop operates in; they are not themselves in the loop. Field/holon/membrane are distinct primitives (ADR-0016): a holon-at-scale-N is NOT the same as a field-at-scale-N+1; the field is the space between holons while a holon is a bounded recursive unit.

- *Holon irreducibility* (ADR-0050): The whole is not reducible to an aggregation of parts. A holon exhibits organizational invariants and emergent properties that cannot be analytically recovered from constituent-analysis alone (List/Pettit supervenience-without-reducibility; Thompson dynamic co-emergence). ADR-0050 closes capstone §2's "holon is mere aggregation" concern by making the irreducibility clause explicit where ADR-0016 was agnostic. This structural commitment pairs with the operational Side-B commitment (joint-commitment as sui generis verb at the collective-compositional layer): irreducibility lives at both layers.
- *Holon terminological-orphan note* (ADR-0053): The part-whole-recursive *structure* is widely supported across coordination traditions (VSM Recursive System Theorem — logically equivalent to holon though Beer does not cite Koestler; List/Pettit supervenient group agents; Distributed Systems Holonic Modeling; REA Plan>Process>Event; Commons nested-enterprise Principle 8) while the *Koestler 1967 vocabulary* is rare in modern coordination-theory literature; adjacent terms (whole-part-relation, nested-agency, scale-linked-agency) overlap in pattern. Canon retains `holon` as the load-bearing slug per capstone §4 *"Not a demotion; a provenance note."*
- *Membrane permeability and double-boundary* (ADR-0053): Two paired analytic axes name how Membranes operate — **permeability** (Bollier-Helfrich semi-permeable; selectively gated passage of intents/commitments/signals/evidence) and **double-boundary** (Ostrom Cox-1A/1B; social inclusion boundary distinguished analytically from ecological / resource boundary). Both axes coexist with `asymmetric-membrane` (ADR-0047 Layer 3 power-axis on the authorize function) and remain distinct from frozen-vocab `filtering-membrane` (an opposition primitive about over-filtering as failure mode).
- *Relational identity* (ADR-0051): `relational-identity` names the whole-emergent-property of a holon — the sense in which a holon's identity is constituted through relations (ties, kinship, structural coupling, commons-nesting, recursion-closure) rather than carried by any individual part. Five load-bearing traditions converge (VSM S5-closure; Care Ethics constitutive-relations; Pluriversal kinship-constitution; Autopoiesis/4E structural-coupling; Commons Nested-I). Capstone §3 paraphrase-test verdict is partial: Pluriversal kinship-identity is flagged as "irreducible" and operates compatibly under both the canon-posture frame (relational-identity as Holon property) and the held-tension frame (ADR-0001 pluriversal-incommensurability) per ADR-0050's dual-operability pattern. `relational-identity` is a property-of-primitive, not a separate primitive; cryptographic / persistent-handle / authorship identity-readings coexist as distinct buckets in their existing canonical homes (Membrane signing; federation-protocol persistence; Commitment/Evidence attribution).

**Field is internally stratified into rule-levels** (ADR-0046; Ostrom 2005; Kiser & Ostrom 1982). Three levels of action-situation rules operate within a field: *operational* rules govern day-to-day coordination (how verb-loop operations are surfaced, entered, recorded); *collective-choice* rules govern how operational rules are made, modified, and enforced; *constitutional* rules govern who is eligible to participate at the collective-choice level. The levels are recursive — action at each level is itself an action situation. Seven rule-type axes decompose any rule configuration inside a level (boundary / position / choice / aggregation / information / payoff / scope); these are available analytic machinery for protocol design, not a required enumeration. The rule-in-use / rule-in-form distinction extends ADR-0041's text-authoritative discipline to the rule layer: working rules are canonical, formal rules project that practice, and divergence is surfaced as operational discrepancy requiring explicit reconciliation. Rule-operation intuitions are already covered by the coordination verbs — enact-rule is a Commitment, invoke-rule is a Signal, propose-amendment is an Intent — so the stratification supplies the *level* at which a rule operates rather than a new primitive for rule-operation itself. Commoning-school refusal of the stratification (Bollier-Helfrich OntoShift) and Pluriversal customary-ritual framings (held under ADR-0001) are acknowledged at frame-level; the stratification is a scoped-grammar commitment, not a universal-ontological claim.

**Power across primitives** (ADR-0047 + ADR-0048). Power/Authority is not a distinct primitive; it is articulated across the grammar as three modes-across-primitives (Johar, *Power Cannot Be Allocated*, 2026): **allocational** (structural capacity; stabilizes), **expressive** (articulation of meaning, desire, dissent; vitalizes), **constructed** (situational agency emerging in response; adapts). All three modes are canon-necessary; over-engineering any one produces recognizable failure.

*Allocational mode* (ADR-0047) decomposes across three asymmetry axes: Field carries `authority-over-rule-levels` (the capacity to set, amend, and enforce rules at each level of the 3-level stack — Debian §4/§5/§6 enumerated powers, PEP 13 Steering Council, V. Ostrom polycentric governance, VSM S5/S3 stratification); Commitment carries `asymmetric-commitment` (party-structure asymmetry where one party binds disproportionately — Baier/Jackson/Folbre/Kittay asymmetric-vulnerability; caregiver/dependent; state/subject; sacred-commitment with ancestor/land); Membrane carries `asymmetric-membrane` (authorize-capacity distributed asymmetrically — platform-gatekeeping, admission authority, ritual/elder-consensus authorization). The three axes together express the four structural modes identified in the 17-tradition corpus (enumerated-powers, asymmetric-vulnerability, power-capture-as-compound-failure, relational-spiritual authority).

*Expressive mode* (ADR-0048) operates across the coordination verbs: Intent carries articulation of meaning, desire, and dissent (where plurality enters the grammar); Signal transmits dissent and reframing as live perturbation; Evidence attests perception including contested perception. Expressive power is not a primitive but a quality of how these verbs operate; Spore's contestability commitment binds the grammar to enable rather than suppress it.

*Constructed mode* (ADR-0048) operates across verb-loop dynamics: agency emerges situationally through the Intent → Commitment → Evidence → Intent cycle under conditions that exceed pre-allocated structure. Signal's pre-reactive propagation is one mechanism; the loop's metabolic rate (Johar's "feel → narrate → respond → learn → re-feel" loop-fluency framing) is the substrate. Constructed power cannot be codified or role-assigned — it emerges from skilled loop-participation under space, mission, resources, and distributed knowledge (Johar's four enabling conditions).

Johar's **substitution trap** (*"One can assign authority without generating comprehension. One can define responsibility without producing commitment."*) is canon-legible failure mode complementing ADR-0005's decentralization-myth bundle: allocational moves that substitute for rather than support expressive/constructed capacity reproduce the same failure-shape decentralization-theater names at bundle level. ADR-0005's critique-frame composes with ADR-0047's allocational substrate and ADR-0048's expressive/constructed mode articulation; canon expresses critique, mechanism, and animating dynamics together. Care-commoning (ADR-0045) is the doctrine-lens over Commitment's asymmetric-care-relations; `asymmetric-commitment` is the underlying party-asymmetry axis — lens and machinery coexist without conflict. Johar's power-with-via-care framing deepens ADR-0045's doctrine-layer commitment without modifying its structure.

**Artifact-types some coordination contexts author.** Visions, roadmaps, agreements, policies, role definitions, domain definitions — organize intents and commitments at longer time-horizons. These are not primitives in Spore's grammar; they are how some contexts (typically large-collective, long-horizon) structure their intent+commitment loops. Their presence is a property of the coordination context, not a requirement of the grammar. Learning is a meta-property of a healthy loop, not a separable element in it. Encounter (federation meetings, match-events, design-reviews, cross-federation compose-events) is an event-scope-shape hosting Signal + Joint-commitment + Intent + Evidence composition within bounded Field-conditions — not a primitive but a compositional pattern canon-legibly anchored via ADR-0055 (derived glossary slug `encounter`; framing-note at `docs/research/connections/canon-framing-encounter-as-composition.md`; federation-encounter parked as pattern-library admission candidate under triggers E-1 through E-5).

**Phase-dependence.** In discovery and complex-coordination phases, the full primitive loop is load-bearing. In maintenance-mode and routine-operation contexts where commitment terms are stable, the loop can compress to commitment → evidence, with new intents surfaced only when evidence reveals divergence. The salience of each primitive varies by coordination phase; the primitive status does not.

**Loop-fluency and constructed power** (ADR-0048). The metabolic rate at which the verb-loop cycles — Johar's "feel → narrate → respond → learn → re-feel" (*Mycelial Sovereignty*, 2026) — is the dynamic substrate for constructed power. Compressed cycles convert environmental drift into adaptive momentum; stretched cycles ossify coordination around outdated intents and commitments. Loop-fluency is not a primitive but a property of how the loop is allowed to operate: over-determined interfaces force premature categorization and lengthen cycle time; distributed comprehension and space-for-interpretation shorten it. Smallest-viable-locus-of-sensing-and-learning (Johar's mycelial sovereignty) is the design principle that places loop-authority at the scale where feedback is tightest.

## Dual Representation

Every governance artifact has two representations:

- **Narrative form** for humans — text as constitutional statement, readable and debatable
- **Graph projection** for machines — queryable, composable, diffable, groundable to world state within a node or through selectively materialized views

Text is authoritative; graph is a derived view. Constitutional artifacts are authored as text, and graph representations are generated from that text through tooling (frontmatter parsers, dependency extractors, entity resolvers — currently `koi-processor/scripts/ingest_spec_dag.py`). When text and graph disagree, text is canonical and the graph is regenerated to match. This direction of derivation is deliberate: text is the surface where humans debate and revise, and graph is the surface where machines query and compose. The two representations are complementary but not peers — one authors, one derives.

## Graph Projections

Different aspects of the coordination ecology are best captured by different graph structures. These are not separate databases — they are projections of one living system, each revealing different structure. They should be read as conceptual projections over a coordination ecology, not as one always-available distributed query substrate.

**Tier structure** (ADR-0058, Phase 2c Item 3 realignment per Phase 1 §C-11 earning-test): three *primary projections* operate at foundation-level — each has an independent schema, a materialization path in a running-system implementation, a query pattern, and a non-join use case. Five *view-templates* are composable over the primaries rather than primary at foundation-level — each is a legitimate useful view whose schema is a specialization of one or more primaries, or whose materialization is substantially derivable from joins over primary storage. The "graph" label is a convenience: different projections differ structurally (DAGs for dependency, hypergraphs for n-ary relations, labelled multigraphs for value-relations), and structure-distinctness does not by itself earn primary-at-foundation-level status. ADR-0058 supersedes-via-prose ADR-0036's primary-set (Constitutional / Roadmap DAG / Intent hypergraph) on earning-test grounds; ADR-0036's graph-structure-distinctness reading remains available as research-lens.

### Primary projections

1. **Constitutional graph** — values, goals, principles, constraints, domains, and the relations between them (supports, constrains, conflicts_with, governs). **Materialization**: spec-DAG tooling (`koi-processor/scripts/ingest_spec_dag.py`) parses frontmatter `depends_on` + related_to relationships, extracting governance-memory structure from text-authoritative canon (per ADR-0041).
2. **Commitment graph** — actors, pools, offers, needs, attestations, fulfillment state. **Materialization**: running commitment-pool state in BKC/Octo federation and Poietic Match. Party-structure, attestation-weight, and fulfillment-tracking are stored and queried live. Joint-commitment binding (ADR-0050) and asymmetric-commitment binding (ADR-0047 Layer 2) are both representable in this projection.
3. **Epistemic graph** (public-facing gloss: "knowledge graph") — entities, claims, evidence, attestations, provenance, sensor outputs. **Materialization**: KOI substrate — `personal-koi` + `unified-search` — provides entity resolution, knowledge facts, and bridge-note intake across federation. Tracks not just what is known, but what counts as knowing.

### View-templates composable over primaries

- **Roadmap DAG** — specialization-view of Constitutional ordering initiatives, milestones, and dependencies by sequence. Directed acyclic: work flows from vision toward evidence. Derivable from Constitutional + sequence annotations.
- **Intent hypergraph** — Commitment-pre-stage expressing multi-party offers, needs, and conditions as hyperedges (one intent connecting several actors, resources, timeframes, conditions) before the pre-commitment stage concludes in either binding or retirement. Binary edges are too small for this stage — hypergraphs capture the true structure.
- **Event graph** — temporal projection over Commitment + Epistemic event streams (what changed, when, due to what, from which node). Useful as a temporal-dynamics query layer; substantially surfaceable from commitment + attestation tables without a separate Event schema.
- **Routing/flow graph** — Commitment-pool flow projection showing how resources, obligations, and information circulate through pools and networks. Where abundance meets need, where capacity flows. Substantially covered by Commitment's pool + attestation structure.
- **Discourse graph** — governance-revision layer over Constitutional + Epistemic — questions, proposals, arguments, objections, decisions. The self-reflective layer: how the coordination ecology reasons about and governs its own evolution. Argument-attestation is a distinct attestation class (claim-against-claim rather than claim-about-world); whether Discourse earns primary-at-foundation-level status if and when a full implementation matures is a future question, parked.

Each view-template is a legitimate projection; none has the independent schema + materialization + non-join use case profile that earns primary-at-foundation-level status under ADR-0058's earning-test. Detailed specification of view-templates is parked for a future pattern-library doc.

## Holon and Field at Adjacent Scales

A node is often a coherent interface over an internal graph. From outside, a team is a point — one participant in a network. From inside, it is a graph of roles, commitments, intents, and evidence.

That scale relation does not make `holon` and `field` synonymous. The node is holonic when what matters is bounded recursive agency: a whole that is also a part. The graph-like coordination surface inside or around that node is field-like when what matters is distributed relational medium: the substrate through which intents, commitments, evidence, and learning circulate. At the next scale up, the coordination surface produced by lower-scale holons may appear as a field for higher-scale holons. That is the scale relation between the primitives, not a reason to collapse them.

## Grounding Through Sensors

Constitutional artifacts describe desired states. Sensors describe actual states. The gap between them is what drives action.

- **Sensor nodes** ingest external state (wikis, repositories, web content, IoT data) into the epistemic graph
- **Ecological sensors** — water quality, species counts, land use
- **Economic sensors** — commitment pool state, token flows, settlement events
- **Social sensors** — meeting transcripts, governance decisions, community signals

When normative graphs (what we intend) and epistemic graphs (what we observe) are both machine-readable, the gap between them becomes computable. This connects to active inference: mismatch between vision graph and sensor graph creates latent intent-pressure — the directional force that, when made legible, becomes an inferred intent.

## Structure and Flow in the Graph Model

The graph types relate to the dual-axis architecture:

- **DAGs** provide acyclic dependency resolution for artifact derivation and document provenance (the governance-memory discipline) — derivation cycles would create undefined source-of-truth, so spec-DAG structure is acyclic by necessity. This is a document-discipline property, not a claim about governance authority. Real governance structures may include authority cycles (mutual accountability, polycentric cross-oversight, recursive democratic revision); legitimacy depends on structural coupling of authority to consequence — see [Structural Legitimacy](./structural-legitimacy.md) — not on acyclicity of authority flow. Value relations can also be cyclic: justice ↔ equality is a reinforcing loop, not a paradox. DAGs apply to artifact derivation and dependency, not to all relations.
- **Hypergraphs** represent multi-party relational entanglement (the network axis) — cyclic graphs here create resilience through mutual commitment and feedback. Stability does not require acyclicity: triangles are stable in engineering, feedback loops create homeostasis in biology. DAGs compress dependency; cycles create resilience. Both are needed.
- **Event graphs** represent temporal flow
- **Epistemic graphs** represent semantic structure, provenance, and what counts as knowing
- **Routing/flow graphs** represent circulation of resources and information

Structure without flow is a dead archive. Flow without structure is noise. The coordination ecology needs both: the DAG that orders governance and dependency (where circularity creates paradox), and the hypergraph/flow that routes what's alive (where circularity creates resilience).
