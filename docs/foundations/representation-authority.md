# Representation Authority

Spore canon exists simultaneously across multiple representation layers — markdown text authored by humans, graph projections derived by tooling, sensor readings grounded in phenomena, human attestation grounded in witness, and agent-generated summaries distilled by AI. Representation-authority is the doctrine that specifies *which layer wins when they disagree, in which direction derivation flows, and how inter-layer conflicts are adjudicated.*

## Core Claim

Canon representations can and do disagree. A vision declares a pool is funded, and the pool-balance sensor shows zero. A graph projection lists a dependency the text has since removed. An attestation records a meeting the transcript-summarizer missed. A human reviewer re-reads a passage differently than the AI summary asserted. Without an explicit inter-layer precedence doctrine, every such case becomes a hidden authority conflict: two readers of the same canon reach different conclusions about what canon "actually says," without any principled way to adjudicate.

ADR-0041 (text-authoritative-representation, 2026-04-22) closed this defect at the text-vs-graph layer: text is authoritative; graph is a derived view; when they disagree, text wins and graph regenerates. F4 representation-authority extends that same direction-of-derivation discipline across the full five-layer canon representation surface (text / graph / sensor / attestation / agent-summary).

The extension is not a simple generalization. At the text-vs-graph layer, direction-of-derivation is clean: humans author text; tooling parses text into graph. At the sensor-vs-text layer, direction-of-derivation inverts depending on text-type: for specification-text (vision, ADRs, doctrine), text authors and sensor reads; for fact-reporting-text (pool balance, attestation record, sensor readout), sensor grounds and text records. The load-bearing principled-rule is the **fact-vs-specification text-type distinction** — the inter-layer precedence depends on whether the text in question specifies what should be or reports what is.

Sensor-and-oracle-governance (F1, ADR-0073) established intra-modality governance within each of sensor / attestation / agent-summary — who selects a sensor, who maintains it, how disagreements within a modality are resolved. F4 is the complementary layer: when representation-sources across modalities disagree about a specific claim, which representation is canonically authoritative, and how is the adjudication carried out?

## Scope

A **representation layer** is a canon surface with a distinct provenance: markdown authored by humans, graph derived by tooling, sensor readings from instruments, attestation from witnesses, and agent-generated summaries from AI. Five layers are in scope:

- **Text-authoritative canon** — markdown with YAML frontmatter, authored by humans under canon-review discipline. The source surface for specification-text (vision, ADRs, doctrine, commitments-as-authored).
- **Graph-derived canon** — the spec-DAG, entity registry, and relation graph generated by tooling (currently `koi-processor/scripts/ingest_spec_dag.py`) from text-authoritative markdown. A projection, not an authored surface.
- **Sensor readings** — outputs of instrumented devices or computed pipelines (per F1 sensor-oracle-governance.md §2): ecological, economic, social sensors. Grounded in phenomenon.
- **Attestation** — sworn statements, witnessed signals, or community attestation produced by humans in declared attester-roles (per F1 §2). Grounded in human judgment.
- **Agent-summary** — LLM summaries, agent-produced synthesis reports, signal-chain distillations (per F1 §2). Derived from inputs via an agent process.

**In scope** for this doctrine: inter-layer precedence — what wins when representations across these five layers disagree about the same subject, and how conflicts are adjudicated.

**Out of scope** for this doctrine:

- **Intra-modality governance** — how a single layer's selection, calibration, maintenance, contestation, disagreement, interpretation, and absence operate within that layer. That is F1 sensor-oracle-governance scope.
- **Response-to-mismatch doctrine** — what canon or an instance does operationally after inter-layer precedence resolves who-is-authoritative (alert, proposal, routing, human review, no change). That is F5 actuator-logic scope (forthcoming).
- **Failure-mode taxonomy** — systematic enumeration of how representation-authority can break (maintainer capture, stale text, corrupt graph, adversarial attestation). That is F6 failure-modes scope (forthcoming); this doctrine names failure-shapes in context but does not enumerate taxonomically.
- **Meta-layers** — operator-ratification (the process producing text-authoritative canon), historical-ADR state (the lifecycle machinery per ADR-0067 enum), session-memory / claude-mem / retrospective / synthesis (operator-facing meta-canon surfaces). These are not representation layers of the same kind; some may be addressed by F8 external-validation-loop if authored.

The five-layer inventory is held via principled-rule throughout: wherever a specific representation layer is named, it appears as one instance of the broader pattern. The governance-shape is one doctrine (inter-layer precedence); the adjudications are many.

## Structural Doctrine — Rule-Level Stratification

Representation-authority operates across the three Ostrom rule-levels inherited from ADR-0046 (Field rule-level stratification) and applied at the Phase 4 foundation layer. F1 established the inheritance pattern; F4 is the second application.

- **Constitutional rule layer** — who has standing to establish precedence-hierarchies for an instance or federation; what membership or federation-participation requires to contribute to representation-governance; the boundary of representation-sovereignty at federation scale. Constitutional-rule decisions establish *who may play the game* of representation-precedence at all.
- **Collective-choice rule layer** — how precedence is assigned, revised, or contested within the constitutional frame; the protocol for declaring a context-override; the protocol for appealing an inter-layer adjudication. Collective-choice decisions establish *the rules of play* for precedence within the constitutional frame.
- **Operational rule layer** — day-to-day application of precedence: which layer wins in a specific dispute; routine adjudication of inter-layer conflicts that fall within already-declared default + context-override structures. Operational decisions are *moves within the game*.

Rule-level separation is structural clarity, not three separate doctrines. A single representation-authority act often invokes all three levels: routine adjudication is operational; disputes about which context-override applies escalate to collective-choice; challenges to the precedence-regime itself escalate to constitutional.

Routing through the rule-stack is how contested inter-layer cases resolve without doctrine-specified algorithms (see §5 Precedence Rule and §4.5 below).

## Doctrine Per Layer

The five layers are presented with a principle statement (authority-claim at the layer), a rule-level decomposition (who sets / contests / applies precedence at the layer), and inter-layer-conflict treatment (when this layer conflicts with others, how resolved).

### §4.1 Text-Authoritative Canon

**Principle**: text-authoritative canon is the source surface for specification-text per ADR-0041. Humans author markdown with YAML frontmatter under canon-review discipline; the text IS the specification. When canon is a specification of what should be (vision, doctrine, agreement, design choice, ADR disposition, commitment-as-authored), text wins against derived layers.

**Rule-level decomposition**:

- Constitutional-rule: who has standing to author canon; canon-review protocol participation; federation-level canon authorship.
- Collective-choice-rule: the canon-review protocol by which text is amended; ADR machinery; merge discipline.
- Operational-rule: individual text edits, pull requests, draft→active transitions.

**Inter-layer-conflict treatment**: when text-authoritative canon specifying what-should-be disagrees with any other layer's reading, text wins by construction. The other layer's reading is either out-of-date (graph not yet regenerated), concerns a different subject (sensor measures X, canon specifies Y), or is evidence of a condition the text has not yet incorporated. No layer demotes text-authoritative specification-text; however, specification-text can be amended through canon-review when evidence from other layers motivates revision.

### §4.2 Graph-Derived Canon

**Principle**: graph is derived from text. The spec-DAG, entity registry, and relation graph are projections of text-authoritative canon, generated by tooling. Graph has no independent authority; it regenerates from text whenever text changes.

**Rule-level decomposition**:

- Constitutional-rule: what tooling is authorized to derive graph from text; tooling-maintainer assignment; federation-level graph-sovereignty.
- Collective-choice-rule: the derivation protocol (which fields are parsed, which relations are extracted, what enum values are valid); tooling amendments.
- Operational-rule: actual derivation runs; validator execution; graph-query responses.

**Inter-layer-conflict treatment**: when graph disagrees with text, the graph is regenerated (per ADR-0041). When graph disagrees with sensor / attestation / agent-summary, graph's authority depends on the text it derives from: if derived from specification-text, graph inherits specification-authority; if derived from fact-reporting-text, graph inherits fact-reporting-authority (and may be behind live sensor/attestation data until next regeneration). Graph never wins on its own authority; it wins only by reflecting the layer that grounds it.

### §4.3 Sensor Readings

**Principle**: sensor readings ground coordination in what actually happened or is actually the case (per F1 §1 Core Claim). Sensor-authority operates over fact-reporting-text-types — claims about what is the case at a given time, place, or under specified conditions.

**Rule-level decomposition**:

- Constitutional-rule: who has standing to declare a sensor as canonical for a given phenomenon; federation-level sensor-sovereignty.
- Collective-choice-rule: the sensor-selection protocol (F1 §4.1); interpretation-authority grant (F1 §4.6); context-override authoring.
- Operational-rule: routine reading capture; oracle interpretation; evidence-logging.

**Inter-layer-conflict treatment**: when sensor readings disagree with text about a fact-reporting subject (the pool balance, a specific measurement, a meeting occurrence), sensor wins in its canonical domain per §5 context-override machinery. Text remains authoritative for the specification that governs what the sensor is measuring for (what counts as a valid reading; what thresholds trigger action). When sensor disagrees with attestation on the same fact-reporting subject, F1 §4.5 multi-sensor-disagreement discipline applies (protocol-bound resolution; unresolved disagreement as held-epistemic-tension). When sensor disagrees with agent-summary derived from that sensor's readings, sensor wins (agent-summary derives).

### §4.4 Attestation

**Principle**: attestation grounds coordination in human judgment — sworn witness, in-role observation, community signal (per F1 §2). Attestation-authority operates over fact-reporting-text-types where human judgment is load-bearing for the claim (a meeting occurred; a commitment was fulfilled; a stewardship role was exercised).

**Rule-level decomposition**:

- Constitutional-rule: who is eligible to attest in which role; federation-level attester-sovereignty.
- Collective-choice-rule: attester-selection and reliability-tracking (F1 §4.1 + §4.2); context-override authoring for attestation-privileged domains.
- Operational-rule: actual attestation events; attestation-log maintenance.

**Inter-layer-conflict treatment**: when attestation disagrees with text about a fact-reporting subject for which attestation is the canonical grounding (meeting-attendance; fulfillment claims; witnessed events), attestation wins in its canonical domain per §5 context-override machinery. When attestation disagrees with sensor on the same subject, F1 §4.5 multi-sensor-disagreement discipline applies. When attestation disagrees with agent-summary derived from that attester's statements, attestation wins (agent-summary derives). When two attestations disagree, F1 §4.5 discipline routes through the rule-stack; if unresolved, held-epistemic-tension.

### §4.5 Agent-Summary

**Principle**: agent-summary is post-hoc derivation — LLM distillation, agent-produced synthesis, summarizer output. The summary derives from inputs (text, sensor readings, attestations, transcripts) via an agent process. Agent-summary carries no independent grounding; it inherits authority-weight from the provenance of its inputs and the quality of the derivation process.

Per I1 NARROW scope: F4 treats agent-summary as post-hoc description. It does NOT treat canonical-AI-interpretations routinely consulted as authority (I2 scope; deferred pending operational pressure). It does NOT treat session-memory / claude-mem / retrospective / synthesis artifacts (F8 external-validation-loop territory if authored; meta-canon-and-operator-memory surface, not a representation layer of the same kind).

**Rule-level decomposition**:

- Constitutional-rule: what agent processes are authorized to summarize canon; model-and-prompt disclosure requirements (F1 §4.2); federation-level AI-summary-sovereignty.
- Collective-choice-rule: summary-process selection and lifecycle management (F1 §4.3); regeneration-audit protocols; context-override authoring for AI-summary roles.
- Operational-rule: actual summary generation; summary-log retention; re-generation audits.

**Inter-layer-conflict treatment**: when agent-summary disagrees with any source layer it derives from (text, sensor, attestation), the source layer wins. Agent-summary is always lower-priority than its inputs. When agent-summary disagrees with another agent-summary over the same inputs, F1 §4.5 multi-summary-disagreement discipline applies (regeneration-audit; protocol-bound resolution; held-epistemic-tension if unresolved). Sense-making-mode scope-conditioning (per ADR-0063): agent-summaries produced in sender-receiver-transmission mode (pure distillation) carry different weight than summaries produced in constitutively-interactive-emergence mode (iterative refinement between operator and agent). Foundation doctrine accepts both modes; mode is situational; neither is privileged as canon-authoritative over the source layer, but the mode affects how regeneration-audit is interpreted.

Asymmetry acknowledgment (per F1 §6 Open Questions): AI-summary processes carry a specific authority-decay challenge. The model-provider is often outside the instance's governance reach, and structural-legitimacy coupling (per `docs/foundations/structural-legitimacy.md`) is weaker by construction. Foundation doctrine names this explicitly; operational protocols (disclosure, regeneration-audit, fallback-to-human-attester) are pattern-layer work.

## Precedence Rule — Default, Context-Overrides, Appeal-Protocol

The doctrine's central move: how are inter-layer conflicts adjudicated? The hybrid precedence-shape commits to three components — a default rule that resolves the common case, context-overrides that carry domain-specific precedence, and an appeal-protocol that routes genuinely-contested cases through the rule-stack.

### §5.1 Default

The default precedence depends on **text-type**. This is the load-bearing principled-rule of the doctrine: the inter-layer precedence-direction depends on whether the text in question specifies what should be or reports what is. The distinction is context-of-use, not intrinsic-to-the-sentence — the same prose could function as specification in one context (in a vision doc authoring what the pool aims to do) and as report in another (in an evidence log recording what the pool did).

- **Specification-text default** (text-about-what-should-be): **text-authoritative** per ADR-0041. Specification-text includes vision, ADRs, doctrine, commitments-as-authored, design choices, agreements, protocol declarations. Graph derives from text. Sensor / attestation are read-but-not-authoritative for the specification itself (they may inform whether to amend the specification, but the specification's current content is what text says). Agent-summary derives from text plus cited sources.
- **Fact-reporting-text default** (text-about-what-is): **sensor / attestation authoritative** where available. Fact-reporting-text includes pool-balance records, attestation entries, sensor readouts, meeting-occurrence logs, evidence claims, and other text that reports sensed or attested reality rather than specifying a normative commitment. Text is a derived record of the fact-reporting-layer's authority; graph inherits that derived status; agent-summary derives from the fact-reporting inputs.

The distinction preserves ADR-0041's text-authoritative doctrine for the vast majority of canon (most canon body is specification-text) while acknowledging that fact-reporting contexts have a different natural authority-direction. Canon body is mixed-text-type at the page level: a single foundation doc can carry specification-text in its doctrine sections and fact-reporting-text in its examples or evidence-log annexes.

### §5.2 Context-Overrides

Instance-level or federation-level decisions can declare specific domains where a non-default precedence applies. Context-overrides are collective-choice-rule decisions (per §3) that stabilize a domain-specific precedence in advance of contested cases arising.

Examples of context-overrides that commonly apply in Spore instances:

- **Commitment-pool balance**: sensor > text. The on-chain or ledger-recorded balance is canonical; text statements about the balance are reports subject to the sensor reading.
- **Pluriversal-context**: held-tension per ADR-0001. No single layer wins; explicit non-resolution is canon-legible. This override applies when multiple traditions interpret the same sensor-reading or attestation through structurally different ontologies.
- **Reproduction-continuity Evidence**: F1 §5 governs longitudinal-attestation vs. single-reading Evidence subspecies. F4 routes to F1 for these cases; the precedence within longitudinal-attestation Evidence (the sustained track-record) applies rather than any single-moment reading.
- **Federation-scale sensor-sovereignty**: federated precedence-rules may diverge from instance-level defaults when a sensor is consumed by multiple federations with different rule-stacks (flagged in §6 as open concern pending F7 minimum-viable-spore-instance work).
- **Canon-review-in-progress**: during active canon-review, specification-text adjacent to contested content is treated as held rather than authoritative for the scope of the review. This is a procedural override; once the review closes, normal default applies.

Context-overrides are canon-legible: an instance or federation publishes its declared overrides; undeclared context-overrides are not authoritative.

### §5.3 Appeal-Protocol

When default plus declared context-overrides still produce ambiguity — or when a context-override itself is contested — the doctrine routes adjudication through the Ostrom 3-level rule-stack:

- **Operational-rule routing** — routine application of precedence by those with operational standing in the domain (a pool-operator resolving a pool-balance discrepancy between sensor and text; an attester's statement reconciling a meeting-attendance disagreement).
- **Collective-choice escalation** — disputes about which context-override applies, or whether the default is being correctly invoked, escalate to those with collective-choice standing (canon-review participants; context-override authors; protocol-amendment authors).
- **Constitutional-rule escalation** — disputes about the precedence-regime itself (should text-authoritative be the default for this class of canon? should a specific domain have a context-override? does pluriversal-tension apply here?) escalate to those with constitutional standing (federation members; canon-review at full ceremony).

Routing through the rule-stack is the foundation-layer commitment. The algorithm used at each level (voting threshold, consensus rule, ombuds review, held-tension declaration) is pattern or protocol-layer work, not foundation-layer.

### §5.4 Unresolved Inter-Layer Disagreement as Held-Epistemic-Tension

When default, context-override, and rule-stack appeal all fail to produce unambiguous resolution, the disagreement is canonically held rather than force-resolved. This inherits F1 §4.5 multi-sensor-disagreement discipline and ADR-0001 pluriversal-incommensurability held-tension pattern.

Three properties of held-epistemic-tension at the inter-layer level:

1. **Disagreement is canon-legible** — it is recorded in canon, not silently collapsed into one layer winning by fiat.
2. **Holding is provisional** — held-tension invites further work (additional sensors, additional attestation, revised context-override, canon-review). It is not permanent closure.
3. **Downstream coordination continues** — holding an inter-layer disagreement does not block downstream coordination that depends on what IS agreed across layers; it scopes the disagreement narrowly.

Held-epistemic-tension is canon-legible failure of first-pass resolution, not canon-legible failure per se. It is a honest acknowledgment that some inter-layer conflicts are not adjudicable by rule-stack routing alone.

## Open Questions

Representation-authority is a foundational commitment but not a solved problem. Several concerns are flagged here as foundation-layer residue; operational treatment is deferred to pattern / protocol layer or to downstream foundation docs.

- **Pluriversal interpretation-authority across layers**: when multiple traditions interpret the same sensor-reading, attestation, or agent-summary through structurally different ontologies, the default precedence-direction itself may be contested. Canon-legible signal: this is likely a held-tension in the shape of ADR-0001, not a resolvable disagreement. Operational-layer treatment — how instances and federations coordinate across pluriversal interpretations of shared representations — is deferred to future pattern / protocol work.
- **AI-summary-authority-decay and model-lifecycle coupling**: the authority-weight of agent-summary depends on model provenance, prompt disclosure, and regeneration-auditability (per F1 §4.2). Current canon treats model-providers as external-to-instance governance, which makes the structural-legitimacy coupling (per `docs/foundations/structural-legitimacy.md`) weaker by construction. Whether agent-summary authority should be re-evaluated if canonical-AI-interpretations become routinely consulted (I2 scope) is an open question; F4 defers this to future pattern-layer work or a scope-expansion ADR.
- **Cross-modality oracle composition precedence**: when a compound oracle composes machine-sensor + human-attester + AI-summary inputs into a single interpretation (per F1 §6), the compound oracle's precedence relative to its individual-modality inputs is not covered by F4 (F4 treats modalities as distinct layers). Open question: does compound-oracle output inherit the highest-authority input's weight, or does it carry distinct compound-authority? Deferred to pattern-layer composition work.
- **Federated precedence across overlapping federations**: when a representation-authority decision is governed by multiple federations with different rule-stacks, precedence-at-the-federation-scale becomes contested. Parked for F7 minimum-viable-spore-instance (membership-and-boundary foundation) and ADR-0068 federation-encounter pattern (federation-scale coordination-recipe) to operationalize jointly.
- **Revision-triggers under which F4 doctrine may need updating**: F4 is authored under thin operational-evidence base. Few inter-layer conflicts have fired in Spore's current state; the doctrine is proactive canon rather than reactive canon. Explicit revision-triggers for F4 are: (i) observed cases where the default + context-override + rule-stack structure produces operationally-problematic outcomes; (ii) cross-tradition convergence on a different precedence-shape (e.g., if multiple real-world implementations converge on strict D1 ordering, F4's D4 hybrid should be reassessed); (iii) F8 external-validation-loop feedback if authored; (iv) federation-scale operational pressure (per above).
- **Phase 5 section-level status labels**: this foundation doc is deliberately tag-agnostic. The Phase 5 sweep (canon-rebuild-arc follow-on) will tag sections across all canon docs in one pass with status labels. Sections here are structured to be tag-ready but are not pre-tagged.

These open questions are foundation-layer concerns. Operational mechanisms for resolving them are pattern, protocol, or deployment-layer work.
