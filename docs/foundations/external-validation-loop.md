# External-Validation-Loop

How Spore canon makes external readings of itself canon-legible — when non-Spore agents, external witnesses, or cross-tradition reviewers read Spore canon and produce feedback that may revise the canon, how that feedback enters governance without collapsing canon-authorship into canon-readership.

<!-- held-tension: 0001-pluriversal-incommensurability -->

## Scope

This foundation doc names a doctrine, not a feedback-channel. The reading-of-Spore-canon by entities outside the canon-authoring boundary — Claude Code agents interpreting Spore canon for downstream coordination, Hermes agents implementing Spore-adjacent membrane patterns, comparative-intake bridge notes capturing how external stacks materialize Spore primitives, AI-summary processes synthesizing canon for operator reading, peer-tradition reviewers evaluating Spore against their own primitives — produces structural feedback. That feedback must be canon-legible at intake time, attributable to identifiable witness contexts per F3 actor-governance, and route through governance-body channels per F3 §4.7 without short-circuiting the canon-authorship discipline.

The doctrine is broader than comparative-intake. Per F4 §43, §115, and §184 §revision-trigger-iii, F8 territory includes the meta-canon-and-operator-memory surfaces (operator-ratification process, historical-ADR state, session-memory / claude-mem / retrospective / synthesis artifacts) that F4 explicitly leaves out of representation-layer scope while flagging as F8 concern. F8 doctrine names the validation-loop discipline at foundation layer; specific intake protocols, scoring algorithms, and witness-weighting mechanisms are pattern-layer per the foundation-vs-pattern boundary inherited from F9 §4.3.

This doctrine is grounded jointly in:

- **ADR-0042 structural-legitimacy** — coupling-to-consequence as the legitimacy ground for any authority-bearing role; F8 carries the question of how external-witness feedback couples to canon revision without the witness bearing canon-authorship
- **F4 representation-authority** — the inter-layer precedence doctrine; F8 is the meta-canon complement F4 explicitly forward-references at §43, §115, and §184
- **F3 actor-governance** — governance-body composition + standing-grant + appeal-protocol routing; F8 routes external feedback through governance-body channels per F3 §4.7
- **F1 sensor-oracle-governance** — sensor-as-input-modality discipline (machine / human / AI-summary); F8 is the meta-canon parallel of F1's sensor pattern, where the "sensor" is an external agent reading canon and the "reading" is feedback rather than measurement
- **`/comparative-intake` skill + 3 bridge notes** (`docs/research/connections/claude-code-membrane-control.md`, `docs/research/connections/hermes-agent-adversarial-self-trust.md`, `docs/research/connections/claude-code-flow-coding.md`) — operational-evidence anchors documenting non-Spore agents already reading Spore canon

## Rule-Stratification (per ADR-0046)

External-validation-loop decisions stratify across the Ostrom three-level rule-stack. The rule-stratification is convention across Phase 4 admissions and applies here unmodified (8th application after F1 / F4 / F6 / F5 / F3 / F7 / F9):

- **Operational-rule** — actual external readings, intake of comparative-bridge-notes, recording of external-witness feedback, AI-summary regeneration audits, retrospective synthesis cycles.
- **Collective-choice-rule** — protocols by which a federation amends its external-validation-loop discipline: which witness types are admitted at which standing, how feedback enters canon-review queues, what evidence threshold a single external reading carries before it warrants a canon-revision proposal.
- **Constitutional-rule** — what external-validation-loop arrangements are eligible at all; how external-witness standing is established without collapsing into canon-authorship; how the loop's scope is bounded against the meta-canon surfaces F4 §43 names.

Foundation-doctrine commits to each rule-level being canon-legible. Specific protocols at collective-choice and the algorithms at operational layer are pattern-layer work; foundation doctrine commits that **an external-validation-loop arrangement without canon-legible rule-level is canon-illegible** at the corresponding scope.

## §4 Doctrine — Five-Category External-Validation-Loop Substrate

F8 carries doctrine across five categories with B5 SELECTIVE per-category synthesis-depth (1 HEAVY net-new substrate + 4 SELECTIVE substrate-rich-cite-and-layer). Per ADR-0077 method-precedent 1 + ADR-0079 §Consequences method-precedent 2, B5 honest-rigor-tracks substrate heterogeneity rather than forcing uniform B1 or B2 across heterogeneous categories.

### §4.1 External-Witness Reading-of-Canon as Authoritative-Input Source

**Principle** (HEAVY — central doctrine; net-new substrate). Spore canon is read by entities outside its canon-authoring boundary. The reading is itself an event with structural consequences for canon: an external reader's interpretation may surface a gap, an inconsistency, a translation-failure, a substrate-mismatch, or a constructive extension that canon-authors did not anticipate. F8 doctrine commits to the **external-witness-as-input** stance at three rule-levels:

- **Constitutional-rule**: every Spore federation MUST treat external-witness readings as canon-legible inputs at admission rather than as foreign objects requiring conversion before consideration. External-witness standing is GRANTED-AS-WITNESS (not granted-as-author): the witness's reading enters canon as Evidence per F1 §1, attributable to the witness context per F3 §4.1, but does NOT carry canon-authorship per ADR-0041 text-authoritative-representation. The asymmetry is load-bearing: external readings shape canon revision without becoming canon-authoritative themselves.
- **Collective-choice-rule**: federations MUST have canon-legible protocols for surfacing external-witness readings (intake mechanisms, comparative-bridge-note authorship per `/comparative-intake` skill, retrospective synthesis incorporating external feedback). Surfacing is not adoption; surfacing IS canon-legibility, which is the foundation-doctrine commitment. Specific intake-prioritization, witness-credibility weighting, and feedback-routing protocols are pattern-layer per §4.5 below.
- **Operational-rule**: external-witness reading Evidence MUST be authorable by the witness (when the witness is a known-actor with standing per F3) or by a comparative-intake-bridge-author (when the witness is an external system, agent runtime, or non-aligned reader). Canon-legible attribution MUST identify the witness context per F3 §4.1 admission discipline; AI-summary witnesses (LLM agents reading canon) inherit F4 §4.5 + F1 §6 model-provenance discipline.

**Cross-modality scope**: external-witness readings span modalities — machine-witness readings (agent-runtime parsing of canon for downstream coordination per `claude-code-membrane-control.md`), human-witness readings (cross-tradition reviewers from peer-tradition or external organization), AI-summary witnesses (LLM agents synthesizing canon for operator reading or for cross-system translation). Foundation doctrine commits to canon-legibility across modalities; specific tracking mechanisms (witness registries, intake queues, feedback dashboards) are pattern-layer.

**Asymmetry acknowledgment**: external-witness reading is NOT canon-authorship. The foundational asymmetry — readings shape revision but do not constitute canon — preserves the canon-authorship discipline per ADR-0041 (text-authoritative-representation) and the structural-legitimacy coupling per ADR-0042 (canon-authors bear coupling to canon's consequences in a way external witnesses structurally cannot). Collapsing the asymmetry would let any sufficiently-prolific external reader effectively rewrite canon by volume; preserving the asymmetry is what makes the loop a loop rather than an open-mic.

### §4.2 Routing Through Governance-Bodies (per F3 §4.7)

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite F3 §4.7 wholesale; layer external-witness-specific standing). F3 §4.7 establishes governance-body composition and member-legitimacy doctrine. F8 §4.2 commits to: **external-validation-loop feedback routes through F3 governance-body channels** rather than producing canon revisions directly. The governance-body adjudicates whether a given external-witness reading warrants:

- **Acknowledge-and-record only** (per F5 R1) — feedback enters retrospective record without further action; canon unchanged
- **Canon-review queue admission** — feedback enters the canon-review priority queue for next cycle's evaluation per `docs/research/planning/canon-review-protocol.md`
- **Immediate canon-revision proposal** — feedback warrants out-of-cycle ADR consideration (rare; reserved for structural defects or substrate-shifts)
- **Triggered foundation-doc revision** (per F4 §184 §revision-trigger-iii) — feedback fires a named revision-trigger on a specific foundation doc; the trigger's discipline applies

The governance-body's role is the routing gate. External-witness feedback that bypasses the governance-body to write canon directly violates the canon-authorship asymmetry per §4.1; the governance-body's standing per F3 §4.7 is what preserves the asymmetry in practice.

**Inheritance**: F3 §4.7 governance-body composition discipline (canon-legible scope / membership / admission-recall protocols) applies wholesale. F8 §4.2 names the external-validation-loop scope as one of the canon-relevant domains a governance-body can govern; specific governance-body authoring for external-validation-loop scope is pattern-layer.

**Multi-modality routing**: machine-witness readings + human-witness readings + AI-summary witness readings may route through the same governance-body or through modality-specific governance-bodies depending on instance composition per F7. Foundation doctrine commits to: routing-shape MUST be canon-legible at governance-body admission per F3 §4.7; ad-hoc routing (different witnesses routed differently without canon-legible policy) is canon-illegible.

### §4.3 Revision-Trigger Discipline (per F4 §184)

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite F4 §184 wholesale; operationalize the loop-back). F4 §184 names "(iii) F8 external-validation-loop feedback if authored" as one of four explicit revision-triggers for F4 representation-authority itself. F8 §4.3 generalizes the revision-trigger discipline as foundation-layer doctrine for ANY foundation doc receiving external-validation-loop feedback:

- **Named revision-triggers as canon-discipline** — every foundation doc SHOULD include a §Open Questions or §Revision-Triggers subsection naming the conditions under which external-validation feedback warrants reopening the doctrine. F4 §184 already does this; F8 §4.3 names it as canon-discipline rather than F4-specific. Future foundation-doc admissions inherit the discipline.
- **Trigger-firing as governance-body input** — when an external-witness reading fires a named trigger on a foundation doc, the trigger's firing routes to the governance-body per §4.2. The trigger does NOT auto-revise the doctrine; it admits the feedback as eligible-for-canon-review at the next cycle. The two-step (trigger fires → governance-body adjudicates) is what preserves the canon-authorship discipline.
- **Trigger-state tracking** — fired-but-unresolved triggers are canon-legible state. Foundation doctrine commits to: a foundation doc with one or more fired-but-unresolved revision-triggers operates at *triggered-pending-review* state for the trigger's scope. Other clauses of the doc remain canon-authoritative; the triggered scope is held-pending-review per ADR-0001 / ADR-0050 held-tension precedent.

**Inheritance from F4 §184**: F4 §184 names four revision-triggers (i operationally-problematic outcomes; ii cross-tradition convergence on different precedence-shape; iii F8 feedback if authored; iv federation-scale operational pressure). F8 §4.3 inherits the four-trigger shape as a template-pattern other foundation docs can adopt; the specific triggers are doc-specific, but the discipline is canon-wide.

### §4.4 Comparative-Intake-Protocol as Canonical Pipeline

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite `/comparative-intake` skill + existing bridge-note pattern; recognize as canonical F8 pipeline). The `/comparative-intake` skill (`~/projects/canon-review/skills/comparative-intake.md` per skill-plugin convention) is the canonical Spore-side pipeline for ingesting external-agent readings of Spore-adjacent material. Three bridge notes already use it: `docs/research/connections/claude-code-membrane-control.md`, `docs/research/connections/hermes-agent-adversarial-self-trust.md`, `docs/research/connections/claude-code-flow-coding.md`.

F8 §4.4 commits to:

- **Comparative-intake as canon-legible pipeline** — comparative-bridge-note authorship via `/comparative-intake` is the canon-legible intake pathway for machine-witness and AI-summary-witness readings of Spore. Bridge notes operate as Evidence per F1 §1; the bridge note's `disposition` field per learning-field-intake-protocol carries the bridge-author's preliminary judgment about how the comparative finding relates to Spore canon (clarify existing term / promote to pattern / decline-with-rationale / hold).
- **Bridge-note governance** — comparative-bridge-notes per F8 §4.4 route through governance-body channels per §4.2 when their findings warrant canon-revision proposal. Bridge notes themselves are not canon-revisions; they are external-witness Evidence per §4.1 admitted through canonical pipeline. The two-step (bridge-note authoring → governance-body adjudication) preserves the asymmetry.
- **Cross-modality bridge-notes** — bridge notes can document machine-witness readings (Claude Code parsing canon for permission-system implementation), AI-summary witness readings (LLM-mediated cross-system translation), human-witness readings (cross-tradition reviewers documenting their reading of Spore via bridge-note authoring). Foundation doctrine commits to canon-legibility across all three modalities through the same canonical pipeline.

**Inheritance from learning-field-intake-protocol v1**: the protocol's discipline (frozen vocabulary, two-phase pattern, opposition-note allowance, coverage-tracking gap-areas) carries forward as F8's intake-discipline reference. Specific intake-prioritization, batch-cadence, and bridge-author standing are pattern-layer per §4.5.

### §4.5 Cross-Modality Witness Inheritance (per F1 §2)

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite F1 §2 modality decomposition; layer external-witness-specific concerns). F1 §2 decomposes sensor-input across machine / human / AI-summary modalities. F8 §4.5 commits to: **external-witness readings inherit the same modality decomposition** with witness-specific governance concerns at each modality:

- **Machine-witness readings** (agent runtimes, automated readers, downstream coordination systems parsing canon) — inherit F1 §2 machine-sensor governance with the addition of *interpretive-stance disclosure*: the machine-witness's reading is mediated by its own implementation choices (which canon sections it parsed; how it interpreted ambiguity; which downstream actions it took based on the reading). Canon-legibility per §4.1 includes the interpretive-stance disclosure; readings without disclosed interpretive-stance operate at lower canon-legibility.
- **Human-witness readings** (cross-tradition reviewers, peer-organization reviewers, external researchers reading Spore canon) — inherit F1 §2 human-attestation governance with the addition of *tradition-attribution*: the human-witness's reading is shaped by their tradition, their relationship to Spore-adjacent practice, and their evaluative criteria. Canon-legibility per §4.1 includes tradition-attribution; anonymized or unattributed external-witness readings operate at lower canon-legibility (matches F4 §4.4 attestation-attribution discipline).
- **AI-summary witness readings** (LLM agents synthesizing canon for operator reading or cross-system translation) — inherit F1 §2 AI-summary governance + F4 §4.5 sense-making-mode scope-conditioning per ADR-0063. The asymmetry F4 §4.5 acknowledges (AI-summary processes carry authority-decay challenges; model-provider often outside instance governance reach; structural-legitimacy coupling weaker by construction) applies fully to AI-summary external-witness readings. F8 commits to: AI-summary witness readings MUST disclose model + prompt + interaction-mode (sender-receiver vs constitutively-interactive per ADR-0063); undisclosed AI-summary witness readings operate at minimum canon-legibility.

**Cross-modality compound witnesses**: when a witness reading is itself compound (e.g., human-author writing a bridge note about a machine-witness's reading of canon mediated by AI-summary), the canon-legibility of the compound reading inherits the WEAKEST constituent-modality's discipline per F1 §6 compound-oracle precedent. Foundation doctrine commits to the conservative-binding rule; specific compound-witness handling is pattern-layer.

## §6 Open Questions

External-validation-loop is a foundational commitment but not a solved problem. Several concerns are flagged here as foundation-layer residue; operational treatment is deferred to pattern, protocol, or deployment layer.

- **External-witness credibility weighting** (§4.1 + §4.5 acknowledged) — when multiple external-witness readings disagree, the governance-body per §4.2 must adjudicate; specific credibility-weighting protocols (witness-track-record scoring; cross-witness convergence; tradition-distance discounting) are pattern-layer. Foundation doctrine commits to: credibility-weighting MUST be canon-legible at governance-body protocol admission; ad-hoc weighting is canon-illegible.
- **Cross-tradition translation cost** — when an external-witness reading is from a tradition whose primitives don't map cleanly to Spore's primitives, the translation-labour to surface the reading carries reproductive-substrate cost per F9 §4.4. Foundation doctrine commits to canon-legibility of who-bears-the-translation-cost; specific cost-allocation is pattern-layer.
- **AI-summary witness regeneration-audit cadence** — AI-summary witness readings carry the regeneration-auditability concern per F1 §4.2 + F4 §125. Specific audit cadences (per-reading / per-batch / on-trigger) are pattern-layer.
- **Adversarial external-witness readings** — when an external-witness reading is adversarial (e.g., a peer organization with conflicting interests reading Spore canon to identify weaknesses), F8 doctrine commits to: adversarial readings are canon-legible Evidence per §4.1 just as cooperative readings are; the distinction is the governance-body's adjudication concern per §4.2. Specific adversarial-witness handling protocols (red-team-style intake; structured rebuttal pathways; counter-Evidence authoring) are pattern-layer; the cluster (b) red-teaming substrate per ADR-0081 §4.1 supports the foundation-doctrine without prescribing specific protocols.
- **Pluriversal external-validation** — under ADR-0001 held-tension, federations operating under structurally different ontologies of authority, evidence, and witness-standing may carry external-validation-loop doctrines incommensurable with the F8 articulation. Foundation doctrine names the residue at this level; canonical resolution remains held under ADR-0001.
- **Phase 5 section-level status labels** — this foundation doc is deliberately tag-agnostic. The Phase 5 sweep will tag sections across all canon docs in one pass.
- **DH-PM-1 PM accounting-dependence** — F8 doctrine-layer commitments are at canon-legibility + governance-body-routing scope. PM accounting-dependence held-tension operates at OPERATIONAL-INSTANCE matchmaking layer downstream of any external-validation-loop pattern admission. F8 doctrine makes no operational-matchmaking commitments; the held-tension remains intact.
- **Wave-N+ cross-repo IC + PM external-validation alignment** — IC and PM may admit their own external-validation-loop deficits in future Wave-N rounds; cross-repo translation pressure may surface (analogous to F2 ADR-0080 trigger (a)). Foundation doctrine commits to: cross-repo external-validation alignment is canon-legible at Wave-N admission discipline; the doctrine itself does not prescribe sibling-repo shape.

These open questions are foundation-layer concerns. Operational mechanisms for resolving them are pattern, protocol, or deployment layer work.
