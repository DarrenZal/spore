# Maintenance Economics

How Spore canon makes the care, provisioning, maintenance, and translation labour that reproduces coordination practice canon-legible — without prescribing which compensation protocols a federation must adopt.

<!-- held-tension: 0001-pluriversal-incommensurability -->

## Scope

This foundation doc names a doctrine, not a market. The labour that sustains coordination — sensor maintenance, governance-body upkeep, federation translation between local ontologies, the relational work that holds bilateral trust together, the unglamorous repair that keeps protocols answering reality — must be canon-legible at admission time, attributable to identifiable actors per F3 actor-governance, and grounded in coupling-to-consequence per ADR-0042 structural-legitimacy. Foundation doctrine commits to the legibility and the boundary; specific compensation-protocols (rates, pricing, allocation algorithms, currency choices) are pattern-layer per F3 §4.6 and §4.3 below.

The doctrine is broader than compensation. Per `docs/foundations/federation-protocol.md` §"Reproductive-labour invisibilisation" (the load-bearing 5-citation tradition cluster — Federici, Mies, Bresnihan, Bhattacharya, Gibson-Graham), reproductive infrastructure includes care for participants, provisioning of substrate (compute, hosting, bandwidth, attention), maintenance of running systems (sensors, attesters, agent processes, governance bodies), and translation labour across federation interfaces. All four are first-order coordination content. A federation that moves events reliably while invisibilising the work that reproduces it has not worked correctly under the commoning reading; it has extracted.

This doctrine is grounded jointly in:

- **ADR-0042 structural-legitimacy** — coupling-to-consequence as the legitimacy ground for every authority-bearing role; F9 layers the economic substrate over which couplings operate
- **ADR-0002 reproductive-commoning** — reproductive labour as a cross-cutting doctrine; F9 operationalizes the canon-legibility requirements at foundation layer
- **ADR-0049 reproduction-continuity primitive** — reproduction as the 8th primitive verb; F9 doctrine names the economic substrate within which the primitive operates at cross-episode scale
- **F1 sensor-oracle-governance §4.3 maintainer-assignment** — every sensor has a named maintainer structurally coupled to consequence; F9 §4.2 carries the economic-substrate intersection
- **F3 actor-governance §4.6 sub-shape 4 (digital-labor-as-free-gift)** — reproductive-labor recognition + labor-class standing-to-contest at actor-side; F9 §4.7 carries the economic-substrate complement and §4.3 names the foundation-vs-pattern boundary

## Rule-Stratification (per ADR-0046)

Maintenance-economics decisions stratify across the Ostrom three-level rule-stack. The rule-stratification is convention across Phase 4 admissions and applies here unmodified:

- **Operational-rule** — actual compensation events, maintenance acts, infrastructure-provisioning transactions, translation-labour outputs.
- **Collective-choice-rule** — protocols by which a federation amends its compensation arrangements: rate-setting procedures, allocation rules, attribution mechanisms, infrastructure-cost-sharing protocols.
- **Constitutional-rule** — what compensation arrangements are eligible at all; how labor-class standing is established; how reproductive-infrastructure scope is bounded; how foundation-vs-pattern boundary is maintained.

Foundation-doctrine commits to each rule-level being canon-legible. The specific protocols at collective-choice and the algorithms at operational layer are pattern-layer work; foundation doctrine commits that **a maintenance-economics decision without canon-legible rule-level is canon-illegible** at the corresponding scope.

## §4 Doctrine — Eight-Category Maintenance-Economics Substrate

F9 carries doctrine across eight categories with B5 SELECTIVE per-category synthesis-depth (2 HEAVY net-new substrate + 6 SELECTIVE substrate-rich-cite-and-layer). Per ADR-0077 method-precedent 1, B5 honest-rigor-tracks substrate heterogeneity rather than forcing uniform B1 or B2.

### §4.1 Reproductive-Labor Visibility

**Principle** (HEAVY — central doctrine; load-bearing substrate cluster). Federation-protocol.md:28 names the historically-prior capture mechanism (Federici, Mies, Bresnihan, Bhattacharya, Gibson-Graham): naturalising, de-valorising, or invisibilising the care / provisioning / maintenance / translation work that reproduces associational practice. Foundation doctrine commits to reproductive-labour visibility as first-order coordination content at three rule-levels:

- **Constitutional-rule**: every federation-protocol specification MUST identify what reproductive-infrastructure (care work, provisioning, maintenance, translation labour) is required for the protocol to function. Specifications that name only the "productive" coordination flows (events, commitments, attestations, signals) without naming the reproductive substrate are constitutional-level invisibilisation; they reproduce the substrate move that destroyed the historical commons.
- **Collective-choice-rule**: federations MUST have canon-legible protocols for surfacing reproductive-labour as Evidence (per F1 §5 longitudinal-attestation; per ADR-0049 reproduction-continuity primitive). Surfacing is not the same as compensating; surfacing IS canon-legibility, which is the foundation-doctrine commitment. Compensation-protocols are pattern-layer per §4.3 below.
- **Operational-rule**: reproductive-labour Evidence (longitudinal-attestation per ADR-0049 + ADR-0073 §5) MUST be authorable by the labourer or by a structurally-coupled witness; canon-legible attribution MUST identify the labouring actor per F3 §4.1 admission discipline.

**Cross-modality note**: reproductive-labour spans multiple modalities — physical infrastructure maintenance (machine sensors, hosting, bandwidth), human attestation work (witness practice, governance-body participation), translation labour (cross-ontology mapping, federation interface upkeep, AI-summary regeneration-audit per F1 §4.5). Foundation doctrine commits to canon-legibility across modalities; specific tracking mechanisms (timesheets, attestation registries, infrastructure-cost ledgers) are pattern-layer.

**Inheritance**: ADR-0002 reproductive-commoning extends here as cross-cutting doctrine; ADR-0049 reproduction-continuity provides the primitive-layer verb; F1 §4.3 maintainer-assignment provides the actor-attribution machinery. The 5-citation tradition cluster from federation-protocol.md:28 is the load-bearing canon-anchor.

### §4.2 Maintainer-Economics

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite F1 §4.3 + ADR-0042 wholesale; layer economic-substrate). F1 §4.3 names every sensor as having a named maintainer structurally coupled to consequence per ADR-0042. F9 layers the economic-substrate intersection: the maintainer's role implies an economic-substrate question (who bears the cost of mis-maintenance; who bears the cost of correct-maintenance; how is the labour made canon-legible without prescribing a specific compensation protocol).

Foundation doctrine commits to:

- **Maintainer-economics canon-legibility** — every maintainer-role admission per F1 §4.3 collective-choice-rule MUST include canon-legible specification of: (a) what reproductive-infrastructure the maintainer attends to (per §4.1 inheritance), (b) how the labour is canon-legibly attributable to the maintainer (per F3 §4.1), (c) how mis-maintenance consequence couples back to the maintainer (per ADR-0042). Specific compensation arrangements are pattern-layer per §4.3.
- **Maintainer-succession economic continuity** — F1 §4.3 names succession as load-bearing for replication-regime Evidence. F9 layers the economic-continuity question: maintainer-succession protocols MUST include canon-legible specification of how the economic-substrate (compensation arrangement, infrastructure-cost commitment, translation-labour load) transfers across succession. Succession protocols that transfer authority without transferring economic-substrate are decoupled-by-construction.

**Cross-modality**: machine-sensor maintainers carry compute / hosting / bandwidth costs; human-attestation maintainers carry witness-practice attention costs; AI-summary maintainers carry model-and-prompt-lifecycle costs. Foundation doctrine names the cross-modality without flattening to single-currency.

### §4.3 Foundation-vs-Pattern Boundary

**Principle** (HEAVY — net-new substrate; load-bearing). F3 §4.6 sub-shape 4 establishes that "Specific compensation-protocols are pattern-layer." F9 doctrine commits to the boundary itself as canon-legible:

- **Foundation-doctrine commits to**: (a) the canon-legibility of reproductive-infrastructure per §4.1; (b) the maintainer-economics canon-legibility per §4.2; (c) the labor-class standing-to-contest per §4.7; (d) the cross-federation portability of the doctrine itself per §4.8. Foundation-doctrine does NOT commit to: rates, pricing, allocation algorithms, currency choices, settlement mechanisms, accounting systems, payment frequencies, distribution formulas.
- **Pattern-layer holds**: the specific compensation-protocols a federation chooses (e.g., commitment-pool dividends per `docs/patterns/commitment-pooling.md`, mutual-credit arrangements per intake bridge-notes, time-banking, federated payroll, infrastructure-cost-sharing pacts, translation-labour grant pools). These are pattern-admissions per ADR-0065 pattern-library-infrastructure-spec.
- **Boundary canon-legibility requirement**: every pattern-layer compensation-protocol admitted to canon MUST cite F9 doctrine at admission and identify which foundation-doctrine clauses it operationalizes (which reproductive-infrastructure scope it covers per §4.1; which maintainer-economics clause it operationalizes per §4.2; which labor-class standing-to-contest it provides per §4.7). A compensation-protocol that does not cite F9 is not foundation-grounded; it operates as a market mechanism rather than as a coordination-grammar artifact.
- **Substitution-trap-economics-prevention** (cross-ref §4.6 below): F3 §4.6 sub-shape 4 names the digital-labor-as-free-gift failure mode. F9 §4.3 names the foundation-doctrine boundary that prevents the inverse failure mode: foundation-doctrine substituting compensation-protocol prescription for coupling-restoration. Per ADR-0048: "One can assign authority without generating comprehension." Foundation-doctrine prescribing a specific compensation-protocol would assign economic structure without generating coupling-to-consequence; the protocol-design is allocational, the coupling is generative; the former does not substitute for the latter.

The boundary is itself a doctrinal commitment, not a procedural caveat. F9 carries the foundation-doctrine; the patterns carry the protocols.

### §4.4 Translation-Labor

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite federation-protocol.md:28 + project-vision.md:283 + ADR-0034). Federation-protocol.md:28 explicitly names "the translation labour across ontologies" within the reproductive-labour cluster. Project-vision.md:283 names "Explicit translation mappings between local ontologies and shared concepts" as a Spore mechanism. ADR-0034 separates principles from mechanisms and identifies translation-mapping as mechanism-layer.

Foundation doctrine commits to:

- **Translation-labour canon-legibility** — the work of mapping local ontologies to shared concepts (or vice versa) is canon-legible reproductive-infrastructure per §4.1. Translation-labour MUST be attributable to identifiable actors per F3 §4.1; structural-legitimacy coupling per ADR-0042 applies.
- **Cross-federation translation as scope-conditioned** — translation labour at federation interfaces (where two federations with structurally different concept-vocabularies must map between each other) is canonically distinct from intra-federation translation (where one federation's local-ontology must map to its own shared-concept frame). The cross-federation case carries higher reproductive-substrate cost because both federations bear the labour; foundation doctrine commits to canon-legibility of WHICH federation bears WHICH portion at admission time.
- **AI-translation asymmetry** — when translation labour is delegated to AI agents (LLM-mediated cross-ontology mapping, agent-summary translation), F1 §6 AI-summary asymmetry applies: model-provider is often outside the instance's governance reach, and structural-legitimacy coupling is weaker. Foundation doctrine commits to: AI-translation cost-bearing MUST be canon-legible (who bears the inference cost, who bears the regeneration-audit cost, who bears the reliability cost when translation-quality decays).

Specific translation-mapping protocols (vocab-versioning, alignment-graph maintenance, cross-walk authorship) are pattern-layer per §4.3.

### §4.5 Infrastructure-Economics

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — thin substrate; honest-rigor acknowledgment). The substrate of running coordination systems (compute, hosting, bandwidth, energy, storage, network connectivity) is a reproductive-infrastructure cost per §4.1 with no canonical-anchor in current canon prior to F9. Foundation doctrine commits at minimum-viable scope:

- **Infrastructure-cost canon-legibility** — every Spore instance per F7 spore-instance-model.md MUST have canon-legible infrastructure-cost specification at admission time: who bears the compute / hosting / bandwidth / energy / storage costs; how the cost-bearing is canon-legibly attributed; how cost-shifts (scaling-up; provider-changes; outages) are surfaced as Evidence.
- **Infrastructure-substrate as reproductive-labour reified** — infrastructure costs are not separate from reproductive-labour; they are reproductive-labour reified into machine-readable resources. The same canon-legibility discipline applies: invisibilisation of who-pays-the-server-bill is structurally identical to invisibilisation of who-does-the-care-work.

Foundation doctrine acknowledges substrate thinness honestly: deeper articulation (cost-pooling protocols, infrastructure-commons formation, cross-federation infrastructure-sharing) is pattern-layer. F9 §6 Open Questions surfaces infrastructure-economics as future foundation-doctrine elaboration when substrate matures.

### §4.6 Substitution-Trap-Economics

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite ADR-0048 mode; layer economic-application). ADR-0048 names substitution-trap as a mode-across-primitives: assigning structural capacity without generating the grammar-dynamic. F9 §4.6 names the economic-application of substitution-trap discipline:

- **Compensation-without-coupling is substitution-trap** — when a federation assigns compensation arrangements without restoring or maintaining structural-legitimacy coupling per ADR-0042, the compensation operates as substitution: it allocates economic flow without generating the coupling-to-consequence the flow was meant to support. Foundation doctrine commits to: compensation-protocols admitted under §4.3 MUST identify which coupling-shape (per ADR-0042 + F3 §4.1) the compensation supports; protocols that allocate without coupling-identification are substitution-trapped.
- **Market-substitution failure mode** — when a federation's coordination becomes load-bearingly dependent on market-pricing for reproductive-infrastructure (e.g., infrastructure-costs paid via for-profit cloud providers; translation-labour paid via gig-platform piecework; care-work outsourced to extraction-economy services), the federation has substitution-trapped its reproductive-substrate. The market-mechanism allocates resource-flow but generates no coupling between the federation's own coordination-grammar and the labour the market mediates. Foundation doctrine names this as a failure-mode-of-foundation-doctrine; pattern-layer compensation-protocols MUST surface their market-dependency at admission.
- **Polanyi (1944) commodification critique** — the substitution-trap-economics frame inherits Polanyi's "fictitious commodities" (labour, land, money treated as market goods despite not being produced for sale). When reproductive-labour, federation-substrate, or translation-work is treated as fictitious commodity, the substrate move named at federation-protocol.md:28 has occurred; foundation-doctrine commits to making this visible.

Specific market-decoupling mechanisms (commitment-pool isolation from external market pricing, mutual-credit denomination, time-banking) are pattern-layer.

### §4.7 Labor-Class Standing-to-Contest

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — cite F3 §4.6 sub-shape 4 wholesale; layer F9-side complement). F3 §4.6 sub-shape 4 (digital-labor-as-free-gift) commits to "labor-class standing-to-contest" at actor-side: those bearing uncompensated reproductive-labour MUST have canon-legible standing to surface the asymmetry, route a §4.3 Layer-2 contest, and (if remediation fails) exit per §4.5 federation-encounter discipline.

F9 §4.7 commits to the economic-substrate complement:

- **Standing requires canon-legible labour-attribution** — for labor-class standing-to-contest to operate, the labour itself MUST be canon-legible per §4.1. A labourer cannot contest invisibilised labour because the labour does not exist in canon to ground the contest. Foundation doctrine commits to: every reproductive-labour Evidence per §4.1 GENERATES the standing-to-contest at the same canon-legibility-level the Evidence operates.
- **Routing inheritance** — labor-class contestation routes through F4 §5.3 Appeal-Protocol per F3 §4.7 inheritance; F9 doctrine cites this routing wholesale rather than authoring a parallel mechanism. The standing-grant per F3 §4.1 + the coupling-evidence per ADR-0042 + the labour-attribution per §4.1 + the rule-level routing per §3 + the appeal mechanics per F4 §5.3 compose into the labor-class contestation pathway.
- **Multi-modality standing** — standing operates across the three reproductive-labour modalities (machine maintenance, human attestation, AI-translation). Each modality has its own attribution-shape and consequence-shape; standing-grant MUST adapt to modality (a server-operator's standing-to-contest infrastructure-cost-shifts is different in shape from a translator's standing-to-contest interpretation-asymmetry; both are canon-legible).
- **AI-actor labour standing asymmetry** — when reproductive-labour is performed by AI agents (translation, summarization, governance-body assistance), F3 §4.1 AI-actor admission asymmetry applies: AI-actors carry weaker structural-legitimacy coupling, and labour-class standing-to-contest must be exercised by the operator-of-record per F3 §6 + F1 §6 open-question precedent. Foundation doctrine names the asymmetry without forcing premature operationalization.

### §4.8 Cross-Federation Portability

**Principle** (SELECTIVE — F3 §6 cross-federation portability deferral applies). F3 §6 Open Questions defers cross-federation actor-portability to F7 minimum-viable-spore-instance + ADR-0068 federation-encounter pattern composition work. F9 §4.8 inherits the deferral and adds the maintenance-economics dimension:

- **Maintenance-economics is federation-bounded by default** — a federation's compensation arrangements per §4.3 + maintainer-economics per §4.2 + translation-labour per §4.4 + infrastructure-economics per §4.5 are bounded to that federation's instance-composition per F7. Foundation doctrine commits to: maintenance-economics arrangements are NOT automatically portable across federations.
- **Portability requires canon-legible compatibility** — when an actor or labour-flow seeks portability across federations, the receiving federation MUST audit the source federation's maintenance-economics arrangements against its own §4.1 reproductive-infrastructure scope, §4.2 maintainer-economics, §4.3 foundation-vs-pattern-boundary discipline, §4.7 labor-class standing. Compatibility is admitted per §4.1-§4.7 alignment, not per source-federation declaration alone.
- **Reproductive-infrastructure may be cross-federation-shared** — distinct from actor-portability, reproductive-infrastructure (compute substrate, translation-vocabulary alignment, witness-practice protocols) MAY be canonically shared across federations under ADR-0068 federation-encounter pattern composition work. Foundation doctrine commits to canon-legibility of the sharing arrangement (who bears which portion of cost; how Evidence is jointly attributable; how decay surfaces to which standing-to-contest); specific cross-federation cost-sharing protocols are pattern-layer.

## §6 Open Questions

Maintenance-economics is a foundational commitment but not a solved problem. Several concerns are flagged here as foundation-layer residue; operational treatment is deferred to pattern, protocol, or deployment layer.

- **Infrastructure-economics deeper articulation** (§4.5 acknowledged thinness) — when substrate matures via concrete cross-federation infrastructure-sharing arrangements or commitment-pool-funded infrastructure-commons formations, foundation doctrine may extend §4.5 with named protocols. Substrate-readiness check at canon-review-protocol cadence.
- **Specific compensation-protocols admission** (per §4.3 pattern-layer) — pattern-admissions for compensation (commitment-pool dividend protocols, mutual-credit arrangements, time-banking, federated payroll, infrastructure-cost-sharing pacts, translation-labour grant pools) operate per ADR-0065 pattern-library-infrastructure-spec workflow; F9 doctrine inherits no admission preferences but commits to: every compensation-pattern admitted MUST cite F9 doctrine at admission per §4.3 boundary-canon-legibility requirement.
- **AI-actor labour-rights expansion** (per §4.7 + F3 §6 + F1 §6 inheritance) — as AI-actor admission asymmetry operationalizes per F3 §6 open-questions evolution, labour-class standing-to-contest will require corresponding operational protocols. Foundation doctrine declines to pick a resolution-shape pre-maturely.
- **Cross-federation cost-sharing protocols** (per §4.8 deferred-pending) — when concrete cross-federation infrastructure-sharing or translation-vocabulary alignment-sharing arrangements emerge, pattern-layer protocols can be admitted under §4.3 boundary-canon-legibility discipline.
- **Maintenance-economics temporal dynamics** — long-time-horizon cost-decay; succession-discipline at infrastructure-substrate layer (e.g., what happens when a maintainer-of-a-maintainer exits); cross-generational reproductive-labour attribution. Some substrate exists at ADR-0049 + F1 §5; specific temporal-dynamics protocols are pattern-layer.
- **Pluriversal maintenance-economics** — under ADR-0001 held-tension, federations operating under structurally different ontologies of labour, compensation, value, and infrastructure may carry maintenance-economics doctrines incommensurable with the F9 articulation. Foundation doctrine names the residue at this level; canonical resolution remains held under ADR-0001.
- **Phase 5 section-level status labels** — this foundation doc is deliberately tag-agnostic. The Phase 5 sweep will tag sections across all canon docs in one pass.
- **DH-PM-1 PM accounting-dependence** — F9 doctrine-layer commitments are at canon-legibility + foundation-vs-pattern boundary scope. PM accounting-dependence held-tension operates at OPERATIONAL-INSTANCE pricing layer downstream of any compensation-pattern admission. F9 doctrine makes no operational-pricing commitments; the held-tension remains intact.

These open questions are foundation-layer concerns. Operational mechanisms for resolving them are pattern, protocol, or deployment layer work.
